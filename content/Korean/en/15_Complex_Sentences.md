## 1. Noun Modifiers (관형사형) — Korean Relative Clauses

In English, relative clauses come **after** the noun: "the book **that I read**." In Korean, the modifying clause comes **before** the noun: "**내가 읽은** 책." This is one of the most important structural differences between English and Korean.

### 1.1 Present Tense Modifiers

The form depends on whether the verb is an **action verb** or a **descriptive verb** (adjective).

#### Action Verbs: -는

| Verb | + -는 + Noun | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 읽다 (read) | **읽는** 책 | the book (someone) is reading |
| 먹다 (eat) | **먹는** 음식 | the food (someone) is eating |
| 살다 (live) | **사는** 곳 | the place where (someone) lives |
| 일하다 (work) | **일하는** 사람 | the person who works |
| 가다 (go) | **가는** 길 | the road/way one is going |

> **Note for ㄹ stems**: ㄹ drops before -는: 살다 → 사는, 만들다 → 만드는, 알다 → 아는

**Examples**:
- 지금 **읽는** 책이 뭐예요? (What is the book you're reading now?)
- 저기에서 **일하는** 사람이 제 친구예요. (The person working over there is my friend.)
- 서울에 **사는** 사람이 많아요. (There are many people living in Seoul.)

#### Descriptive Verbs (Adjectives): -(으)ㄴ

| Verb | + -(으)ㄴ + Noun | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 예쁘다 (pretty) | **예쁜** 꽃 | a pretty flower |
| 크다 (big) | **큰** 집 | a big house |
| 작다 (small) | **작은** 가방 | a small bag |
| 좋다 (good) | **좋은** 날씨 | good weather |
| 재미있다 (fun) | **재미있는** 영화 | a fun movie |

> **Note**: 있다/없다 take **-는** (not -(으)ㄴ): 재미있**는**, 맛없**는**

**Examples**:
- **예쁜** 꽃을 샀어요. (I bought a pretty flower.)
- **큰** 도시에 살고 싶어요. (I want to live in a big city.)
- **재미있는** 이야기를 해 주세요. (Please tell me an interesting story.)

### 1.2 Past Tense Modifier: -(으)ㄴ

For **action verbs**, -(으)ㄴ indicates a completed action.

| Verb | + -(으)ㄴ + Noun | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 읽다 | **읽은** 책 | the book (someone) read |
| 먹다 | **먹은** 음식 | the food (someone) ate |
| 만나다 | **만난** 사람 | the person (someone) met |
| 가다 | **간** 곳 | the place (someone) went |
| 만들다 | **만든** 케이크 | the cake (someone) made |

**Examples**:
- 어제 **읽은** 책이 재미있었어요. (The book I read yesterday was interesting.)
- 지난주에 **만난** 사람이 기억나요? (Do you remember the person we met last week?)
- 엄마가 **만든** 음식이 제일 맛있어요. (The food mom made is the most delicious.)

### 1.3 Future Tense Modifier: -(으)ㄹ

Indicates something that **will happen** or is **intended/expected**.

| Verb | + -(으)ㄹ + Noun | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 읽다 | **읽을** 책 | the book to read |
| 먹다 | **먹을** 것 | something to eat |
| 가다 | **갈** 곳 | a place to go |
| 하다 | **할** 일 | work to do |
| 살다 | **살** 집 | a house to live in |

**Examples**:
- **읽을** 책이 많아요. (I have many books to read.)
- **먹을** 것이 없어요. (There's nothing to eat.)
- 내일 **할** 일이 많아요. (I have a lot of work to do tomorrow.)
- **살** 집을 찾고 있어요. (I'm looking for a house to live in.)

### 1.4 Past Habitual: -던

Indicates an action or state that **used to happen** or was **in progress** (but may no longer be the case).

| Verb | + -던 + Noun | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 살다 | **살던** 곳 | the place where one used to live |
| 다니다 | **다니던** 학교 | the school one used to attend |
| 좋아하다 | **좋아하던** 노래 | the song one used to like |
| 읽다 | **읽던** 책 | the book one was reading (unfinished) |
| 먹다 | **먹던** 음식 | the food one was eating / used to eat |

**Examples**:
- 어렸을 때 **살던** 곳이 그리워요. (I miss the place where I used to live as a child.)
- 대학교 때 **다니던** 카페가 아직 있어요. (The cafe I used to go to in college is still there.)
- **읽던** 책을 다시 펼쳤어요. (I reopened the book I had been reading.)

### -(으)ㄴ vs. -던 for Past

| Form | Nuance | Example |
|---|---|---|
| -(으)ㄴ | Completed fact | **간** 곳 = a place I went (completed visit) |
| -던 | Habitual/incomplete | **가던** 곳 = a place I used to go / was going |

- **만난** 사람 = a person I met (specific completed meeting)
- **만나던** 사람 = a person I used to meet / was meeting (ongoing or habitual)

### 1.5 Modifier Summary Table

| | Action verb | Descriptive verb |
|---|---|---|
| **Present** | -는 (먹는 음식) | -(으)ㄴ (맛있는 음식*) |
| **Past** | -(으)ㄴ (먹은 음식) | — |
| **Future** | -(으)ㄹ (먹을 음식) | -(으)ㄹ (맛있을 음식) |
| **Past habitual** | -던 (먹던 음식) | -던 (맛있던 음식) |

> *있다/없다 are special: they take -는 for present despite being stative.

## 2. Nominalization

Nominalization turns a verb or clause into a noun. Korean has three main ways to nominalize.

### 2.1 -기 Nominalization

**Formation**: Verb stem + -기

Used to create abstract noun phrases. Common in certain fixed patterns.

| Verb | Nominalized | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 수영하다 | **수영하기** | swimming |
| 읽다 | **읽기** | reading |
| 쓰다 | **쓰기** | writing |
| 듣다 | **듣기** | listening |
| 말하다 | **말하기** | speaking |

**Common Patterns with -기**:

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| -기 쉽다 | easy to | 한국어는 배우**기** 쉬워요. (Korean is easy to learn.) |
| -기 어렵다 | hard to | 발음하**기** 어려워요. (It's hard to pronounce.) |
| -기 시작하다 | begin to | 비가 오**기** 시작했어요. (It started to rain.) |
| -기 전에 | before doing | 먹**기** 전에 손을 씻으세요. (Wash your hands before eating.) |
| -기로 하다 | decide to | 한국에 가**기로** 했어요. (I decided to go to Korea.) |
| -기 바라다 | hope/wish | 건강하**기** 바랍니다. (I wish you good health.) |
| -기 때문에 | because | 바쁘**기** 때문에 못 가요. (I can't go because I'm busy.) |
| -기 위해(서) | in order to | 합격하**기** 위해 공부해요. (I study in order to pass.) |

**Sentences**:
- **수영하기**를 좋아해요? (Do you like swimming?) — less common than -는 것
- 일찍 일어나**기**가 힘들어요. (Getting up early is hard.)
- 한국어를 배우**기** 시작했어요. (I started learning Korean.)
- 출발하**기** 전에 전화해 주세요. (Please call me before departing.)

### 2.2 -(으)ㅁ Nominalization

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)ㅁ

More formal and literary than -기. Common in written notices, official documents, and formal expressions.

| Verb | Nominalized | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 있다 | **있음** | existing / there is |
| 없다 | **없음** | not existing / there is not |
| 알다 | **앎** | knowing |
| 다르다 | **다름** | difference / being different |
| 감사하다 | **감사함** | gratitude |

**Common Usages**:

| Context | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Written notices | 주차 금지**임** | Parking is prohibited |
| Forms/documents | 이상 **없음** | No abnormalities |
| Written endings | 위와 같**음** | Same as above |
| Formal expression | 다름이 아니라... | It's nothing other than... (formal opening) |

**Sentences**:
- 문제가 **없음**을 확인했습니다. (We confirmed that there are no problems.)
- 이 사실을 **알림**니다. (We inform you of this fact.)
- **다름**이 아니라 부탁드릴 것이 있습니다. (Actually, I have a favor to ask.) — formal letter opening

### 2.3 -는 것 Nominalization

**Formation**: Modifier form + 것 (thing)

The most versatile and commonly used nominalization. Works in virtually all contexts.

| Tense | Formation | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Present (action) | V-는 것 | **먹는 것** | eating / the act of eating |
| Present (desc.) | A-(으)ㄴ 것 | **좋은 것** | a good thing |
| Past | V-(으)ㄴ 것 | **먹은 것** | what (someone) ate |
| Future | V-(으)ㄹ 것 | **먹을 것** | what (someone) will eat |

> **Spoken contraction**: 것 → 거 in casual speech
> - 먹는 것 → 먹는 **거**, 좋은 것 → 좋은 **거**

**Examples**:
- **먹는 것**을 좋아해요. (I like eating.)
- 한국어를 **배우는 것**이 재미있어요. (Learning Korean is fun.)
- 어제 **한 것**을 말해 주세요. (Tell me what you did yesterday.)
- **읽은 것**을 이해했어요? (Did you understand what you read?)
- 내일 **할 것**이 많아요. (I have a lot to do tomorrow.)

### -기 vs. -는 것

| Feature | -기 | -는 것 |
|---|---|---|
| Register | Slightly formal | Versatile (spoken + written) |
| Used as subject | ✅ (less common) | ✅ (very common) |
| Used as object | ⚠️ Limited patterns | ✅ Broadly |
| Fixed expressions | 많다 (-기 시작하다, -기 전에) | 많다 (-는 것 같다, -는 것이 좋다) |
| "I like X-ing" | 수영하기를 좋아해요 (OK but stiff) | 수영하는 것을 좋아해요 / 수영하는 걸 좋아해요 (natural) |

## 3. Sentence-Final Patterns (종결어미)

These endings appear at the end of sentences and add nuance, attitude, or interpersonal meaning beyond simple statement or question.

### 3.1 -거든요 — Providing Reason / Background

**Formation**: Verb stem + -거든요

**Meaning**: "You see, ..." / "The thing is, ..." — provides background explanation or justification. Often used when the listener doesn't know the reason.

**Examples**:
- 오늘 못 만나요. 내일 시험이 있**거든요**. (I can't meet today. You see, I have an exam tomorrow.)
- 택시를 탔어요. 버스가 안 오**거든요**. (I took a taxi. The thing is, the bus wasn't coming.)
- 한국 음식을 좋아해요. 매운 음식을 좋아하**거든요**. (I like Korean food. You see, I like spicy food.)

> **Usage note**: -거든요 typically appears in the **second** sentence, explaining the **first**. It answers an implicit "why?"

### 3.2 -잖아요 — Pointing Out Known Information

**Formation**: Verb stem + -잖아요

**Meaning**: "You know..." / "As you know..." — reminds the listener of something they should already know. Can sound slightly reproachful.

**Examples**:
- 제가 말했**잖아요**. (I told you, didn't I? / I said so!)
- 내일 시험이**잖아요**. 공부하세요. (Tomorrow is the exam, you know. Study.)
- 저도 학생이**잖아요**. 돈이 없어요. (I'm also a student, you know. I don't have money.)
- 약속했**잖아요**. 왜 안 왔어요? (You promised, didn't you? Why didn't you come?)

### 3.3 -네요 — Mild Surprise / Realization

**Formation**: Verb stem + -네요

**Meaning**: "Oh, I see..." / "Wow..." — expresses the speaker's realization or mild surprise upon discovering something new.

**Examples**:
- 한국어를 잘하시**네요**! (Oh, you speak Korean well!)
- 날씨가 정말 좋**네요**. (Oh, the weather is really nice.)
- 여기 사람이 많**네요**. (Wow, there are a lot of people here.)
- 벌써 12시**네요**. (Oh, it's already 12 o'clock.)
- 이 케이크 맛있**네요**. (Oh, this cake is delicious.)

### 3.4 -군요 / -구나 — Exclamatory Discovery

**Formation**:
- Polite: V/A-군요, N-이군요
- Casual (반말): V/A-구나, N-이구나

**Meaning**: "Oh! So that's how it is!" — stronger realization or discovery than -네요. Often used when learning new information.

**Examples**:
- 여기가 서울이**군요**! (Oh, so this is Seoul!)
- 한국어를 공부하는**군요**. (Oh, so you're studying Korean.)
- 정말 맛있**군요**! (Oh, it's really delicious!)
- 그렇**구나**. (Oh, I see. / Oh, is that so.) — casual
- 벌써 졸업했**구나**! (Oh, you already graduated!) — casual

### 3.5 -(으)ㄹ게요 — Promise / Speaker's Decision

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ게요

**Meaning**: "I will..." — the speaker makes a decision or promise. Only used for **first person** (I/we).

**Examples**:
- 내일 전화**할게요**. (I'll call you tomorrow.)
- 제가 **할게요**. (I'll do it.)
- 내일 일찍 **갈게요**. (I'll go early tomorrow.)
- 다음에 꼭 **올게요**. (I'll definitely come next time.)
- 열심히 공부**할게요**. (I'll study hard.)

> **-(으)ㄹ게요 vs. -(으)ㄹ 거예요**:
> - -(으)ㄹ게요: Promise/decision made now, directed at listener
> - -(으)ㄹ 거예요: Pre-existing plan or prediction, can be about anyone

### 3.6 -(으)ㄹ까요? — Suggestion / Wondering

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ까요?

**Meaning**: Two uses — (1) making a suggestion, (2) wondering about something.

**Suggestion (shall we...?)**:
- 같이 **갈까요**? (Shall we go together?)
- 뭐 먹**을까요**? (What shall we eat?)
- 창문을 열**까요**? (Shall I open the window?)

**Wondering (I wonder if...)**:
- 내일 비가 올**까요**? (I wonder if it will rain tomorrow.)
- 그 사람이 올**까요**? (I wonder if that person will come.)
- 이게 맞을**까요**? (I wonder if this is correct.)

### Sentence-Final Patterns Summary

| Pattern | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| -거든요 | Background reason | 내일 시험이 있거든요. |
| -잖아요 | Reminding known info | 제가 말했잖아요. |
| -네요 | Mild surprise | 한국어를 잘하시네요! |
| -군요/-구나 | Exclamatory discovery | 여기가 서울이군요! |
| -(으)ㄹ게요 | Promise/decision (1st person) | 내일 전화할게요. |
| -(으)ㄹ까요? | Suggestion / wondering | 같이 갈까요? |

## 4. Combining Multiple Patterns

Real Korean sentences often layer multiple grammar patterns. Here are examples showing how the patterns from this lesson and previous lessons combine.

### Pattern Layering Examples

**Modifier + Nominalization**:
- 어제 **읽은 것**이 기억나요? (Do you remember what you read yesterday?)
- 제가 **만든 것**을 드세요. (Please eat what I made.)

**Modifier + Sentence-Final**:
- 지금 **읽는** 책이 정말 재미있**네요**. (Oh, the book you're reading now is really interesting.)
- 어제 **만난** 사람이 민수**였군요**! (Oh, the person I met yesterday was Minsu!)

**Connective + Modifier**:
- 한국에서 **사는** 것이 재미있**지만** 가끔 힘들어요. (Living in Korea is fun, but sometimes it's hard.)
- **읽을** 책이 많**아서** 바빠요. (I have many books to read, so I'm busy.)

**Multiple Patterns in One Sentence**:
- 어제 **만난** 사람이 한국어를 정말 잘하**는** 사람이**었거든요**. (You see, the person I met yesterday was someone who speaks Korean really well.)
- 내일 **할** 일이 많**으니까** 오늘 일찍 자**ㄹ게요**. (Since I have a lot to do tomorrow, I'll go to bed early today.)
- 제가 **만든** 음식을 **드시는** 것을 보**니까** 기분이 좋**네요**. (Seeing you eat the food I made, I feel good.)

### Building Complex Sentences Step by Step

Let's build: "The Korean food that I ate at the restaurant my friend recommended was really delicious."

1. Start with the core: 한국 음식이 정말 맛있었어요. (The Korean food was really delicious.)
2. Add "that I ate": **먹은** 한국 음식이 정말 맛있었어요.
3. Add "at the restaurant": 식당에서 **먹은** 한국 음식이 정말 맛있었어요.
4. Add "that my friend recommended": 친구가 **추천한** 식당에서 **먹은** 한국 음식이 정말 맛있었어요.

Final: **친구가 추천한 식당에서 먹은 한국 음식이 정말 맛있었어요.**

Combine the given elements into a single Korean sentence.

1. [the person who came yesterday] + [is my friend]
   → Hint: 어제 / 오다 (past modifier) / 사람 / 제 친구예요

2. [what I want to eat] + [is bibimbap]
   → Hint: 먹다 (future modifier — desire) / 것 / 비빔밥이에요

3. [I like] + [watching movies]
   → Hint: 영화를 보다 (-는 것) / 좋아해요

4. [since I have a lot to do tomorrow] + [I'll sleep early today]
   → Hint: 내일 / 하다 (future modifier) / 일 / 많다 / -(으)니까 / 일찍 자다 / -(으)ㄹ게요

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 어제 **온** 사람이 제 친구예요. (The person who came yesterday is my friend.)
2. **먹고 싶은 것**은 비빔밥이에요. (What I want to eat is bibimbap.) — or: 먹을 것은 비빔밥이에요.
3. 영화를 **보는 것을** 좋아해요. (I like watching movies.) — or: 영화 보는 걸 좋아해요.
4. 내일 **할** 일이 **많으니까** 오늘 일찍 **잘게요**. (Since I have a lot to do tomorrow, I'll sleep early today.)

</details>

### Exercise 5: Translation

Translate into Korean, using the patterns you have learned.

1. The place where I used to live was Busan.
2. It's hard to wake up early in the morning.
3. Oh, this coffee is really delicious!
4. You know tomorrow is a holiday, right? Let's rest.
5. I can't go today. You see, I'm sick.

<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 제가 **살던** 곳은 부산이었어요.
2. 아침에 일찍 일어나**기**가 힘들어요.
3. 이 커피 정말 맛있**네요**!
4. 내일 휴일이**잖아요**. 쉽시다. / 쉬어요.
5. 오늘 못 가요. 아프**거든요**.

</details>

### Exercise 6: Modifier Tense Distinction

Choose the correct modifier form for each context.

1. 지금 _______ 사람이 누구예요? (노래하다 — "the person singing now")
2. 아까 _______ 사람이 누구였어요? (노래하다 — "the person who sang earlier")
3. 다음에 _______ 사람은 김민수 씨입니다. (노래하다 — "the person who will sing next")
4. 학교 다닐 때 항상 _______ 사람이 그리워요. (노래하다 — "the person who always used to sing")

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 지금 **노래하는** 사람 (-는 present action)
2. 아까 **노래한** 사람 (-(으)ㄴ past action)
3. 다음에 **노래할** 사람 (-(으)ㄹ future)
4. 항상 **노래하던** 사람 (-던 past habitual)

</details>

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Noun modifiers** are Korean's equivalent of relative clauses — the modifier comes **before** the noun
2. **-는** (action, present), **-(으)ㄴ** (descriptive, present / action, past), **-(으)ㄹ** (future), **-던** (habitual past)
3. **-는 것** is the most versatile nominalization for everyday speech
4. **-기** is preferred in fixed patterns (-기 전에, -기 시작하다, -기 쉽다/어렵다)
5. **Sentence-final patterns** add critical nuance — learn when to use -거든요 (explaining), -잖아요 (reminding), -네요 (surprise), -군요 (discovery)
