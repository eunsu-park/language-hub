## 1. Korean Adjectives = Descriptive Verbs

Unlike English, where adjectives are a separate part of speech (e.g., "The house is **big**"), Korean adjectives are a type of verb called **descriptive verbs** (형용사, hyeong-yong-sa). They conjugate just like action verbs.

> **Korean Adjectives vs. English Adjectives**
>
> - **English:** "The house is big." — noun + be-verb + adjective
> - **Korean:** "집이 커요." (jib-i keo-yo) — noun + particle + descriptive verb
>
> Key insight: 크다 (keuda, "to be big") IS the adjective.
> There is no separate "is" -- the adjective already contains it.
>
> - Dictionary form: 크다 (to be big)
> - Conjugated: 커요 (is big -- polite)
> - Attributive: 큰 + noun (big [noun])

This means:

- **You do NOT need 이다** (the copula "to be") with adjectives
- Adjectives go at the **end of the sentence** (verb position, following SOV order)
- Adjectives conjugate for **tense, politeness, and mood** just like verbs

| English | Korean (Dictionary) | Korean (Polite) | Literal Meaning |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| The room is small. | 방이 작다. | 방이 작아요. | Room [subj] is-small. |
| The weather is good. | 날씨가 좋다. | 날씨가 좋아요. | Weather [subj] is-good. |
| The food is delicious. | 음식이 맛있다. | 음식이 맛있어요. | Food [subj] is-delicious. |

## 2. Common Adjectives

### 2.1 Size and Quantity

| Korean | Romanization | Meaning | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------|
| 크다 | keuda | to be big | 이 집이 커요. (This house is big.) |
| 작다 | jakda | to be small | 고양이가 작아요. (The cat is small.) |
| 많다 | manta | to be many/much | 사람이 많아요. (There are many people.) |
| 적다 | jeokda | to be few/little | 돈이 적어요. (There is little money.) |

### 2.2 Quality and Value

| Korean | Romanization | Meaning | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------|
| 좋다 | jota | to be good | 날씨가 좋아요. (The weather is good.) |
| 나쁘다 | nappeuda | to be bad | 기분이 나빠요. (The mood is bad.) |
| 새롭다 | saeropda | to be new | 이 기술이 새로워요. (This technology is new.) |
| 오래되다 | oraedoeda | to be old (things) | 이 건물이 오래됐어요. (This building is old.) |

### 2.3 Price and Taste

| Korean | Romanization | Meaning | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------|
| 비싸다 | bissada | to be expensive | 이 가방이 비싸요. (This bag is expensive.) |
| 싸다 | ssada | to be cheap | 여기 음식이 싸요. (Food here is cheap.) |
| 맛있다 | masitda | to be delicious | 김치가 맛있어요. (Kimchi is delicious.) |
| 맛없다 | maseoptda | to be not tasty | 이 빵이 맛없어요. (This bread is not tasty.) |

### 2.4 Interest and Appearance

| Korean | Romanization | Meaning | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------|
| 재미있다 | jaemiitda | to be fun/interesting | 이 영화가 재미있어요. (This movie is fun.) |

| 예쁘다 | yeppeuda | to be pretty | 꽃이 예뻐요. (The flowers are pretty.) |

## 3. Adjective Conjugation in 해요체

Korean adjectives follow the same vowel harmony rules as action verbs for the polite informal (해요체) conjugation.

### 3.1 Basic Conjugation Rules

> **Adjective Conjugation Rules (해요체)**
>
> 1. Remove 다 from dictionary form → get the STEM
> 2. Look at the LAST VOWEL of the stem
> 3. Apply vowel harmony:
>    - Last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ → stem + 아요
>    - Last vowel is anything else → stem + 어요
>    - Stem ends in 하 → stem + 여요 (= 해요)

### 3.2 Conjugation Examples

| Dictionary | Stem | Last Vowel | Rule | 해요체 |
|-----------|------|-----------|------|-------|
| 크다 (big) | 크 | ㅡ | + 어요 | 커요 (ㅡ+ㅓ contract) |
| 작다 (small) | 작 | ㅏ | + 아요 | 작아요 |
| 좋다 (good) | 좋 | ㅗ | + 아요 | 좋아요 |
| 나쁘다 (bad) | 나쁘 | ㅡ | + 어요 | 나빠요 (ㅡ drops, ㅃ) |
| 많다 (many) | 많 | ㅏ | + 아요 | 많아요 |
| 비싸다 (expensive) | 비싸 | ㅏ | + 아요 | 비싸요 (ㅏ+ㅏ contract) |
| 예쁘다 (pretty) | 예쁘 | ㅡ | + 어요 | 예뻐요 (ㅡ drops) |
| 덥다 (hot) | 덥 | ㅓ | + 어요 | 더워요 (ㅂ irregular) |
| 춥다 (cold) | 춥 | ㅜ | + 어요 | 추워요 (ㅂ irregular) |

> **Note on ㅂ-irregular adjectives**: Many adjectives ending in ㅂ are irregular. The ㅂ changes to 우 before a vowel ending: 덥다 → 더워요, 춥다 → 추워요, 새롭다 → 새로워요.

### 3.3 Past Tense

To form past tense, replace 요 with ㅆ어요:

| Present (해요체) | Past (해요체) | Meaning |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| 커요 | 컸어요 | was big |
| 작아요 | 작았어요 | was small |
| 좋아요 | 좋았어요 | was good |
| 더워요 | 더웠어요 | was hot |
| 추워요 | 추웠어요 | was cold |
| 예뻐요 | 예뻤어요 | was pretty |

## 4. Attributive Form: Adjectives Before Nouns

When an adjective directly modifies a noun (e.g., "big house"), Korean uses the **attributive form** with -ㄴ/은.

### 4.1 Formation Rule

> **Attributive Form of Adjectives (-ㄴ/은)**
>
> - Stem ends in **VOWEL** → stem + ㄴ: 크 → 큰, 싸 → 싼, 예쁘 → 예쁜
> - Stem ends in **CONSONANT** → stem + 은: 작 → 작은, 많 → 많은, 좋 → 좋은
> - **ㅂ-irregular**: ㅂ → 우 + ㄴ = 운: 덥 → 더운, 춥 → 추운, 새롭 → 새로운

### 4.2 Examples

| Adjective | Attributive | + Noun | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|--------|---------|
| 크다 (big) | 큰 | 큰 집 | big house |
| 작다 (small) | 작은 | 작은 고양이 | small cat |
| 좋다 (good) | 좋은 | 좋은 날씨 | good weather |
| 나쁘다 (bad) | 나쁜 | 나쁜 사람 | bad person |
| 비싸다 (expensive) | 비싼 | 비싼 옷 | expensive clothes |
| 싸다 (cheap) | 싼 | 싼 가격 | cheap price |
| 새롭다 (new) | 새로운 | 새로운 시작 | new beginning |
| 덥다 (hot) | 더운 | 더운 날 | hot day |
| 예쁘다 (pretty) | 예쁜 | 예쁜 꽃 | pretty flower |
| 맛있다 (delicious) | 맛있는* | 맛있는 음식 | delicious food |

> **Important**: 있다/없다 compounds (맛있다, 재미있다, 멋있다) use **-는** instead of -ㄴ/은 for the attributive form because they contain 있다 (an existence verb, not a pure adjective).

### 4.3 Using Attributive Adjectives in Sentences

```
큰 집에서 살고 싶어요.
keun jib-eseo salgo sipeoyo.
I want to live in a big house.

작은 가방을 주세요.
jageun gabang-eul juseyo.
Please give me a small bag.

좋은 아이디어예요!
joeun aidieo-yeyo!
That's a good idea!

맛있는 한국 음식을 먹었어요.
masinneun hanguk eumsig-eul meogeosseoyo.
I ate delicious Korean food.
```

## 5. Comparative Expressions

Korean comparatives use the structure: **더 (deo, "more") + adjective + 보다 (boda, "than")**.

### 5.1 Basic Comparative: A가 B보다 더 [adjective]

> **Comparative Structure**
>
> A가/이 B보다 더 [adjective]
> (subj) (than) (more) (descriptive verb)
>
> Example: 사과가 배보다 더 커요.
> (sagwa-ga bae-boda deo keoyo)
> Apples are bigger than pears.
> (apple [subj] pear [than] more is-big)

### 5.2 Comparative Examples

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 한국어가 일본어보다 더 쉬워요. | Korean is easier than Japanese. |
| 여름이 겨울보다 더 더워요. | Summer is hotter than winter. |
| 이 식당이 그 식당보다 더 맛있어요. | This restaurant is more delicious than that one. |
| 오빠가 저보다 더 키가 커요. | My older brother is taller than me. |

> **Note**: 더 can be omitted when 보다 already implies comparison: 사과가 배보다 커요 is also correct.

### 5.3 "Less" Comparative: 덜

To say "less," use **덜 (deol)** instead of 더:

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 오늘이 어제보다 덜 추워요. | Today is less cold than yesterday. |
| 이것이 그것보다 덜 비싸요. | This is less expensive than that. |

## 6. Superlative Expressions

Korean has two words for "most": **가장 (gajang)** (standard/written) and **제일 (jeil)** (colloquial/spoken).

### 6.1 Structure: 가장/제일 + [adjective]

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 가장 큰 나라 | the biggest country |
| 제일 맛있는 음식 | the most delicious food |
| 한국에서 가장 높은 산은 한라산이에요. | The tallest mountain in Korea is Hallasan. |
| 반에서 제일 똑똑한 학생이에요. | (He/She) is the smartest student in the class. |
| 이것이 가장 좋아요. | This is the best. |

### 6.2 가장 vs. 제일

| 가장 | 제일 |
|-----|-----|
| Standard, literary | Colloquial, spoken |
| Used in formal writing | Used in everyday speech |
| 가장 중요한 일 | 제일 중요한 일 |

Both are correct in most contexts. Use 가장 for essays and formal situations; use 제일 in daily conversation.

## 7. Negative Adjectives

Korean has two ways to negate adjectives, identical to verb negation.

### 7.1 Short Negation: 안 + adjective

Place **안** directly before the conjugated adjective:

| Positive | Negative | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 커요 | 안 커요 | is not big |
| 좋아요 | 안 좋아요 | is not good |
| 비싸요 | 안 비싸요 | is not expensive |
| 예뻐요 | 안 예뻐요 | is not pretty |

### 7.2 Long Negation: stem + -지 않다

Attach **-지 않다** to the adjective stem, then conjugate 않다:

| Positive | Negative (Long) | Meaning |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| 크다 | 크지 않아요 | is not big |
| 좋다 | 좋지 않아요 | is not good |
| 비싸다 | 비싸지 않아요 | is not expensive |
| 덥다 | 덥지 않아요 | is not hot |

> **Nuance**: The long form (-지 않다) sounds slightly softer and more formal. The short form (안) is more common in casual speech.

### 7.3 Examples in Context

```
오늘 날씨가 안 추워요.
oneul nalssi-ga an chuwoyo.
The weather isn't cold today.

이 영화가 재미있지 않았어요.
i yeonghwa-ga jaemiitji anasseoyo.
This movie wasn't interesting.

그 식당은 안 비싸요. 괜찮아요.
geu sikdang-eun an bissayo. gwaenchanayo.
That restaurant isn't expensive. It's okay.
```

## 8. Adjective Pairs and Opposites

Learning adjectives in pairs aids memorization and broadens vocabulary.

### 8.1 Physical Properties

| Korean | Meaning | Opposite | Meaning |
|--------|---------|----------|---------|
| 크다 | big | 작다 | small |
| 길다 | long | 짧다 | short |
| 높다 | high/tall | 낮다 | low/short |
| 넓다 | wide | 좁다 | narrow |
| 무겁다 | heavy | 가볍다 | light |
| 두껍다 | thick | 얇다 | thin |

### 8.2 Quality and Value

| Korean | Meaning | Opposite | Meaning |
|--------|---------|----------|---------|
| 좋다 | good | 나쁘다 | bad |
| 새롭다 | new | 오래되다 | old (things) |
| 비싸다 | expensive | 싸다 | cheap |
| 깨끗하다 | clean | 더럽다 | dirty |
| 쉽다 | easy | 어렵다 | difficult |

### 8.3 Feelings and Sensations

| Korean | Meaning | Opposite | Meaning |
|--------|---------|----------|---------|
| 덥다 | hot (weather) | 춥다 | cold (weather) |
| 뜨겁다 | hot (object) | 차갑다 | cold (object) |
| 기쁘다 | happy/glad | 슬프다 | sad |
| 재미있다 | fun | 재미없다 | boring |
| 바쁘다 | busy | 한가하다 | free/idle |

## 9. Special Adjective Patterns

### 9.1 있다/없다 Compound Adjectives

Several common adjectives are compounds of a noun + 있다 (to have/exist) or 없다 (to not have/not exist):

| Compound | Components | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| 맛있다 | 맛 (taste) + 있다 | delicious (lit. "has taste") |
| 맛없다 | 맛 (taste) + 없다 | not tasty (lit. "has no taste") |
| 재미있다 | 재미 (fun) + 있다 | fun/interesting |
| 재미없다 | 재미 (fun) + 없다 | boring |
| 멋있다 | 멋 (style) + 있다 | cool/stylish |
| 멋없다 | 멋 (style) + 없다 | uncool |

> **Remember**: These use **-는** (not -ㄴ/은) in attributive form: 맛있**는** 음식, 재미있**는** 영화.

### 9.2 하다 Adjectives

Many Korean adjectives are formed with **noun + 하다**:

| Korean | Meaning | Attributive |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 행복하다 | happy | 행복한 |
| 건강하다 | healthy | 건강한 |
| 조용하다 | quiet | 조용한 |
| 깨끗하다 | clean | 깨끗한 |
| 유명하다 | famous | 유명한 |
| 중요하다 | important | 중요한 |
| 필요하다 | necessary | 필요한 |
| 특별하다 | special | 특별한 |

These are easy to conjugate: just treat 하다 as the verb.

- 행복하다 → 행복해요 (is happy) → 행복했어요 (was happy)
- 조용하다 → 조용해요 (is quiet) → 조용한 방 (quiet room)
