## 1. Overview of Korean Speech Levels

Korean has **7 traditional speech levels**, but only **4 are commonly used** in modern Korean. The speech level is encoded in the **verb ending** at the end of every sentence.

### The 7 Speech Levels

| Level | Name | Register | Modern usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **합쇼체** (하십시오체) | Formal polite | ✅ Very common |
| 2 | **해요체** | Informal polite | ✅ Most common |
| 3 | **하오체** | Formal neutral | ❌ Archaic (historical dramas) |
| 4 | **하게체** | Semi-formal | ⚠️ Limited use |
| 5 | **해라체** | Formal plain | ⚠️ Writing, quotes, diary |
| 6 | **해체** (반말) | Informal plain | ✅ Very common |
| 7 | **해체** (intimate) | Intimate | (merged with 해체) |

In daily life, you primarily need: **합쇼체, 해요체, 해체**. 하게체 appears in specific contexts.

### Quick Comparison with 가다 (to go)

| Speech level | Present | Past | Future |
|---|---|---|---|
| 합쇼체 (formal polite) | 갑니다 | 갔습니다 | 갈 겁니다 |
| 해요체 (informal polite) | 가요 | 갔어요 | 갈 거예요 |
| 해체 (반말) | 가 | 갔어 | 갈 거야 |
| 하게체 (semi-formal) | 가네 | 갔네 | 갈 걸세 |

## 2. 합쇼체 — Formal Polite

The highest commonly used speech level. Shows strong respect and formality.

### Formation

| Sentence type | Vowel stem | Consonant stem | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Statement | -ㅂ니다 | -습니다 | 갑니다 / 먹습니다 |
| Question | -ㅂ니까? | -습니까? | 갑니까? / 먹습니까? |
| Suggestion | -ㅂ시다 | -읍시다 | 갑시다 / 먹읍시다 |
| Command | -(으)십시오 | -(으)십시오 | 가십시오 / 드십시오 |

### When to Use 합쇼체

- **News broadcasts and announcements**: 다음 뉴스를 전해 드리겠습니다. (I will deliver the next news.)
- **Formal presentations**: 지금부터 발표를 시작하겠습니다. (I will now begin the presentation.)
- **Military and police**: 충성! 보고합니다! (Loyalty! Reporting!)
- **First meetings with elders**: 처음 뵙겠습니다. (Nice to meet you.)
- **Customer service**: 어서 오십시오. (Welcome.)
- **Public speeches**: 국민 여러분, 감사합니다. (Citizens, thank you.)

### Example Sentences

- 저는 김민수**입니다**. (I am Kim Minsu.)
- 한국어를 공부**합니다**. (I study Korean.)
- 어디에서 오셨**습니까**? (Where did you come from?)
- 같이 **갑시다**. (Let's go together.)
- 여기 앉으**십시오**. (Please sit here.)

## 3. 해요체 — Informal Polite

The **default** speech level for most everyday conversations. Polite but not stiff.

### Formation

Verb stem + -아/어요 (following vowel harmony rules from Lesson 11)

| Sentence type | Formation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Statement | -아/어요 | 가요 / 먹어요 |
| Question | -아/어요? (rising intonation) | 가요? / 먹어요? |
| Suggestion | -아/어요 (or -(으)ㄹ까요?) | 가요 / 갈까요? |
| Command | -아/어요 (or -(으)세요) | 가요 / 가세요 |

### When to Use 해요체

- **Most daily conversations**: with acquaintances, colleagues, strangers
- **Shopping and restaurants**: 이거 주세요. (Please give me this.)
- **With people your age at first meeting**: safe default choice
- **With in-laws or spouse's family**: respectful yet warm
- **Workplace**: most common between colleagues

### Example Sentences

- 오늘 날씨가 좋**아요**. (Today's weather is nice.)
- 뭐 먹을 거**예요**? (What are you going to eat?)
- 저는 학생이**에요**. (I'm a student.)
- 같이 가**요**. (Let's go together.)
- 천천히 말해 주**세요**. (Please speak slowly.)

### 합쇼체 vs. 해요체

| Situation | 합쇼체 | 해요체 |
|---|---|---|
| Job interview | ✅ Preferred | ⚠️ Acceptable |
| Chatting with coworker | ❌ Too stiff | ✅ Natural |
| News broadcast | ✅ Standard | ❌ Too casual |
| Talking to friend's parent | ⚠️ Very formal | ✅ Natural |
| Wedding speech | ✅ Expected | ⚠️ Too casual |

---

## 4. 해체 — Informal / 반말

Used among close friends, to children, or to people clearly younger. Using 반말 to the wrong person is a serious social breach.

### Formation

Same as 해요체 but **drop the 요**.

| 해요체 | 해체 (반말) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 가요 | 가 | go |
| 먹어요 | 먹어 | eat |
| 했어요 | 했어 | did |
| 좋아요 | 좋아 | good |
| 갈 거예요 | 갈 거야 | will go |
| 뭐예요? | 뭐야? | what is it? |
| -이에요/예요 | -이야/야 | is (copula) |

### When to Use 반말

- Among **close friends of the same age** (or with explicit permission)
- Speaking to **children**
- Speaking to someone **clearly younger** (with care — age difference matters)
- **Couples** (once the relationship is established)
- In your **own thoughts** or talking to yourself

### Example Sentences

- 어디 **가**? (Where are you going?)
- 밥 **먹었어**? (Did you eat?)
- 이거 진짜 맛있**어**! (This is really delicious!)
- 내일 뭐 **할 거야**? (What are you going to do tomorrow?)
- 나 학생**이야**. (I'm a student.)

### ⚠️ Social Rules for 반말

| Scenario | Appropriate? |
|---|---|
| To a close friend your age | ✅ Yes |
| To a stranger your age | ❌ No (use 해요체) |
| To a child | ✅ Yes |
| To an older person | ❌ Never (unless they ask) |
| After 반말 허락 (permission) | ✅ Yes |

---

## 5. 하게체 — Semi-Formal

A middle level between 해요체 and 반말. Less common but still encountered.

### Formation

| Sentence type | Ending | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Statement | -네 / -(으)ㄹ세 | 가네 / 갈세 |
| Question | -나? / -(으)ㄴ가? | 가나? / 먹었는가? |
| Command | -게 | 가게 / 앉게 |
| Suggestion | -세 | 가세 / 앉으세 |

### When to Use 하게체

- **Older adults speaking to younger adults** (e.g., a professor to a student they know)
- **Father-in-law to son-in-law** (traditional)
- **Literary and academic writing** (older style)
- **Some dialects** (more common in certain regions)

### Example Sentences

- 자네, 요즘 어떻**나**? (How have you been these days?) — older person to younger adult
- 그래, 잘 먹었**네**. (Yes, I ate well.)
- 어서 앉**게**. (Go ahead and sit.)
- 이리 와 보**게**. (Come here and look.)

> **For learners**: You don't need to actively use 하게체, but you should recognize it when you hear it, especially from older Koreans or in Korean dramas.

## 6. Subject Honorific -(으)시-

The **subject honorific** suffix -(으)시- is inserted into the verb to show respect for the **subject** of the sentence (the person doing the action). This is independent of speech level.

### Formation

| Stem ending | Honorific suffix | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Vowel | -시- | 가다 → 가**시**다 |
| Consonant | -으시- | 읽다 → 읽**으시**다 |
| ㄹ ending | -시- (ㄹ drops) | 살다 → 사**시**다 |

### Combined with Speech Levels

| Base | 합쇼체 | 해요체 | Past |
|---|---|---|---|
| 가다 | 가십니다 | 가세요 | 가셨어요 |
| 먹다 | 드십니다 | 드세요 | 드셨어요 |
| 읽다 | 읽으십니다 | 읽으세요 | 읽으셨어요 |
| 살다 | 사십니다 | 사세요 | 사셨어요 |
| 있다 | 계십니다 | 계세요 | 계셨어요 |

### When to Use -(으)시-

| Subject | Use -(으)시-? | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Teacher, professor | ✅ Yes | 선생님이 오**셨**어요. (The teacher came.) |
| Parent, grandparent | ✅ Yes | 어머니가 요리를 하**세요**. (Mother is cooking.) |
| Boss, senior colleague | ✅ Yes | 부장님이 말씀하**셨**어요. (The manager said.) |
| Friend (same age) | ❌ No | 민수가 왔어요. (Minsu came.) |
| Yourself | ❌ Never | ~~제가 가셨어요~~ → 제가 갔어요 |
| Customer (in service) | ✅ Yes | 주문하**시**겠어요? (Would you like to order?) |

### Common -(으)시- Patterns

- **-(으)세요**: polite imperative/question — 앉으세요. (Please sit.)
- **-(으)셨어요**: past honorific — 잘 주무셨어요? (Did you sleep well?)
- **-(으)실 거예요**: future honorific — 언제 오실 거예요? (When will you come?)
- **-(으)시겠어요**: polite intention/offer — 뭐 드시겠어요? (What would you like to eat?)

## 7. Honorific Vocabulary Pairs

Korean has special **honorific words** used when the subject is someone you respect. Using these is just as important as adding -(으)시-.

### Verb Pairs

| Plain | Honorific | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 먹다 | **드시다** / **잡수시다** | to eat |
| 마시다 | **드시다** | to drink |
| 자다 | **주무시다** | to sleep |
| 있다 (to exist/be) | **계시다** | to be (somewhere) |
| 없다 | **안 계시다** | to not be (somewhere) |
| 죽다 | **돌아가시다** | to die (pass away) |
| 말하다 | **말씀하시다** | to speak |
| 아프다 | **편찮으시다** | to be ill |
| 주다 | **(주시다)** | to give (add -시-) |

### Noun Pairs

| Plain | Honorific | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 나이 | **연세** | age |
| 밥 | **진지** | meal |
| 집 | **댁** | house/home |
| 이름 | **성함** | name |
| 말 | **말씀** | words/speech |
| 생일 | **생신** | birthday |
| 사람 | **분** | person |

### Particle Pairs

| Plain | Honorific | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 이/가 | **께서** | Subject marker |
| 에게/한테 | **께** | To (someone) |

### Example Sentences

- 할머니, **진지** 드셨어요? (Grandmother, have you eaten?) — *not* ~~밥 먹었어요~~
- 선생님**께서** 교실에 **계세요**. (The teacher is in the classroom.)
- **성함**이 어떻게 되세요? (What is your name?) — *not* ~~이름이 뭐예요~~
- 할아버지 **연세**가 어떻게 되세요? (How old is your grandfather?)
- 아버지가 작년에 **돌아가셨어요**. (My father passed away last year.) — *not* ~~죽었어요~~
- 사장님**께** 말씀 **드리겠습니다**. (I will speak to the boss.)

## 8. Humble / Lowering Forms

When speaking to someone of higher status, you **lower yourself** using humble forms. This complements the honorific system.

### Pronoun Changes

| Plain | Humble | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| 나 | **저** | I/me (polite) |
| 우리 | **저희** | we/our (polite) |
| 내 (나의) | **제 (저의)** | my (polite) |

### Humble Verb Forms

| Plain | Humble | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 주다 | **드리다** | to give (to a superior) |
| 묻다/물어보다 | **여쭙다/여쭤보다** | to ask (a superior) |
| 만나다 | **뵙다/뵈다** | to meet (a superior) |
| 데리다 | **모시다** | to accompany/serve |
| 보다 | **뵙다** | to see (a superior) |
| 말하다 | **말씀드리다** | to tell (a superior) |
| 있다 | **있겠습니다** (humble context) | to be/exist |

### How Humble and Honorific Work Together

In a single sentence, you can elevate the other person while lowering yourself:

| Sentence | Analysis |
|---|---|
| **제**가 선생님**께** 말씀 **드리겠습니다**. | 제 (humble I) + 께 (hon. particle) + 드리다 (humble give/tell) |
| **처음 뵙겠습니다**. | 뵙다 (humble meet) — "I am meeting you for the first time" |
| 선생님을 **모시고** 왔습니다. | 모시다 (humble accompany) — "I brought the teacher (respectfully)" |
| **여쭤볼** 것이 있습니다. | 여쭤보다 (humble ask) — "I have something to ask you" |

### Common Humble Expressions

- **처음 뵙겠습니다.** (Nice to meet you.) — very formal first meeting
- **말씀 드릴 게 있습니다.** (I have something to tell you.) — to a superior
- **제가 모시겠습니다.** (I will accompany/escort you.)
- **감사 드립니다.** (I give you my thanks.) — humble + formal

## 9. When to Switch Levels

### Factors Determining Speech Level

| Factor | Higher level (존댓말) | Lower level (반말) |
|---|---|---|
| **Age** | Older than you | Younger than you |
| **Social status** | Boss, teacher, client | Subordinate, student |
| **Relationship** | Acquaintance, stranger | Close friend, family |
| **Setting** | Formal (office, interview) | Casual (home, bar) |
| **First meeting** | Always 존댓말 | Never 반말 |

### The Age Factor

In Korean culture, even **one year** of age difference can affect speech level:

- **Same age (동갑)**: Start with 존댓말, then negotiate 반말
- **1-2 years older (형/누나/오빠/언니)**: Usually 존댓말 unless close
- **3+ years older**: 존댓말 expected
- **Younger**: You may use 반말 after establishing rapport

### Common Switching Scenarios

| Situation | Typical level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Meeting someone new | 해요체 | Safe default |
| Job interview | 합쇼체 | Show maximum respect |
| After drinking together | May shift to 해요체 → 반말 | Alcohol loosens formality |
| Online (same age group) | 반말 common | Internet culture is less formal |
| Workplace superior | 합쇼체 or 해요체 + -(으)시- | Depends on company culture |

## 10. Cultural Notes

### 반말 허락 — Getting Permission to Use Informal Speech

In Korean culture, you cannot simply start using 반말 with someone. The transition is a **social milestone** that must be negotiated.

**How it happens**:
1. Two people meet and use 존댓말 (polite speech).
2. One person (usually the older one) says: "우리 말 놓을까요?" or "반말 할까요?" (Shall we drop formalities?)
3. The other agrees: "네, 좋아요!" (Yes, sounds good!)
4. Both switch to 반말 (or the younger person may continue using 해요체 while the older uses 반말).

**Important**: The **older** or **higher-status** person typically initiates. It would be presumptuous for the younger person to suggest it.

### 나이 묻기 — Asking About Age

Koreans ask about age early in relationships — not out of rudeness, but to determine the correct speech level.

Common ways to ask:
- 몇 살이에요? (How old are you?) — casual polite
- 나이가 어떻게 되세요? (How old are you?) — respectful
- 연세가 어떻게 되세요? (How old are you?) — to elders
- 몇 년생이에요? (What year were you born?) — common among young people

### Mixing Levels Is a Mistake

Using the wrong speech level can cause serious social awkwardness:

| Mistake | Perception |
|---|---|
| 반말 to older stranger | Extremely rude, potentially offensive |
| 합쇼체 to close friend | Cold, creating emotional distance |
| 반말 to boss | Career-ending social error |
| 존댓말 to a child | Strange, overly formal |

### Exercise 3: Humble Forms

Fill in the blank with the appropriate humble expression.

1. 선생님을 _______ 왔습니다. (to come to meet — humble)
