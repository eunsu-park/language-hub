## 1. Why Indirect Speech Matters

Indirect speech (reported speech) is used **far more frequently** in Korean than in English. In English, you might say "He said he's coming" and move on. In Korean, indirect speech patterns appear constantly in everyday conversation:

- Relaying what someone said: "My friend said she can't come."
- Reporting news: "They said it will rain tomorrow."
- Expressing hearsay: "I heard that restaurant is good."
- Quoting thoughts: "I think Korean is interesting."
- Asking for clarification: "Did you say you're a student?"

Korean indirect speech has **four distinct forms** depending on the sentence type being quoted: statements, questions, commands, and suggestions. Each follows a specific grammatical pattern.

> **Direct vs. Indirect Speech**:
> - Direct: 민수가 "나는 학생이다"라고 말했어요. (Minsu said, "I am a student.")
> - Indirect: 민수가 학생**이라고** 했어요. (Minsu said he is a student.)

In everyday spoken Korean, indirect speech is overwhelmingly preferred over direct quotation.

## 2. Statement Quotation (-다고 하다)

Used to report what someone **said** (a declarative sentence).

### 2.1 Present Tense Statements

The form depends on whether the quoted word is an action verb, descriptive verb, or noun.

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Action verb | V-ㄴ/는다고 하다 | 간**다고** 해요 (says [someone] is going) |
| Descriptive verb (adj.) | A-다고 하다 | 춥**다고** 해요 (says it's cold) |
| Noun + 이다 | N-(이)라고 하다 | 학생**이라고** 해요 (says [someone] is a student) |
| Noun + 아니다 | N-이/가 아니라고 하다 | 학생이 **아니라고** 해요 (says [someone] is not a student) |

**Action Verb Detail**:

| Stem ending | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Vowel | -ㄴ다고 | 가다 → 간다고 (says [one] goes) |
| Consonant | -는다고 | 먹다 → 먹는다고 (says [one] eats) |
| ㄹ ending | -ㄴ다고 (ㄹ drops) | 살다 → 산다고 (says [one] lives) |

**Examples**:
- 그 사람이 한국에 **간다고** 했어요. (That person said they're going to Korea.)
- 오늘 날씨가 **춥다고** 해요. (They say today's weather is cold.)
- 민수 씨가 **학생이라고** 했어요. (Minsu said he's a student.)
- 지영 씨가 한국 사람이 **아니라고** 했어요. (Jiyoung said she's not Korean.)

### 2.2 Past Tense Statements (-았/었다고 하다)

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Action / Descriptive | V/A-았/었다고 하다 | 갔**다고** 해요, 추웠**다고** 해요 |
| Noun + 이다 | N-이었/였다고 하다 | 학생이었**다고** 해요 |

**Examples**:
- 어제 비가 **왔다고** 해요. (They say it rained yesterday.)
- 그 영화가 **재미있었다고** 했어요. (They said that movie was interesting.)
- 전에 선생님**이었다고** 했어요. (They said they used to be a teacher.)

### 2.3 Future Tense Statements (-(으)ㄹ 거라고 하다)

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| V-(으)ㄹ 거라고 하다 | 갈 **거라고** 해요 (says [someone] will go) |
| V-겠다고 하다 | 가**겠다고** 해요 (says [someone] will/intends to go) |

**Examples**:
- 내일 올 **거라고** 했어요. (They said they'll come tomorrow.)
- 열심히 하**겠다고** 했어요. (They said they would work hard.)

## 3. Question Quotation (-냐고 하다)

Used to report what someone **asked** (an interrogative sentence).

### Formation

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Action verb | V-느냐고 / V-냐고 하다 | 가**냐고** 했어요 (asked if [one] is going) |
| Descriptive verb | A-(으)냐고 하다 | 춥**냐고** 했어요 (asked if it's cold) |
| Noun + 이다 | N-(이)냐고 하다 | 학생**이냐고** 했어요 (asked if [one] is a student) |
| Past | -았/었냐고 하다 | 갔**냐고** 했어요 (asked if [one] went) |

> **Note**: -느냐고 is the traditional/formal form, but -냐고 is much more common in modern spoken Korean.

### Common Reporting Verbs for Questions

- **물어보다** (to ask): 뭐 먹냐고 **물어봤어요**. (They asked what I'm eating.)
- **묻다** (to ask, formal): 어디 가냐고 **물었어요**. (They asked where I'm going.)

**Examples**:
- 뭐 먹**냐고** 물어봤어요. (They asked what I'm eating.)
- 어디 사**냐고** 했어요. (They asked where I live.)
- 한국 사람**이냐고** 물어봤어요. (They asked if I'm Korean.)
- 언제 왔**냐고** 했어요. (They asked when I came.)
- 내일 시간이 있**냐고** 했어요. (They asked if I have time tomorrow.)

---

## 4. Command Quotation (-(으)라고 하다)

Used to report what someone **told someone to do** (an imperative sentence).

### Formation

| Stem ending | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Vowel | -라고 하다 | 가다 → 가**라고** 했어요 |
| Consonant | -으라고 하다 | 먹다 → 먹**으라고** 했어요 |

> **Note**: For negative commands, use -지 말라고 하다.

**Examples**:
- 빨리 오**라고** 했어요. (They told me to come quickly.)
- 조용히 하**라고** 했어요. (They told me to be quiet.)
- 숙제를 하**라고** 했어요. (They told me to do homework.)
- 많이 먹**으라고** 했어요. (They told me to eat a lot.)
- 늦지 말**라고** 했어요. (They told me not to be late.)
- 걱정하지 말**라고** 했어요. (They told me not to worry.)

### Polite Command Reporting

When reporting a polite command or request, you can use -달라고 하다 (asking someone to do something for the speaker):

- 도와**달라고** 했어요. (They asked me to help them.)
- 전화해 **달라고** 했어요. (They asked me to call them.)
- 기다려 **달라고** 했어요. (They asked me to wait for them.)

> **-(으)라고 vs. -달라고**: -(으)라고 is a general command. -달라고 is a request for the listener's benefit directed toward the speaker ("please do X for me").

---

## 5. Suggestion Quotation (-자고 하다)

Used to report what someone **suggested** (a propositive sentence).

### Formation

Verb stem + -자고 하다

**Examples**:
- 같이 가**자고** 했어요. (They suggested we go together.)
- 밥 먹**자고** 했어요. (They suggested we eat.)
- 주말에 만나**자고** 했어요. (They suggested we meet on the weekend.)
- 영화를 보**자고** 했어요. (They suggested we watch a movie.)
- 내일 다시 이야기하**자고** 했어요. (They suggested we talk again tomorrow.)

For negative suggestions:
- 오늘은 하지 말**자고** 했어요. (They suggested we not do it today.)

## 6. Shortened / Contracted Forms

In spoken Korean, indirect speech forms are frequently contracted. These shortened forms are extremely common and essential for natural conversation.

### Contraction Table

| Full form | Contracted | Type | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| -다고 해요 | **-대요** | Statement | 간**대요** (says [one] is going) |
| -냐고 해요 | **-냬요** | Question | 뭐 먹**냬요** (asks what [one] is eating) |
| -(으)라고 해요 | **-래요** | Command | 오**래요** (tells [one] to come) |
| -자고 해요 | **-재요** | Suggestion | 가**재요** (suggests going) |

### Detailed Contraction Patterns

**Statement (-대요)**:

| Original | Contracted | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 간다고 해요 | **간대요** | Says [one] is going |
| 춥다고 해요 | **춥대요** | Says it's cold |
| 학생이라고 해요 | **학생이래요** | Says [one] is a student |
| 갔다고 해요 | **갔대요** | Says [one] went |
| 올 거라고 해요 | **올 거래요** | Says [one] will come |

**Question (-냬요)**:

| Original | Contracted | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 뭐 하냐고 해요 | **뭐 하냬요** | Asks what [one] is doing |
| 어디 가냐고 해요 | **어디 가냬요** | Asks where [one] is going |

**Command (-래요)**:

| Original | Contracted | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 오라고 해요 | **오래요** | Tells [one] to come |
| 먹으라고 해요 | **먹으래요** | Tells [one] to eat |
| 하지 말라고 해요 | **하지 말래요** | Tells [one] not to do it |

**Suggestion (-재요)**:

| Original | Contracted | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 가자고 해요 | **가재요** | Suggests going |
| 먹자고 해요 | **먹재요** | Suggests eating |

### Spoken Examples

- 내일 비가 온**대요**. (They say it'll rain tomorrow.)
- 선생님이 뭐 했**냬요**. (The teacher asks what you did.)
- 엄마가 빨리 오**래요**. (Mom says to come quickly.)
- 친구가 같이 가**재요**. (My friend suggests we go together.)

## 7. Common Patterns with Indirect Speech

### 7.1 -다고 들었어요 — "I heard that..."

Used to report hearsay or information received from others.

- 한국어가 어렵**다고 들었어요**. (I heard that Korean is difficult.)
- 그 식당이 맛있**다고 들었어요**. (I heard that restaurant is delicious.)
- 내일 회의가 취소됐**다고 들었어요**. (I heard the meeting tomorrow was cancelled.)
- 민수 씨가 결혼한**다고 들었어요**. (I heard Minsu is getting married.)

### 7.2 -다고 생각하다 — "I think that..."

Used to express opinions and thoughts.

- 한국어가 재미있**다고 생각해요**. (I think Korean is interesting.)
- 이게 맞**다고 생각해요**. (I think this is correct.)
- 그 사람이 좋은 사람이**라고 생각해요**. (I think that person is a good person.)

> **Alternative**: -(으)ㄴ/는 것 같다 is more commonly used for softer opinions in spoken Korean:
> - 한국어가 재미있는 **것 같아요**. (I think Korean is interesting.)

### 7.3 -다고 쓰여 있다 — "It says that... / It is written that..."

Used for written information on signs, notices, books, etc.

- 여기에 금연이**라고 쓰여 있어요**. (It says "no smoking" here.)
- 메뉴에 만 원이**라고 쓰여 있어요**. (It says 10,000 won on the menu.)

### 7.4 -(이)라고 불리다/부르다 — "Is called..."

Used to explain names or titles.

- 이 음식은 비빔밥**이라고 불려요**. (This food is called bibimbap.)
- 저를 수지**라고 불러 주세요**. (Please call me Suji.)

### 7.5 Other Reporting Verbs

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 말하다 | to say | 갈 거라고 말했어요. |
| 대답하다 | to answer | 네, 맞다고 대답했어요. |
| 약속하다 | to promise | 다시는 안 한다고 약속했어요. |
| 전하다 | to relay/convey | 잘 지내라고 전해 주세요. |
| 부탁하다 | to request | 도와달라고 부탁했어요. |

## 9. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Convert to Indirect Speech

Change the direct quote to indirect speech using -다고/냐고/라고/자고 하다.

1. 민수: "나는 한국 사람이다." → 민수가 _______ 했어요.
2. 선생님: "숙제를 하세요." → 선생님이 _______ 했어요.
3. 유미: "같이 영화 보자." → 유미가 _______ 했어요.
4. 친구: "어디 살아?" → 친구가 _______ 했어요.
5. 뉴스: "내일 비가 올 것이다." → 뉴스에서 _______ 했어요.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 민수가 한국 사람**이라고** 했어요.
2. 선생님이 숙제를 하**라고** 했어요.
3. 유미가 같이 영화 보**자고** 했어요.
4. 친구가 어디 사**냐고** 했어요.
5. 뉴스에서 내일 비가 올 **거라고** 했어요.

</details>

### Exercise 2: Use Contracted Forms

Rewrite using the shortened forms (-대요, -냬요, -래요, -재요).

1. 내일 시험이 있다고 해요. →
2. 몇 시에 오냐고 해요. →
3. 빨리 가라고 해요. →
4. 점심 같이 먹자고 해요. →
5. 어제 비가 왔다고 해요. →

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 내일 시험이 있**대요**.
2. 몇 시에 오**냬요**.
3. 빨리 가**래요**.
4. 점심 같이 먹**재요**.
5. 어제 비가 왔**대요**.

</details>

### Exercise 3: Choose the Correct Quotation Type

Fill in the blank with the appropriate indirect speech form.

1. 엄마가 빨리 집에 _______. (told me to come home quickly)
2. 민수가 내일 시간이 _______. (asked if I have time tomorrow)
3. 지영 씨가 학생_______. (said she is a student)
4. 친구가 주말에 _______. (suggested we meet on the weekend)
5. 뉴스에서 내일 _______. (said it will snow tomorrow)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 엄마가 빨리 집에 **오라고 했어요**. (command)
2. 민수가 내일 시간이 **있냐고 했어요**. (question)
3. 지영 씨가 **학생이라고 했어요**. (statement with noun)
4. 친구가 주말에 **만나자고 했어요**. (suggestion)
5. 뉴스에서 내일 **눈이 온다고 했어요** / **올 거라고 했어요**. (statement)

</details>

### Exercise 4: -다고 들었어요 / -다고 생각해요

Complete the sentences using the pattern indicated.

1. 제주도가 아름답다 (-다고 들었어요) →
2. 한국어 공부가 재미있다 (-다고 생각해요) →
3. 그 가게가 문을 닫았다 (-다고 들었어요) →
4. 이 방법이 좋다 (-다고 생각해요) →

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 제주도가 **아름답다고 들었어요**. (I heard Jeju Island is beautiful.)
2. 한국어 공부가 **재미있다고 생각해요**. (I think studying Korean is fun.)
3. 그 가게가 **문을 닫았다고 들었어요**. (I heard that store closed.)
4. 이 방법이 **좋다고 생각해요**. (I think this method is good.)

</details>

### Exercise 5: Translation

Translate into Korean.

1. My friend said Korean food is delicious.
2. The teacher asked what my name is.
3. Mom told me not to come home late.
4. They suggested we study together.
5. I heard that movie is really good.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 친구가 한국 음식이 **맛있다고** 했어요.
2. 선생님이 이름이 **뭐냐고** 물어봤어요.
3. 엄마가 늦게 집에 오지 **말라고** 했어요.
4. 같이 공부하**자고** 했어요.
5. 그 영화가 정말 **좋다고 들었어요**.

</details>
