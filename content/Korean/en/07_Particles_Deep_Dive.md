## 1. Object Marker: 을/를

The object marker identifies the **direct object** — the thing being acted upon by the verb.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              Object Marker: 을/를                          │
│                                                           │
│  Noun ends in CONSONANT  →  noun + 을                      │
│    밥을 (rice), 책을 (book), 물을 (water)                    │
│                                                           │
│  Noun ends in VOWEL  →  noun + 를                           │
│    커피를 (coffee), 우유를 (milk), 사과를 (apple)              │
│                                                           │
│  Pattern: [subject]이/가 [object]을/를 [verb]               │
│  저는 밥을 먹어요. (I eat rice.)                              │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 1.1 Examples

| Korean | Translation | Object |
|--------|------------|--------|
| 밥을 먹어요. | I eat rice. | 밥 (rice) |
| 물을 마셔요. | I drink water. | 물 (water) |
| 책을 읽어요. | I read a book. | 책 (book) |
| 커피를 마셔요. | I drink coffee. | 커피 (coffee) |
| 영화를 봐요. | I watch a movie. | 영화 (movie) |
| 한국어를 공부해요. | I study Korean. | 한국어 (Korean) |
| 음악을 들어요. | I listen to music. | 음악 (music) |

### 1.2 Object Marker Omission

In casual spoken Korean, 을/를 is frequently dropped:

```
Formal:  커피를 마실래요?  (Would you like to drink coffee?)
Casual:  커피 마실래요?    (same meaning, particle dropped)

Formal:  영화를 봤어요.    (I watched a movie.)
Casual:  영화 봤어요.      (same meaning, particle dropped)
```

> **Tip**: While 을/를 can be omitted in speech, always include it in writing and when you need clarity.

## 2. Location Markers: 에 vs. 에서

These two particles are among the most commonly confused by learners. Understanding the difference is critical.

### 2.1 에 — Static Location, Destination, and Time

The particle **에** has three main uses:

#### Use 1: Location of existence (with 있다/없다)

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 고양이가 집에 있어요. | The cat is at home. |
| 책이 책상 위에 있어요. | The book is on the desk. |
| 학교에 학생이 많아요. | There are many students at school. |

#### Use 2: Destination (with movement verbs: 가다, 오다, 다니다)

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 학교에 가요. | I go to school. |
| 한국에 왔어요. | I came to Korea. |
| 서울에 살아요.* | I live in Seoul. |

> *살다 (to live) takes 에 because it indicates the place of residence (static).

#### Use 3: Time

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 3시에 만나요. | Let's meet at 3 o'clock. |
| 월요일에 수업이 있어요. | There is class on Monday. |
| 아침에 운동해요. | I exercise in the morning. |

### 2.2 에서 — Location of Action

**에서** marks the place **where an action occurs**:

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 도서관에서 공부해요. | I study at the library. |
| 식당에서 밥을 먹어요. | I eat at a restaurant. |
| 집에서 영화를 봐요. | I watch movies at home. |
| 회사에서 일해요. | I work at the company. |

### 2.3 에 vs. 에서 — The Critical Difference

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    에 vs. 에서                                 │
│                                                               │
│  에                              에서                          │
│  ──                              ────                          │
│  Static location (있다/없다)      Location of ACTION            │
│  Destination (가다/오다)          Where something HAPPENS        │
│  Time point                      Starting point ("from")       │
│                                                               │
│  학교에 있어요.                    학교에서 공부해요.               │
│  (I am at school.)              (I study at school.)           │
│                                                               │
│  집에 가요.                       집에서 쉬어요.                   │
│  (I go home.)                   (I rest at home.)              │
│                                                               │
│  Key test: Is there an ACTION happening at the location?       │
│  YES → 에서    NO (just existing/going) → 에                   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 2.4 에서 as "From" (Starting Point)

에서 also marks the starting point of movement:

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 서울에서 부산까지 KTX로 2시간 30분 걸려요. | From Seoul to Busan, it takes 2h30m by KTX. |
| 어디에서 왔어요? | Where did you come from? |
| 미국에서 왔어요. | I came from America. |

## 3. Direction and Means: 으로/로

The particle **으로/로** expresses direction, means, or transformation.

### 3.1 Selection Rule

| Noun Ending | Particle | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Consonant (except ㄹ) | 으로 | 집으로 (toward home) |
| Vowel | 로 | 서울로 (toward Seoul) |
| ㄹ | 로 | 지하철로 (by subway) |

### 3.2 Use 1: Direction ("toward")

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 이쪽으로 오세요. | Please come this way. |
| 오른쪽으로 가세요. | Please go to the right. |
| 서울로 이사했어요. | I moved to Seoul. |

### 3.3 Use 2: Means/Method ("by")

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 버스로 가요. | I go by bus. |
| 지하철로 출근해요. | I commute by subway. |
| 젓가락으로 먹어요. | I eat with chopsticks. |
| 한국어로 말해 주세요. | Please speak in Korean. |

### 3.4 Use 3: Material/Cause ("with, because of")

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 나무로 만들었어요. | It was made with wood. |
| 감기로 회사에 못 갔어요. | I couldn't go to work because of a cold. |

## 4. Comitative Particles: With Someone

Korean has three ways to say "with" or "and" when connecting nouns.

| Particle | Formality | Example |
|----------|----------|---------|
| 와/과 | Formal/written | 친구**와** 같이 갔어요. |
| 하고 | Neutral/informal | 친구**하고** 같이 갔어요. |
| (이)랑 | Casual/spoken | 친구**랑** 같이 갔어요. |

### 4.1 Selection Rules for 와/과

| Noun Ending | Particle | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Vowel | 와 | 커피**와** 빵 (coffee and bread) |
| Consonant | 과 | 밥**과** 국 (rice and soup) |

### 4.2 Selection Rules for (이)랑

| Noun Ending | Particle | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Vowel | 랑 | 언니**랑** (with older sister) |
| Consonant | 이랑 | 동생**이랑** (with younger sibling) |

### 4.3 Connecting Nouns ("and")

All three particles also function as "and" when listing nouns:

```
사과와 바나나를 샀어요.        (formal)
사과하고 바나나를 샀어요.      (neutral)
사과랑 바나나를 샀어요.        (casual)
All mean: I bought apples and bananas.
```

### 4.4 "Together with"

Add **같이 (gachi)** or **함께 (hamkke)** to emphasize "together":

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 친구와 같이 영화를 봤어요. | I watched a movie together with a friend. |
| 가족과 함께 여행을 갔어요. | I went on a trip together with my family. |

## 5. Person-Directed Particles: To and From

### 5.1 "To" a Person: 에게 / 한테

| Particle | Formality | Usage |
|----------|----------|-------|
| 에게 | Formal/written | Used in writing, news, formal speech |
| 한테 | Informal/spoken | Used in daily conversation |
| 께 | Honorific | Used for respected persons (선생님께) |

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 친구에게 선물을 줬어요. | I gave a present to my friend. (formal) |
| 친구한테 선물을 줬어요. | I gave a present to my friend. (informal) |
| 선생님께 인사를 드렸어요. | I greeted the teacher. (honorific) |

### 5.2 "From" a Person: 에게서 / 한테서

| Particle | Formality | Usage |
|----------|----------|-------|
| 에게서 | Formal/written | Formal contexts |
| 한테서 | Informal/spoken | Daily conversation |

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 친구에게서 편지를 받았어요. | I received a letter from my friend. (formal) |
| 엄마한테서 전화가 왔어요. | A call came from mom. (informal) |

> **Note**: For animals, use 에게/한테. For places and organizations, use 에서 instead.

## 6. Range: 부터...까지

### 6.1 부터 (From) and 까지 (To/Until)

These particles express the start and end points of a range.

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 9시부터 6시까지 일해요. | I work from 9 to 6. |
| 월요일부터 금요일까지 수업이 있어요. | I have class from Monday to Friday. |
| 서울부터 부산까지 멀어요. | It's far from Seoul to Busan. |
| 1번부터 10번까지 하세요. | Please do numbers 1 through 10. |

### 6.2 Using 까지 Alone ("until, up to, even")

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 몇 시까지 해요? | Until what time is it open? |
| 내일까지 내세요. | Please submit by tomorrow. |
| 저까지 세 명이에요. | Including me, there are three people. |

### 6.3 Using 부터 Alone ("from, starting from")

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 지금부터 시작해요. | Let's start from now. |
| 내일부터 운동할 거예요. | I will exercise starting from tomorrow. |

## 7. Auxiliary Particles

These particles add nuance and emphasis. They can attach after other particles.

### 7.1 도 — "Also, Too, Even"

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 저도 한국어를 공부해요. | I also study Korean. |
| 여기도 좋아요. | Here is good too. |
| 주말에도 일해요. | I work even on weekends. |

> **Note**: 도 replaces 은/는, 이/가, 을/를 but attaches after other particles (에도, 에서도, 한테도).

### 7.2 만 — "Only, Just"

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 물만 주세요. | Please give me only water. |
| 한국어만 공부해요. | I study only Korean. |
| 조금만 기다려 주세요. | Please wait just a moment. |
| 주말에만 쉬어요. | I rest only on weekends. |

### 7.3 밖에 — "Only, Nothing but" (+ negative verb)

**밖에** always requires a **negative verb**:

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 돈이 천 원밖에 없어요. | I have only 1,000 won. (lit. "nothing but 1,000 won") |
| 커피밖에 안 마셔요. | I drink nothing but coffee. |
| 한 명밖에 안 왔어요. | Only one person came. |

> **만 vs. 밖에**: Both mean "only," but 밖에 emphasizes insufficiency. 만 is neutral; 밖에 implies "that's all there is (and it's not enough)."

### 7.4 의 — Possessive ("of, 's")

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 한국의 문화 | Korea's culture |
| 나의 책 = 내 책 | my book |
| 친구의 집 | friend's house |

> **Note**: In spoken Korean, 의 is often omitted: 친구 집 (friend's house), 학교 선생님 (school teacher).

### 7.5 처럼 — "Like, Similar to"

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 한국 사람처럼 말해요. | You speak like a Korean person. |
| 꿈처럼 아름다운 곳이에요. | It's a place as beautiful as a dream. |

### 7.6 보다 — "Than" (Comparison)

| Korean | Translation |
|--------|------------|
| 어제보다 따뜻해요. | It's warmer than yesterday. |
| 생각보다 어려워요. | It's harder than I thought. |

## 8. Particle Stacking

Korean particles can be **stacked** — combining multiple particles on a single noun. The order matters.

### 8.1 Common Stacking Patterns

| Stacked Form | Components | Example | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| 에서부터 | 에서 + 부터 | 서울에서부터 | from Seoul (emphatic) |
| 에서도 | 에서 + 도 | 집에서도 | even at home |
| 에서는 | 에서 + 는 | 한국에서는 | in Korea (as for) |
| 에게도 | 에게 + 도 | 친구에게도 | to a friend too |
| 에는 | 에 + 는 | 학교에는 | at school (topic) |
| 까지도 | 까지 + 도 | 저까지도 | even including me |
| 만으로 | 만 + 으로 | 노력만으로 | by effort alone |
| 부터는 | 부터 + 는 | 내일부터는 | from tomorrow (topic) |

### 8.2 Examples in Sentences

```
한국에서는 나이가 중요해요.
hanguk-eseoneun nai-ga jungyohaeyo.
In Korea (as for Korea), age is important.

친구에게도 말했어요.
chingu-egedo malhaesseoyo.
I told my friend too.

집에서도 일해요.
jib-eseodo ilhaeyo.
I work even at home.

서울에서부터 부산까지 운전했어요.
seoul-eseobuto busan-kkaji unjeonhaesseoyo.
I drove from Seoul all the way to Busan.
```

## 10. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Fill in the Correct Particle

Choose the appropriate particle from: 을/를, 에, 에서, 으로/로

1. 도서관___ 책___ 읽어요. (I read books at the library.)
2. 학교___ 가요. (I go to school.)
3. 버스___ 출근해요. (I commute by bus.)
4. 3시___ 만나요. (Let's meet at 3 o'clock.)
5. 집___ 쉬어요. (I rest at home.)
6. 서울___ 부산___ KTX___ 가요. (I go from Seoul to Busan by KTX.)

### Exercise 2: Choose 에 or 에서

1. 방___ 있어요. (I am in the room.)
2. 방___ 공부해요. (I study in the room.)
3. 한국___ 왔어요. (I came to Korea.)
4. 한국___ 일해요. (I work in Korea.)
5. 카페___ 친구를 만나요. (I meet a friend at a cafe.)

### Exercise 3: Comitative Particles

Rewrite each sentence in all three formality levels.

1. I went to a restaurant with my friend.
   - Formal: 친구___ 식당에 갔어요.
   - Neutral: 친구___ 식당에 갔어요.
   - Casual: 친구___ 식당에 갔어요.

### Exercise 4: Translate to Korean

1. I gave a present to my friend.
2. I work from 9 to 6.
3. I drink only water.
4. I eat with chopsticks.
5. It's harder than I thought.
6. I study Korean even on weekends.
