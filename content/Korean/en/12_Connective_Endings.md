# 12. Connective Endings (연결 어미)

## Learning Objectives

- Master the major connective endings for reason, contrast, condition, purpose, and sequence
- Distinguish between similar connective endings (e.g., -(으)니까 vs. -아/어서)
- Understand grammatical restrictions on each ending (e.g., which allow imperatives)
- Combine clauses naturally using appropriate connective endings

## 1. Reason and Cause

Korean has three primary reason/cause connectives. Choosing the right one is one of the most tested points on TOPIK.

### 1.1 -(으)니까 — Subjective Reason

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)니까

| Stem ending | Attachment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Vowel or ㄹ | -니까 | 가다 → 가니까, 살다 → 사니까 |
| Consonant | -으니까 | 먹다 → 먹으니까, 읽다 → 읽으니까 |
| Past tense | -았/었으니까 | 갔으니까, 먹었으니까 |

**Key Features**:
- Expresses the speaker's **subjective reason or discovery**
- **CAN** be followed by imperative (-세요), propositive (-ㅂ시다/자), and request sentences
- Often used when giving advice, commands, or suggestions

**Examples**:
- 비가 오**니까** 우산을 가져가세요. (It's raining, so take an umbrella.)
- 시간이 없**으니까** 빨리 갑시다. (We don't have time, so let's go quickly.)
- 배고프**니까** 밥 먹자. (I'm hungry, so let's eat.)
- 직접 가 보**니까** 정말 좋았어요. (When I went and saw for myself, it was really good.)

### 1.2 -아/어서 — Natural/Objective Cause

**Formation**: Verb stem + -아/어서 (following vowel harmony)

| Last vowel | Attachment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ㅏ or ㅗ | -아서 | 만나다 → 만나서, 오다 → 와서 |
| Others | -어서 | 먹다 → 먹어서, 읽다 → 읽어서 |
| 하다 | -해서 | 공부하다 → 공부해서 |

**Key Features**:
- Expresses a **natural, objective, or factual cause-and-effect**
- **CANNOT** be followed by imperative or propositive endings
- Cannot take past tense marker in the -아/어서 clause (~~갔어서~~ is incorrect — use 가서)

**Examples**:
- 배가 아파**서** 병원에 갔어요. (My stomach hurt, so I went to the hospital.)
- 만나**서** 반갑습니다. (Nice to meet you.) — *lit. "Meeting you, I'm glad."*
- 늦어**서** 죄송합니다. (I'm sorry for being late.)
- 비가 와**서** 못 갔어요. (It rained, so I couldn't go.)

> **Common mistake**: ~~배가 아파서 약을 드세요.~~ → This is incorrect because -아/어서 cannot precede an imperative. Use: 배가 아프**니까** 약을 드세요.

### 1.3 -기 때문에 — Formal/Written Reason

**Formation**: Verb stem + -기 때문에

**Key Features**:
- More formal and explicit than -(으)니까 or -아/어서
- Common in writing, news, academic contexts
- Can take past tense: -았/었기 때문에

**Examples**:
- 시간이 없**기 때문에** 참석할 수 없습니다. (Because there is no time, I cannot attend.)
- 한국어를 공부했**기 때문에** 한국 드라마를 이해할 수 있어요. (Because I studied Korean, I can understand Korean dramas.)
- 교통사고가 났**기 때문에** 길이 막혔습니다. (Because there was a traffic accident, the road was jammed.)

### ⚠️ Critical Comparison: -(으)니까 vs. -아/어서

This distinction is one of the most important grammar points in intermediate Korean.

| Feature | -(으)니까 | -아/어서 |
|---|---|---|
| Nuance | Subjective reason, discovery | Natural/objective cause |
| + Imperative (command) | ✅ OK | ❌ NOT OK |
| + Propositive (suggestion) | ✅ OK | ❌ NOT OK |
| Past tense in clause | ✅ -았/었으니까 | ❌ No past tense |
| Formality | Neutral | Neutral |
| Typical use | Advice, commands, justification | Factual explanation, apologies |

**Comparison Examples**:

| Situation | Correct | Incorrect |
|---|---|---|
| Giving advice | 추우**니까** 코트를 입으세요. | ~~추워서 코트를 입으세요.~~ |
| Making suggestion | 날씨가 좋**으니까** 산책합시다. | ~~날씨가 좋아서 산책합시다.~~ |
| Explaining fact | 비가 와**서** 못 갔어요. | (비가 오니까 못 갔어요 is also OK) |
| Apologizing | 늦어**서** 죄송합니다. | (늦었으니까 죄송합니다 — less natural) |

## 2. Contrast and Concession

### 2.1 -지만 — Simple Contrast ("but")

**Formation**: Verb stem + -지만 (attaches directly, no vowel harmony needed)

**Key Features**:
- The most straightforward "but" connector
- Can attach to any verb/adjective, in any tense
- Neutral register

**Examples**:
- 비싸**지만** 좋아요. (It's expensive, but it's good.)
- 한국어가 어렵**지만** 재미있어요. (Korean is difficult, but interesting.)
- 어제 공부했**지만** 시험을 못 봤어요. (I studied yesterday, but did poorly on the exam.)
- 먹었**지만** 아직 배고파요. (I ate, but I'm still hungry.)

### 2.2 -(으)ㄴ데/는데 — Background/Soft Contrast

**Formation**:

| Type | Present | Past |
|---|---|---|
| Action verb | -는데 | -(으)ㄴ데 → -았/었는데 |
| Descriptive verb (adj.) | -(으)ㄴ데 | -았/었는데 |
| Noun + 이다 | -인데 | -이었는데/였는데 |

**Key Features**:
- Provides **background information** before making a statement, question, or request
- Softer contrast than -지만
- Extremely versatile and common in spoken Korean
- Often used to set the scene or introduce a topic before asking

**Examples**:
- 날씨가 좋**은데** 산책할까요? (The weather is nice — shall we take a walk?)
- 시간이 있**는데** 영화 볼래요? (I have time — want to watch a movie?)
- 한국어를 공부하**는데** 너무 어려워요. (I'm studying Korean, and it's so hard.)
- 배가 고프**ㄴ데** 뭐 먹을까요? (I'm hungry — what shall we eat?)
- 학생**인데** 할인 받을 수 있어요? (I'm a student — can I get a discount?)

### 2.3 -아/어도 — Concession ("even if/though")

**Formation**: Verb stem + -아/어도 (following vowel harmony)

**Key Features**:
- Expresses "even if" or "even though"
- The result happens regardless of the condition
- Can take past tense: -았/었어도

**Examples**:
- 비가 와**도** 갈 거예요. (Even if it rains, I'll go.)
- 많이 먹어**도** 안 살쪄요. (Even though I eat a lot, I don't gain weight.)
- 바빠**도** 운동해야 돼요. (Even if you're busy, you need to exercise.)
- 아무리 공부해**도** 어려워요. (No matter how much I study, it's difficult.)

> **Pattern**: 아무리 + V-아/어도 = "No matter how much..."

## 3. Condition

### 3.1 -(으)면 — General Condition ("if/when")

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)면

| Stem ending | Attachment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Vowel or ㄹ | -면 | 가다 → 가면, 살다 → 살면 |
| Consonant | -으면 | 먹다 → 먹으면, 읽다 → 읽으면 |

**Examples**:
- 시간이 있**으면** 같이 가요. (If you have time, let's go together.)
- 봄이 **오면** 꽃이 펴요. (When spring comes, flowers bloom.)
- 열심히 공부하**면** 합격할 수 있어요. (If you study hard, you can pass.)
- 모르**면** 물어보세요. (If you don't know, please ask.)

### 3.2 -다면 — Hypothetical Condition ("if it were the case that...")

**Formation**: Verb stem + -다면 (or -ㄴ다면 for action verbs, -라면 for nouns)

**Key Features**:
- More hypothetical or counterfactual than -(으)면
- Often used for imaginary situations or formal conditions

**Examples**:
- 백만 원이 있**다면** 뭘 할 거예요? (If you had a million won, what would you do?)
- 시간을 되돌릴 수 있**다면** 뭘 바꾸고 싶어요? (If you could turn back time, what would you change?)
- 내가 새**라면** 하늘을 날 거예요. (If I were a bird, I would fly in the sky.)
- 그게 사실이**라면** 큰일이에요. (If that's true, it's a big deal.)

### 3.3 -(으)려면 — Conditional Purpose ("if you intend to")

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)려면

**Key Features**:
- Combines intention (-(으)려고) with condition (-(으)면)
- "If you want to / If you intend to..."
- Often followed by advice or a necessary action

**Examples**:
- 한국어를 잘하**려면** 매일 연습하세요. (If you want to be good at Korean, practice every day.)
- 건강하**려면** 운동을 해야 돼요. (If you want to be healthy, you need to exercise.)
- 의사가 되**려면** 오래 공부해야 해요. (If you want to become a doctor, you have to study for a long time.)
- 여기 오**려면** 몇 시간 걸려요? (If you're coming here, how many hours does it take?)

## 4. Purpose

### 4.1 -(으)러 — Going/Coming to Do

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)러

**Key Features**:
- Used **only** with verbs of movement: 가다, 오다, 다니다, etc.
- The main verb must indicate motion

**Examples**:
- 밥 먹**으러** 가요. (I'm going to eat.)
- 친구를 만나**러** 왔어요. (I came to meet a friend.)
- 운동하**러** 헬스장에 가요. (I go to the gym to exercise.)
- 한국어를 배우**러** 한국에 왔어요. (I came to Korea to learn Korean.)

> **Restriction**: ~~밥 먹으러 공부해요~~ is incorrect because 공부하다 is not a movement verb.

### 4.2 -(으)려고 — Intending To

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)려고

**Key Features**:
- Expresses intention or plan
- No restriction on the following verb
- Cannot be followed by imperative or propositive (use -(으)려면 instead)

**Examples**:
- 한국어를 배우**려고** 한국에 왔어요. (I came to Korea intending to learn Korean.)
- 돈을 모으**려고** 아르바이트를 해요. (I'm doing a part-time job intending to save money.)
- 일찍 출발하**려고** 알람을 맞췄어요. (I set an alarm intending to leave early.)

### 4.3 -기 위해(서) — In Order To (Formal)

**Formation**: Verb stem + -기 위해(서)

**Key Features**:
- More formal and deliberate than -(으)려고
- Common in written Korean, speeches, and formal contexts
- The subject of both clauses should be the same

**Examples**:
- 건강을 **위해서** 운동해요. (I exercise for my health.) — *noun + 위해서*
- 합격하**기 위해** 열심히 공부했어요. (I studied hard in order to pass.)
- 환경을 보호하**기 위해서** 분리수거를 해야 합니다. (In order to protect the environment, we must recycle.)

### Purpose Endings Comparison

| Ending | Restriction | Register | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| -(으)러 | Movement verb only | Casual/neutral | 밥 먹으러 가요. |
| -(으)려고 | No imperative after | Neutral | 밥 먹으려고 식당에 갔어요. |
| -기 위해(서) | Same subject | Formal | 건강을 위해 운동합니다. |

## 5. Sequence and Addition

### 5.1 -고 — "And" / "And Then"

**Formation**: Verb stem + -고

**Key Features**:
- Lists actions or states, or sequences events
- Tense is marked only on the final verb
- Most basic connector in Korean

**Examples (Listing)**:
- 그 사람은 키가 크**고** 잘생겼어요. (That person is tall and handsome.)
- 커피도 마시**고** 케이크도 먹었어요. (I drank coffee and ate cake.)

**Examples (Sequence)**:
- 손을 씻**고** 밥을 먹었어요. (I washed my hands and then ate.)
- 숙제를 하**고** 잤어요. (I did homework and then slept.)

### 5.2 -아/어서 — "And Then" (Causal Sequence)

**Formation**: Verb stem + -아/어서

**Key Features**:
- When used for sequence (not reason), implies the first action is a **prerequisite** for the second
- Cannot be separated in time — the actions are closely linked

**Examples**:
- 백화점에 가**서** 옷을 샀어요. (I went to the department store and bought clothes.)
- 택시를 타**서** 갔어요. (I took a taxi and went.)
- 앉아**서** 기다리세요. (Please sit down and wait.)

> **-고 vs. -아/어서 for sequence**:
> - 집에 가**고** 공부했어요. = I went home, and (separately) I studied.
> - 집에 가**서** 공부했어요. = I went home and (then, there) studied. (causal link)

### 5.3 -(으)며 — "And" / "While" (Formal)

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)며

**Key Features**:
- Formal/literary equivalent of -고 (listing) or -(으)면서 (simultaneous)
- Common in written Korean, news, formal presentations

**Examples**:
- 그는 학생이**며** 운동선수입니다. (He is a student and an athlete.)
- 이 제품은 가볍**으며** 튼튼합니다. (This product is light and sturdy.)

## 6. Simultaneous Actions

### -(으)면서 — While Doing

**Formation**: Verb stem + -(으)면서

| Stem ending | Attachment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Vowel or ㄹ | -면서 | 가다 → 가면서, 만들다 → 만들면서 |
| Consonant | -으면서 | 먹다 → 먹으면서, 읽다 → 읽으면서 |

**Key Features**:
- Two actions happen at the same time by the **same subject**
- Cannot have different subjects for each clause

**Examples**:
- 음악을 들**으면서** 공부해요. (I study while listening to music.)
- 커피를 마시**면서** 이야기했어요. (We talked while drinking coffee.)
- 걸**으면서** 전화하지 마세요. (Don't talk on the phone while walking.)
- TV를 보**면서** 밥을 먹어요. (I eat while watching TV.)

### Contrast Use of -(으)면서

-(으)면서 can also express contradiction (similar to "while" in English as contrast):

- 돈이 있**으면서** 안 쓰는 사람이에요. (He's a person who has money but doesn't spend it.)
- 알**면서** 왜 안 알려 줬어요? (You knew, so why didn't you tell me?)

## 7. Connective Endings Summary Chart

| Function | Ending | Key Feature | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reason** | -(으)니까 | + imperative OK | 추우니까 코트 입으세요. |
| | -아/어서 | Objective cause, no imperative | 늦어서 죄송합니다. |
| | -기 때문에 | Formal/written | 시간이 없기 때문에... |
| **Contrast** | -지만 | Simple "but" | 비싸지만 좋아요. |
| | -(으)ㄴ데/는데 | Background + soft contrast | 날씨가 좋은데 나갈까요? |
| | -아/어도 | "Even if/though" | 비가 와도 갈 거예요. |
| **Condition** | -(으)면 | General "if/when" | 시간이 있으면 가요. |
| | -다면 | Hypothetical "if" | 새라면 날 거예요. |
| | -(으)려면 | "If you intend to" | 잘하려면 연습하세요. |
| **Purpose** | -(으)러 | + movement verb only | 먹으러 가요. |
| | -(으)려고 | Intention | 배우려고 왔어요. |
| | -기 위해(서) | Formal purpose | 합격하기 위해 공부했어요. |
| **Sequence** | -고 | "And / and then" | 씻고 잤어요. |
| | -아/어서 | Prerequisite sequence | 가서 샀어요. |
| | -(으)며 | Formal "and" | 가볍으며 튼튼합니다. |
| **Simultaneous** | -(으)면서 | Same subject, same time | 들으면서 공부해요. |

## 8. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: -(으)니까 vs. -아/어서

Choose the correct ending. Some sentences may have only one correct answer.

1. 비가 _______ 우산을 가져가세요. (오다)
2. 만나_______ 반갑습니다. (만나다)
3. 늦_______ 미안해요. (늦다)
4. 날씨가 좋_______ 산책합시다. (좋다)
5. 너무 피곤해_______ 일찍 잤어요. (피곤하다)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **오니까** (imperative follows → must use -(으)니까)
2. **서** → 만나서 (fixed expression: 만나서 반갑습니다)
3. **어서** → 늦어서 (apology, natural cause → -아/어서)
4. **으니까** (propositive follows → must use -(으)니까)
5. **서** → 피곤해서 (factual cause-result → -아/어서 is natural; 피곤하니까 is also grammatically OK)

</details>

### Exercise 2: Choose the Correct Connective

Fill in the blank with the most appropriate connective ending.

1. 한국어가 어렵_______ 재미있어요. (but)
2. 돈을 모으_______ 아르바이트를 해요. (intending to)
3. 음악을 들_______ 공부해요. (while)
4. 밥 먹_______ 식당에 가요. (going to do)
5. 비가 와_______ 갈 거예요. (even if)
6. 시간이 있_______ 같이 가요. (if)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **지만** (simple contrast)
2. **으려고** (intention: -(으)려고)
3. **으면서** (simultaneous: -(으)면서)
4. **으러** (purpose + movement: -(으)러)
5. **도** (concession: -아/어도)
6. **으면** (condition: -(으)면)

</details>

### Exercise 3: Combine the Sentences

Combine each pair using the connective ending indicated in parentheses.

1. 배가 아프다 + 병원에 갔어요. (-아/어서)
2. 날씨가 좋다 + 산책할까요? (-(으)ㄴ데)
3. 돈이 없다 + 여행을 못 가요. (-기 때문에)
4. 손을 씻다 + 밥을 먹어요. (-고)
5. 한국에 오다 + 한국어를 배우다. (-(으)려고)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 배가 **아파서** 병원에 갔어요. (My stomach hurt, so I went to the hospital.)
2. 날씨가 **좋은데** 산책할까요? (The weather is nice — shall we take a walk?)
3. 돈이 **없기 때문에** 여행을 못 가요. (Because I have no money, I can't travel.)
4. 손을 **씻고** 밥을 먹어요. (I wash my hands and then eat.)
5. 한국어를 **배우려고** 한국에 왔어요. (I came to Korea intending to learn Korean.)

</details>

### Exercise 4: Error Correction

Find and correct the error in each sentence.

1. 비가 와서 우산을 가져가세요.
2. 어제 늦었어서 미안합니다.
3. 커피를 마시면서 갔어요. (different subject: 나 / 그)
4. 영화를 보러 공부해요.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 비가 **오니까** 우산을 가져가세요. (-아/어서 cannot precede imperative)
2. 어제 **늦어서** 미안합니다. (-아/어서 cannot take past tense in the subordinate clause)
3. 커피를 **마시고** 갔어요. (-(으)면서 requires same subject; -고 allows different subjects or sequential meaning)
4. 영화를 **보러** 극장에 **가요**. (-(으)러 requires a movement verb; 공부하다 is not a movement verb)

</details>

### Exercise 5: Translation

Translate into Korean using the ending specified.

1. If you want to speak Korean well, you should practice every day. (-(으)려면)
2. I came to Korea to study Korean. (-(으)러)
3. Even though it was expensive, I bought it. (-아/어도)
4. I'm a student — is there a discount? (-(으)ㄴ데)
5. No matter how much I study, it's difficult. (아무리 + -아/어도)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 한국어를 잘하**려면** 매일 연습해야 돼요.
2. 한국어를 공부하**러** 한국에 왔어요.
3. 비싸**도** 샀어요.
4. 학생**인데** 할인 있어요?
5. 아무리 공부해**도** 어려워요.

</details>
