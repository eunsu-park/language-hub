## 2. At a Restaurant: Ordering

### 2.1 Essential Restaurant Phrases

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           Restaurant Survival Phrases (식당 필수 표현)           │
│                                                               │
│  Entering:                                                    │
│  여기요! / 저기요!          Excuse me! (calling staff)          │
│  몇 명이세요?              How many people? (staff asks)       │
│  두 명이요.                Two people.                         │
│                                                               │
│  Ordering:                                                    │
│  메뉴판 주세요.            Please give me the menu.            │
│  이것 주세요.              Please give me this.                │
│  _____ 하나 주세요.        One _____, please.                  │
│  _____ 두 개 주세요.       Two _____, please.                  │
│  추천 메뉴가 뭐예요?       What do you recommend?              │
│                                                               │
│  During the meal:                                             │
│  물 좀 주세요.             Water, please.                      │
│  반찬 더 주세요.           More side dishes, please.           │
│  맵지 않게 해 주세요.      Please make it not spicy.           │
│  맛있어요!                 It's delicious!                     │
│                                                               │
│  Paying:                                                      │
│  계산해 주세요.            Check, please.                      │
│  카드로 계산해 주세요.      I'll pay by card.                   │
│  현금으로 계산할게요.       I'll pay with cash.                 │
│  영수증 주세요.            Receipt, please.                    │
│  잘 먹었습니다.            Thank you for the meal.             │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 2.2 Ordering Dialogue

```
Waiter: 어서 오세요. 몇 명이세요?
        (Welcome. How many people?)

You:    두 명이요.
        (Two people.)

Waiter: 이쪽으로 앉으세요. 메뉴 여기 있어요.
        (Please sit this way. Here's the menu.)

You:    비빔밥 하나하고 불고기 하나 주세요.
        (One bibimbap and one bulgogi, please.)

Waiter: 음료는요?
        (Drinks?)

You:    물 주세요.
        (Water, please.)

[After eating]

You:    계산해 주세요. 카드로 할게요.
        (Check, please. I'll pay by card.)

You:    잘 먹었습니다!
        (Thank you for the meal!)
```

## 9. Cultural Notes

### 9.1 Korean Dining Culture

- **밥 먹었어요?** ("Have you eaten?") is a common greeting, equivalent to "How are you?" It reflects the importance of meals in Korean culture.
- Side dishes (반찬) are always shared and free refills (리필) are standard.
- Elders eat first. Wait for the oldest person to start eating before you begin.
- Pouring drinks: Pour with two hands for elders. Turn your head away when drinking in front of someone older.
- **잘 먹겠습니다** (before eating) = "I will eat well" (expressing gratitude).
- **잘 먹었습니다** (after eating) = "I ate well" (thank you for the meal).

### 9.2 Shopping in Korea

- **Traditional markets** (시장) allow bargaining: 깎아 주세요 (Please give me a discount).
- Department stores and chain shops have **fixed prices** — do not bargain.
- Tax-free shopping (면세) is available at airports and designated stores for tourists.
- Korea is highly cashless — credit and debit cards are accepted almost everywhere, even at small street food stalls.

### 9.3 The T-money Card (교통카드)

T-money is a rechargeable transit card used across South Korea for buses, subways, taxis, and even convenience store purchases. Buy one at any convenience store and recharge (충전) as needed. Tap on when boarding, tap off when exiting.
