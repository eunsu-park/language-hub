## 1. Content Words vs Function Words

### 1.1 The Two Categories

Every word in Classical Chinese belongs to one of two broad categories:

> **Content Words (實詞/실사) vs Function Words (虛詞/허사)**
>
> 實詞 (실사) Content Words:
>   - Have independent meaning
>   - Nouns, verbs, adjectives
>   - Examples: 人(person), 學(learn), 大(big), 山(mountain)
>
> 虛詞 (허사) Function Words:
>   - No independent lexical meaning
>   - Indicate grammatical relationships
>   - Like Korean 은/는, 이/가, 을/를, -다
>   - Examples: 之, 乎, 者, 也, 矣, 焉, 哉, 夫
>
> Classical Chinese has only ~20-30 common function words,
> but they appear in nearly every sentence. Mastering them
> is the key to reading fluency.

### 1.2 Overview of Eight Key Function Words

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Primary Function | Korean Equivalent |
|-----------|------|------|-----------------|------------------|
| 之 | 지 (ji) | 갈 지 | Possessive; pronoun; structural | 의; 그것을; — |
| 乎 | 호 (ho) | 어조사 호 | Question marker; exclamation | -ㄴ가?; -도다 |
| 者 | 자 (ja) | 놈 자 | Nominalizer; topic marker | -는 것; -는 자 |
| 也 | 야 (ya) | 어조사 야 | Copula; assertion | -이다; -이니라 |
| 矣 | 의 (ui) | 어조사 의 | Completion; change of state | -했다; -게 되었다 |
| 焉 | 언 (eon) | 어조사 언 | Location; rhetorical marker | 거기에; -는가 |
| 哉 | 재 (jae) | 어조사 재 | Exclamation (strong) | -도다!; -로다! |
| 夫 | 부 (bu) | 지아비 부 / 어조사 부 | Discourse opener; topic | 무릇; 대저 |

## 2. 之 — The Most Versatile Particle

之 is the most frequently occurring character in Classical Chinese texts. It has three major functions:

### 2.1 Function 1: Possessive Marker (소유격 조사, "의")

之 between two nouns creates a possessive relationship, like Korean 의:

| Example | Reading | Translation | Structure |
|---------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| 人之心 | 인지심 | A person's heart | A 之 B = A의 B |
| 天之道 | 천지도 | The way of heaven | 天의 道 |
| 國之本 | 국지본 | The foundation of the state | 國의 本 |
| 仁之道 | 인지도 | The way of benevolence | 仁의 道 |
| 父之敎 | 부지교 | A father's teaching | 父의 敎 |
| 民之心 | 민지심 | The heart of the people | 民의 心 |
| 學之始 | 학지시 | The beginning of learning | 學의 始 |
| 天地之間 | 천지지간 | Between heaven and earth | 天地의 間 |

### 2.2 Function 2: Object Pronoun (대명사, "그것을")

之 replaces a previously mentioned noun as a pronoun, like "it" or "them":

| Example | Reading | Translation | 之 Refers To |
|---------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 學而時習之 | 학이시습지 | Learn and practice it | What was learned |
| 知之 | 지지 | Know it | The topic at hand |
| 愛之 | 애지 | Love it/them | The object of love |
| 取之 | 취지 | Take it | Something mentioned |
| 觀之 | 관지 | Observe it | Something being discussed |

### 2.3 Function 3: Structural Particle (구조조사)

之 can appear between a modifying clause and a noun, with no direct English equivalent:

| Example | Reading | Translation | Function |
|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| 不義之財 | 불의지재 | Unjust wealth | Connects modifier to noun |
| 可愛之人 | 가애지인 | A lovable person | Connects modifier to noun |
| 千里之行 | 천리지행 | A journey of a thousand li | Connects modifier to noun |

### 2.4 Summary of 之 Functions

> **之 at a Glance**
>
> - ① Possessive (의): 人之心 = 사람의 마음 (a person's heart)
> - ② Pronoun (그것을): 學而習之 = 배워서 그것을 익힌다
> - ③ Structural: 千里之行 = 천리의 행 (a journey of 1000 li)
>
> How to tell them apart:
>   - Noun 之 Noun → Possessive
>   - Verb 之 → Pronoun (object of the verb)
>   - Modifier 之 Noun → Structural

## 3. 乎 — Questions and Exclamations

### 3.1 Function 1: Question Marker (의문 조사, "-ㄴ가?")

乎 at the end of a sentence creates a question, like Korean -ㄴ가? or -습니까?:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 可乎 | 가호 | Is it acceptable? |
| 有乎 | 유호 | Is there [any]? |
| 知之乎 | 지지호 | Do you know it? |
| 學而時習之 不亦說乎 | 학이시습지 불역열호 | Is it not also a pleasure? |
| 有朋自遠方來 不亦樂乎 | 유붕자원방래 불역락호 | Is it not also delightful? |

### 3.2 Function 2: Exclamation (감탄, "-도다!")

乎 can also express wonder or exclamation:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 大哉乎 | 대재호 | How great! |
| 善乎 | 선호 | How good! |
| 美乎 | 미호 | How beautiful! |

### 3.3 Function 3: Preposition ("in, at, from" = 於의 대용)

In some texts, 乎 functions like the preposition 於 (어, "in/at/from"):

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 生乎吾前 | 생호오전 | Born before me |
| 出乎其外 | 출호기외 | Going beyond it |

## 4. 者 — The Nominalizer

### 4.1 Function 1: Nominalizer ("-는 것/사람")

者 turns a preceding verb or adjective into a noun phrase:

| Example | Reading | Translation | Structure |
|---------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| 學者 | 학자 | Learner; scholar | Verb + 者 = one who [verbs] |
| 仁者 | 인자 | Benevolent person | Adj + 者 = one who is [adj] |
| 知者 | 지자 | Wise person | Verb + 者 = one who knows |
| 勇者 | 용자 | Brave person | Adj + 者 = one who is brave |
| 言者 | 언자 | Speaker | Verb + 者 = one who speaks |
| 善者 | 선자 | Good person | Adj + 者 = one who is good |

### 4.2 Function 2: Topic Marker in 者...也 Pattern

者 marks the topic of an equational sentence (see Lesson 05):

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 學者 覺也 | 학자 각야 | Learning is awakening |
| 仁者 愛人也 | 인자 애인야 | Benevolence is loving others |
| 民者 國之本也 | 민자 국지본야 | The people are the foundation of the nation |

### 4.3 Function 3: Pause/Enumeration

者 can create a pause in complex sentences, listing conditions or categories:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 生者必死 | 생자필사 | All who live must die |
| 知者樂水 仁者樂山 | 지자요수 인자요산 | The wise enjoy water, the benevolent enjoy mountains |

## 5. 也 — Copula and Assertion

### 5.1 Function 1: Copula ("-이다")

也 at the end of a sentence asserts that the subject equals the predicate:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 善也 | 선야 | It is good |
| 然也 | 연야 | It is so |
| 是也 | 시야 | That is correct |
| 非也 | 비야 | It is not so |
| 孔子聖人也 | 공자성인야 | Confucius is a sage |

### 5.2 Function 2: Emphasis/Explanation ("-이니라")

也 can add emphatic assertion or explanatory nuance:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 天命也 | 천명야 | It is the decree of heaven (indeed) |
| 此仁也 | 차인야 | This is indeed benevolence |

### 5.3 Function 3: Mid-Sentence Pause

也 occasionally appears mid-sentence to mark a pause or transition:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 大學之道也 在明明德 | 대학지도야 재명명덕 | The Way of the Great Learning — it lies in illuminating bright virtue |

## 6. 矣 — Completed Action and Change

### 6.1 Function 1: Completion ("-했다, -되었다")

矣 indicates that an action or change has been completed or a new situation has arisen:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 知矣 | 지의 | [Now I] understand |
| 已矣 | 이의 | It is finished; that's it |
| 足矣 | 족의 | It is sufficient (now) |
| 可矣 | 가의 | It is acceptable (now) |
| 死矣 | 사의 | [He] has died |

### 6.2 Function 2: Change of State ("-게 되었다")

矣 signals a change from a previous condition:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 吾老矣 | 오노의 | I have grown old |
| 天下平矣 | 천하평의 | The world has become peaceful |
| 國亡矣 | 국망의 | The country has perished |

### 6.3 也 vs 矣

| Particle | Function | Nuance | Example |
|----------|----------|--------|---------|
| 也 | Copula/assertion | Timeless truth, definition | 仁者愛人也 (Benevolence is loving others — always true) |
| 矣 | Completion/change | New situation, just occurred | 吾知矣 (I now understand — just realized) |

---

## 7. 焉 — Location and Rhetorical Marker

### 7.1 Function 1: Locative ("there, in it" = 於此/어차)

焉 can mean "there" or "in this":

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 心不在焉 | 심부재언 | The mind is not present (there) |
| 有子存焉 | 유자존언 | There are sons living there |

### 7.2 Function 2: Sentence-Final Particle

焉 at the end of a sentence adds a mild assertive or rhetorical tone:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 三人行必有我師焉 | 삼인행필유아사언 | When three walk together, there must be my teacher among them |

---

## 8. 哉 — Exclamation

### 8.1 Strong Exclamation ("-도다! -구나!")

哉 expresses strong emotion — admiration, surprise, or lament:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 大哉 | 대재 | How great! |
| 善哉 | 선재 | How wonderful! Excellent! |
| 悲哉 | 비재 | How sad! Alas! |
| 壯哉 | 장재 | How magnificent! |
| 危哉 | 위재 | How dangerous! |

### 8.2 Rhetorical Questions with 哉

哉 combined with question words creates emphatic rhetorical questions:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 何哉 | 하재 | What could it be?! |
| 豈不痛哉 | 기불통재 | Is it not painful indeed?! |
| 何足道哉 | 하족도재 | How is that worth mentioning?! |

---

## 9. 夫 — Discourse Opener

### 9.1 Function 1: Topic Introducer ("무릇, 대저")

When 夫 appears at the beginning of a sentence (read as 부, not the noun 부/husband), it introduces a topic for discussion:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 夫仁者 | 부인자 | Now, as for the benevolent person... |
| 夫天地者 | 부천지자 | Now, as for heaven and earth... |
| 夫學者 | 부학자 | Now, as for the scholar... |

### 9.2 Function 2: Demonstrative ("that, those")

夫 can also function like a demonstrative pronoun:

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 夫人 | 부인 | That person; madam |
| 夫子 | 부자 | That master; (Confucius) |

## 10. Comparative Usage Table

### 10.1 Sentence-Final Particles Compared

| Particle | Tone | When to Use | Example |
|----------|------|------------|---------|
| 也 | Declarative, calm | Definitions, timeless truths | 學者覺也 (Learning is awakening) |
| 矣 | Assertive, new | Completed actions, changes | 吾知矣 (I now know) |
| 乎 | Interrogative | Questions | 知之乎 (Do you know it?) |
| 哉 | Exclamatory | Strong emotion | 善哉 (How wonderful!) |
| 焉 | Mild assertion | Softened statement, location | 有師焉 (There is a teacher there) |

### 10.2 Multiple Functions Summary

| Character | Function 1 | Function 2 | Function 3 |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 之 | Possessive (의) | Pronoun (그것을) | Structural particle |
| 乎 | Question (-ㄴ가?) | Exclamation (-도다) | Preposition (에서) |
| 者 | Nominalizer (-는 자) | Topic marker (者...也) | Pause/enumeration |
| 也 | Copula (-이다) | Emphasis (-이니라) | Mid-sentence pause |

5. 吾老矣

```

2. 知[之][乎]
   之 = pronoun (그것을), 乎 = question marker
   Translation: Do you know it?

   Translation: Learning is awakening.

### Exercise 4: Translation with Function Words

Translate each sentence, paying careful attention to function words:

```
1. 天之道遠矣
2. 學者不知乎
3. 夫仁者 人之本也
4. 大哉 天地之德
5. 三人行 必有我師焉
```

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

```
1. 天之道遠矣 (천지도원의)
   The way of heaven has become distant.
   之 = possessive, 矣 = change of state

2. 學者不知乎 (학자부지호)
   Does the scholar not know?
   者 = nominalizer, 乎 = question

3. 夫仁者 人之本也 (부인자 인지본야)
   Now, benevolence — it is the root of being human.
   夫 = topic opener, 者 = topic marker, 之 = possessive, 也 = copula

   How great is the virtue of heaven and earth!
   哉 = exclamation, 之 = possessive

   When three people walk together, there must be my teacher among them.
   焉 = "there/among them" (locative)
