## 1. People Characters: Social Roles

### 1.1 Fundamental Person Characters

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Strokes | Formation | Notes |
|-----------|------|------|---------|-----------|-------|
| 人 | 인 (in) | 사람 인 | 2 | 象形: Side view of a walking person | Most basic "person" character |
| 女 | 여 (yeo) | 계집 녀 | 3 | 象形: Person kneeling with arms crossed | Used as radical in many characters |
| 子 | 자 (ja) | 아들 자 | 3 | 象形: Swaddled infant with arms | Also means "master" (孔子, 孟子) |
| 男 | 남 (nam) | 사내 남 | 7 | 會意: 田 (field) + 力 (strength) | "One who exerts strength in the field" |
| 夫 | 부 (bu) | 지아비 부 | 4 | 指事: 大 (person) + 一 (hairpin) | Adult man with hairpin (coming of age) |
| 婦 | 부 (bu) | 며느리 부 | 11 | 會意: 女 (woman) + 帚 (broom) | Married woman |

### 1.2 Person Character Variants

The character 人 appears in several forms depending on position:

| Form | Position | Name | Example |
|------|----------|------|---------|
| 人 | Standalone or bottom | 사람인 | 人, 大人 |
| 亻 | Left side (radical) | 사람인변 | 休 (rest), 仁 (benevolence) |
| 入 | — | 들입 | Different character: "enter" |

### 2.3 Filial Piety (孝)

The character 孝 (효, hyo) — filial piety — is formed from 老 (old) on top and 子 (child) below, symbolizing a child supporting an elder. This concept is central to Confucian ethics.

> **孝 (효) — Filial Piety**
>
> - **Structure**: 老 (old/elder) above + 子 (child) below
> - **Meaning**: A child carrying/supporting an elder
>
> Key compounds:
> - 孝道 (효도) — the way of filial piety
> - 孝子 (효자) — filial son; devoted child
> - 孝女 (효녀) — filial daughter
> - 不孝 (불효) — unfilial; failing in filial duty
>
> Classical quote:
> 百善孝爲先 (백선효위선)
> "Of all virtues, filial piety is the first."

### 5.2 The Heart-Mind Connection

In Classical Chinese thought, 心 (심) represents both the physical heart and the mind/consciousness. This is reflected in the many emotional and cognitive characters that use 心/忄 as their radical:

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Category | Connection to 心 |
|-----------|------|------|----------|-----------------|
| 思 | 사 (sa) | 생각할 사 | Cognition | Thinking — an activity of 心 |
| 想 | 상 (sang) | 생각할 상 | Cognition | Imagining — 心 + 相 (appearance) |
| 情 | 정 (jeong) | 뜻 정 | Emotion | Feeling — inner state of 心 |
| 性 | 성 (seong) | 성품 성 | Character | Nature — innate quality of 心 |
| 愛 | 애 (ae) | 사랑 애 | Emotion | Love — 心 within the character |
| 忠 | 충 (chung) | 충성 충 | Virtue | Loyalty — 中 (center) + 心 (heart) |
| 怒 | 노 (no) | 성낼 노 | Emotion | Anger — 奴 (phonetic) + 心 |
| 悲 | 비 (bi) | 슬플 비 | Emotion | Sadness — 非 (not) + 心 |
| 恐 | 공 (gong) | 두려울 공 | Emotion | Fear — 巩 (phonetic) + 心 |
| 慈 | 자 (ja) | 사랑할 자 | Virtue | Compassion — 茲 (phonetic) + 心 |

## 8. Action Characters: Communication and Knowledge

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Meaning | Formation |
|-----------|------|------|---------|-----------|
| 見 | 견 (gyeon) | 볼 견 | See, view | 目 (eye) on legs — person looking |
| 聞 | 문 (mun) | 들을 문 | Hear, listen | 耳 (ear) at 門 (gate) — listening at the door |
| 言 | 언 (eon) | 말씀 언 | Speak, words | Tongue with sound waves above |
| 書 | 서 (seo) | 글 서 | Write, book | Hand holding brush writing on paper |
| 讀 | 독 (dok) | 읽을 독 | Read | 言 (speech) + 賣 (phonetic) |

| 敎 | 교 (gyo) | 가르칠 교 | Teach | Complex: hand + child + action = instructing |
| 話 | 화 (hwa) | 말씀 화 | Speech, talk | 言 (speech) + 舌 (tongue) |

```
1. ___入 (출입, coming and going): 出 + ?
2. ___校 (입교, entering a school): ? + 校
3. ___國 (귀국, returning to one's country): ? + 國
4. ___立 (독립, independence): 獨 + ?
5. ___行 (보행, walking on foot): 步 + ?
```

<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

```
1. 出入 (출입): 出 + 入 (exit + enter = coming and going)
2. 入校 (입교): 入 + 校 (enter + school = entering school)
3. 歸國 (귀국): 歸 + 國 (return + country = returning to one's country)
4. 獨立 (독립): 獨 + 立 (alone + stand = independence)
5. 步行 (보행): 步 + 行 (step + go = walking)
```

### Exercise 5: Four-Character Phrase Interpretation

Explain the meaning of each phrase:

```
1. 人山人海 (인산인해)
2. 言行一致 (언행일치)
3. 父子有親 (부자유친)
4. 手不釋卷 (수불석권)
5. 口是心非 (구시심비)
```

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

```
1. 人山人海 (인산인해)
   Literal: Person-mountain, person-sea
   Meaning: A huge crowd; throngs of people as dense as mountains and seas

2. 言行一致 (언행일치)
   Literal: Words-actions-one-match
   Meaning: One's words match one's actions; integrity; walking the talk

3. 父子有親 (부자유친)
   Literal: Father-son-have-affection
   Meaning: There should be natural affection between parent and child
   (One of the Five Confucian Relationships)

4. 手不釋卷 (수불석권)
   Literal: Hand-not-release-book
   Meaning: Constantly reading; a devoted reader who never puts down a book

5. 口是心非 (구시심비)
   Literal: Mouth-yes-heart-no
   Meaning: Saying one thing but meaning another; hypocrisy; lip service
```

### Exercise 6: Simple Sentence Reading

Translate the following Classical Chinese phrases:

```
1. 子曰 (자왈)
2. 人皆有心 (인개유심)
3. 王愛民 (왕애민)
4. 兄弟有信 (형제유신)
5. 學而不止 (학이불지)
```

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

```
1. 子曰 (자왈) — The Master said (Confucius said)
   子 = Master/teacher, 曰 = said

2. 人皆有心 (인개유심) — All people have a heart/mind
   人 = person, 皆 = all, 有 = have, 心 = heart/mind

3. 王愛民 (왕애민) — The king loves the people
   王 = king, 愛 = love, 民 = people

4. 兄弟有信 (형제유신) — Between brothers there is trust
   兄弟 = brothers, 有 = have, 信 = trust

   學 = learn, 而 = and/yet, 不 = not, 止 = stop
```
