## 1. Korean Hanmun Literature: Overview

Korean 한문학(漢文學, hanmun literature) refers to the body of literature written by Korean authors in Classical Chinese. From the Three Kingdoms period through the end of the Joseon Dynasty, Classical Chinese served as the primary written language of Korean scholarship, government, and high literature.

### Timeline of Korean Hanmun Literature

| Period | Era | Key Developments |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| Three Kingdoms | ~57 BCE - 668 CE | Adoption of Chinese writing; earliest inscriptions |
| Unified Silla | 668-935 CE | First major Korean hanmun writers (최치원) |
| Goryeo | 918-1392 | Buddhist literature; civil service exam essays |
| Early Joseon | 1392-1592 | Neo-Confucian scholarship; philosophical treatises |
| Late Joseon | 1592-1897 | Silhak (practical learning); literary criticism |

### Why Study Korean Hanmun?

| Reason | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Historical records | Korean history was recorded in Classical Chinese |
| Philosophy | Korean Confucian thought was expressed in hanmun |
| Literature | Poetry, essays, and fiction in hanmun form a vast corpus |
| Legal documents | Laws, edicts, and memorials are in hanmun |
| Cultural identity | Understanding hanmun connects to Korean cultural heritage |

## 2. Choe Chiwon: Korea's First Literary Master

### 최치원(崔致遠, 857-?) — Scholar, Poet, Diplomat

Choe Chiwon passed the Tang Dynasty civil service examination at age 18 and served in the Tang court before returning to Silla. He is considered Korea's greatest Classical Chinese writer.

### 토황소격문/討黃巢檄文 (Manifesto Against Huang Chao)

This proclamation, written on behalf of a Tang general against the rebel Huang Chao, is considered a masterpiece of military rhetoric. It reportedly made Huang Chao fall from his seat in terror.

**Famous excerpt**:

```
不惟天下之人 皆欲殺汝
불유천하지인 개욕살여

抑亦天下之鬼 皆欲殺汝
억역천하지귀 개욕살여
```

### Character Analysis

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 不惟 | 불유 (bulyu) | not only |
| 天下 | 천하 (cheonha) | all under heaven |
| 之 | 지 (ji) | (possessive) |
| 人 | 인 (in) | people |
| 皆 | 개 (gae) | all |
| 欲 | 욕 (yok) | desire to |
| 殺 | 살 (sal) | kill |
| 汝 | 여 (yeo) | you |
| 抑 | 억 (eok) | moreover |
| 亦 | 역 (yeok) | also |
| 鬼 | 귀 (gwi) | ghosts, spirits |

**Translation**: "Not only do all the people under heaven desire to kill you, but moreover all the ghosts under heaven also desire to kill you."

### Rhetorical Analysis

| Device | Instance |
|--------|----------|
| Parallelism | 天下之人 ↔ 天下之鬼 |
| Hyperbole | Even the ghosts want you dead |
| Repetition | 皆欲殺汝 repeated |
| Climax | From humans to spirits — escalating condemnation |

### Choe Chiwon's Poetry

**秋夜雨中 (추야우중, On a Rainy Autumn Night)**

```
秋風唯苦吟    추풍유고음
世路少知音    세로소지음
窗外三更雨    창외삼경우
燈前萬里心    등전만리심
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 秋風唯苦吟 | In the autumn wind, only bitter chanting |
| 世路少知音 | On life's road, few who understand my music |
| 窗外三更雨 | Outside the window, rain at the third watch |
| 燈前萬里心 | Before the lamp, a heart ten thousand li away |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 秋 | 추 (chu) | autumn |
| 苦 | 고 (go) | bitter, painful |
| 吟 | 음 (eum) | to chant, recite |
| 知音 | 지음 (jieum) | one who understands, kindred spirit |
| 三更 | 삼경 (samgyeong) | third watch (11pm-1am) |
| 萬里 | 만리 (malli) | ten thousand li (very far) |

## 3. Jeong Yagyong: Scholar of the Joseon Renaissance

### 정약용(丁若鏞, 1762-1836) — Dasan, Polymath and Reformer

Jeong Yagyong, pen name 다산(茶山, Dasan), was a leading figure of the Silhak (실학/實學, Practical Learning) movement. During his 18-year exile, he wrote over 500 volumes covering governance, law, medicine, engineering, and poetry.

### Selected Poetry

**竹蘭詩 (죽란시, Poem of Bamboo and Orchid)**

```
竹有節而虛    죽유절이허
蘭有香而幽    란유향이유
君子取其德    군자취기덕
不以色爲求    불이색위구
```

### Line-by-Line Analysis

| Line | Reading | Translation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 竹有節而虛 | 죽유절이허 | Bamboo has joints yet is hollow |
| 蘭有香而幽 | 란유향이유 | Orchid has fragrance yet is secluded |
| 君子取其德 | 군자취기덕 | The gentleman takes their virtue |
| 不以色爲求 | 불이색위구 | Does not seek outward appearance |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 竹 | 죽 (juk) | bamboo |
| 節 | 절 (jeol) | joint, integrity |
| 虛 | 허 (heo) | hollow, humble |
| 蘭 | 란 (ran) | orchid |
| 香 | 향 (hyang) | fragrance |
| 幽 | 유 (yu) | secluded, quiet |
| 德 | 덕 (deok) | virtue |
| 色 | 색 (saek) | color, appearance |

### From 목민심서/牧民心書 (Admonishing on Governing the People)

```
牧之義 養也
목지의 양야

牧民者 養民也
목민자 양민야

非以民養己也
비이민양기야
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 牧之義 養也 | The meaning of "shepherding" is "nurturing" |
| 牧民者 養民也 | One who shepherds the people nurtures the people |
| 非以民養己也 | Not using the people to nurture oneself |

## 4. Yi Hwang: The Confucian Sage of Korea

### 이황(李滉, 1501-1570) — Toegye, Master of Neo-Confucianism

Yi Hwang, pen name 퇴계(退溪, Toegye), is considered Korea's greatest Confucian philosopher. His debates on the nature of 이(理, principle) and 기(氣, material force) with 이이(李珥, Yulgok) represent the pinnacle of Korean philosophical thought.

### From 퇴계집/退溪集 — On Learning

```
爲學之要 在於窮理
위학지요 재어궁리

窮理之要 在於讀書
궁리지요 재어독서

讀書之法 必先端心
독서지법 필선단심

正坐緩讀
정좌완독
```

### Analysis

| Line | Reading | Translation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 爲學之要 在於窮理 | 위학지요 재어궁리 | The essence of learning lies in exhaustively investigating principle |
| 窮理之要 在於讀書 | 궁리지요 재어독서 | The essence of investigating principle lies in reading |
| 讀書之法 必先端心 | 독서지법 필선단심 | The method of reading must first rectify the mind |
| 正坐緩讀 | 정좌완독 | Sit properly and read slowly |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 爲 | 위 (wi) | to do, practice |
| 要 | 요 (yo) | essence, key point |
| 窮 | 궁 (gung) | to exhaust, investigate fully |
| 理 | 리 (ri) | principle, pattern |
| 端 | 단 (dan) | to rectify, make straight |
| 正 | 정 (jeong) | correct, upright |
| 緩 | 완 (wan) | slow, unhurried |

### 도산십이곡/陶山十二曲 (Twelve Songs of Dosan)

Though originally composed with Korean lyrics, Toegye's preface was written in Classical Chinese, explaining his poetic philosophy:

```
古人之作歌 蓋出於性情之正
고인지작가 개출어성정지정
```

"The songs composed by the ancients all arose from the rectitude of their innate nature and emotions."

## 5. Korean-Specific Usage Patterns

### Differences from Standard Classical Chinese

Korean hanmun developed distinctive characteristics that set it apart from Chinese-authored Classical Chinese.

| Feature | Standard Classical Chinese | Korean Hanmun |
|---------|--------------------------|---------------|
| Word order | Closer to Chinese grammar | Occasional Korean SOV influence |
| Vocabulary | Standard literary Chinese | Includes Korea-specific terms |
| Proper nouns | Chinese names | Korean names with Chinese characters |
| Administrative terms | Chinese bureaucratic terms | Korean-adapted terms |
| Reading method | Direct reading | 현토(懸吐) — adding Korean particles |

### Korean-Specific Vocabulary

| Term | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|---------|
| Korea | 朝鮮 / 東國 | 조선/동국 | Joseon / Eastern Country |
| King | 殿下 | 전하 | Your Majesty (Korean usage) |
| Capital | 漢城 / 漢陽 | 한성/한양 | Hanseong/Hanyang (Seoul) |
| Province | 道 | 도 | Provincial unit |
| County | 郡 | 군 | County unit |
| Official robe | 冠帶 | 관대 | Court dress |
| Civil service exam | 科擧 | 과거 | State examination |
| Scholar | 儒生 | 유생 | Confucian scholar |
| Academy | 書院 | 서원 | Private Confucian academy |

### 현토/懸吐 (Reading Marks)

Korean scholars developed a system of adding Korean grammatical particles to Classical Chinese text to facilitate reading in Korean word order:

**Example**: 學而時習之

- Original: 學而時習之
- With 현토: 學하고 而 時로 習하야 之를
- Korean reading: 배우고 때로 익히어 이를

## 6. The Sangso Format

### 상소문/上疏文 (Memorial to the Throne)

The 상소문 was a formal document submitted by officials to the king, a crucial genre of Korean hanmun.

### Standard Structure

| Section | Hanja | Reading | Function |
|---------|-------|---------|----------|
| Address | 伏以 | 복이 | "Prostrating, I submit that..." |
| Background | 竊惟 | 절유 | "I humbly consider that..." |
| Main argument | 臣以爲 | 신이위 | "Your servant believes that..." |
| Request | 伏望 | 복망 | "I humbly hope that..." |
| Closing | 臣不勝 | 신불승 | "Your servant cannot contain [his feelings]" |

### Common Formulaic Expressions

| Expression | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|-------|---------|---------|
| Your servant | 臣 | 신 (sin) | First person (to king) |
| I humbly | 竊 | 절 (jeol) | Humble self-reference |
| Prostrating | 伏 | 복 (bok) | Showing extreme deference |
| Your Majesty | 殿下 | 전하 (jeonha) | Addressing the king |
| Ten thousand deaths | 萬死 | 만사 (mansa) | "At risk of death" (formulaic) |

### Sample Opening

```
臣某 誠惶誠恐 頓首頓首
신모 성황성공 돈수돈수
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 臣某 | 신모 | Your servant So-and-so |
| 誠惶誠恐 | 성황성공 | Truly terrified, truly fearful |
| 頓首頓首 | 돈수돈수 | Bowing head repeatedly |

This formulaic opening expresses the writer's humble position before the throne.

## 7. Other Major Korean Hanmun Writers

### Survey of Important Authors

| Author | Hanja | Period | Major Works |
|--------|-------|--------|-------------|
| 김부식 | 金富軾 | 1075-1151 | 삼국사기(三國史記), official history |
| 일연 | 一然 | 1206-1289 | 삼국유사(三國遺事), folklore and legends |
| 이규보 | 李奎報 | 1168-1241 | 동국이상국집(東國李相國集), poetry |
| 정도전 | 鄭道傳 | 1342-1398 | 조선경국전(朝鮮經國典), statecraft |
| 김시습 | 金時習 | 1435-1493 | 금오신화(金鰲新話), first Korean fiction |
| 허균 | 許筠 | 1569-1618 | 홍길동전(洪吉童傳), social criticism |
| 박지원 | 朴趾源 | 1737-1805 | 열하일기(熱河日記), travel literature |

### 김시습(金時習): From 금오신화/金鰲新話

The 금오신화 is considered the first Korean fiction written in Classical Chinese. Each story explores themes of love, death, and the supernatural.

**Opening of 만복사저포기/萬福寺樗蒲記**:

```
南原府有萬福寺
남원부유만복사

寺有古殿
사유고전

梁某秀才 棲遲其中
양모수재 서지기중
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 南原府有萬福寺 | In Namwon Prefecture there was Manbok Temple |
| 寺有古殿 | The temple had an old hall |
| 梁某秀才 棲遲其中 | A scholar surnamed Yang lodged within it |

## 8. Korean Hanmun Poetry Traditions

### Genres of Korean Hansi (漢詩)

| Genre | Hanja | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 送別詩 | 송별시 | Farewell poems |
| 詠物詩 | 영물시 | Poems on objects (philosophical metaphor) |
| 田園詩 | 전원시 | Pastoral poems |
| 懷古詩 | 회고시 | Poems reflecting on history |
| 感懷詩 | 감회시 | Poems of personal reflection |
| 山水詩 | 산수시 | Landscape poems |

### Korean Poetic Conventions

| Convention | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Place names | Korean locations written in hanja |
| Seasonal imagery | Korean-specific natural references |
| Historical allusions | References to Korean history |
| Confucian values | Loyalty, filial piety, scholarly life |
| Exile poetry | Common theme due to political exile system |

### 이규보(李奎報): 新春 (신춘, New Spring)

```
去年今日花猶在    거년금일화유재
今年今日花又開    금년금일화우개
花開花落年年事    화개화락연연사
人去人來日日催    인거인래일일최
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 去年今日花猶在 | Last year on this day the flowers were still there |
| 今年今日花又開 | This year on this day the flowers bloom again |
| 花開花落年年事 | Flowers blooming and falling — a yearly affair |
| 人去人來日日催 | People going and coming — day by day hastened |

## 9. Historical Documents in Hanmun

### Types of Korean Historical Hanmun Documents

| Document Type | Hanja | Reading | Purpose |
|--------------|-------|---------|---------|
| Official history | 正史 | 정사 | Authorized dynastic histories |
| Annals | 實錄 | 실록 | Daily records of the court |
| Genealogy | 族譜 | 족보 | Family lineage records |
| Land records | 量田文 | 양전문 | Land survey documents |
| Diplomatic letters | 國書 | 국서 | Letters between states |
| Inscriptions | 碑文 | 비문 | Stone stele inscriptions |

### The 조선왕조실록/朝鮮王朝實錄 (Annals of the Joseon Dynasty)

The most extensive historical record in Korean hanmun, covering 472 years (1392-1863) of the Joseon Dynasty. It was designated a UNESCO Memory of the World in 1997.

**Sample entry format**:

```
[Year] 月 [Day]
[Official] 上疏曰...
上曰...
```

| Element | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| 月 (월) | Month |
| 上疏曰 (상소왈) | Submitted a memorial saying... |
| 上曰 (상왈) | The King said... |

### 삼국사기/三國史記 — Opening passage

```
始祖姓朴氏 諱赫居世
시조성박씨 휘혁거세
```

"The founding ancestor's surname was Bak (Park), and his taboo name was Hyeokgeose."

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 始祖 | 시조 (sijo) | founding ancestor |
| 姓 | 성 (seong) | surname |
| 諱 | 휘 (hwi) | taboo name (posthumous) |
