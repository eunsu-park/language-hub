## 1. Introduction to Classical Chinese Poetry

Classical Chinese poetry (한시/漢詩, hansi) represents one of the highest achievements of East Asian literary culture. For over two millennia, educated individuals across China, Korea, Japan, and Vietnam composed poetry in Classical Chinese.

### Key Terms

| Term | Hanja | Korean Reading | Meaning |
|------|-------|----------------|---------|
| Poetry | 詩 | 시 (si) | poem, verse |
| Verse/Song | 歌 | 가 (ga) | song, ballad |
| Rhyme | 韻 | 운 (un) | rhyme |
| Tone | 聲 | 성 (seong) | sound, tone |
| Couplet | 聯 | 련/연 (ryeon/yeon) | linked pair of lines |
| Stanza | 首 | 수 (su) | counter for poems |

### Historical Development

| Period | Korean | Features |
|--------|--------|----------|
| 詩經 (시경) | Book of Songs (~1000 BCE) | 4-character lines, folk songs |
| 楚辭 (초사) | Songs of Chu (~300 BCE) | Irregular meter, 兮(혜) particle |
| 漢樂府 (한악부) | Han Music Bureau | Narrative ballads |
| 古體詩 (고체시) | Old-style verse | Free-form, no tonal rules |
| 近體詩 (근체시) | Regulated verse (Tang) | Strict tonal and structural rules |

The Tang Dynasty (唐/당, 618-907 CE) is widely considered the golden age of Chinese poetry, producing masters such as 李白(이백, Li Bai), 杜甫(두보, Du Fu), and 王維(왕유, Wang Wei).

## 2. Poetic Forms: Jueju and Lüshi

### 절구 (絕句, Jueju) — Quatrain

A quatrain consists of **four lines** with a fixed number of characters per line.

| Form | Hanja | Characters per Line | Total Characters |
|------|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| Five-character quatrain | 오언절구 (五言絕句) | 5 | 20 |
| Seven-character quatrain | 칠언절구 (七言絕句) | 7 | 28 |

### Structure of a Quatrain

| Line | Role | Korean | Function |
|------|------|--------|----------|
| Line 1 | 기 (起) | 기구 (起句) | Opening — introduces the scene |
| Line 2 | 승 (承) | 승구 (承句) | Development — elaborates the opening |
| Line 3 | 전 (轉) | 전구 (轉句) | Turn — shifts perspective or mood |
| Line 4 | 결 (結) | 결구 (結句) | Conclusion — resolves or reflects |

This 기승전결(起承轉結) structure is fundamental to East Asian composition and applies beyond poetry.

### 율시 (律詩, Lüshi) — Regulated Verse

An eight-line poem with strict rules:

| Feature | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Lines | 8 lines |
| Characters per line | 5 or 7 |
| Couplets 2 and 3 | Must be parallel (대구/對句) |
| Rhyme | Required at lines 2, 4, 6, 8 (and optionally line 1) |
| Tonal pattern | Follows 평측(平仄) rules |

## 3. Tonal Rules: Pingze Basics

Classical Chinese had a tonal system that governed poetic meter, called 평측(平仄, pingze).

### The Two Tone Categories

| Category | Hanja | Korean | Description |
|----------|-------|--------|-------------|
| Level tone | 平 | 평 (pyeong) | Even, sustained tone |
| Oblique tones | 仄 | 측 (cheuk) | Rising, departing, or entering tones |

The oblique category includes three sub-tones:

| Tone | Hanja | Korean | Character |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| Rising | 上聲 | 상성 (sangseong) | Rises from low to high |
| Departing | 去聲 | 거성 (geoseong) | Falls sharply |
| Entering | 入聲 | 입성 (ipseong) | Short, stopped (with -k, -t, -p ending) |

### Basic Pingze Pattern for 오언절구 (Five-Character Quatrain)

Using ○ for 평(level) and ● for 측(oblique):

```
Pattern A:
Line 1: ●●○○●    (oblique-oblique-level-level-oblique)
Line 2: ○○●●○    (level-level-oblique-oblique-level) — rhyme
Line 3: ○○○●●    (level-level-level-oblique-oblique)
Line 4: ●●●○○    (oblique-oblique-oblique-level-level) — rhyme
```

> **Important**: The rule of 일삼오불론(一三五不論) states that the 1st, 3rd, and 5th characters in a line may deviate from the prescribed tone. The 2nd, 4th, and 6th must be correct.

## 4. Li Bai: Quiet Night Thought

### 이백(李白): 靜夜思 (고요한 밤의 생각, Thoughts on a Quiet Night)

This is perhaps the most famous Chinese poem, memorized by virtually every Chinese student.

```
靜 夜 思
정 야 사

床 前 明 月 光
상 전 명 월 광

疑 是 地 上 霜
의 시 지 상 상

舉 頭 望 明 月
거 두 망 명 월

低 頭 思 故 鄉
저 두 사 고 향
```

### Character-by-Character Analysis

**Title: 靜夜思**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 靜 | 정 (jeong) | quiet, still |
| 夜 | 야 (ya) | night |
| 思 | 사 (sa) | to think, thoughts |

**Line 1: 床前明月光** — "Before the bed, bright moonlight"

| Character | Reading | Meaning | Role |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|
| 床 | 상 (sang) | bed | Subject location |
| 前 | 전 (jeon) | before, in front | Postposition |
| 明 | 명 (myeong) | bright | Adjective |
| 月 | 월 (wol) | moon | Modifier |
| 光 | 광 (gwang) | light | Object |

**Line 2: 疑是地上霜** — "I suspect it is frost upon the ground"

| Character | Reading | Meaning | Role |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|
| 疑 | 의 (ui) | to doubt, suspect | Verb |
| 是 | 시 (si) | to be, is | Copula |
| 地 | 지 (ji) | ground, earth | Noun |
| 上 | 상 (sang) | upon, on | Postposition |
| 霜 | 상 (sang) | frost | Object |

**Line 3: 舉頭望明月** — "I raise my head and gaze at the bright moon"

| Character | Reading | Meaning | Role |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|
| 舉 | 거 (geo) | to raise, lift | Verb |
| 頭 | 두 (du) | head | Object |
| 望 | 망 (mang) | to gaze at, look far | Verb |
| 明 | 명 (myeong) | bright | Adjective |
| 月 | 월 (wol) | moon | Object |

**Line 4: 低頭思故鄉** — "I lower my head and think of my old home"

| Character | Reading | Meaning | Role |
|-----------|---------|---------|------|
| 低 | 저 (jeo) | to lower | Verb |
| 頭 | 두 (du) | head | Object |
| 思 | 사 (sa) | to think of | Verb |
| 故 | 고 (go) | old, former | Adjective |
| 鄉 | 향 (hyang) | hometown, village | Object |

### Poetic Analysis

- **Form**: 오언절구(五言絕句, five-character quatrain)
- **Rhyme**: 光(광), 霜(상), 鄉(향) share the -ang rhyme group
- **Imagery**: Moon → frost → moon → hometown creates a circular emotional journey
- **Theme**: 망향(望鄉) — longing for home, a universal human emotion

## 5. Du Fu: Spring View

### 두보(杜甫): 春望 (봄날의 풍경, Spring View)

Written during the An Lushan Rebellion (755-763), this poem captures the devastation of war.

```
春 望
춘 망

國 破 山 河 在
국 파 산 하 재

城 春 草 木 深
성 춘 초 목 심

感 時 花 濺 淚
감 시 화 천 루

恨 別 鳥 驚 心
한 별 조 경 심
```

### Line-by-Line Analysis

**Line 1: 國破山河在** — "The nation is shattered, yet mountains and rivers remain"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 國 | 국 (guk) | nation, country |
| 破 | 파 (pa) | to break, shatter |
| 山 | 산 (san) | mountain |
| 河 | 하 (ha) | river |
| 在 | 재 (jae) | to exist, remain |

**Line 2: 城春草木深** — "The city in spring — grass and trees grow dense"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 城 | 성 (seong) | city walls, city |
| 春 | 춘 (chun) | spring |
| 草 | 초 (cho) | grass |
| 木 | 목 (mok) | tree |
| 深 | 심 (sim) | deep, dense |

**Lines 3-4**: The flowers make one weep, the birds startle the heart — nature mirrors human sorrow.

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 感 | 감 (gam) | to feel, be moved |
| 時 | 시 (si) | time, season |
| 花 | 화 (hwa) | flower |
| 濺 | 천 (cheon) | to splash, shed |
| 淚 | 루 (ru) | tears |
| 恨 | 한 (han) | to resent, grieve |
| 別 | 별 (byeol) | parting, separation |
| 鳥 | 조 (jo) | bird |
| 驚 | 경 (gyeong) | to startle |
| 心 | 심 (sim) | heart, mind |

### Poetic Analysis

- **Contrast**: Enduring nature vs. destroyed human civilization
- **Technique**: Personification — flowers "shed tears," birds "startle the heart"
- **Historical context**: Written when the capital Chang'an (장안/長安) fell to rebels

## 6. Wang Zhihuan: Climbing Stork Tower

### 왕지환(王之渙): 登鸛雀樓 (관작루에 오르며, Climbing Stork Tower)

```
登 鸛 雀 樓
등 관 작 루

白 日 依 山 盡
백 일 의 산 진

黃 河 入 海 流
황 하 입 해 류

欲 窮 千 里 目
욕 궁 천 리 목

更 上 一 層 樓
갱 상 일 층 루
```

### Character-by-Character Analysis

**Title: 登鸛雀樓**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 登 | 등 (deung) | to ascend, climb |
| 鸛 | 관 (gwan) | stork |
| 雀 | 작 (jak) | sparrow |
| 樓 | 루 (ru) | tower, pavilion |

**Line 1: 白日依山盡** — "The white sun leans against the mountains and sinks away"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 白 | 백 (baek) | white |
| 日 | 일 (il) | sun, day |
| 依 | 의 (ui) | to lean on, rely |
| 山 | 산 (san) | mountain |
| 盡 | 진 (jin) | to exhaust, end |

**Line 2: 黃河入海流** — "The Yellow River enters the sea and flows on"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 黃 | 황 (hwang) | yellow |
| 河 | 하 (ha) | river |
| 入 | 입 (ip) | to enter |
| 海 | 해 (hae) | sea |
| 流 | 류 (ryu) | to flow |

**Line 3: 欲窮千里目** — "If you wish to exhaust the view of a thousand li"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 欲 | 욕 (yok) | to desire, wish |
| 窮 | 궁 (gung) | to exhaust, reach the limit |
| 千 | 천 (cheon) | thousand |
| 里 | 리 (ri) | li (unit of distance) |
| 目 | 목 (mok) | eye, vision |

**Line 4: 更上一層樓** — "Climb up one more floor of the tower"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 更 | 갱 (gaeng) | further, more |
| 上 | 상 (sang) | to ascend |
| 一 | 일 (il) | one |
| 層 | 층 (cheung) | floor, layer |
| 樓 | 루 (ru) | tower |

### Poetic Analysis

- **Form**: 오언절구(五言絕句)
- **Rhyme**: 流(류), 樓(루) — rhyme pair
- **Philosophical reading**: To see further in life, one must rise higher — a metaphor for continuous effort and aspiration
- **Famous quotation**: 更上一層樓 (갱상일층루) became a proverb meaning "to go one step further"

## 8. Reading Poetry: Method and Approach

### Step-by-Step Method for Reading 한시

1. **Identify the form**: Count characters per line and number of lines
2. **Read the title**: Often reveals theme, occasion, or setting
3. **Parse each line**: Identify subject, verb, object patterns
4. **Check the rhyme**: Note which final characters rhyme
5. **Find parallel couplets**: Look for structural balance between lines
6. **Identify imagery**: What natural or cultural symbols appear?
7. **Determine the mood**: What emotions does the poet convey?
8. **Consider context**: When and why was the poem written?

### Common Sentence Patterns in Poetry

| Pattern | Example | Structure |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Subject + Verb | 月出 (월출) | Moon rises |
| Subject + Adjective | 山高 (산고) | Mountains are high |
| Place + Object + Verb | 江上行人 (강상행인) | On the river, a traveler |
| Verb + Object | 望月 (망월) | Gaze at the moon |
| Adverb + Verb + Object | 獨坐幽篁 (독좌유황) | Sit alone in a quiet bamboo grove |

### Korean Reading Order (현토/懸吐)

When reading Classical Chinese poetry in Korean, the word order is rearranged and Korean grammatical particles are added:

**Example**: 舉頭望明月

- Chinese order: 舉(raise) 頭(head) 望(gaze) 明(bright) 月(moon)
- Korean reading: 頭를 舉하여 明月을 望하다 → 머리를 들어 밝은 달을 바라보다

## 9. Additional Poems for Study

### 맹호연(孟浩然): 春曉 (봄날 새벽, Spring Dawn)

```
春 眠 不 覺 曉
춘 면 불 각 효

處 處 聞 啼 鳥
처 처 문 제 조

夜 來 風 雨 聲
야 래 풍 우 성

花 落 知 多 少
화 락 지 다 소
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 春眠不覺曉 | In spring slumber, one does not notice the dawn |
| 處處聞啼鳥 | Everywhere, the singing of birds is heard |
| 夜來風雨聲 | In the night came the sounds of wind and rain |
| 花落知多少 | How many flowers have fallen, who can know? |

**Key vocabulary**:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 眠 | 면 (myeon) | to sleep |
| 覺 | 각 (gak) | to notice, awaken |
| 曉 | 효 (hyo) | dawn |
| 處 | 처 (cheo) | place |
| 聞 | 문 (mun) | to hear |
| 啼 | 제 (je) | to cry, sing (birds) |
| 落 | 락 (rak) | to fall |

### 왕유(王維): 鹿柴 (녹채, Deer Enclosure)

```
空 山 不 見 人
공 산 불 견 인

但 聞 人 語 響
단 문 인 어 향

返 景 入 深 林
반 경 입 심 림

復 照 靑 苔 上
부 조 청 태 상
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 空山不見人 | In the empty mountain, no one is seen |
| 但聞人語響 | Only the echoes of human voices are heard |
| 返景入深林 | Returning sunlight enters the deep forest |
| 復照靑苔上 | And shines again upon the green moss |
