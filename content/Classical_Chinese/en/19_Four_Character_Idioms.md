## 1. What Are Four-Character Idioms?

사자성어(四字成語, sajaseongeeo) are fixed four-character expressions from Classical Chinese that convey complete ideas, moral lessons, or vivid imagery. They are deeply embedded in Korean language and culture.

### Key Terms

| Term | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|---------|
| Four-character idiom | 四字成語 | 사자성어 | Fixed 4-character expression |
| Idiom/Proverb | 成語 | 성어 | Established saying |
| Origin/Source | 出典 | 출전 | Source text |
| Meaning | 意味 | 의미 | Meaning |

### Structure Types

Most 사자성어 follow one of these structural patterns:

| Pattern | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| 2+2 compound | AB + CD | 溫故知新 (온고 + 지신) |
| Subject + Predicate | SP + SP | 山高水長 (산고 + 수장) |
| Verb + Object pair | VO + VO | 切磋琢磨 (절차 + 탁마) |
| Modifier + Noun pair | MN + MN | 明月清風 (명월 + 청풍) |
| Narrative condensation | Full sentence compressed | 臥薪嘗膽 (와신 + 상담) |

## 2. Idioms About Learning and Knowledge

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning | Source |
|-------|---------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| 溫故知新 | 온고지신 | Warm the old, know the new | Review the past to learn the new | 論語 |
| 切磋琢磨 | 절차탁마 | Cut, file, chisel, polish | Diligent mutual study and refinement | 詩經 |
| 博學多聞 | 박학다문 | Broad learning, much hearing | Widely learned and well-informed | 論語 |
| 學而不厭 | 학이불염 | Study without tiring | Never weary of learning | 論語 |
| 不恥下問 | 불치하문 | Not ashamed to ask below | Not embarrassed to ask those of lower status | 論語 |
| 讀書百遍 | 독서백편 | Read a book a hundred times | Thorough reading brings understanding | 三國志 |
| 螢雪之功 | 형설지공 | Merit of firefly and snow | Studying under difficult conditions | 晉書 |
| 敎學相長 | 교학상장 | Teaching and learning mutually grow | Teaching and learning benefit each other | 禮記 |

### Detailed Analysis

**溫故知新 (온고지신)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 溫 | 온 (on) | to warm, review |
| 故 | 고 (go) | old, past |
| 知 | 지 (ji) | to know |
| 新 | 신 (sin) | new |

**Source**: 논어/論語 — 子曰: "溫故而知新 可以爲師矣"
"The Master said: 'One who reviews the old and knows the new can be a teacher.'"

**切磋琢磨 (절차탁마)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 切 | 절 (jeol) | to cut (bone) |
| 磋 | 차 (cha) | to file (ivory) |
| 琢 | 탁 (tak) | to chisel (jade) |
| 磨 | 마 (ma) | to polish (stone) |

Originally describing the process of crafting precious materials, it came to mean refining one's character and scholarship through mutual effort.

## 3. Idioms About Character and Virtue

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning | Source |
|-------|---------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| 言行一致 | 언행일치 | Words and actions match | Practice what you preach | 論語 |
| 自强不息 | 자강불식 | Self-strengthen without rest | Constant self-improvement | 周易 |
| 克己復禮 | 극기복례 | Overcome self, return to propriety | Self-mastery and adherence to ritual | 論語 |
| 安貧樂道 | 안빈낙도 | Content in poverty, joyful in the Way | Finding happiness in virtue despite hardship | 論語 |
| 先公後私 | 선공후사 | Public first, private later | Put the public good before personal interest | — |
| 見利思義 | 견리사의 | See profit, think of righteousness | When seeing gain, consider what is right | 論語 |
| 正心修身 | 정심수신 | Rectify mind, cultivate self | Self-cultivation through inner rectification | 大學 |
| 以身作則 | 이신작칙 | With the body set the standard | Lead by personal example | — |

### Detailed Analysis

**克己復禮 (극기복례)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 克 | 극 (geuk) | to overcome, conquer |
| 己 | 기 (gi) | self, oneself |
| 復 | 복 (bok) | to return to |
| 禮 | 례 (rye) | ritual propriety, decorum |

**Source**: 논어/論語 — 顏淵問仁。子曰："克己復禮爲仁。"
"Yan Yuan asked about benevolence. The Master said: 'To overcome the self and return to propriety — that is benevolence.'"

**自强不息 (자강불식)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 自 | 자 (ja) | self |
| 强 | 강 (gang) | to strengthen |
| 不 | 불 (bul) | not |
| 息 | 식 (sik) | to cease, rest |

**Source**: 주역/周易 — "天行健 君子以自强不息"
"Heaven's movement is vigorous; the noble person accordingly strengthens himself without ceasing."

## 4. Idioms About Nature

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| 山高水長 | 산고수장 | Mountains high, waters long | Enduring virtue or lasting reputation |
| 花鳥風月 | 화조풍월 | Flower, bird, wind, moon | Natural beauty; poetic subjects |
| 風前燈火 | 풍전등화 | Lamp before the wind | Precarious situation |
| 明月清風 | 명월청풍 | Bright moon, clear wind | Serene natural beauty |
| 春花秋月 | 춘화추월 | Spring flowers, autumn moon | Beautiful scenery through the seasons |
| 水魚之交 | 수어지교 | Relationship of water and fish | Inseparable friendship |
| 落花流水 | 낙화유수 | Falling flowers, flowing water | Things declining or ending |
| 靑山流水 | 청산유수 | Green mountains, flowing water | Eloquent speech; natural beauty |

### Detailed Analysis

**花鳥風月 (화조풍월)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 花 | 화 (hwa) | flower |
| 鳥 | 조 (jo) | bird |
| 風 | 풍 (pung) | wind |
| 月 | 월 (wol) | moon |

These four elements represent the quintessential subjects of East Asian art and poetry. Together they encompass the beauty of nature across all senses: visual (flowers, moon), auditory (birdsong, wind), and atmospheric.

**山高水長 (산고수장)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 山 | 산 (san) | mountain |
| 高 | 고 (go) | high |
| 水 | 수 (su) | water |
| 長 | 장 (jang) | long, enduring |

Used to praise someone whose virtue or influence is as lasting as high mountains and long rivers.

## 5. Idioms About Adversity and Perseverance

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning | Source |
|-------|---------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| 臥薪嘗膽 | 와신상담 | Lie on brushwood, taste gall | Endure hardship to achieve revenge/goals | 史記 |
| 塞翁之馬 | 새옹지마 | The old man of the border's horse | Misfortune may be a blessing in disguise | 淮南子 |
| 刻舟求劍 | 각주구검 | Carve the boat, seek the sword | Rigidly following outdated methods | 呂氏春秋 |
| 磨斧爲針 | 마부위침 | Grind an axe into a needle | Persistent effort achieves anything | 이백 legend |
| 前車之覆 | 전거지복 | Overturning of the front cart | Learn from others' mistakes | 漢書 |
| 苦盡甘來 | 고진감래 | Bitterness ends, sweetness comes | After hardship comes happiness | — |
| 轉禍爲福 | 전화위복 | Turn disaster into fortune | Make the best of a bad situation | 史記 |
| 百折不屈 | 백절불굴 | A hundred bends, not yield | Indomitable spirit | — |

### Detailed Analysis

**臥薪嘗膽 (와신상담)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 臥 | 와 (wa) | to lie down on |
| 薪 | 신 (sin) | firewood, brushwood |
| 嘗 | 상 (sang) | to taste |
| 膽 | 담 (dam) | gallbladder, bile |

**Source**: 사기/史記 — King Goujian of Yue (구천/勾踐) was defeated by King Fuchai of Wu. During his captivity, Goujian slept on brushwood and tasted bitter gall every day to remind himself of his humiliation and fuel his desire for revenge. He eventually destroyed Wu.

**塞翁之馬 (새옹지마)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 塞 | 새 (sae) | border, frontier |
| 翁 | 옹 (ong) | old man |
| 之 | 지 (ji) | (possessive) |
| 馬 | 마 (ma) | horse |

**Source**: 회남자/淮南子 — An old man near the frontier lost his horse. His neighbors commiserated, but he said, "How do you know this isn't a blessing?" The horse returned, bringing a fine stallion. "How do you know this isn't a misfortune?" His son rode it and broke his leg. "How do you know this isn't a blessing?" War came, and the son was spared from conscription.

## 6. Idioms About Human Relations

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning | Source |
|-------|---------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| 同苦同樂 | 동고동락 | Same suffering, same joy | Sharing hardship and happiness | — |
| 以心傳心 | 이심전심 | Heart transmit to heart | Wordless understanding | Buddhist |
| 莫逆之友 | 막역지우 | Friend without disagreement | Inseparable friend, bosom buddy | 莊子 |
| 管鮑之交 | 관포지교 | Friendship of Guan and Bao | Profound, selfless friendship | 史記 |
| 結草報恩 | 결초보은 | Tie grass to repay kindness | Repaying a favor even after death | 左傳 |
| 同床異夢 | 동상이몽 | Same bed, different dreams | Outwardly together but with different goals | — |
| 刎頸之交 | 문경지교 | Friendship of the severed neck | Friendship unto death | 史記 |
| 知己之友 | 지기지우 | Friend who knows oneself | A true understanding friend | — |

### Detailed Analysis

**以心傳心 (이심전심)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 以 | 이 (i) | with, by means of |
| 心 | 심 (sim) | heart, mind |
| 傳 | 전 (jeon) | to transmit |
| 心 | 심 (sim) | heart, mind |

**Origin**: From Buddhist tradition — the Buddha once held up a flower before an assembly. Only his disciple Mahakasyapa smiled, understanding without words. The Buddha said, "I have the treasury of the true dharma eye; I transmit it to Mahakasyapa." This became the founding legend of Zen/Seon Buddhism.

**管鮑之交 (관포지교)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 管 | 관 (gwan) | (surname) Guan |
| 鮑 | 포 (po) | (surname) Bao |
| 之 | 지 (ji) | (possessive) |
| 交 | 교 (gyo) | friendship |

**Origin**: Guan Zhong (관중/管仲) and Bao Shuya (포숙아/鮑叔牙) were friends in ancient China. Despite being on opposite political sides, Bao always defended and supported Guan. When Guan made mistakes in business, Bao said, "He is not stupid; his luck is bad." When Guan took more than his share, Bao said, "He is not greedy; he has a family to feed." This idiom represents utterly selfless friendship.

## 7. Origin Stories

### 각주구검/刻舟求劍 (Carving the Boat to Seek the Sword)

**Story**: A man from the state of Chu was crossing a river by boat when his sword fell into the water. He immediately carved a mark on the side of the boat, saying, "This is where my sword fell in." When the boat reached the shore, he dove into the water from where the mark was.

**Characters in the story**:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 刻 | 각 (gak) | to carve, engrave |
| 舟 | 주 (ju) | boat |
| 求 | 구 (gu) | to seek |
| 劍 | 검 (geom) | sword |

**Moral**: The boat has moved, but the mark has not. One cannot solve problems by clinging to outdated reference points. Circumstances change; methods must change with them.

### 와신상담/臥薪嘗膽 (Sleeping on Brushwood, Tasting Gall)

**Full narrative**: After King Goujian (구천/勾踐) of Yue was defeated by King Fuchai (부차/夫差) of Wu in 494 BCE, he became a virtual prisoner. For three years he served Fuchai humbly. Upon returning to Yue, he:

1. **臥薪 (와신)**: Slept on a bed of thorny brushwood instead of a comfortable mattress
2. **嘗膽 (상담)**: Hung a bitter gallbladder and tasted it every day

He asked himself daily: "Have you forgotten the shame of Kuaiji?" After twenty years of patient preparation, he destroyed the state of Wu in 473 BCE.

**Moral**: With sufficient determination and willingness to endure hardship, even the greatest setback can be overcome.

### 형설지공/螢雪之功 (The Merit of Fireflies and Snow)

**Story**: Two stories combined:
- **車胤 (차윤)**: Too poor to afford lamp oil, he caught fireflies (螢/형) in a silk bag to read by their light.
- **孫康 (손강)**: He read by the reflected light of snow (雪/설) on winter nights.

**Moral**: Poverty is no excuse for not studying. Where there is will, there is a way.

## 8. Structural Patterns

### Pattern Analysis

Understanding the grammatical structure helps memorize and understand idioms:

| Pattern | Example | Analysis |
|---------|---------|----------|
| S+V / S+V | 山高水長 | mountain-high / water-long |
| V+O / V+O | 溫故知新 | warm-old / know-new |
| V+N / V+N | 臥薪嘗膽 | lie-on-brushwood / taste-gall |
| Adj+N / Adj+N | 明月清風 | bright-moon / clear-wind |
| Neg+V / Neg+V | 不言不語 | not-speak / not-talk |
| N之N | 塞翁之馬 | border-old-man's horse |
| V+O+V+O | 刻舟求劍 | carve-boat / seek-sword |

### Parallelism Within Idioms

Most four-character idioms are internally parallel:

| Idiom | First Half | Second Half | Relationship |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| 溫故知新 | 溫故 (review old) | 知新 (know new) | Complementary |
| 花鳥風月 | 花鳥 (flower, bird) | 風月 (wind, moon) | Enumeration |
| 言行一致 | 言行 (word, deed) | 一致 (unity) | Subject + predicate |
| 同苦同樂 | 同苦 (share sorrow) | 同樂 (share joy) | Antithetical parallel |
| 自强不息 | 自强 (self-strengthen) | 不息 (not cease) | Verb + adverb |

## 9. Idioms in Context

### Using Idioms in Sentences

| Idiom | Korean Sentence | English |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| 溫故知新 | 온고지신의 정신으로 복습합시다 | Let us review with the spirit of "reviewing the old to know the new" |
| 와신상담 | 그는 와신상담의 각오로 시험을 준비했다 | He prepared for the exam with the resolve of "sleeping on brushwood and tasting gall" |
| 새옹지마 | 실패했지만 새옹지마라는 말도 있잖아요 | I failed, but as they say, "the old man's horse" (misfortune may be a blessing) |
| 이심전심 | 우리는 이심전심으로 통한다 | We communicate heart to heart |
| 절차탁마 | 서로 절차탁마하며 실력을 키웠다 | We improved our skills by polishing each other |

### Idioms in Classical Chinese Passages

**From 논어/論語**:

```
子曰 溫故而知新 可以爲師矣
자왈 온고이지신 가이위사의
```

"The Master said: 'One who reviews the old and learns the new can become a teacher.'"

**From 주역/周易**:

```
天行健 君子以自强不息
천행건 군자이자강불식
```

"Heaven moves vigorously; the noble person accordingly strengthens himself without ceasing."
