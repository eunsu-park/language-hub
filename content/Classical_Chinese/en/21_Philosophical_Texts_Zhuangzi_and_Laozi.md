## 1. The Zhuangzi: Overview

The 장자/莊子 (Zhuangzi) is one of the two foundational texts of Daoism, attributed to the philosopher 장주/莊周 (Zhuang Zhou, c. 369-286 BCE). Unlike the terse, enigmatic style of the 도덕경, the Zhuangzi uses extended narratives, dialogues, parables, and humor to convey its philosophical vision.

### Structure of the Zhuangzi

| Section | Hanja | Korean | Chapters | Authorship |
|---------|-------|--------|----------|------------|
| Inner Chapters | 內篇 | 내편 (naepyeon) | 1-7 | Likely Zhuangzi himself |
| Outer Chapters | 外篇 | 외편 (oepyeon) | 8-22 | Disciples and later editors |
| Miscellaneous Chapters | 雜篇 | 잡편 (jappyeon) | 23-33 | Various hands |

### Key Chapters

| Chapter | Hanja | Korean | Theme |
|---------|-------|--------|-------|
| Free and Easy Wandering | 逍遙遊 | 소요유 | Absolute freedom |
| On the Equality of Things | 齊物論 | 제물론 | Relativity of perspective |
| Nourishing Life | 養生主 | 양생주 | Preserving one's nature |
| The Human World | 人間世 | 인간세 | Living in society |
| Signs of Full Virtue | 德充符 | 덕충부 | Inner virtue |
| The Great Teacher | 大宗師 | 대종사 | Ultimate reality |
| Emperor Kings | 應帝王 | 응제왕 | Governance |

## 2. Xiaoyaoyou: Free and Easy Wandering

### 소요유/逍遙遊 — Chapter 1 of the Zhuangzi

The opening chapter presents the Zhuangzi's vision of absolute freedom through the parable of the enormous Peng bird.

### Title Analysis

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 逍 | 소 (so) | to ramble |
| 遙 | 요 (yo) | far away |
| 遊 | 유 (yu) | to wander, roam |

逍遙遊 literally means "free and easy wandering" — the state of moving through life without constraints, dependence, or anxiety.

## 3. The Peng Bird: Boundless Perspective

### Opening Passage

```
北冥有魚 其名爲鯤
북명유어 기명위곤

鯤之大 不知其幾千里也
곤지대 부지기기천리야

化而爲鳥 其名爲鵬
화이위조 기명위붕

鵬之背 不知其幾千里也
붕지배 부지기기천리야
```

### Character-by-Character Analysis

**Line 1: 北冥有魚 其名爲鯤**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 北 | 북 (buk) | north |
| 冥 | 명 (myeong) | dark, deep sea |
| 有 | 유 (yu) | there is |
| 魚 | 어 (eo) | fish |
| 其 | 기 (gi) | its |
| 名 | 명 (myeong) | name |
| 爲 | 위 (wi) | is called |
| 鯤 | 곤 (gon) | Kun (enormous fish) |

Translation: "In the dark northern sea there is a fish; its name is Kun."

**Line 2: 鯤之大 不知其幾千里也**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 之 | 지 (ji) | (possessive) |
| 大 | 대 (dae) | big, great |
| 不知 | 부지 (buji) | do not know |
| 幾 | 기 (gi) | how many |
| 千里 | 천리 (cheonri) | thousand li |
| 也 | 야 (ya) | (emphasis) |

Translation: "The Kun is so large that no one knows how many thousand li it measures."

**Line 3: 化而爲鳥 其名爲鵬**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 化 | 화 (hwa) | to transform |
| 而 | 이 (i) | and then |
| 鳥 | 조 (jo) | bird |
| 鵬 | 붕 (bung) | Peng (enormous bird) |

Translation: "It transforms and becomes a bird; its name is Peng."

### Continuation

```
怒而飛 其翼若垂天之雲
노이비 기익약수천지운

是鳥也 海運則將徙於南冥
시조야 해운즉장사어남명
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 怒而飛 | When it rouses itself and flies |
| 其翼若垂天之雲 | Its wings are like clouds hanging from the sky |
| 是鳥也 | This bird |
| 海運則將徙於南冥 | When the seas churn, it will journey to the southern dark sea |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 怒 | 노 (no) | to rouse, exert energy |
| 翼 | 익 (ik) | wing |
| 若 | 약 (yak) | like, as if |
| 垂 | 수 (su) | to hang down |
| 雲 | 운 (un) | cloud |
| 徙 | 사 (sa) | to journey, migrate |

### The Small and the Great

The chapter continues by contrasting the vast Peng bird with small creatures:

```
蜩與學鳩笑之曰
조여학구소지왈

我決起而飛 搶榆枋而止
아결기이비 창유방이지

時則不至 而控於地而已矣
시즉불지 이공어지이이의
```

"The cicada and the young dove laugh at [the Peng], saying: 'We spring up and fly, reach the elm or sandalwood and stop; sometimes we do not even reach them and simply settle on the ground.'"

**Philosophical point**: The small creatures cannot comprehend the Peng's journey because their own experience is too limited. Similarly, people with narrow perspectives cannot understand those with broader vision.

## 4. Qiwulun: On the Equality of Things

### 제물론/齊物論 — Chapter 2 of the Zhuangzi

This chapter argues that all distinctions — right/wrong, beautiful/ugly, large/small — are relative and ultimately illusory.

### Key Passage: The Pipe of Heaven

```
天地與我並生
천지여아병생

而萬物與我爲一
이만물여아위일
```

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 天地 | 천지 (cheonji) | heaven and earth |
| 與 | 여 (yeo) | with, together with |
| 我 | 아 (a) | I, me |
| 並 | 병 (byeong) | together |
| 生 | 생 (saeng) | born, arise |
| 萬物 | 만물 (manmul) | ten thousand things (all things) |
| 爲一 | 위일 (wiil) | become one |

Translation: "Heaven and earth were born together with me, and the ten thousand things and I are one."

### On Perspectival Relativity

```
方生方死 方死方生
방생방사 방사방생

方可方不可 方不可方可
방가방불가 방불가방가
```

| Phrase | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| 方生方死 | At the moment of birth, there is already death |
| 方死方生 | At the moment of death, there is already birth |
| 方可方不可 | What is acceptable is simultaneously unacceptable |
| 方不可方可 | What is unacceptable is simultaneously acceptable |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 方 | 방 (bang) | just at the moment of, simultaneously |
| 可 | 가 (ga) | acceptable, affirmable |

## 5. The Butterfly Dream

### 장주몽접/莊周夢蝶 — Zhuangzi's Butterfly Dream

The most famous passage in all of Chinese philosophy:

```
昔者莊周夢爲胡蝶
석자장주몽위호접

栩栩然胡蝶也
허허연호접야

自喩適志與
자유적지여

不知周也
부지주야

俄然覺 則蘧蘧然周也
아연각 즉거거연주야

不知周之夢爲胡蝶與
부지주지몽위호접여

胡蝶之夢爲周與
호접지몽위주여
```

### Detailed Analysis

**Line 1: 昔者莊周夢爲胡蝶**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 昔者 | 석자 (seokja) | formerly, once |
| 莊周 | 장주 (jangju) | Zhuang Zhou (Zhuangzi) |
| 夢 | 몽 (mong) | to dream |
| 爲 | 위 (wi) | to become |
| 胡蝶 | 호접 (hojeop) | butterfly |

"Once upon a time, Zhuang Zhou dreamed he was a butterfly."

**Line 2: 栩栩然胡蝶也**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 栩栩然 | 허허연 (heoheoyeon) | fluttering, vivid |
| 也 | 야 (ya) | (emphasis: truly!) |

"A fluttering butterfly, truly!"

**Line 3: 自喩適志與**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 自 | 자 (ja) | himself |
| 喩 | 유 (yu) | to feel, experience |
| 適 | 적 (jeok) | at ease, content |
| 志 | 지 (ji) | intention, spirit |
| 與 | 여 (yeo) | (exclamatory) |

"He felt completely content and at ease!"

**Line 4: 不知周也**

"He did not know he was Zhou."

**Line 5: 俄然覺 則蘧蘧然周也**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 俄然 | 아연 (ayeon) | suddenly |
| 覺 | 각 (gak) | to awaken |
| 蘧蘧然 | 거거연 (geogeo-yeon) | solidly, tangibly |

"Suddenly he awoke, and there he was, solidly and unmistakably Zhou."

**Lines 6-7**: The philosophical crux:

"He did not know whether he was Zhou who had dreamed he was a butterfly, or a butterfly dreaming it was Zhou."

### Philosophical Significance

| Concept | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| 물화(物化) | "Transformation of things" — the boundaries between self and other, dream and reality, are fluid |
| Identity | Who we are is not fixed but constantly transforming |
| Reality | We cannot ultimately distinguish dream from waking |
| Freedom | True freedom comes from releasing attachment to fixed identity |

## 6. Laozi: Advanced Passages

### 도덕경/道德經 Chapter 42 — The Origin of All Things

```
道生一    도생일
一生二    일생이
二生三    이생삼
三生萬物  삼생만물
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 道生一 | The Way gives birth to One |
| 一生二 | One gives birth to Two |
| 二生三 | Two gives birth to Three |
| 三生萬物 | Three gives birth to the ten thousand things |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 生 | 생 (saeng) | to give birth to, produce |
| 一 | 일 (il) | one (primordial unity) |
| 二 | 이 (i) | two (yin and yang) |
| 三 | 삼 (sam) | three (yin, yang, and their interaction) |

### Chapter 81 — The Final Chapter

```
信言不美    신언불미
美言不信    미언불신

善者不辯    선자불변
辯者不善    변자불선

知者不博    지자불박
博者不知    박자불지
```

| Pair | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 信言不美 / 美言不信 | Truthful words are not beautiful; beautiful words are not truthful |
| 善者不辯 / 辯者不善 | The good are not argumentative; the argumentative are not good |
| 知者不博 / 博者不知 | The wise are not broadly learned; the broadly learned are not wise |

## 7. Water and Softness

### 도덕경/道德經 Chapter 78

```
天下莫柔弱於水
천하막유약어수

而攻堅強者莫之能勝
이공견강자막지능승

以其無以易之
이기무이역지
```

### Detailed Analysis

**Line 1: 天下莫柔弱於水 (천하막유약어수)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 天下 | 천하 (cheonha) | under heaven, in all the world |
| 莫 | 막 (mak) | nothing |
| 柔弱 | 유약 (yuyak) | soft and weak |
| 於 | 어 (eo) | than (comparison) |
| 水 | 수 (su) | water |

Translation: "In all the world, nothing is softer and weaker than water."

**Line 2: 而攻堅強者莫之能勝 (이공견강자막지능승)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 攻 | 공 (gong) | to attack |
| 堅 | 견 (gyeon) | hard, solid |
| 強 | 강 (gang) | strong |
| 者 | 자 (ja) | that which |
| 莫之能勝 | 막지능승 | nothing can surpass it |

Translation: "Yet for attacking the hard and strong, nothing can surpass it."

### The Principle of Yielding

| Concept | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|
| Softness overcomes hardness | 柔勝剛 | 유승강 | The soft conquers the hard |
| Weakness overcomes strength | 弱勝強 | 약승강 | The weak conquers the strong |
| The lowest position wins | 下善利 | 하선리 | The low position brings benefit |

## 8. Knowledge and Self-Knowledge

### 도덕경/道德經 Chapter 33

```
知人者智    지인자지
自知者明    자지자명

勝人者有力    승인자유력
自勝者強      자승자강
```

### Analysis

| Line | Reading | Translation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 知人者智 | 지인자지 | One who knows others is clever |
| 自知者明 | 자지자명 | One who knows himself is enlightened |
| 勝人者有力 | 승인자유력 | One who defeats others has strength |
| 自勝者強 | 자승자강 | One who overcomes himself is truly strong |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 智 | 지 (ji) | clever, intelligent |
| 明 | 명 (myeong) | bright, enlightened |
| 勝 | 승 (seung) | to overcome, defeat |
| 力 | 력 (ryeok) | strength, power |
| 強 | 강 (gang) | truly strong |

### Philosophical Contrast

| External | Internal | Evaluation |
|----------|----------|------------|
| 知人 (knowing others) | 自知 (knowing self) | Self-knowledge is higher |
| 勝人 (defeating others) | 自勝 (overcoming self) | Self-mastery is higher |
| 智 (cleverness) | 明 (enlightenment) | Enlightenment surpasses cleverness |
| 有力 (having strength) | 強 (true strength) | Inner strength surpasses outer |

The passage builds from the external to the internal, arguing that true wisdom and true power are found not in mastering others but in mastering oneself.

## 9. Philosophical Vocabulary

### Core Terms from Zhuangzi

| Term | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|---------|
| Free wandering | 逍遙 | 소요 | Unfettered spiritual freedom |
| Transformation of things | 物化 | 물화 | Merging of self and world |
| Sitting and forgetting | 坐忘 | 좌망 | Meditative dissolution of ego |
| Fasting of the mind | 心齋 | 심재 | Emptying the mind |
| Uselessness | 無用 | 무용 | Usefulness of being useless |
| Equality of things | 齊物 | 제물 | All things are equal |
| Great knowledge | 大知 | 대지 | Broad, comprehensive understanding |
| Small knowledge | 小知 | 소지 | Narrow, limited understanding |
| True person | 眞人 | 진인 | Realized, authentic person |
| Forgetting distinctions | 忘彼此 | 망피차 | Forgetting self and other |

### Core Terms from Laozi

| Term | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|---------|
| The Way | 道 | 도 | Ultimate principle |
| Virtue/Power | 德 | 덕 | Inherent power |
| Non-action | 無爲 | 무위 | Effortless action |
| Naturalness | 自然 | 자연 | Spontaneity, being so of itself |
| Uncarved block | 朴 | 박 | Original simplicity |
| Valley spirit | 谷神 | 곡신 | Receptive emptiness |
| Mysterious female | 玄牝 | 현빈 | Creative source |
| Return | 反/返 | 반 | Returning to the root |
| Nameless | 無名 | 무명 | Beyond conceptualization |
| Constant | 常 | 상 | Eternal, unchanging |

### Argumentative Structures

| Structure | Example | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 不...而... | 不言而信 (불언이신) | Without speaking, yet trusted |
| 無...有... | 無爲而無不爲 (무위이무불위) | Without doing, yet nothing undone |
| A者B也 | 知人者智 (지인자지) | One who knows others is clever |
| 莫...於... | 莫大於心 (막대어심) | Nothing greater than the mind |

**Exercise 1**: 1) In the dark northern sea there is a fish; its name is Kun 2) Heaven and earth were born together with me, and the ten thousand things and I are one 3) Once, Zhuang Zhou dreamed he was a butterfly

**Exercise 2**: The butterfly dream raises the question of whether we can ever truly distinguish dreaming from waking, or know which state is "real." When Zhuangzi was a butterfly, he had no knowledge of being Zhuangzi — the butterfly's reality was complete. Upon waking, he cannot determine if he is Zhuangzi who dreamed of being a butterfly or a butterfly now dreaming of being Zhuangzi. This illustrates the concept of 물화(物化) — the fluid transformation between self and other, challenging our assumption of fixed identity.

**Exercise 3**: 1) One who knows others is clever; one who knows himself is enlightened 2) In all the world, nothing is softer and weaker than water 3) The Way gives birth to One; One gives birth to Two; Two gives birth to Three; Three gives birth to the ten thousand things

**Exercise 4**: 鯤(곤, enormous fish), 鵬(붕, enormous bird), 蝶(접, butterfly), 覺(각, to awaken), 柔(유, soft), 勝(승, to overcome), 智(지, clever), 明(명, enlightened)

**Exercise 6**: Laozi contrasts 知人(knowing others) with 自知(knowing self), and 勝人(defeating others) with 自勝(overcoming self). In each pair, the internal version is valued higher. This reflects the Daoist emphasis on inward cultivation over outward achievement.

**Exercise 7**: 而(conjunction, "yet") + 攻(verb, "attack") + 堅強(object, "the hard and strong") + 者(nominalizer, "that which") + 莫(subject, "nothing") + 之(pronoun, "it") + 能(auxiliary, "can") + 勝(verb, "surpass"). Translation: "Yet for attacking the hard and strong, nothing can surpass it."
