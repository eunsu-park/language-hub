## 1. Overview of Questions in Classical Chinese

### 1.1 Two Types of Questions

Classical Chinese forms questions in two main ways:

> **Question Formation in Classical Chinese**
>
> Type 1: WH-Questions (using interrogative words)
> - **何 (하)** = what — 何人 = what person? = who?
> - **誰 (수)** = who — 誰知 = who knows?
> - **孰 (숙)** = which/who — 孰是 = which is right?
> - **安 (안)** = where/how — 安在 = where is it?
> - **焉 (언)** = where/how — 焉知 = how would one know?
> - **何以 (하이)** = why/how — 何以知之 = how do you know?
>
> Type 2: Yes/No Questions (using sentence-final particles)
> - **乎 (호)** = standard — 知之乎 = do you know it?
> - **邪/耶 (사/야)** = doubt — 是邪 = is it so? (skeptical)
> - **歟 (여)** = gentle — 可歟 = is it acceptable? (soft)
> - **豈 (기)** = rhetorical — 豈不善乎 = is it not good?!

## 2. 何 — "What"

### 2.1 Basic Usage

何 (하, ha) is the most versatile interrogative word. It can mean "what," "why," "how," or "which" depending on context.

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Functions |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 何 | 하 (ha) | 어찌 하 | What; why; how; which |

### 2.2 何 as "What"

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 何物 | 하물 | What thing? |
| 何事 | 하사 | What matter? |
| 何人 | 하인 | What person? (= Who?) |
| 何名 | 하명 | What name? |
| 子曰何 | 자왈하 | What did the Master say? |
| 此何也 | 차하야 | What is this? |

### 2.3 何以 — "Why / By What Means"

何以 (하이) combines 何 with the preposition 以 to ask "by what means" or "why":

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 何以知之 | 하이지지 | How do you know it? (By what means?) |
| 何以言之 | 하이언지 | Why do you say so? |
| 何以治國 | 하이치국 | How does one govern the country? |
| 何以異於人 | 하이이어인 | How is this different from others? |

### 2.4 何為 — "Why / For What Purpose"

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 何為 | 하위 | For what purpose? Why? |
| 子何為 | 자하위 | What are you doing? / Why? |
| 何為不去 | 하위불거 | Why not leave? |

### 2.5 何如 — "What Is It Like / How About"

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 何如 | 하여 | What is it like? How about it? |
| 此人何如 | 차인하여 | What is this person like? |
| 天下何如 | 천하하여 | What is the state of the world? |

## 3. 誰 — "Who"

### 3.1 Basic Usage

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Function |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 誰 | 수 (su) | 누구 수 | Who |

### 3.2 誰 Examples

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 誰知 | 수지 | Who knows? |
| 誰為之 | 수위지 | Who did it? |
| 誰能行之 | 수능행지 | Who can carry it out? |
| 誰與歸 | 수여귀 | With whom shall I return? |
| 子誰也 | 자수야 | Who are you? |
| 天下誰人不知 | 천하수인부지 | Who in the world does not know? |

### 3.3 誰 in Rhetorical Context

When the answer is obvious, 誰 creates a rhetorical question:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 誰不知之 | 수부지지 | Who does not know it? (= Everyone knows) |
| 誰不願 | 수불원 | Who does not wish for it? (= Everyone does) |

## 4. 孰 — "Which / Who" (Comparison)

### 4.1 Basic Usage

孰 (숙, suk) is used for comparison — "which [of these options]" or "who [among these people]":

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Function |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 孰 | 숙 (suk) | 누구 숙 / 어느 숙 | Which; who (in comparison) |

### 4.2 孰 for Comparison

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 孰是 | 숙시 | Which is correct? |
| 孰大 | 숙대 | Which is greater? |
| 仁與義 孰先 | 인여의 숙선 | Between benevolence and righteousness, which comes first? |
| 孰與之 | 숙여지 | Who would give it? |

### 4.3 孰與 Pattern — "Which Is Better, A or B?"

The 孰與 (숙여) pattern is used for direct comparison:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 我孰與城北徐公美 | 아숙여성북서공미 | Who is more handsome, I or Xu Gong north of the city? |
| 學孰與不學 | 학숙여불학 | Which is better, studying or not studying? |
| 生孰與死 | 생숙여사 | Which [matters more], living or dying? |

## 5. 安 and 焉 — "Where / How"

### 5.1 安 (안) — "Where / How"

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Functions |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 安 | 안 (an) | 편안할 안 / 어찌 안 | Where; how; how could |

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 安在 | 안재 | Where is it? |
| 安得 | 안득 | How can one obtain...? |
| 安知 | 안지 | How would [one] know? |
| 王安在 | 왕안재 | Where is the king? |
| 安能行之 | 안능행지 | How can one carry it out? |
| 安得仁人 | 안득인인 | How can one find a benevolent person? |

### 5.2 焉 (언) — "Where / How" (Literary)

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Functions |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 焉 | 언 (eon) | 어찌 언 | Where; how; there (also sentence-final particle) |

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 焉知 | 언지 | How would [one] know? |
| 焉得 | 언득 | Where can one get...? |
| 焉用之 | 언용지 | What use is it? |
| 未知生 焉知死 | 미지생 언지사 | Not yet understanding life, how can one understand death? |

### 5.3 安 vs 焉

| Word | Nuance | Example | Translation |
|------|--------|---------|-------------|
| 安 | More concrete "where" / "how" | 王安在 | Where is the king? |
| 焉 | More rhetorical / philosophical | 焉知魚之樂 | How can you know the joy of fish? |

## 6. 乎 — Yes/No Question Marker

### 6.1 Standard Question Formation

Adding 乎 (호) at the end of a statement turns it into a yes/no question:

| Statement | + 乎 | Reading | Translation |
|-----------|------|---------|-------------|
| 可 (acceptable) | 可乎 | 가호 | Is it acceptable? |
| 善 (good) | 善乎 | 선호 | Is it good? |
| 知之 (know it) | 知之乎 | 지지호 | Do you know it? |
| 有禮 (has propriety) | 有禮乎 | 유례호 | Is there propriety? |

### 6.2 乎 in Full Sentences

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 子知之乎 | 자지지호 | Do you know it? |
| 王仁乎 | 왕인호 | Is the king benevolent? |
| 此可行乎 | 차가행호 | Can this be carried out? |
| 道可學乎 | 도가학호 | Can the Way be studied? |
| 天命可知乎 | 천명가지호 | Can the mandate of heaven be known? |

### 6.3 不亦...乎 Pattern (Rhetorical)

This pattern asks "Is it not also...?" expecting a positive answer:

| Sentence | Reading | Literal | Real Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|-------------|
| 不亦說乎 | 불역열호 | Is it not also pleasant? | It IS pleasant! |
| 不亦樂乎 | 불역락호 | Is it not also joyful? | It IS joyful! |
| 不亦君子乎 | 불역군자호 | Is that not [the mark of] a gentleman? | It IS! |
| 不亦善乎 | 불역선호 | Is it not also good? | It IS good! |

## 7. 邪/耶 — Rhetorical and Skeptical Questions

### 7.1 Basic Usage

邪/耶 (사/야) express doubt or skepticism in a question:

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Nuance |
|-----------|------|------|--------|
| 邪 | 사 (sa) | 간사할 사 | Question with doubt/challenge |
| 耶 | 야 (ya) | 어조사 야 | Same function, variant character |

### 7.2 邪/耶 Examples

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 是邪非邪 | 시사비사 | Is it so or not? (expressing real doubt) |
| 然邪 | 연사 | Is it really so? |
| 信邪 | 신사 | Is it trustworthy? (skeptical) |
| 夢邪覺邪 | 몽사각사 | Is it a dream or reality? |

### 7.3 乎 vs 邪/耶

| Particle | Tone | Example | Nuance |
|----------|------|---------|--------|
| 乎 | Neutral question | 善乎 | Is it good? (genuine question) |
| 邪/耶 | Skeptical/doubting | 善邪 | Is it really good? (I doubt it) |

## 8. 豈 — Rhetorical "How Could...?"

### 8.1 Basic Usage

豈 (기, gi) creates strong rhetorical questions implying the answer is obvious:

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Function |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 豈 | 기 (gi) | 어찌 기 | Rhetorical: "how could...?"; "surely not" |

### 8.2 豈 Examples

| Sentence | Reading | Rhetorical Meaning |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| 豈不善乎 | 기불선호 | How could it not be good? → It is certainly good! |
| 豈可行乎 | 기가행호 | How could this be acceptable? → It is NOT! |
| 豈人也 | 기인야 | How is that [being] human?! → That is NOT human! |
| 豈不痛哉 | 기불통재 | How could it not be painful?! → It is truly painful! |

### 8.3 豈...哉 Pattern (Strong Rhetorical)

Combining 豈 with the exclamatory 哉 creates the strongest rhetorical emphasis:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 豈不悲哉 | 기불비재 | How could one not be sad?! (One MUST be sad!) |
| 豈非天意哉 | 기비천의재 | Is this not the will of heaven?! (It IS!) |
| 何足道哉 | 하족도재 | How is that worth mentioning?! (It is NOT!) |

### 8.4 Summary: Rhetorical vs Genuine Questions

> **Genuine vs Rhetorical Questions**
>
> Genuine (expects an answer):
> - 何... = What is...?
> - 誰... = Who is...?
> - ...乎 = Is it...?
>
> Rhetorical (answer is implied):
> - 豈... = How could...?! (obviously not / obviously yes)
> - 不亦...乎 = Is it not...? (yes, it is!)
> - 誰不... = Who doesn't...? (everyone does!)
> - 何足... = How is it worth...? (it's not!)
> - 邪... = Is it really...? (skepticism)

## 9. Questions in Classical Texts

### 9.1 From the Analects (論語)

**Passage 1: Three Pleasures**
```
學而時習之 不亦說乎
有朋自遠方來 不亦樂乎
人不知而不慍 不亦君子乎
```

All three use 不亦...乎 (rhetorical questions expecting "yes"):
- Is it not pleasant? → YES
- Is it not joyful? → YES
- Is it not [the mark of] a gentleman? → YES

**Passage 2: Confucius asked**
```
子曰 知之者 不如好之者 好之者 不如樂之者
```

Not a direct question, but contains an implicit comparison — "who is better?"

### 9.2 From Mencius (孟子)

**Passage: Fish and Bear's Paw**
```
魚我所欲也 熊掌亦我所欲也
二者不可得兼 舍魚而取熊掌者也
生亦我所欲也 義亦我所欲也
二者不可得兼 舍生而取義者也
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 魚我所欲也 | 어아소욕야 | Fish is what I desire |
| 熊掌亦我所欲也 | 웅장역아소욕야 | Bear's paw is also what I desire |
| 二者不可得兼 | 이자불가득겸 | If I cannot have both |
| 舍魚而取熊掌者也 | 사어이취웅장자야 | I give up fish and take bear's paw |
| 生亦我所欲也 | 생역아소욕야 | Life is also what I desire |
| 義亦我所欲也 | 의역아소욕야 | Righteousness is also what I desire |
| 二者不可得兼 | 이자불가득겸 | If I cannot have both |
| 舍生而取義者也 | 사생이취의자야 | I give up life and take righteousness |

### 9.3 From Zhuangzi (莊子)

```
子非魚 安知魚之樂
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 子非魚 | 자비어 | You are not a fish |
| 安知魚之樂 | 안지어지락 | How can you know the joy of fish? |

> This famous philosophical question uses 非 (identity negation) and 安 (how/where interrogative).
