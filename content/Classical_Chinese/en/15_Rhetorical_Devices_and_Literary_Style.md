## 1. Overview of Classical Chinese Rhetoric

Classical Chinese literature developed a rich system of rhetorical devices (수사법/修辭法). These techniques served not only aesthetic purposes but also aided memorization, persuasion, and the compression of complex ideas into minimal text.

### Key Rhetorical Terms

| Device | Hanja | Korean Reading | English |
|--------|-------|----------------|---------|
| Parallelism | 對句 | 대구 (daegu) | Balanced sentence pairs |
| Allusion | 典故 | 전고 (jeongo) | Reference to classical texts |
| Metaphor | 比喻 | 비유 (biyu) | Comparison, figurative language |
| Hyperbole | 誇張 | 과장 (gwajang) | Exaggeration |
| Repetition | 反復 | 반복 (banbok) | Repeated elements |
| Contrast | 對比 | 대비 (daebi) | Juxtaposition of opposites |
| Rhetorical question | 反問 | 반문 (banmun) | Question expecting no answer |
| Personification | 擬人 | 의인 (uiin) | Giving human traits to non-human |

## 2. Parallelism: Duiju

### Definition

대구(對句) is the pairing of two lines or clauses that mirror each other in grammatical structure, word class, and often meaning. It is the most fundamental device in Classical Chinese literature.

### Rules of Parallelism

| Rule | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Character count | Both lines must have equal length | 5+5 or 7+7 |
| Word class match | Nouns pair with nouns, verbs with verbs | 山↔水, 來↔去 |
| Tonal contrast | Corresponding positions should alternate 평/측 | 平↔仄 |
| Semantic pairing | Related or contrasting meanings | 天↔地, 古↔今 |

### Examples from Classical Texts

**Example 1**: From 滕王閣序 (등왕각서, Preface to the Pavilion of Prince Teng)

```
落霞與孤鶩齊飛    낙하여고목제비
秋水共長天一色    추수공장천일색
```

| Line 1 | Analysis | Line 2 | Analysis |
|--------|----------|--------|----------|
| 落霞 (낙하) | falling clouds | 秋水 (추수) | autumn water |
| 與 (여) | with | 共 (공) | together with |
| 孤鶩 (고목) | lone wild duck | 長天 (장천) | vast sky |
| 齊飛 (제비) | fly together | 一色 (일색) | one color |

Translation: "Falling clouds and a lone wild duck fly together / Autumn waters and the vast sky share one color"

**Example 2**: From 논어/論語 (Analerta)

```
學而不思則罔    학이불사즉망
思而不學則殆    사이불학즉태
```

| Element | Line 1 | Line 2 |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Action | 學 (학, learn) | 思 (사, think) |
| Connector | 而 (이, and yet) | 而 (이, and yet) |
| Negation | 不 (불, not) | 不 (불, not) |
| Complement | 思 (사, think) | 學 (학, learn) |
| Result word | 則 (즉, then) | 則 (즉, then) |
| Consequence | 罔 (망, deluded) | 殆 (태, imperiled) |

Translation: "To learn without thinking is to be deluded; to think without learning is to be imperiled"

**Example 3**: Simple parallel pairs

| Pair A | Reading | Pair B | Reading | Relationship |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| 天高 | 천고 (sky is high) | 地厚 | 지후 (earth is thick) | Complementary |
| 日出 | 일출 (sun rises) | 月落 | 월락 (moon sets) | Antithetical |
| 花開 | 화개 (flowers bloom) | 葉落 | 엽락 (leaves fall) | Sequential |
| 山明 | 산명 (mountains bright) | 水秀 | 수수 (waters beautiful) | Complementary |

## 3. Allusion: Diangu

### Definition

전고(典故) is the practice of referencing well-known classical stories, historical events, or earlier literary works. A skilled reader recognizes these allusions and understands the layers of meaning they add.

### Common Allusions

| Allusion | Hanja | Source | Meaning |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|
| Dreaming butterfly | 夢蝶 (몽접) | 莊子 (장자) | Uncertainty of reality |
| Climbing tower | 登樓 (등루) | Various | Aspiration, longing |
| Burning books | 焚書 (분서) | 史記 (사기) | Tyranny, destruction of knowledge |
| Fishing at Wei River | 渭濱 (위빈) | 呂望 (여망) story | Waiting for opportunity |
| Breaking cauldrons | 破釜 (파부) | 項羽 (항우) | Total commitment, no retreat |
| Three visits to the hut | 三顧 (삼고) | 三國志 (삼국지) | Sincerely seeking talent |
| Cutting the mat | 割席 (할석) | 世說新語 (세설신어) | Breaking off friendship |

### How Allusions Work

**Example**: A poet writes 渭水垂竿處 (위수수간처) — "the place where a fishing rod hangs at the Wei River"

This is not literally about fishing. It alludes to the story of 강태공/姜太公 (Jiang Taigong), who fished with a straight hook at the Wei River, waiting for a worthy ruler to recognize him. The allusion conveys "waiting patiently for one's opportunity to serve."

### Allusions from the Four Books (四書/사서)

| Source | Allusion | Hanja | Meaning |
|--------|----------|-------|---------|
| 論語 | Asking at the ford | 問津 (문진) | Seeking guidance |
| 孟子 | Ox Mountain | 牛山 (우산) | Original goodness of human nature |
| 大學 | Renewal daily | 日新 (일신) | Constant self-improvement |
| 中庸 | Middle way | 中庸 (중용) | Balance, moderation |

## 4. Metaphor and Simile: Biyu

### Types of Figurative Comparison

| Type | Hanja | Korean | Structure |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| Explicit simile | 明喻 | 명유 (myeongyu) | A 如/若 B (A is like B) |
| Implicit metaphor | 暗喻 | 암유 (amyu) | A 是 B (A is B) |
| Metonymy | 借喻 | 차유 (chayu) | B stands for A |

### Simile Markers

| Marker | Hanja | Reading | Usage |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| Like | 如 | 여 (yeo) | A如B — A is like B |
| Like | 若 | 약 (yak) | A若B — A resembles B |
| Seem | 似 | 사 (sa) | A似B — A seems like B |
| Compare | 猶 | 유 (yu) | A猶B — A is still like B |

### Examples

**Simile**: 人生如夢 (인생여몽) — "Life is like a dream"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 人 | 인 (in) | person |
| 生 | 생 (saeng) | life |
| 如 | 여 (yeo) | like, as |
| 夢 | 몽 (mong) | dream |

**Metaphor**: 上善若水 (상선약수) — "Supreme goodness is like water" (from 도덕경/道德經)

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 上 | 상 (sang) | supreme, highest |
| 善 | 선 (seon) | goodness, virtue |
| 若 | 약 (yak) | like, resembles |
| 水 | 수 (su) | water |

Water benefits all things without competing — it embodies the ideal of effortless virtue.

**Extended metaphor from 맹자/孟子**:

```
民爲貴 社稷次之 君爲輕
민위귀 사직차지 군위경
```

"The people are the most valuable; the state altars come next; the ruler is the least important." Here the ranking metaphor subverts the expected hierarchy.

## 5. Hyperbole: Guazhang

### Definition

과장(誇張) uses deliberate exaggeration to emphasize scale, emotion, or significance. It is especially common in poetry and persuasive writing.

### Examples

**From 이백(李白): 將進酒**

```
君不見黃河之水天上來    군불견황하지수천상래
奔流到海不復回          분류도해불부회
```

"Do you not see the waters of the Yellow River, descending from Heaven? / Racing to the sea, never to return."

The Yellow River does not literally come from heaven — this hyperbole emphasizes the grandeur and relentlessness of nature.

**From 이백(李白): 望廬山瀑布**

```
飛流直下三千尺    비류직하삼천척
疑是銀河落九天    의시은하락구천
```

"The flying stream plunges straight down three thousand feet / I suspect the Milky Way has fallen from the ninth heaven"

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 飛 | 비 (bi) | to fly |
| 流 | 류 (ryu) | stream, flow |
| 直 | 직 (jik) | straight |
| 下 | 하 (ha) | down |
| 三千 | 삼천 (samcheon) | three thousand |
| 尺 | 척 (cheok) | chi (unit of length) |
| 銀河 | 은하 (eunha) | Milky Way |
| 九天 | 구천 (gucheon) | ninth heaven (highest) |

### Categories of Hyperbole

| Type | Hanja | Example | Translation |
|------|-------|---------|-------------|
| Numeric | 數字誇張 | 白髮三千丈 (백발삼천장) | White hair three thousand lengths |
| Spatial | 空間誇張 | 一覽衆山小 (일람중산소) | One glance makes all mountains small |
| Temporal | 時間誇張 | 千秋萬歲 (천추만세) | A thousand autumns, ten thousand years |
| Emotional | 感情誇張 | 愁腸百結 (수장백결) | Sorrowful intestines knotted a hundred times |

## 6. Repetition: Fanfu

### Definition

반복(反復) employs the deliberate recurrence of words, phrases, or structures to create emphasis, rhythm, or emotional intensity.

### Types of Repetition

| Type | Hanja | Description | Example |
|------|-------|-------------|---------|
| Word repetition | 疊字 (첩자) | Same character repeated | 處處 (처처, everywhere) |
| Phrase repetition | 疊句 (첩구) | Same phrase repeated | Structure repeated across lines |
| Sound repetition | 疊韻 (첩운) | Same rhyme sounds | Onomatopoeia, alliteration |

### Examples

**Reduplication (疊字/첩자)**:

| Reduplication | Reading | Meaning | Usage |
|--------------|---------|---------|-------|
| 處處 | 처처 | everywhere | 處處聞啼鳥 |
| 年年 | 연연 | every year | 年年歲歲花相似 |
| 漠漠 | 막막 | vast, desolate | 漠漠水田飛白鷺 |
| 悠悠 | 유유 | distant, prolonged | 悠悠蒼天 |
| 蒼蒼 | 창창 | blue-green, vast | 天蒼蒼 野茫茫 |

**Structural repetition from 시경/詩經**:

```
關關雎鳩    관관저구
在河之洲    재하지주
窈窕淑女    요조숙녀
君子好逑    군자호구
```

The reduplication 關關(관관) imitates the sound of the osprey bird calling, while 窈窕(요조) describes the graceful beauty of the woman. These sound-pairs create musical quality.

## 7. Contrast: Duibi

### Definition

대비(對比) places opposing concepts, images, or situations side by side to highlight differences and deepen meaning.

### Types of Contrast

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Temporal | Past vs. present | 舊時王謝堂前燕 / 飛入尋常百姓家 |
| Spatial | Here vs. there | 此地 vs. 彼方 |
| Moral | Virtue vs. vice | 君子 vs. 小人 |
| Natural | Permanence vs. change | 山河在 vs. 國破 |

### Examples

**Contrast in 두보(杜甫): 春望**

```
國破山河在    국파산하재
城春草木深    성춘초목심
```

| Contrast | Element A | Element B |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| State | 國破 (국파, nation shattered) | 山河在 (산하재, mountains/rivers remain) |
| Scene | 城春 (성춘, city in spring) | 草木深 (초목심, overgrown vegetation) |
| Theme | Human destruction | Natural endurance |

**Contrast in 논어/論語**:

```
君子坦蕩蕩    군자탄탕탕
小人長戚戚    소인장척척
```

| Element | 君子 (군자, noble person) | 小人 (소인, petty person) |
|---------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| Manner | 坦蕩蕩 (탄탕탕, broad and open) | 長戚戚 (장척척, always anxious) |

Translation: "The noble person is broad and open; the petty person is always anxious."

## 8. Rhetorical Questions and Exclamations

### Rhetorical Questions (反問/반문)

Rhetorical questions in Classical Chinese use question markers but expect no answer — they serve to emphasize a point.

| Marker | Hanja | Reading | Usage |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| How? | 何 | 하 (ha) | 何以...? (How can one...?) |
| Who? | 誰 | 수 (su) | 誰能...? (Who can...?) |
| How? | 豈 | 기 (gi) | 豈不...乎? (Is it not...?) |
| Is it not? | 不亦...乎 | 불역...호 | 不亦樂乎? (Is it not joyful?) |

### Examples

**From 논어/論語**:

```
學而時習之 不亦說乎
학이시습지 불역열호
```

"To study and practice regularly — is that not a pleasure?"

The 不亦...乎 construction creates an emphatic affirmation through a negative question.

**From 맹자/孟子**:

```
天時不如地利 地利不如人和
천시불여지리 지리불여인화
```

"Heaven's timing is not as good as geographic advantage; geographic advantage is not as good as human harmony."

### Exclamatory Particles

| Particle | Reading | Emotion |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 哉 | 재 (jae) | Admiration or deep emotion |
| 矣 | 의 (ui) | Finality, certainty |
| 乎 | 호 (ho) | Wonder, question |
| 也 | 야 (ya) | Assertion, emphasis |
| 歟 | 여 (yeo) | Doubt, uncertainty |

**Example**: 善哉善哉 (선재선재) — "Excellent! Excellent!" (expressing admiration)

## 9. Analyzing Rhetoric in Extended Passages

### Passage: From 출사표/出師表 (Memorial on Sending Out the Troops) by 제갈량/諸葛亮

```
臣本布衣 躬耕於南陽
신본포의 궁경어남양
苟全性命於亂世 不求聞達於諸侯
구전성명어난세 불구문달어제후
```

"Your servant was originally a commoner, personally plowing in Nanyang. I sought merely to preserve my life in a chaotic age, not seeking fame among the lords."

**Rhetorical analysis**:

| Device | Instance | Effect |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Contrast | 布衣(포의, commoner) vs. 諸侯(제후, lords) | Emphasizes humble origins |
| Parallelism | 苟全...亂世 / 不求...諸侯 | Balanced pair reinforces modesty |
| Self-deprecation | 臣本布衣 (신본포의) | Creates ethos through humility |

### Passage: From 적벽부/赤壁賦 (Red Cliff Ode) by 소식/蘇軾

```
寄蜉蝣於天地    기부유어천지
渺滄海之一粟    묘창해지일속
```

"We are like mayflies entrusted to heaven and earth / Tiny as a single grain in the vast blue sea"

| Device | Instance | Effect |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Simile | 蜉蝣(부유, mayfly) | Human brevity |
| Hyperbole | 滄海之一粟(창해지일속) | Cosmic insignificance |
| Parallelism | Both lines have matching structure | Rhythmic emphasis |
