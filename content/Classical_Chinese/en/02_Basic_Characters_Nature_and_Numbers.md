## 1. Pictographic Characters: The Natural World

### 1.1 Sky and Earth

The most fundamental characters represent heaven/sky and earth — the two great forces of the classical Chinese worldview.

| Character | 음독 (Sound) | 훈독 (Meaning) | Strokes | Pictographic Origin |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| 天 | 천 (cheon) | 하늘 천 (sky, heaven) | 4 | Person (大) with line above (一) = what is above a person |
| 地 | 지 (ji) | 땅 지 (earth, ground) | 6 | 土 (earth) + 也 (phonetic) = the ground beneath |

**Example compounds:**
- 天地 (천지) — heaven and earth; the world; the universe
- 天下 (천하) — "under heaven"; the whole world
- 地上 (지상) — on the ground; terrestrial

### 1.2 Celestial Bodies

| Character | 음독 (Sound) | 훈독 (Meaning) | Strokes | Pictographic Origin |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| 日 | 일 (il) | 날 일 (sun, day) | 4 | Circle with dot inside — the sun |
| 月 | 월 (wol) | 달 월 (moon, month) | 4 | Crescent moon shape |
| 星 | 성 (seong) | 별 성 (star) | 9 | 日 (celestial) + 生 (生, phonetic) |

**Example compounds:**
- 日月 (일월) — sun and moon; time; days and months
- 明月 (명월) — bright moon
- 日出 (일출) — sunrise
- 月光 (월광) — moonlight
- 星光 (성광) — starlight

### 1.3 Landscape

| Character | 음독 (Sound) | 훈독 (Meaning) | Strokes | Pictographic Origin |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| 山 | 산 (san) | 메 산 (mountain) | 3 | Three peaks rising |
| 水 | 수 (su) | 물 수 (water) | 4 | Flowing stream with drops |
| 川 | 천 (cheon) | 내 천 (river, stream) | 3 | Three flowing lines — a river |
| 田 | 전 (jeon) | 밭 전 (field, paddy) | 5 | Divided plot of farmland |

**Example compounds:**
- 山水 (산수) — mountains and water; landscape; scenery
- 山川 (산천) — mountains and rivers; natural scenery
- 水田 (수전) — paddy field (irrigated rice field)

### 1.4 The Five Elements (五行)

The Five Elements (오행/五行) are central to classical East Asian thought. Each element corresponds to a fundamental character:

| Character | 음독 (Sound) | 훈독 (Meaning) | Strokes | Element | Direction | Season |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|---------|---------|-----------|--------|
| 木 | 목 (mok) | 나무 목 (tree, wood) | 4 | Wood (목) | East (동) | Spring (춘) |
| 火 | 화 (hwa) | 불 화 (fire) | 4 | Fire (화) | South (남) | Summer (하) |
| 土 | 토 (to) | 흙 토 (earth, soil) | 3 | Earth (토) | Center (중) | Late summer |
| 金 | 금 (geum) | 쇠 금 (metal, gold) | 8 | Metal (금) | West (서) | Autumn (추) |
| 水 | 수 (su) | 물 수 (water) | 4 | Water (수) | North (북) | Winter (동) |

> **The Five Elements Cycle (五行相生)**
>
> 火 (Fire/South/Summer)
> ↗                ↘
> 木 (Wood/East)          土 (Earth/Center)
> ↑                         ↓
> 水 (Water/North) ←——— 金 (Metal/West)
>
> - **Generation cycle (相生)**: 木→火→土→金→水→木
> - Wood feeds Fire, Fire creates Earth (ash),
>   Earth bears Metal, Metal carries Water (condensation),
>   Water nourishes Wood

## 2. The Ten Heavenly Stems and Five Elements

### 2.1 天干 (천간) — Ten Heavenly Stems

The Heavenly Stems are an ancient numbering system used in calendars, astrology, and naming:

| Stem | Character | 음독 | Meaning | Associated Element |
|------|-----------|------|---------|-------------------|
| 1st | 甲 | 갑 (gap) | First, armor | 木 (Wood) |
| 2nd | 乙 | 을 (eul) | Second | 木 (Wood) |
| 3rd | 丙 | 병 (byeong) | Third, bright | 火 (Fire) |
| 4th | 丁 | 정 (jeong) | Fourth, nail | 火 (Fire) |
| 5th | 戊 | 무 (mu) | Fifth | 土 (Earth) |
| 6th | 己 | 기 (gi) | Sixth, self | 土 (Earth) |
| 7th | 庚 | 경 (gyeong) | Seventh | 金 (Metal) |
| 8th | 辛 | 신 (sin) | Eighth, bitter | 金 (Metal) |
| 9th | 壬 | 임 (im) | Ninth | 水 (Water) |
| 10th | 癸 | 계 (gye) | Tenth | 水 (Water) |

> **Cultural note**: The Korean term 갑을관계 (甲乙關係) comes from these stems — 甲 (first/superior) and 乙 (second/subordinate) describe a power relationship.

## 3. Number Characters: Counting in Classical Chinese

### 3.1 Basic Numbers (一 ~ 十)

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Value | Strokes |
|-----------|------|------|-------|---------|
| 一 | 일 (il) | 한 일 | 1 | 1 |
| 二 | 이 (i) | 두 이 | 2 | 2 |
| 三 | 삼 (sam) | 석 삼 | 3 | 3 |
| 四 | 사 (sa) | 넉 사 | 4 | 5 |
| 五 | 오 (o) | 다섯 오 | 5 | 4 |
| 六 | 육 (yuk) | 여섯 육 | 6 | 4 |
| 七 | 칠 (chil) | 일곱 칠 | 7 | 2 |
| 八 | 팔 (pal) | 여덟 팔 | 8 | 2 |
| 九 | 구 (gu) | 아홉 구 | 9 | 2 |
| 十 | 십 (sip) | 열 십 | 10 | 2 |

> **Note**: 一, 二, 三 are the classic examples of 指事 (indicative) characters — their form directly represents the quantity.

### 3.2 Larger Numbers

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Value | Strokes |
|-----------|------|------|-------|---------|
| 百 | 백 (baek) | 일백 백 | 100 | 6 |
| 千 | 천 (cheon) | 일천 천 | 1,000 | 3 |
| 萬 | 만 (man) | 일만 만 | 10,000 | 13 |
| 億 | 억 (eok) | 억 억 | 100,000,000 | 15 |

### 3.3 Expressing Numbers in Classical Chinese

In Classical Chinese, numbers are expressed multiplicatively:

| Expression | Characters | Reading | Value |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------|
| Eleven | 十一 | 십일 | 10 + 1 = 11 |
| Twenty | 二十 | 이십 | 2 × 10 = 20 |
| Thirty-five | 三十五 | 삼십오 | 3 × 10 + 5 = 35 |
| One hundred | 一百 | 일백 | 1 × 100 = 100 |
| Three hundred | 三百 | 삼백 | 3 × 100 = 300 |
| Two thousand | 二千 | 이천 | 2 × 1000 = 2,000 |
| Ten thousand | 一萬 | 일만 | 1 × 10,000 = 10,000 |
| Fifty thousand | 五萬 | 오만 | 5 × 10,000 = 50,000 |

### 3.4 Special Number-Related Characters

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Meaning | Usage |
|-----------|------|------|---------|-------|
| 半 | 반 (ban) | 반 반 | Half | 半日 (반일, half a day) |
| 雙 | 쌍 (ssang) | 쌍 쌍 | Pair, double | 雙方 (쌍방, both sides) |
| 數 | 수 (su) | 셀 수 | Number, count | 數學 (수학, mathematics) |
| 第 | 제 (je) | 차례 제 | Ordinal prefix | 第一 (제일, first/number one) |
| 兩 | 량 (ryang) | 두 량 | Both, two (of a pair) | 兩國 (양국, both countries) |

## 4. Simple Ideographic Characters

### 4.1 Position and Size

These characters express abstract concepts of position and relative size:

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Meaning | Formation |
|-----------|------|------|---------|-----------|
| 上 | 상 (sang) | 위 상 | Above, up, superior | Mark above a baseline |
| 下 | 하 (ha) | 아래 하 | Below, down, inferior | Mark below a baseline |
| 中 | 중 (jung) | 가운데 중 | Middle, center | Line through the center of a box |
| 大 | 대 (dae) | 큰 대 | Big, great | Person (人) with arms spread wide |
| 小 | 소 (so) | 작을 소 | Small, little | Small marks, diminishing |
| 長 | 장 (jang) | 길 장 | Long, senior | Person with long flowing hair |
| 短 | 단 (dan) | 짧을 단 | Short | 矢 (arrow) + 豆 (bean) — short as a bean |

### 4.2 Compounds with Position Characters

| Compound | Reading | Meaning | Analysis |
|----------|---------|---------|----------|
| 上下 | 상하 | Up and down; superior and inferior | 上 + 下 |
| 中心 | 중심 | Center; core; heart | 中 (center) + 心 (heart) |
| 大小 | 대소 | Size; big and small | 大 + 小 |
| 上中下 | 상중하 | Upper, middle, lower | Three grades |
| 大人 | 대인 | Adult; great person | 大 (great) + 人 (person) |
| 中國 | 중국 | China ("Middle Kingdom") | 中 (middle) + 國 (country) |
| 大學 | 대학 | University ("great learning") | 大 (great) + 學 (learning) |
| 下山 | 하산 | Descend a mountain | 下 (down) + 山 (mountain) |

## 5. Character Evolution: From Oracle Bone to Modern

### 5.1 Evolution Examples

Tracing how characters evolved helps understand their original meanings:

**日 (일, sun/day)**

| Period | Form | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Oracle Bone (甲骨文) | Circle with dot | Realistic depiction of the sun |
| Bronze (金文) | Rounder, larger | More decorative, ceremonial |
| Seal Script (篆書) | Oval, symmetrical | Standardized form |
| Clerical (隸書) | Square, angular | Adapted for brush writing |
| Regular (楷書) | 日 | Modern standard form |

**山 (산, mountain)**

| Period | Form | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Oracle Bone (甲骨文) | Three peaks, very pictographic | Clear mountain silhouette |
| Bronze (金文) | Stylized peaks | More decorative |
| Seal Script (篆書) | Symmetrical peaks | Formalized |
| Regular (楷書) | 山 | Recognizable three-peak form |

**水 (수, water)**

| Period | Form | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Oracle Bone (甲骨文) | Wavy lines with dots | Flowing water with splashes |
| Bronze (金文) | Flowing curves | River-like form |
| Seal Script (篆書) | Curved, flowing | Stylized stream |
| Regular (楷書) | 水 | Abstract but still has flowing sense |

### 5.2 The Simplification Question

| Original (正字) | Simplified (簡字) | Korean Usage | Notes |
|----------------|------------------|-------------|-------|
| 萬 | 万 | 萬 (traditional) | Korea uses traditional forms |
| 國 | 国 | 國 (traditional) | Korea uses traditional forms |
| 學 | 学 | 學 (traditional) | Korea uses traditional forms |
| 書 | 书 | 書 (traditional) | Korea uses traditional forms |

> **Important**: Korean 한자 education uses **traditional** (정자/正字) character forms, not the simplified forms used in mainland China.

## 6. Seasonal and Directional Characters

### 6.1 Four Seasons (四季)

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Season | Associated Element |
|-----------|------|------|--------|-------------------|
| 春 | 춘 (chun) | 봄 춘 | Spring | 木 (Wood) |
| 夏 | 하 (ha) | 여름 하 | Summer | 火 (Fire) |
| 秋 | 추 (chu) | 가을 추 | Autumn | 金 (Metal) |
| 冬 | 동 (dong) | 겨울 동 | Winter | 水 (Water) |

**Season compounds:**
- 春夏秋冬 (춘하추동) — the four seasons
- 春風 (춘풍) — spring breeze
- 夏至 (하지) — summer solstice
- 秋夕 (추석) — autumn evening; Chuseok (Korean harvest festival)
- 冬至 (동지) — winter solstice

### 6.2 Four Directions (四方)

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Direction | Associated Element |
|-----------|------|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 東 | 동 (dong) | 동녘 동 | East | 木 (Wood) |
| 西 | 서 (seo) | 서녘 서 | West | 金 (Metal) |
| 南 | 남 (nam) | 남녘 남 | South | 火 (Fire) |
| 北 | 북 (buk) | 북녘 북 | North | 水 (Water) |

**Direction compounds:**
- 東西南北 (동서남북) — the four directions; all directions
- 東方 (동방) — the East; the Orient
- 西方 (서방) — the West; the Occident
- 南北 (남북) — north and south; North and South (Korea)
- 東西 (동서) — east and west; things (in Korean usage)

## 7. Basic Compounds from Nature Characters

### 7.1 Two-Character Compounds

Understanding how characters combine reveals the logic of Chinese word formation:

| Compound | Reading | Characters | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| 天地 | 천지 | 天 + 地 | sky + earth | The world; universe |
| 天下 | 천하 | 天 + 下 | sky + below | Under heaven; the world |
| 天上 | 천상 | 天 + 上 | sky + above | In the sky; heavenly |
| 山水 | 산수 | 山 + 水 | mountain + water | Landscape; scenery |
| 山火 | 산화 | 山 + 火 | mountain + fire | Wildfire |
| 水火 | 수화 | 水 + 火 | water + fire | Water and fire; conflict |
| 風水 | 풍수 | 風 + 水 | wind + water | Feng shui; geomancy |
| 日月 | 일월 | 日 + 月 | sun + moon | Sun and moon; time |
| 火山 | 화산 | 火 + 山 | fire + mountain | Volcano |
| 金山 | 금산 | 金 + 山 | gold + mountain | Gold mountain; great wealth |

### 7.2 Nature in Classical Phrases

| Phrase | Reading | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 天高馬肥 | 천고마비 | "Sky high, horses fat" — describes the clear autumn sky |
| 山高水長 | 산고수장 | "Mountains high, waters long" — enduring virtue |
| 上善若水 | 상선약수 | "The highest good is like water" — Laozi, Daodejing |
