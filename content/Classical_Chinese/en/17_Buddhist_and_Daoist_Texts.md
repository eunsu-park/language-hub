## 1. Introduction: Buddhism and Daoism in East Asia

Buddhism and Daoism profoundly shaped East Asian thought, literature, and culture. Their classical texts, written or translated into Classical Chinese, became part of the shared literary heritage across China, Korea, Japan, and Vietnam.

### Historical Context

| Tradition | Origin | Entry to Korea | Key Period |
|-----------|--------|---------------|------------|
| Buddhism (佛敎/불교) | India, ~5th c. BCE | 372 CE (Goguryeo) | Goryeo Dynasty (918-1392) |
| Daoism (道敎/도교) | China, ~4th c. BCE | Early Three Kingdoms | Joseon Dynasty (philosophical) |

### Textual Traditions

| Text Type | Buddhist | Daoist |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Core scriptures | 경(經, sutra) | 경(經, classic) |
| Commentaries | 론(論, shastra) | 주(注, commentary) |
| Discipline codes | 률(律, vinaya) | 계(戒, precepts) |
| Collections | 대장경(大藏經, Tripitaka) | 도장(道藏, Daoist Canon) |

## 2. Heart Sutra: Opening Passage

### 반야심경/般若心經 (Heart Sutra) — The Most Recited Buddhist Text

The Heart Sutra condenses the vast Prajnaparamita literature into roughly 260 characters. It is chanted daily across East Asian Buddhist traditions.

### Opening Lines

```
觀自在菩薩
관자재보살

行深般若波羅蜜多時
행심반야바라밀다시

照見五蘊皆空
조견오온개공

度一切苦厄
도일체고액
```

### Character-by-Character Analysis

**Line 1: 觀自在菩薩**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 觀 | 관 (gwan) | to observe, contemplate |
| 自 | 자 (ja) | self |
| 在 | 재 (jae) | to be present, exist |
| 菩 | 보 (bo) | bodhi (enlightenment) |
| 薩 | 살 (sal) | sattva (being) |

觀自在菩薩 (관자재보살) = Avalokiteshvara Bodhisattva, "The Bodhisattva Who Observes the Sounds of the World" — known in Korean as 관세음보살(觀世音菩薩).

**Line 2: 行深般若波羅蜜多時**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 行 | 행 (haeng) | to practice |
| 深 | 심 (sim) | deep, profound |
| 般若 | 반야 (banya) | prajna (wisdom) |
| 波羅蜜多 | 바라밀다 (baramilta) | paramita (perfection) |
| 時 | 시 (si) | time, when |

Translation: "When practicing the deep Prajnaparamita..."

**Line 3: 照見五蘊皆空**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 照 | 조 (jo) | to illuminate, shine on |
| 見 | 견 (gyeon) | to see, perceive |
| 五 | 오 (o) | five |
| 蘊 | 온 (on) | aggregate, heap |
| 皆 | 개 (gae) | all |
| 空 | 공 (gong) | empty, void |

The 오온(五蘊, five aggregates) are:

| Aggregate | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|-------|---------|---------|
| Form | 色 | 색 (saek) | Material form, body |
| Sensation | 受 | 수 (su) | Feeling, reception |
| Perception | 想 | 상 (sang) | Conception, thought |
| Mental formations | 行 | 행 (haeng) | Volition, will |
| Consciousness | 識 | 식 (sik) | Awareness, knowing |

**Line 4: 度一切苦厄**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 度 | 도 (do) | to ferry across, save |
| 一切 | 일체 (ilche) | all, everything |
| 苦 | 고 (go) | suffering |
| 厄 | 액 (aek) | hardship, calamity |

Translation: "[He] perceived that the five aggregates are all empty, and thereby saved [beings from] all suffering and hardship."

## 3. Heart Sutra: Core Teaching

### The Most Famous Line

```
色即是空    空即是色
색즉시공    공즉시색

色不異空    空不異色
색불이공    공불이색
```

### Detailed Analysis

**色即是空 (색즉시공)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 色 | 색 (saek) | form, matter |
| 即 | 즉 (jeuk) | exactly, is precisely |
| 是 | 시 (si) | is |
| 空 | 공 (gong) | emptiness, void |

Translation: "Form is precisely emptiness"

**空即是色 (공즉시색)**

Translation: "Emptiness is precisely form"

**色不異空 (색불이공)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 不 | 불 (bul) | not |
| 異 | 이 (i) | different from |

Translation: "Form is not different from emptiness"

### Philosophical Significance

This passage expresses the central Buddhist concept of 공(空, sunyata/emptiness): all phenomena lack inherent, independent existence. Material form and emptiness are not two separate realities but two aspects of the same truth.

### The Heart Sutra Mantra

```
揭諦揭諦 波羅揭諦 波羅僧揭諦 菩提薩婆訶
아제아제 바라아제 바라승아제 모지사바하
```

This mantra (주/呪, dharani) is a transliteration from Sanskrit: "Gate gate paragate parasamgate bodhi svaha" — "Gone, gone, gone beyond, gone completely beyond, awakening, so be it!"

### The Three Jewels (三寶/삼보)

| Dharma | 法 | 법 (beop) | The teaching, cosmic law |
| Sangha | 僧 | 승 (seung) | The community of practitioners |

### Essential Buddhist Terms

| Term | Hanja | Reading | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|---------|

| Non-self | 無我 | 무아 (mua) | No fixed, permanent self |
| Karma | 業 | 업 (eop) | Action and its consequences |
| Nirvana | 涅槃 | 열반 (yeolban) | Cessation of suffering |
| Samsara | 輪廻 | 윤회 (yunhoe) | Cycle of rebirth |

## 5. Tao Te Ching: Chapter 1

### 도덕경/道德經 제1장 — The Opening of the Tao Te Ching

The Tao Te Ching (도덕경), attributed to 노자/老子 (Laozi), is the foundational text of Daoism. It consists of 81 short chapters written in enigmatic, poetic Classical Chinese.

```
道可道 非常道
도가도 비상도

名可名 非常名
명가명 비상명

無名天地之始
무명천지지시

有名萬物之母
유명만물지모
```

### Line-by-Line Analysis

**Line 1: 道可道 非常道 (도가도 비상도)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 道 (1st) | 도 (do) | the Way, Dao (noun) |
| 可 | 가 (ga) | can, able to |
| 道 (2nd) | 도 (do) | to speak, express (verb) |
| 非 | 비 (bi) | is not |
| 常 | 상 (sang) | eternal, constant |
| 道 (3rd) | 도 (do) | the Way (noun) |

Translation: "The Way that can be spoken of is not the eternal Way"

**Line 2: 名可名 非常名 (명가명 비상명)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 名 (1st) | 명 (myeong) | name (noun) |
| 可 | 가 (ga) | can |
| 名 (2nd) | 명 (myeong) | to name (verb) |
| 非 | 비 (bi) | is not |
| 常 | 상 (sang) | eternal |
| 名 (3rd) | 명 (myeong) | name (noun) |

Translation: "The name that can be named is not the eternal name"

**Lines 3-4**:

| Line | Reading | Translation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 無名天地之始 | 무명천지지시 | The nameless is the beginning of heaven and earth |
| 有名萬物之母 | 유명만물지모 | The named is the mother of the ten thousand things |

### Key Vocabulary

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 始 | 시 (si) | beginning, origin |
| 母 | 모 (mo) | mother |
| 萬物 | 만물 (manmul) | ten thousand things (all things) |
| 天地 | 천지 (cheonji) | heaven and earth |

### Philosophical Significance

This opening establishes the central Daoist paradox: the ultimate reality (道) transcends language and conceptualization. The moment you define it, you have departed from it. This theme of ineffability runs throughout the entire text.

## 6. Tao Te Ching: Chapter 8

### 도덕경/道德經 제8장 — Water as Supreme Goodness

```
上善若水
상선약수

水善利萬物而不爭
수선리만물이불쟁

處衆人之所惡
처중인지소오

故幾於道
고기어도
```

### Analysis

**Line 1: 上善若水 (상선약수)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 上 | 상 (sang) | supreme, highest |
| 善 | 선 (seon) | goodness, virtue |
| 若 | 약 (yak) | is like |
| 水 | 수 (su) | water |

Translation: "Supreme goodness is like water"

**Line 2: 水善利萬物而不爭 (수선리만물이불쟁)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 水 | 수 (su) | water |
| 善 | 선 (seon) | is good at |
| 利 | 리 (ri) | benefiting |
| 萬物 | 만물 (manmul) | all things |
| 而 | 이 (i) | yet (adversative) |
| 不 | 불 (bul) | not |
| 爭 | 쟁 (jaeng) | to contend, compete |

Translation: "Water is good at benefiting all things yet does not compete"

**Line 3: 處衆人之所惡 (처중인지소오)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 處 | 처 (cheo) | to dwell in |
| 衆人 | 중인 (jungin) | the multitude, most people |
| 之 | 지 (ji) | (possessive) |
| 所 | 소 (so) | that which |
| 惡 | 오 (o) | to dislike, detest |

Translation: "It dwells in places that the multitude detests"

**Line 4: 故幾於道 (고기어도)**

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 故 | 고 (go) | therefore |
| 幾 | 기 (gi) | close to, nearly |
| 於 | 어 (eo) | to (preposition) |
| 道 | 도 (do) | the Way |

Translation: "Therefore it is close to the Way"

### The Seven Qualities of Water

The chapter continues to enumerate seven qualities:

| Quality | Hanja | Reading | Translation |
|---------|-------|---------|-------------|
| Dwelling | 居善地 | 거선지 | In dwelling, choose low ground |
| Heart | 心善淵 | 심선연 | In heart, be deep like a pool |
| Giving | 與善仁 | 여선인 | In giving, be kind |
| Speech | 言善信 | 언선신 | In speech, be trustworthy |
| Governance | 正善治 | 정선치 | In governance, bring order |
| Affairs | 事善能 | 사선능 | In affairs, be capable |
| Timing | 動善時 | 동선시 | In action, be timely |

| Yang | 陽 | 양 (yang) | Sunlight, masculine principle |

|--------|-------|---------|---------|

## 8. Comparing Buddhist and Daoist Language

### Stylistic Differences

| Feature | Buddhist Texts | Daoist Texts |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Vocabulary | Sanskrit transliterations + Chinese | Pure Classical Chinese |
| Sentence length | Often longer, more complex | Short, epigrammatic |
| Tone | Didactic, systematic | Paradoxical, poetic |
| Repetition | Formulaic repetition (liturgical) | Structural parallelism |
| Logic | Categorical analysis (lists) | Intuitive, contradictory |

### Shared Concepts, Different Terms

| Concept | Buddhist Term | Daoist Term |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Ultimate reality | 空 (공, emptiness) | 道 (도, the Way) |
| Non-attachment | 無執 (무집) | 無爲 (무위) |
| Impermanence | 無常 (무상) | 化 (화, transformation) |
| Returning to source | 涅槃 (열반, nirvana) | 歸根 (귀근, return to root) |
| Inner stillness | 禪定 (선정, meditation) | 靜 (정, stillness) |

### Influence on Korean Culture

| Domain | Buddhist | Daoist |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Art | 불화(佛畫), temple murals | 산수화(山水畫), landscape painting |
| Architecture | 사찰(寺刹), temples | 정자(亭子), pavilions |
| Literature | 승려 시(僧侶詩), monk poetry | 은일시(隱逸詩), hermit poetry |
| Philosophy | 화엄(華嚴), 선(禪) | 풍류(風流), aesthetic naturalism |

## 9. Additional Passages for Study

### From 도덕경/道德經 Chapter 76

```
人之生也柔弱    인지생야유약
其死也堅強      기사야견강

萬物草木之生也柔脆    만물초목지생야유취
其死也枯槁            기사야고고
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 人之生也柔弱 | When people are alive, they are soft and yielding |
| 其死也堅強 | When they die, they are hard and stiff |
| 萬物草木之生也柔脆 | When plants are alive, they are soft and tender |
| 其死也枯槁 | When they die, they are withered and dry |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 柔 | 유 (yu) | soft, yielding |
| 弱 | 약 (yak) | weak, tender |
| 堅 | 견 (gyeon) | hard, firm |
| 強 | 강 (gang) | strong, stiff |
| 枯 | 고 (go) | withered |
| 槁 | 고 (go) | dried, dead |

### From 금강경/金剛經 (Diamond Sutra)

```
凡所有相 皆是虛妄
범소유상 개시허망

若見諸相非相 即見如來
약견제상비상 즉견여래
```

| Line | Translation |
|------|-------------|
| 凡所有相 皆是虛妄 | All phenomena with characteristics are illusory |
| 若見諸相非相 即見如來 | If you see that all characteristics are non-characteristics, then you see the Tathagata |

Key vocabulary:

| Character | Reading | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| 凡 | 범 (beom) | all, every |
| 相 | 상 (sang) | characteristic, appearance |
| 虛 | 허 (heo) | empty, unreal |
| 妄 | 망 (mang) | false, illusory |
| 若 | 약 (yak) | if |
| 如來 | 여래 (yeorae) | Tathagata (the Buddha) |
