## 1. Introduction to Historical Writing

### 1.1 Classical Chinese Historical Tradition

> **Classical Chinese Historical Writing Tradition**
>
> Chinese historical writing (史學/사학) is one of the
> oldest continuous historiographic traditions in the world.
>
> Key features:
>   - Emphasis on moral lessons from history
>   - Biographical approach (individual stories illustrate eras)
>   - Official court historians recorded events in real time
>   - "Praise and blame" (褒貶/포폄) tradition
>   - Influenced Korean historiography (삼국사기, 고려사, etc.)
>
> Major Chinese historical works:
> - 春秋 (춘추) — Spring and Autumn Annals (attributed to 孔子)
> - 史記 (사기) — Records of the Grand Historian (司馬遷)
> - 漢書 (한서) — Book of Han (班固)
> - 三國志 (삼국지) — Records of the Three Kingdoms (陳壽)
> - 資治通鑑 (자치통감) — Comprehensive Mirror (司馬光)
```

## 2. The Records of the Grand Historian (史記)

### 2.1 About the 史記

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Title | 史記 (사기, Shiji) — Records of the Grand Historian |
| Author | 司馬遷 (사마천, Sima Qian), ~145-86 BCE |
| Scope | From the mythical Yellow Emperor to Emperor Wu of Han (~2,500 years) |
| Length | 130 chapters (篇), ~526,500 characters |
| Status | The founding work of Chinese biographical historiography |
| Style | Narrative prose with dialogue; vivid characterization |

### 2.2 About 司馬遷 (사마천)

> **司馬遷 (사마천, Sima Qian)**
>
> - **Born**: ~145 BCE
> - **Died**: ~86 BCE
> - **Position**: 太史令 (태사령, Grand Historian) of the Han court
>
> Life story:
>   - Father 司馬談 (사마담) began the 史記 project
>   - Sima Qian continued after his father's death
>   - Defended general 李陵 (이릉), angering Emperor Wu
>   - Sentenced to castration (宮刑/궁형) — chose to endure it rather than die, so he could complete the 史記
>   - His perseverance made him a symbol of dedication to truth
>
> Famous quote (from his letter to Ren An):
> 人固有一死 或重於泰山 或輕於鴻毛
> "All people must die; some deaths are heavier than Mount Tai,
> some lighter than a feather."

## 3. Structure of the 史記

### 3.1 Five Sections

The 史記 pioneered the "biographical-topical" (紀傳體/기전체) format that became the standard for all subsequent Chinese official histories:

| Section | Korean | Chapters | Content | Purpose |
|---------|--------|----------|---------|---------|
| 本紀 | 본기 | 12 | Imperial biographies | Chronicle of rulers/emperors |
| 世家 | 세가 | 30 | Hereditary houses | Stories of feudal lords and their lineages |
| 列傳 | 열전 | 70 | Biographies | Lives of notable individuals |
| 表 | 표 | 10 | Chronological tables | Timelines and charts |
| 書 | 서 | 8 | Treatises | Topics: rites, music, calendar, economics, etc. |

### 3.2 Reading the Section Names

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Meaning in Context |
|-----------|------|------|-------------------|
| 本 | 본 (bon) | 근본 본 | Root, fundamental — the emperor's own record |
| 紀 | 기 (gi) | 기록할 기 | Record, annals |
| 世 | 세 (se) | 대 세 | Generation, hereditary |
| 家 | 가 (ga) | 집 가 | House, family |
| 列 | 렬 (ryeol) | 벌일 렬 | Arranged in order; a series of |
| 傳 | 전 (jeon) | 전할 전 | Biography; to transmit |
| 表 | 표 (pyo) | 겉 표 | Table, chart |
| 書 | 서 (seo) | 글 서 | Treatise, written record |

## 5. 項羽本紀 Excerpt: The Rise of Xiang Yu

### 5.1 Background

項羽 (항우, Xiang Yu, 232-202 BCE) was the most powerful military leader during the fall of the Qin dynasty. His biography in the 史記 is one of the most dramatic and literary passages in all Classical Chinese literature.

### 5.2 Original Text: Young Xiang Yu

```
項籍少時 學書不成 去 學劍 又不成
項梁怒之 籍曰 書足以記名姓而已
劍一人敵 不足學 學萬人敵
於是項梁乃敎籍兵法 籍大喜
```

### 5.3 Character-by-Character Annotation

| Character | 음독 | Meaning in Context |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| 項籍 | 항적 | Xiang Ji (Xiang Yu's original name) |
| 少時 | 소시 | In youth; when young |
| 學 | 학 | Studied |
| 書 | 서 | Writing; calligraphy |
| 不成 | 불성 | Did not succeed; gave up |
| 去 | 거 | Left; abandoned |
| 劍 | 검 | Swordsmanship |
| 又 | 우 | Again; also |
| 項梁 | 항량 | Xiang Liang (his uncle) |
| 怒 | 노 | Was angry at |
| 足以 | 족이 | Sufficient to; enough to |
| 記 | 기 | Record |
| 名姓 | 명성 | Name and surname |
| 而已 | 이이 | Nothing more; that's all |
| 一人敵 | 일인적 | An opponent of one person |
| 不足 | 부족 | Not worth |
| 萬人敵 | 만인적 | An opponent of ten thousand |
| 於是 | 어시 | Thereupon |
| 乃 | 내 | Then; thereupon |
| 兵法 | 병법 | Military strategy; the art of war |
| 大喜 | 대희 | Was greatly pleased |

### 5.4 석독

```
항적(항우)은 어릴 때 글을 배웠으나 이루지 못하고 그만두었다.
검술을 배웠으나 또한 이루지 못하였다.
항량(삼촌)이 그를 꾸짖자, 항적이 말하였다.
"글이란 이름과 성을 쓸 수 있으면 그만이고,
검은 한 사람을 상대하는 것이니 배울 것이 못 됩니다.
만 명을 상대하는 법을 배우겠습니다."
이에 항량이 항적에게 병법을 가르치니, 항적은 크게 기뻐하였다.
```

### 5.5 Grammar Analysis

| Pattern | Example | Function |
|---------|---------|----------|
| 不成 | 學書不成 | Verb + 不成 = "did not succeed in [verb]" |
| 足以 | 足以記名姓 | "Sufficient to [verb]" |
| 而已 | 記名姓而已 | "That's all; nothing more" (sentence-final limitation) |
| 於是...乃 | 於是項梁乃敎 | "Thereupon...then" (narrative transition) |

## 6. 項羽本紀 Excerpt: The Final Battle

### 6.1 Background

Xiang Yu's final defeat at Gaixia (垓下/해하) and his famous "farewell to his beloved" is one of the most poignant scenes in Chinese literature.

### 6.2 Original Text: Surrounded at Gaixia

```
項王軍壁垓下 兵少食盡
漢軍及諸侯兵圍之數重
夜聞漢軍四面皆楚歌
項王乃大驚曰 漢皆已得楚乎
是何楚人之多也
```

### 6.3 Annotation

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 項王軍壁垓下 | 항왕군벽해하 | King Xiang's army was encamped at Gaixia |
| 兵少食盡 | 병소식진 | Soldiers few, provisions exhausted |
| 漢軍及諸侯兵 | 한군급제후병 | The Han army and the armies of various lords |
| 圍之數重 | 위지수중 | Surrounded them in many layers |
| 夜聞漢軍 | 야문한군 | At night, [Xiang Yu] heard from the Han army |
| 四面皆楚歌 | 사면개초가 | On all four sides, all [singing] Chu songs |
| 項王乃大驚曰 | 항왕내대경왈 | King Xiang was greatly alarmed and said: |
| 漢皆已得楚乎 | 한개이득초호 | "Has Han already conquered Chu entirely?" |
| 是何楚人之多也 | 시하초인지다야 | "How is it that there are so many Chu people [with them]?" |

### 6.4 석독

```
항왕의 군대는 해하에 진을 치고 있었는데,
병사는 적고 양식이 다하였다.
한나라 군대와 제후들의 군대가 그를 몇 겹으로 포위하였다.
밤에 한나라 군대 사방에서 모두 초나라 노래를 부르는 것을 들으니,
항왕이 크게 놀라 말하였다.
"한나라가 이미 모두 초나라를 차지하였는가?
어찌 초나라 사람이 이리도 많은가!"
```

### 6.5 Cultural Legacy

This passage gave rise to the famous expression 四面楚歌 (사면초가):

| Expression | Reading | Literal | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| 四面楚歌 | 사면초가 | Chu songs on all four sides | Completely surrounded by enemies; hopeless isolation |

> This is one of the most commonly used 사자성어 in modern Korean, used to describe being surrounded by adversity.

## 7. 列傳 Opening Passages

### 7.1 伯夷列傳 — Biography of Bo Yi (Opening)

The first 열전 in the 史記 sets the philosophical tone for all that follows:

```
伯夷叔齊 孤竹君之二子也
父欲立叔齊 及父卒 叔齊讓伯夷
伯夷曰 父命也 遂逃去
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 伯夷叔齊 | 백이숙제 | Bo Yi and Shu Qi |
| 孤竹君之二子也 | 고죽군지이자야 | Were the two sons of the lord of Guzhu |
| 父欲立叔齊 | 부욕립숙제 | Their father wished to enthrone Shu Qi |
| 及父卒 | 급부졸 | When the father died |
| 叔齊讓伯夷 | 숙제양백이 | Shu Qi yielded to Bo Yi |
| 伯夷曰 | 백이왈 | Bo Yi said: |
| 父命也 | 부명야 | "It was father's command" |
| 遂逃去 | 수도거 | And thereupon fled |

### 7.2 李將軍列傳 — Biography of General Li (Opening)

```
李將軍廣者 隴西成紀人也
其先曰李信 秦時為將
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 李將軍廣者 | 이장군광자 | General Li Guang |
| 隴西成紀人也 | 농서성기인야 | Was a man from Chengji in Longxi |
| 其先曰李信 | 기선왈이신 | His ancestor was Li Xin |
| 秦時為將 | 진시위장 | Who served as a general in the Qin era |

### 7.3 Biographical Opening Formula

Notice the standard opening pattern for 열전 biographies:

```
Pattern: [Name]者 [Place]人也
"[Name] was a person from [Place]"

Examples:
孔子者 魯人也 — Confucius was a man of Lu
司馬遷者 夏陽人也 — Sima Qian was a man of Xiayang
```

## 8. Narrative Prose Techniques

### 8.1 Dialogue Introduction

Historical texts frequently include direct speech:

| Formula | Reading | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| A曰 | A왈 | A said: |
| A謂B曰 | A위B왈 | A said to B: |
| A問B曰 | A문B왈 | A asked B: |
| A對曰 | A대왈 | A replied: |
| A怒曰 | A노왈 | A said angrily: |
| A歎曰 | A탄왈 | A sighed and said: |

### 8.2 Temporal Markers

| Marker | Reading | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 初 | 초 | At first; initially |
| 已 | 이 | Already |
| 及 | 급 | When; by the time |
| 後 | 후 | Afterward; later |
| 遂 | 수 | Thereupon; and so |
| 乃 | 내 | Then; thereupon |
| 於是 | 어시 | At this point; thereupon |
| 卒 | 졸 | Finally; in the end; died |

### 8.3 Narrative Connectives

| Pattern | Reading | Function | Example |
|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| 於是 | 어시 | Transition: "thereupon" | 於是項梁乃敎籍兵法 |
| 遂 | 수 | Consequence: "and so" | 遂逃去 (and so [he] fled) |
| 乃 | 내 | Sequential: "then" | 乃大驚 (then was greatly alarmed) |
| 而 | 이 | Connection: "and/but" | 學書不成 去 (studied but didn't succeed) |
| 及 | 급 | Temporal: "when" | 及父卒 (when the father died) |

| Work | Korean | Author | Period | Content |

```
始祖姓朴氏 諱赫居世

```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 始祖姓朴氏 | 시조성박씨 | The founding ancestor's surname was Park |
| 諱赫居世 | 휘혁거세 | His posthumous name was Hyeokgeose |
| 前漢孝宣帝五鳳元年甲子 | 전한효선제오봉원년갑자 | In the first year of the Wufeng era of Emperor Xuan of Former Han (57 BCE) |
