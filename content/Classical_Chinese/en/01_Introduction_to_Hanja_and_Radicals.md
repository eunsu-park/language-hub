## 1. What is Hanja?

### 1.1 Hanja in Korean Context

Hanja (漢字, 한자) refers to Chinese characters as used in the Korean writing system. While modern Korean primarily uses Hangul (한글), Hanja remains essential for understanding Korean vocabulary, reading historical documents, and studying Classical Chinese literature.

> **Hanja Key Facts**
>
> - **Korean term**: 한자 (漢字)
> - **Origin**: Ancient China (circa 1200 BCE, oracle bone script)
> - **Total characters**: ~50,000+ exist; ~3,500 commonly used
> - **Korean education standard**: 1,800 basic characters
> - **Korean vocabulary impact**: ~60-70% of Korean words are 한자어
>
> Each character has:
>   - 음독 (音讀) — Sound reading (Korean pronunciation)
>   - 훈독 (訓讀) — Meaning reading (Korean meaning)
>   - Radical (部首) — Semantic component
>   - Stroke count (획수) — Number of strokes
>   - Stroke order (필순) — Correct writing sequence

### 1.2 Why Study Hanja?

| Reason | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Understand Korean vocabulary | 학교(學校) = 배울 학 + 학교 교 → "place of learning" |
| Distinguish homophones | 사기: 士氣(morale) vs 詐欺(fraud) vs 史記(historical record) |
| Read classical literature | 논어(論語), 맹자(孟子), 삼국사기(三國史記) |
| Understand legal/academic terms | 헌법(憲法), 민주주의(民主主義) |
| Connect with East Asian culture | Shared character heritage with China and Japan |

### 1.3 Hanja vs Hanzi vs Kanji

| Term | Language | Script Name | Characters Used |
|------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| 한자 (Hanja) | Korean | 漢字 | Traditional + some simplified forms |
| 汉字 (Hanzi) | Chinese | 漢字/汉字 | Simplified (mainland) / Traditional (Taiwan, HK) |
| 漢字 (Kanji) | Japanese | 漢字 | Mix of traditional and Japanese simplifications |

> **Note**: The same character may look slightly different across Korean, Chinese, and Japanese typesetting, but the core forms are recognizable across all three traditions.

## 2. History of Chinese Characters

### 2.1 Evolution of Script

Chinese characters have evolved through several major script stages over 3,000+ years:

> **Evolution of Chinese Characters**
>
> 甲骨文 (갑골문) — Oracle Bone Script (~1200 BCE)
>   Carved on turtle shells and animal bones for divination
> ↓
> 金文 (금문) — Bronze Script (~1000 BCE)
>   Cast on bronze ritual vessels
> ↓
> 篆書 (전서) — Seal Script (~221 BCE)
>   Standardized by Qin dynasty, still used in seals today
> ↓
> 隸書 (예서) — Clerical Script (~200 BCE)
>   Simplified for government clerks, angular strokes
> ↓
> 楷書 (해서) — Regular Script (~200 CE)
>   The standard form used today in printing and education
> ↓
> 行書 (행서) — Running Script
>   Semi-cursive, used in everyday handwriting
> ↓
> 草書 (초서) — Cursive Script
>   Highly abbreviated, artistic calligraphy

### 2.2 Example: Evolution of 馬 (마, horse)

| Stage | Form | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Oracle Bone (甲骨文) | Pictograph of a horse with mane, body, legs, tail | Clearly recognizable as a horse |
| Bronze (金文) | More stylized, still horse-like | Artistic flourishes added |
| Seal Script (篆書) | Symmetrical, formal | Official standardization |
| Clerical (隸書) | Flattened, angular strokes | Easier to write with brush on bamboo |
| Regular (楷書) | 馬 — the modern form | Standard form taught today |

## 3. The Six Types of Characters (六書)

### 3.1 Overview

The traditional classification system, called 六書 (육서, "Six Writings"), describes how Chinese characters were formed. Understanding these categories helps you decode unfamiliar characters.

| # | Type | Chinese | Korean | Method | % of Characters |
|---|------|---------|--------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Pictograph | 象形 | 상형 | Drawing of an object | ~4% |
| 2 | Indicative | 指事 | 지사 | Abstract symbol for a concept | ~1% |
| 3 | Compound Ideograph | 會意 | 회의 | Combining meanings | ~13% |
| 4 | Phono-semantic | 形聲 | 형성 | Meaning part + sound part | ~80% |
| 5 | Derivative | 轉注 | 전주 | Extended meaning | Rare |
| 6 | Phonetic Loan | 假借 | 가차 | Borrowed for sound | Rare |

### 3.2 Pictographs (象形/상형)

Pictographs are stylized drawings of physical objects. They are the oldest and most intuitive character type.

| Character | Reading | Meaning | Pictographic Origin |
|-----------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| 日 | 일 (il) | sun, day | Circle with dot (sun disk) |
| 月 | 월 (wol) | moon, month | Crescent moon shape |
| 山 | 산 (san) | mountain | Three peaks |
| 水 | 수 (su) | water | Flowing stream with drops |
| 木 | 목 (mok) | tree, wood | Tree with branches and roots |
| 火 | 화 (hwa) | fire | Flames rising |
| 口 | 구 (gu) | mouth | Open mouth shape |
| 目 | 목 (mok) | eye | Eye turned sideways |
| 人 | 인 (in) | person | Walking person, side view |
| 馬 | 마 (ma) | horse | Horse with mane and legs |

> **Key insight**: Only ~4% of characters are pure pictographs, but they form the building blocks for thousands of compound characters.

### 3.3 Indicative Characters (指事/지사)

Indicative characters use abstract symbols to represent concepts that cannot be drawn.

| Character | Reading | Meaning | How It Works |
|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|
| 一 | 일 (il) | one | One horizontal stroke |
| 二 | 이 (i) | two | Two horizontal strokes |
| 三 | 삼 (sam) | three | Three horizontal strokes |
| 上 | 상 (sang) | above, up | Mark above a baseline |
| 下 | 하 (ha) | below, down | Mark below a baseline |
| 本 | 본 (bon) | root, origin | Mark at base of tree (木) |
| 末 | 말 (mal) | tip, end | Mark at top of tree (木) |
| 刃 | 인 (in) | blade | Mark on edge of knife (刀) |

### 3.4 Compound Ideographs (會意/회의)

Compound ideographs combine two or more characters to create a new meaning.

| Character | Reading | Components | Combined Meaning |
|-----------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| 明 | 명 (myeong) | 日 (sun) + 月 (moon) | Bright (sun and moon together) |
| 林 | 림 (rim) | 木 (tree) + 木 (tree) | Grove (two trees) |
| 森 | 삼 (sam) | 木 + 木 + 木 | Forest (three trees) |
| 休 | 휴 (hyu) | 人 (person) + 木 (tree) | Rest (person leaning on tree) |
| 信 | 신 (sin) | 人 (person) + 言 (speech) | Trust (person's word) |
| 男 | 남 (nam) | 田 (field) + 力 (strength) | Man (strength in the field) |
| 好 | 호 (ho) | 女 (woman) + 子 (child) | Good (woman with child) |

### 3.5 Phono-semantic Characters (形聲/형성)

This is the most important category, accounting for ~80% of all characters. Each has two parts: a **semantic radical** (meaning hint) and a **phonetic component** (sound hint).

| Character | Reading | Semantic Part | Phonetic Part | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|--------------|--------------|---------|
| 河 | 하 (ha) | 氵(water) | 可 (가, ga) | River |
| 湖 | 호 (ho) | 氵(water) | 胡 (호, ho) | Lake |
| 銅 | 동 (dong) | 金 (metal) | 同 (동, dong) | Copper |
| 鐵 | 철 (cheol) | 金 (metal) | 失 sound component | Iron |
| 請 | 청 (cheong) | 言 (speech) | 青 (청, cheong) | Request |
| 清 | 청 (cheong) | 氵(water) | 青 (청, cheong) | Clear, pure |
| 晴 | 청 (cheong) | 日 (sun) | 青 (청, cheong) | Clear weather |

> **Pattern**: Notice 河, 湖 both have 氵(water radical) — they are water-related. And 請, 清, 晴 all share the phonetic component 青 and similar pronunciation (청).

### 3.6 Derivative and Loan Characters (轉注·假借 / 전주·가차)

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| 轉注 (전주) | Extended/shifted meaning from original | 樂 (악/락): originally "music" (악) → extended to "joy, pleasure" (락) |
| 假借 (가차) | Character borrowed for its sound | 來 (래): originally pictograph of "wheat" → borrowed for verb "to come" |

## 4. Stroke Order Principles

### 4.1 Why Stroke Order Matters

Correct stroke order (필순/筆順) ensures:
- Characters look balanced and natural
- Writing flows efficiently
- Others can read your handwriting
- You can count strokes accurately for dictionary lookup

### 4.2 Eight Basic Rules

| Rule | Principle | Korean | Example |
|------|-----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Top to bottom | 위에서 아래로 | 三: top stroke → middle → bottom |
| 2 | Left to right | 왼쪽에서 오른쪽으로 | 川: left stroke → middle → right |
| 3 | Horizontal before vertical | 가로획 먼저 | 十: horizontal (一) → vertical (丨) |
| 4 | Left-falling before right-falling | 왼삐침 먼저 | 人: left-falling (丿) → right-falling (㇏) |
| 5 | Outside before inside | 바깥에서 안으로 | 月: outer frame → inner strokes |
| 6 | Inside before closing | 안을 채운 후 닫기 | 日: frame → inner horizontal → bottom closing stroke |
| 7 | Center before wings | 가운데 먼저 | 小: center stroke → left dot → right dot |
| 8 | Right-to-left for enclosures | 오른쪽 감싸기 나중 | 口: left vertical → top-right angle → bottom |

### 4.3 Stroke Order Examples

**十 (십, ten)**: 2 strokes
```
Step 1: 一 (horizontal stroke, left to right)
Step 2: 丨 (vertical stroke, top to bottom)
```

**大 (대, big)**: 3 strokes
```
Step 1: 一 (horizontal stroke)
Step 2: 丿 (left-falling stroke)
Step 3: ㇏ (right-falling stroke)
```

**國 (국, country)**: 11 strokes
```
Steps 1-3: Outer frame (口 enclosure, left-top-right)
Steps 4-9: Inner component (或)
Step 10-11: Bottom closing stroke of enclosure
```

## 6. Introduction to Radicals (部首)

### 6.1 What are Radicals?

Radicals (部首, 부수) are the semantic building blocks of Chinese characters. The traditional Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典) system classifies all characters under 214 radicals.

> **Radicals Key Facts**
>
> - **Total radicals**: 214 (Kangxi system)
> - **Purpose**:
>   - Indicate meaning category of a character
>   - Organize characters in dictionaries
>   - Help memorize and distinguish similar characters
>
> A radical can appear in different positions:
> - **偏 (편, left)**: 氵in 河 (water → river)
> - **旁 (방, right)**: 刂in 刊 (knife → carve/publish)
> - **冠 (관, top)**: 雨 in 雪 (rain → snow)
> - **脚 (각, bottom)**: 心 in 思 (heart → think)
> - **圍 (위, enclosure)**: 口 in 國 (enclosure → country)
> - **角 (각, corner)**: 辶 in 道 (walk → road/way)

### 6.2 Radical Position Names

| Position | Korean Name | Chinese Name | Example | Meaning |
|----------|------------|-------------|---------|---------|
| Left | 변 (偏) | 偏 (편) | 氵in 河 | Water radical → river |
| Right | 방 (旁) | 旁 (방) | 刂 in 判 | Knife radical → judge |
| Top | 머리 (冠) | 冠 (관) | 艹 in 花 | Grass radical → flower |
| Bottom | 발 (脚) | 脚 (각) | 灬 in 熱 | Fire radical → heat |
| Enclosure | 에운담 (圍) | 圍 (위) | 囗 in 國 | Enclosure → country |
| Bottom-left | 받침 (繞) | 繞 (요) | 辶 in 道 | Walk radical → road |

### 9.1 How to Use Radicals for Dictionary Lookup

Traditional Chinese dictionaries organize characters by radical. To look up an unknown character:

```
Step 1: Identify the radical (usually left side or top)
Step 2: Count the radical's strokes
Step 3: Find the radical section in the dictionary
Step 4: Count remaining strokes (total - radical strokes)
Step 5: Find the character under that stroke count
```

**Example**: Looking up 語 (말씀 어, language)
```
Radical: 言 (7 strokes) → find 言 section
Remaining: 語 has 14 total strokes - 7 = 7 remaining strokes
Look under 言 radical, 7 additional strokes → 語
```

## 10. How Radicals Help You Learn

### 10.1 Meaning Families

Characters sharing the same radical often belong to the same meaning category:

**水/氵 (water) family:**

| Character | Reading | Meaning | Connection to Water |
|-----------|---------|---------|-------------------|

| 泳 | 영 (yeong) | Swim | Moving in water |
| 洗 | 세 (se) | Wash | Using water to clean |
| 清 | 청 (cheong) | Clear, pure | Quality of water |
| 深 | 심 (sim) | Deep | Depth of water |
| 波 | 파 (pa) | Wave | Movement of water |

**心/忄 (heart/mind) family:**

| Character | Reading | Meaning | Connection to Heart/Mind |
|-----------|---------|---------|------------------------|
| 思 | 사 (sa) | Think | Activity of the mind |
| 情 | 정 (jeong) | Emotion, feeling | State of the heart |
| 忠 | 충 (chung) | Loyalty | Quality of the heart |
| 悲 | 비 (bi) | Sad | Emotion of the heart |
| 怒 | 노 (no) | Anger | Emotion of the heart |
| 愛 | 애 (ae) | Love | Feeling of the heart |
| 恐 | 공 (gong) | Fear | Emotion of the heart |

### 10.2 Sound Families

Characters sharing the same phonetic component often have similar Korean readings:

**青 (청, cheong) phonetic family:**

| Character | Reading | Radical | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| 青 | 청 (cheong) | — | Blue/green |
| 清 | 청 (cheong) | 氵(water) | Clear, pure |
| 晴 | 청 (cheong) | 日 (sun) | Clear weather |
| 請 | 청 (cheong) | 言 (speech) | Request |
| 精 | 정 (jeong) | 米 (rice) | Essence, refined |
| 情 | 정 (jeong) | 忄(heart) | Emotion |

> **Learning tip**: When you encounter a new character, identify both its radical (meaning hint) and its phonetic component (sound hint). This dual-clue system makes memorization much more efficient.

## 11. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Identify the Six Types (六書)

Classify each character by its formation type:

```
1. 日 (sun)
2. 上 (above)
```
