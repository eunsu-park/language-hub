## 1. Hanja in Korean: The 60% Foundation

### 1.1 Why Hanja Matters for Korean

Approximately 60-70% of Korean vocabulary consists of 한자어 (漢字語, Sino-Korean words) — words derived from Chinese characters. Understanding Hanja unlocks the meaning behind most formal, academic, and technical Korean vocabulary.

> **Korean Vocabulary Composition**
>
> - **한자어 (漢字語) Sino-Korean**: ~60-70%
> - **고유어 (固有語) Native Korean**: ~25-30%
> - **외래어 (外來語) Foreign loanwords**: ~5-10%
>
> Example: The word 도서관 (library)
>   - 도(圖) = picture, diagram
>   - 서(書) = book, writing
>   - 관(館) = building, hall
>   - → "building for books and diagrams" = library
>
> Knowing the Hanja reveals the MEANING of the Korean word.

### 1.2 How Hanja Becomes Korean

Each Chinese character has a fixed Korean pronunciation (음독/音讀). When characters combine into compounds, these pronunciations join together:

| Hanja | Individual Readings | Compound | Korean Word |
|-------|-------------------|----------|-------------|
| 學 + 校 | 학 + 교 | 學校 | 학교 (school) |
| 先 + 生 | 선 + 생 | 先生 | 선생 (teacher) |
| 大 + 學 | 대 + 학 | 大學 | 대학 (university) |
| 圖 + 書 + 館 | 도 + 서 + 관 | 圖書館 | 도서관 (library) |

## 2. Two-Character Compound Formation Rules

### 2.1 Structural Patterns

Most Sino-Korean words are two-character compounds. They follow predictable structural patterns:

| Pattern | Structure | Example | Reading | Meaning |
|---------|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Modifier + Modified** | Adjective + Noun | 大學 | 대학 | Great + learning = university |
| **Verb + Object** | Verb + Noun | 讀書 | 독서 | Read + book = reading |
| **Subject + Predicate** | Noun + Verb | 日出 | 일출 | Sun + emerge = sunrise |
| **Parallel** | Noun + Noun (same category) | 父母 | 부모 | Father + mother = parents |
| **Opposite pair** | Antonym + Antonym | 上下 | 상하 | Up + down = above and below |
| **Verb + Complement** | Verb + Result | 說明 | 설명 | Speak + bright = explain |

### 2.2 Modifier + Modified (修飾語 + 被修飾語)

The first character modifies (describes) the second:

| Compound | Reading | Analysis | Meaning |
|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| 新聞 | 신문 | 新 (new) + 聞 (hear) | Newspaper (new things heard) |
| 白馬 | 백마 | 白 (white) + 馬 (horse) | White horse |
| 高山 | 고산 | 高 (high) + 山 (mountain) | High mountain |
| 古文 | 고문 | 古 (old) + 文 (writing) | Classical writing |
| 長男 | 장남 | 長 (eldest) + 男 (male) | Eldest son |
| 小說 | 소설 | 小 (small) + 說 (talk) | Novel (fiction — "small talk") |

### 2.3 Verb + Object (動詞 + 目的語)

The first character (verb) acts on the second (object):

| Compound | Reading | Analysis | Meaning |
|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| 讀書 | 독서 | 讀 (read) + 書 (book) | Reading |
| 登山 | 등산 | 登 (climb) + 山 (mountain) | Mountain climbing |
| 作文 | 작문 | 作 (make) + 文 (writing) | Composition |
| 入學 | 입학 | 入 (enter) + 學 (school) | Enrollment |
| 出國 | 출국 | 出 (exit) + 國 (country) | Departure from country |
| 開門 | 개문 | 開 (open) + 門 (gate) | Opening the gate |

### 2.4 Parallel Compounds (竝列 構造)

Two characters of equal status combined:

| Compound | Reading | Analysis | Meaning |
|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| 山川 | 산천 | 山 (mountain) + 川 (river) | Mountains and rivers |
| 兄弟 | 형제 | 兄 (elder) + 弟 (younger) | Brothers |
| 飮食 | 음식 | 飮 (drink) + 食 (eat) | Food and drink |
| 言語 | 언어 | 言 (words) + 語 (language) | Language |
| 道路 | 도로 | 道 (way) + 路 (road) | Road |

### 2.5 Opposite-Pair Compounds (反義 構造)

Two antonyms combined to express a broader concept:

| Compound | Reading | Analysis | Meaning |
|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| 上下 | 상하 | 上 (up) + 下 (down) | Above and below; all levels |
| 大小 | 대소 | 大 (big) + 小 (small) | Size; magnitude |
| 長短 | 장단 | 長 (long) + 短 (short) | Length; pros and cons |
| 生死 | 생사 | 生 (live) + 死 (die) | Life and death |
| 出入 | 출입 | 出 (out) + 入 (in) | Going in and out; access |
| 男女 | 남녀 | 男 (male) + 女 (female) | Men and women |
| 左右 | 좌우 | 左 (left) + 右 (right) | Left and right; influence |
| 古今 | 고금 | 古 (old) + 今 (now) | Past and present |

## 7. Sino-Korean Number System

### 7.1 Two Number Systems in Korean

Korean uses two number systems. The Sino-Korean system (한자어 수) is based on Chinese characters:

| Sino-Korean | Native Korean | Used For |
|------------|--------------|---------|
| 일(一), 이(二), 삼(三)... | 하나, 둘, 셋... | Sino-Korean: dates, money, phone numbers, math, addresses |
| 일(一), 이(二), 삼(三)... | 하나, 둘, 셋... | Native Korean: age (with 살), counting with counters |

### 7.2 Sino-Korean Numbers in Daily Use

| Usage | Example | Hanja | Korean |
|-------|---------|-------|--------|
| Date | January 1 | 一月 一日 | 일월 일일 |
| Money | 5,000 won | 五千원 | 오천 원 |
| Phone | 02-123-4567 | — | 공이-일이삼-사오육칠 |
| Floor | 3rd floor | 三層 | 삼층 |
| Year | 2026 | 二千二十六年 | 이천이십육 년 |
| Month | March | 三月 | 삼월 |
| Percent | 50% | 五十% | 오십 퍼센트 |

### 7.3 Special Counting Expressions

| Expression | Reading | Literal | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| 第一 | 제일 | Ordinal + one | First; number one |
| 十分 | 십분 | Ten + parts | Fully; completely |
| 百分率 | 백분율 | Hundred + parts + rate | Percentage |
| 千萬 | 천만 | Thousand + ten-thousand | Ten million; "by all means" |
| 萬一 | 만일 | Ten-thousand + one | If by any chance; in case |

## 8. Hanja in Daily Korean Life

### 8.1 Hanja on Signs and Public Spaces

You encounter Hanja daily in Korea, even if you do not realize it:

| Korean | Hanja | Where You See It |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| 출구 | 出口 | Exit signs in subway stations |
| 입구 | 入口 | Entrance signs |
| 남 / 녀 | 男 / 女 | Restroom signs |
| 대한민국 | 大韓民國 | Government buildings, passports |
| 금연 | 禁煙 | No smoking signs |
| 주차금지 | 駐車禁止 | No parking signs |
| 비상구 | 非常口 | Emergency exit |
| 화장실 | 化粧室 | Restroom (lit. "makeup room") |

### 8.2 Hanja in Korean Names

Most Korean names are chosen based on their Hanja meaning:

| Name | Hanja | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| 민수 (Minsu) | 民秀 | People + excellent |
| 지영 (Jiyeong) | 智英 | Wisdom + brilliant |
| 성호 (Seongho) | 成浩 | Achieve + vast |
| 미나 (Mina) | 美娜 | Beautiful + graceful |
| 현우 (Hyeonu) | 賢宇 | Wise + universe |
| 서연 (Seoyeon) | 瑞妍 | Auspicious + beautiful |

### 8.3 Hanja in Formal Writing

| Context | Example | Usage |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Newspapers | 大統領(대통령, president) | Headlines, formal articles |
| Legal documents | 甲(갑), 乙(을) | Contract parties |
| Academic writing | 參考文獻(참고문헌, references) | Scholarly conventions |
| Business cards | 代表理事(대표이사, CEO) | Professional titles |

### Exercise 2: Decompose Korean Words

Write the Hanja and meaning of each component:

```
1. 학교 (school)
2. 병원 (hospital)
3. 도서관 (library)
4. 대한민국 (Republic of Korea)
5. 자연 (nature)
```

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

```
1. 학교 = 學校
   學 (배울 학, learn) + 校 (학교 교, school building)
   → "Place of learning"

2. 병원 = 病院
   病 (병 병, illness) + 院 (집 원, institution)
   → "Institution for illness" = hospital

3. 도서관 = 圖書館
   圖 (그림 도, picture) + 書 (글 서, book) + 館 (집 관, hall)
   → "Hall of pictures and books" = library

4. 대한민국 = 大韓民國
   大 (큰 대, great) + 韓 (나라이름 한, Korea) + 民 (백성 민, people) + 國 (나라 국, country)
   → "Great Korean People's Country" = Republic of Korea

5. 자연 = 自然
   自 (스스로 자, self) + 然 (그러할 연, so/thus)
   → "Being of itself" = nature
```

</details>

### Exercise 4: Hanja in Korean Signs

What do these signs mean? Identify the Hanja:

```
1. 出口
2. 禁煙
3. 男 / 女
4. 非常口
5. 駐車禁止
```

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

```
1. 出口 (출구) = Exit
   出 (나갈 출, go out) + 口 (입 구, opening) → Way out

2. 禁煙 (금연) = No Smoking
   禁 (금할 금, prohibit) + 煙 (연기 연, smoke) → Smoking prohibited

5. 駐車禁止 (주차금지) = No Parking
   駐 (머무를 주, stay) + 車 (수레 차, car) + 禁 (금할 금, prohibit) + 止 (그칠 지, stop)
```
