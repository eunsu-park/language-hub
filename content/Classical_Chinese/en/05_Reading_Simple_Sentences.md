## 1. Classical Chinese vs Modern Languages

### 1.1 Key Differences

Classical Chinese operates very differently from modern Korean, Chinese, or English:

> **Classical Chinese Sentence Characteristics**
>
> 1. No inflection — verbs do not conjugate
> 2. No particles like 이/가, 을/를 (those are added in Korean)
> 3. One character = one word (mostly)
> 4. Word class is flexible — same character can be noun, verb, or adjective depending on position
> 5. Subject is often omitted when contextually clear
> 6. Extremely concise — no wasted characters
> 7. Word order indicates grammatical function
>
> Compare:
> - **Classical Chinese**: 孔子學禮 (4 characters)
> - **Korean**: 공자가 예를 배웠다 (7 syllables + particles)
> - **English**: Confucius studied the rites (5 words)

### 1.2 Reading Direction

Classical Chinese was traditionally written top-to-bottom, right-to-left. In modern educational materials, it is presented left-to-right like modern text. In this course, we use the modern left-to-right format.

## 2. Basic Sentence Structure: Subject + Predicate

### 2.1 The Simplest Sentences

The most basic Classical Chinese sentence has just two elements: **Subject + Predicate**. No particles, no copula needed.

| Sentence | Reading | Subject | Predicate | Translation |
|----------|---------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 天高 | 천고 | 天 (sky) | 高 (high) | The sky is high |
| 日出 | 일출 | 日 (sun) | 出 (emerge) | The sun rises |
| 月明 | 월명 | 月 (moon) | 明 (bright) | The moon is bright |
| 水清 | 수청 | 水 (water) | 清 (clear) | The water is clear |
| 山高 | 산고 | 山 (mountain) | 高 (high) | The mountain is high |
| 花開 | 화개 | 花 (flower) | 開 (open) | Flowers bloom |
| 鳥飛 | 조비 | 鳥 (bird) | 飛 (fly) | Birds fly |
| 風起 | 풍기 | 風 (wind) | 起 (rise) | The wind rises |

> **Key insight**: In Classical Chinese, adjectives function as predicates directly. No "is" or "be" verb is needed. 天高 means "The sky [is] high" — the adjective 高 serves as the predicate.

### 2.2 Extended Subject-Predicate Patterns

| Sentence | Reading | Translation | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------|
| 人善 | 인선 | People are good | Adjective predicate |
| 道遠 | 도원 | The road is long | Adjective predicate |
| 水深 | 수심 | The water is deep | Adjective predicate |
| 民苦 | 민고 | The people suffer | Verb predicate |
| 王怒 | 왕노 | The king is angry | Verb/adjective predicate |
| 子笑 | 자소 | The child laughs | Verb predicate |

## 3. The Copula 也: Equational Sentences

### 3.1 Understanding 也 (야/야)

The character 也 (야, ya) serves as a **copula** or **assertion marker** at the end of sentences. It functions like "is" or "indeed" in English, or "-이다" in Korean.

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Function |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 也 | 야 (ya) | 어조사 야 | Sentence-final copula; assertion; "is"; "indeed" |

### 3.2 Simple A也 Pattern ("A is so")

When 也 appears at the end of a statement, it asserts the truth of what precedes it:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 善也 | 선야 | It is good (indeed) |
| 是也 | 시야 | That is so; correct |
| 然也 | 연야 | It is so; indeed |
| 非也 | 비야 | It is not so |

### 3.3 A B也 Pattern ("A is B")

The basic equational sentence places 也 at the end:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation | Structure |
|----------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| 孔子聖人也 | 공자성인야 | Confucius is a sage | 孔子 = A, 聖人 = B, 也 = copula |
| 水生命也 | 수생명야 | Water is life | 水 = A, 生命 = B, 也 = copula |
| 仁德也 | 인덕야 | Benevolence is a virtue | 仁 = A, 德 = B, 也 = copula |

## 4. Topic-Comment Structure

### 4.1 What is Topic-Comment?

Classical Chinese is a **topic-prominent** language. The first element establishes the topic, and the rest comments on it. This is similar to Korean's topic marker 은/는.

> **Topic-Comment Structure**
>
> [Topic]  [Comment]
>
> 天     高         The sky — [it is] high
> (topic) (comment)
>
> - **Korean equivalent**: 하늘은 높다
> - The 은/는 topic marker = the position of the topic in CC

### 4.2 Topic-Comment Examples

| Sentence | Reading | Topic | Comment | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------|---------|-------------|
| 天高地厚 | 천고지후 | 天 (sky) / 地 (earth) | 高 (high) / 厚 (thick) | The sky is high, the earth is thick |
| 春暖花開 | 춘난화개 | 春 (spring) | 暖 (warm) | Spring is warm, flowers bloom |
| 人心難測 | 인심난측 | 人心 (human heart) | 難測 (hard to fathom) | The human heart is hard to fathom |
| 君子和而不同 | 군자화이부동 | 君子 (gentleman) | 和而不同 (harmonize but not conform) | A gentleman harmonizes but does not blindly conform |

### 4.3 Parallel Topic-Comment

Classical Chinese loves parallel structure — two topic-comment pairs in one sentence:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 天高地卑 | 천고지비 | Heaven is high, earth is low |
| 日往月來 | 일왕월래 | The sun goes, the moon comes |
| 父慈子孝 | 부자자효 | Father is kind, son is filial |
| 兄友弟恭 | 형우제공 | Elder brother is friendly, younger brother is respectful |
| 君仁臣忠 | 군인신충 | The ruler is benevolent, the minister is loyal |

## 5. Simple Predication: Adjective Predicates

### 5.1 Adjective Characters as Predicates

In Classical Chinese, adjectives serve directly as predicates without a linking verb:

| Adjective | 음독 | 훈독 | Example Sentence | Translation |
|-----------|------|------|-----------------|-------------|
| 高 | 고 (go) | 높을 고 | 山高 | The mountain is high |
| 大 | 대 (dae) | 큰 대 | 天大 | Heaven is great |
| 小 | 소 (so) | 작을 소 | 國小 | The country is small |
| 長 | 장 (jang) | 길 장 | 道長 | The road is long |
| 深 | 심 (sim) | 깊을 심 | 水深 | The water is deep |
| 明 | 명 (myeong) | 밝을 명 | 月明 | The moon is bright |
| 善 | 선 (seon) | 착할 선 | 人善 | People are good |
| 惡 | 악 (ak) | 악할 악 | 心惡 | The heart is wicked |
| 難 | 난 (nan) | 어려울 난 | 事難 | The matter is difficult |
| 易 | 이 (i) | 쉬울 이 | 事易 | The matter is easy |

### 5.2 Degree and Comparison

Classical Chinese expresses degree through adverbs placed before the predicate:

| Adverb | 음독 | 훈독 | Function | Example | Translation |
|--------|------|------|----------|---------|-------------|
| 甚 | 심 (sim) | 심할 심 | Very, extremely | 天甚高 | The sky is very high |
| 極 | 극 (geuk) | 극진할 극 | Extremely | 水極深 | The water is extremely deep |
| 最 | 최 (choe) | 가장 최 | Most | 山最高 | The mountain is the highest |
| 尤 | 우 (u) | 더욱 우 | Especially | 月尤明 | The moon is especially bright |

## 6. Subject-Verb-Object Sentences

### 6.1 SVO Pattern

When a verb takes an object, Classical Chinese follows the same SVO order as English (but different from Korean SOV):

```
Classical Chinese:  S + V + O  (Subject-Verb-Object)
Korean:             S + O + V  (Subject-Object-Verb)
English:            S + V + O  (Subject-Verb-Object)
```

| Sentence | Reading | S | V | O | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|---|---|-------------|
| 孔子學禮 | 공자학례 | 孔子 | 學 | 禮 | Confucius studied the rites |
| 王愛民 | 왕애민 | 王 | 愛 | 民 | The king loves the people |
| 人讀書 | 인독서 | 人 | 讀 | 書 | A person reads books |
| 子食飯 | 자식반 | 子 | 食 | 飯 | The child eats rice |
| 民耕田 | 민경전 | 民 | 耕 | 田 | The people plow the fields |
| 師敎學 | 사교학 | 師 | 敎 | 學 | The teacher teaches learning |

### 6.2 VO (Verb-Object) Without Subject

Classical Chinese frequently drops the subject when it is contextually clear:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation | Implied Subject |
|----------|---------|-------------|----------------|
| 讀書 | 독서 | [Someone] reads books | General activity |
| 學禮 | 학례 | [Someone] studies rites | Contextual |
| 治國 | 치국 | [Someone] governs the country | Ruler |
| 愛民 | 애민 | [Someone] loves the people | Ruler |

### 6.3 SVO with Adverbs

Adverbs typically precede the verb:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation | Adverb |
|----------|---------|-------------|--------|
| 人皆讀書 | 인개독서 | Everyone reads books | 皆 (개, all) |
| 王常愛民 | 왕상애민 | The king always loves the people | 常 (상, always) |
| 子勤學 | 자근학 | The child studies diligently | 勤 (근, diligently) |

## 7. Introduction to 者 and 之

### 7.1 者 (자) — "The One Who" / Nominalizer

者 (자, ja) turns a verb or clause into a noun phrase, like "-는 자" or "-는 사람" in Korean:

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Functions |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 者 | 자 (ja) | 놈 자 / 사람 자 | 1. Nominalizer: "one who..." 2. Topic marker in 者...也 pattern |

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 學者 | 학자 | One who learns; a scholar |
| 讀者 | 독자 | One who reads; a reader |
| 仁者 | 인자 | One who is benevolent; a benevolent person |
| 知者 | 지자 | One who knows; a wise person |
| 勇者 | 용자 | One who is brave; a brave person |
| 王者 | 왕자 | One who rules; a ruler |

### 7.2 之 (지) — Possessive / Structural Particle

之 (지, ji) is one of the most important and versatile function words in Classical Chinese:

| Character | 음독 | 훈독 | Functions |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| 之 | 지 (ji) | 갈 지 / 어조사 지 | 1. Possessive "of" (A之B = A의 B) 2. Object pronoun "it/them" 3. Structural particle |

**Function 1: Possessive (의)**

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 人之心 | 인지심 | A person's heart |
| 天之道 | 천지도 | The way of heaven |
| 國之本 | 국지본 | The foundation of a country |
| 學之道 | 학지도 | The way of learning |
| 水之性 | 수지성 | The nature of water |

**Function 2: Object pronoun (그것을)**

| Example | Reading | Translation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 學而時習之 | 학이시습지 | Learn and from time to time practice **it** |
| 知之 | 지지 | Know **it** |
| 愛之 | 애지 | Love **it/them** |

## 8. The A者B也 Pattern

### 8.1 The Definitional Pattern

The A者B也 pattern is one of the most important sentence structures in Classical Chinese. It defines or equates A with B:

> **The A者B也 Pattern**
>
> - **Structure**: A + 者 + B + 也
> - **Meaning**: "A is B" / "As for A, it is B"
> - **Korean**: A라는 것은 B이다
>
> - 者 = topic/subject marker ("as for")
> - 也 = copula/assertion ("is")
>
> Example:
> 學者 覺也   (학자 각야)
> "Learning (者) is awakening (也)"
> 학이란 것은 깨달음이다

### 8.2 Examples of A者B也

| Sentence | Reading | A | B | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|---|-------------|
| 學者覺也 | 학자각야 | 學 (learning) | 覺 (awakening) | Learning is awakening |
| 仁者愛人也 | 인자애인야 | 仁 (benevolence) | 愛人 (loving people) | Benevolence is loving others |
| 義者宜也 | 의자의야 | 義 (righteousness) | 宜 (appropriate) | Righteousness is what is fitting |
| 禮者理也 | 례자리야 | 禮 (ritual) | 理 (principle) | Ritual is principle |
| 孝者德之本也 | 효자덕지본야 | 孝 (filial piety) | 德之本 (root of virtue) | Filial piety is the root of virtue |
| 天命之謂性 | 천명지위성 | 天命 (heaven's decree) | 性 (nature) | What heaven decrees is called nature |

### 8.3 Variations of the Pattern

| Variation | Example | Reading | Translation |
|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|
| A者B | 學者覺 | 학자각 | Learning is awakening (without 也) |
| A B也 | 學覺也 | 학각야 | Learning is awakening (without 者) |
| A者 B之C也 | 孝者 德之本也 | 효자 덕지본야 | Filial piety is the root of virtue |

## 9. Reading Practice: Simple Passages

### 9.1 Passage 1: Nature

```
天高地厚。日月明。山水清。
```

**Character-by-character analysis:**

| Char | Reading | Meaning | Function |
|------|---------|---------|----------|
| 天 | 천 | Sky | Subject |
| 高 | 고 | High | Predicate |
| 地 | 지 | Earth | Subject |
| 厚 | 후 | Thick | Predicate |
| 日 | 일 | Sun | Subject |
| 月 | 월 | Moon | Subject |
| 明 | 명 | Bright | Predicate |
| 山 | 산 | Mountain | Subject |
| 水 | 수 | Water | Subject |
| 清 | 청 | Clear | Predicate |

**Translation**: The sky is high and the earth is thick. The sun and moon are bright. The mountains and waters are clear.

### 9.2 Passage 2: Human Virtues

```
仁者愛人。知者不惑。勇者不懼。
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 仁者愛人 | 인자애인 | The benevolent person loves others |
| 知者不惑 | 지자불혹 | The wise person is not confused |
| 勇者不懼 | 용자불구 | The brave person does not fear |

> **Note**: This is from the Analects (論語). The 者 pattern names a type of person, then describes their defining quality.

### 9.3 Passage 3: Learning

```
學而時習之。不亦說乎。
```

| Char | Reading | Meaning | Function |
|------|---------|---------|----------|
| 學 | 학 | Learn | Verb |
| 而 | 이 | And then | Conjunction |
| 時 | 시 | From time to time | Adverb |
| 習 | 습 | Practice, review | Verb |
| 之 | 지 | It (what was learned) | Object pronoun |
| 不 | 불 | Not | Negative |
| 亦 | 역 | Also | Adverb |
| 說 | 열 | Pleased (=悅) | Adjective |
| 乎 | 호 | ? (question marker) | Particle |

**Translation**: To learn and from time to time practice it — is that not also a pleasure?

### 9.4 Passage 4: Governance

```
王者愛民。民者國之本也。
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 王者愛民 | 왕자애민 | A king loves the people |
| 民者國之本也 | 민자국지본야 | The people are the foundation of the country |
