## 1. Overview of Statement Patterns

### 1.1 Five Core Declarative Patterns

Classical Chinese has five fundamental patterns for making statements:

> **Five Core Statement Patterns**
>
> - ① Subject-Predicate: 天高 — The sky is high
> - ② Subject-Verb-Object: 孔子學禮 — Confucius studied rites
> - ③ Double Object: 王賜臣馬 — The king bestowed a horse
> - ④ 者...也 Definition: 學者覺也 — Learning is awakening
> - ⑤ Existential (有/無): 有朋 — There are friends
>
> Additional modifications:
>   - Adverbs before verb: 人皆讀書 (Everyone reads books)
>   - Conjunctions: 而, 則, 且 connect clauses
>   - Object fronting for emphasis: 詩三百 (The Odes, three hundred)

## 2. Pattern 1: Subject-Predicate (主述)

### 2.1 Adjective Predicates

The simplest statement: Subject + Adjective (no linking verb needed).

| Sentence | Reading | S | P (Adj) | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|---------|-------------|
| 天高 | 천고 | 天 | 高 | The sky is high |
| 地大 | 지대 | 地 | 大 | The earth is vast |
| 道遠 | 도원 | 道 | 遠 | The way is far |
| 人善 | 인선 | 人 | 善 | People are good |
| 水深 | 수심 | 水 | 深 | The water is deep |
| 德厚 | 덕후 | 德 | 厚 | Virtue is deep/thick |
| 事難 | 사난 | 事 | 難 | Matters are difficult |
| 時晚 | 시만 | 時 | 晚 | The time is late |

### 2.2 Verb Predicates (Intransitive)

Subject + Intransitive Verb:

| Sentence | Reading | S | V | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|---|-------------|
| 日出 | 일출 | 日 | 出 | The sun emerges (rises) |
| 月落 | 월락 | 月 | 落 | The moon sets (falls) |
| 花開 | 화개 | 花 | 開 | Flowers bloom (open) |
| 葉落 | 엽락 | 葉 | 落 | Leaves fall |
| 風起 | 풍기 | 風 | 起 | The wind rises |
| 雨降 | 우강 | 雨 | 降 | Rain falls |
| 鳥飛 | 조비 | 鳥 | 飛 | Birds fly |
| 人行 | 인행 | 人 | 行 | A person walks |

### 2.3 Extended Subject-Predicate

With compound subjects or predicates:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------|
| 天高地厚 | 천고지후 | Sky is high, earth is thick | Two parallel SP pairs |
| 春暖夏熱 | 춘난하열 | Spring is warm, summer is hot | Seasonal pairs |
| 日月光明 | 일월광명 | Sun and moon are bright | Compound subject |
| 山水秀麗 | 산수수려 | Mountains and water are beautiful | Compound subject |

## 3. Pattern 2: Subject-Verb-Object (主動賓)

### 3.1 Basic SVO

| Sentence | Reading | S | V | O | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|---|---|-------------|
| 孔子學禮 | 공자학례 | 孔子 | 學 | 禮 | Confucius studied rites |
| 王愛民 | 왕애민 | 王 | 愛 | 民 | The king loves the people |
| 人讀書 | 인독서 | 人 | 讀 | 書 | People read books |
| 民耕田 | 민경전 | 民 | 耕 | 田 | People plow fields |
| 師敎學 | 사교학 | 師 | 敎 | 學 | The teacher teaches learning |
| 子食飯 | 자식반 | 子 | 食 | 飯 | The child eats rice |
| 臣事君 | 신사군 | 臣 | 事 | 君 | The minister serves the ruler |
| 父敎子 | 부교자 | 父 | 敎 | 子 | The father teaches the child |

### 3.2 SVO with 之 as Object

When the object is a pronoun, 之 is used:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation | 之 Refers To |
|---------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 學而時習之 | 학이시습지 | Learn and practice it regularly | What was learned |
| 王聞之 | 왕문지 | The king heard it | The report/news |
| 人皆知之 | 인개지지 | Everyone knows it | The fact |
| 取之不竭 | 취지불갈 | Take from it and it is not exhausted | The source |

### 3.3 Object Fronting for Emphasis

In Classical Chinese, the object can be moved before the verb for emphasis:

| Normal Order | Fronted Order | Reading | Translation |
|-------------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| 讀詩三百 | 詩三百 吾讀之 | 시삼백 오독지 | The three hundred Odes — I have read them |
| 飮酒 | 酒 人皆飮之 | 주 인개음지 | Wine — everyone drinks it |

## 4. Pattern 3: Subject-Verb-Indirect Object-Direct Object

### 4.1 Double Object Verbs

Some verbs take two objects (indirect + direct), similar to English "give someone something":

| Sentence | Reading | S | V | IO | DO | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|---|----|----|-------------|
| 王賜臣馬 | 왕사신마 | 王 | 賜 | 臣 | 馬 | The king bestowed a horse on the minister |
| 師授子書 | 사수자서 | 師 | 授 | 子 | 書 | The teacher gave the student a book |
| 父與子田 | 부여자전 | 父 | 與 | 子 | 田 | The father gave the son a field |
| 君命臣事 | 군명신사 | 君 | 命 | 臣 | 事 | The ruler ordered the minister a task |

### 4.2 Common Double-Object Verbs

| Verb | 음독 | 훈독 | Pattern | Example |
|------|------|------|---------|---------|
| 賜 | 사 (sa) | 줄 사 | Bestow X on Y | 王賜民田 (king bestowed fields on people) |
| 授 | 수 (su) | 줄 수 | Teach/hand X to Y | 師授子經 (teacher taught student the classics) |
| 與 | 여 (yeo) | 줄 여 | Give X to Y | 母與子食 (mother gave child food) |
| 告 | 고 (go) | 알릴 고 | Tell Y about X | 臣告王事 (minister told king of the matter) |
| 問 | 문 (mun) | 물을 문 | Ask Y about X | 子問師道 (student asked teacher about the Way) |

## 5. Pattern 4: 者...也 Definitions

### 5.1 Full Pattern: A者 B也

The definitional pattern equates A with B:

| Sentence | Reading | A | B | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|---|-------------|
| 學者 覺也 | 학자 각야 | 學 (learning) | 覺 (awakening) | Learning is awakening |
| 仁者 愛人也 | 인자 애인야 | 仁 (benevolence) | 愛人 (loving people) | Benevolence is loving others |
| 義者 宜也 | 의자 의야 | 義 (righteousness) | 宜 (fitting) | Righteousness is what is fitting |
| 禮者 理也 | 례자 리야 | 禮 (rites) | 理 (principle) | Rites are principle |
| 信者 言之實也 | 신자 언지실야 | 信 (trust) | 言之實 (truth of words) | Trust is the truth in words |
| 孝者 德之本也 | 효자 덕지본야 | 孝 (filial piety) | 德之本 (root of virtue) | Filial piety is the root of virtue |

### 5.2 Variations

| Variation | Example | Reading | Translation |
|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|
| A B也 (without 者) | 仁 人心也 | 인 인심야 | Benevolence is the human heart |
| A者 B (without 也) | 仁者 愛人 | 인자 애인 | The benevolent one loves others |
| A者 B之C也 | 民者 國之本也 | 민자 국지본야 | The people are the nation's foundation |
| A者 B而C者也 | 君子者 學而不厭者也 | 군자자 학이불염자야 | A gentleman is one who studies without tiring |

## 6. Pattern 5: Existential Sentences with 有

### 6.1 有 (유) — "There is / There are"

有 (유, yu) creates existential sentences, stating that something exists:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 有人 | 유인 | There is a person |
| 有朋自遠方來 | 유붕자원방래 | There are friends coming from afar |
| 有子曰 | 유자왈 | Master You said... |
| 天下有道 | 천하유도 | There is the Way in the world |
| 國有法 | 국유법 | The country has laws |

### 6.2 有 with Subjects

| Sentence | Reading | S | 有 | O | Translation |
|----------|---------|---|----|---|-------------|
| 人有心 | 인유심 | 人 | 有 | 心 | People have hearts |
| 天有日月 | 천유일월 | 天 | 有 | 日月 | Heaven has sun and moon |
| 國有君臣 | 국유군신 | 國 | 有 | 君臣 | A country has ruler and ministers |
| 學有始終 | 학유시종 | 學 | 有 | 始終 | Learning has a beginning and end |
| 父子有親 | 부자유친 | 父子 | 有 | 親 | Father and son have affection |

## 7. Adverbial Modification

### 7.1 Time Adverbs

| Adverb | 음독 | 훈독 | Meaning | Example | Translation |
|--------|------|------|---------|---------|-------------|
| 常 | 상 (sang) | 항상 상 | Always | 王常愛民 | The king always loves the people |
| 時 | 시 (si) | 때 시 | Sometimes | 學而時習之 | Learn and practice it from time to time |
| 已 | 이 (i) | 이미 이 | Already | 已知之矣 | Already know it |
| 先 | 선 (seon) | 먼저 선 | First | 先學禮 | First study the rites |
| 後 | 후 (hu) | 뒤 후 | After | 後行之 | Afterward carry it out |
| 今 | 금 (geum) | 이제 금 | Now | 今人不學 | Now people do not study |

### 7.2 Degree Adverbs

| Adverb | 음독 | 훈독 | Meaning | Example | Translation |
|--------|------|------|---------|---------|-------------|
| 甚 | 심 (sim) | 심할 심 | Very | 德甚厚 | Virtue is very deep |
| 極 | 극 (geuk) | 다할 극 | Extremely | 水極深 | Water is extremely deep |
| 最 | 최 (choe) | 가장 최 | Most | 此最善也 | This is the best |
| 皆 | 개 (gae) | 다 개 | All | 人皆知之 | Everyone knows it |
| 必 | 필 (pil) | 반드시 필 | Certainly | 學必有師 | Study certainly requires a teacher |

### 7.3 Adverb Position

Adverbs always precede the verb or adjective they modify:

```
Subject + [Adverb] + Verb/Adjective + (Object)

人    皆    讀    書
(S)  (Adv)  (V)   (O)
People all  read  books
```

## 8. Compound and Complex Statements

### 8.1 Conjunction 而 (이, "and/but/then")

而 connects two clauses, indicating sequence, contrast, or condition:

| Sentence | Reading | 而 Function | Translation |
|----------|---------|------------|-------------|
| 學而時習之 | 학이시습지 | Sequence (and then) | Learn and then practice it |
| 人不知而不慍 | 인부지이불온 | Concession (yet) | Others do not understand, yet one is not resentful |
| 學而不思則罔 | 학이불사즉망 | Condition (but if) | Learn but do not think, and you will be lost |
| 父慈而子孝 | 부자이자효 | Parallel (and) | Father is kind and son is filial |

### 8.2 Conjunction 則 (즉, "then/in that case")

則 indicates a logical consequence:

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 學則知 | 학즉지 | If you study, then you know |
| 不學則不知 | 불학즉부지 | If you don't study, you won't know |
| 有德則有人 | 유덕즉유인 | If there is virtue, there are people (who follow) |

### 8.3 Conjunction 且 (차, "moreover/also")

| Sentence | Reading | Translation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 且學且問 | 차학차문 | Both study and ask questions |
| 不仁且不義 | 불인차불의 | Neither benevolent nor righteous |

## 9. Reading Practice: Classical Statements

### 9.1 From the Analects (論語)

**Passage 1:**
```
子曰 學而時習之 不亦說乎
有朋自遠方來 不亦樂乎
人不知而不慍 不亦君子乎
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 子曰 | 자왈 | The Master said: |
| 學而時習之 | 학이시습지 | To learn and from time to time practice it — |
| 不亦說乎 | 불역열호 | is this not also a pleasure? |
| 有朋自遠方來 | 유붕자원방래 | To have friends come from far away — |
| 不亦樂乎 | 불역락호 | is this not also a joy? |
| 人不知而不慍 | 인부지이불온 | Others do not understand me, yet I am not resentful — |
| 不亦君子乎 | 불역군자호 | is this not the mark of a gentleman? |

### 9.2 From the Great Learning (大學)

**Passage 2:**
```
大學之道 在明明德 在親民 在止於至善
```

| Phrase | Reading | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 大學之道 | 대학지도 | The way of the Great Learning |
| 在明明德 | 재명명덕 | lies in illuminating bright virtue, |
| 在親民 | 재친민 | in being close to the people, |
| 在止於至善 | 재지어지선 | in resting in the highest good. |

```
1. 人皆有心
2. 王者愛民

4. 父子有親 君臣有義
5. 天下有道 民不苦
```

