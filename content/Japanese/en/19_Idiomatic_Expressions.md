## 1. Body-Part Idioms (体の慣用句)

Japanese is rich in idioms that use body parts. These are called 体の慣用句 (からだのかんようく). The body part gives a clue to the general category of meaning.

```
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|          BODY-PART IDIOM CATEGORIES                       |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                          |
|  目 (eye)   → perception, judgment, attention            |
|  口 (mouth) → speech, personality, appetite              |
|  手 (hand)  → effort, skill, method                      |
|  足 (foot)  → movement, interference, foundation         |
|  耳 (ear)   → hearing, listening, obedience              |
|  頭 (head)  → intelligence, thinking                     |
|  首 (neck)  → dismissal, anticipation                    |
|  顔 (face)  → reputation, shame, familiarity             |
|  腹 (belly) → emotions, hidden feelings                  |
|  肩 (shoulder) → burden, rank, status                    |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
```

## 2. Eye Idioms (目)

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| 目が高い | めがたかい | Eyes are high | To have good taste / discerning eye |
| 目が回る | めがまわる | Eyes spin | To be extremely busy / dizzy |
| 目を通す | めをとおす | Pass eyes through | To look over, skim |
| 目に入る | めにはいる | Enters the eyes | To catch one's eye |
| 目をつぶる | めをつぶる | Close eyes | To turn a blind eye, overlook |
| 目を引く | めをひく | Pull the eyes | To attract attention, eye-catching |
| 目の前 | めのまえ | In front of the eyes | Right before one's eyes |
| 目が覚める | めがさめる | Eyes awaken | To wake up / to have a realization |
| 目を丸くする | めをまるくする | Make eyes round | To be wide-eyed with surprise |
| 白い目で見る | しろいめでみる | Look with white eyes | To look at with disapproval |

### Example Sentences

- さすが、**目が高い**ですね。この絵は本物です。
  (You have a discerning eye, as expected. This painting is authentic.)
- 年末は仕事で**目が回る**ほど忙しい。
  (At year-end, I'm so busy my head spins from work.)
- この書類に**目を通して**いただけますか。
  (Could you look over this document?)
- 彼の無礼な態度に**目をつぶる**わけにはいかない。
  (I can't turn a blind eye to his rude behavior.)

## 3. Mouth Idioms (口)

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| 口が軽い | くちがかるい | Mouth is light | Loose-lipped, can't keep secrets |
| 口が堅い | くちがかたい | Mouth is hard | Tight-lipped, can keep secrets |
| 口が悪い | くちがわるい | Mouth is bad | Sharp-tongued, speaks harshly |
| 口を出す | くちをだす | Put out the mouth | To butt in, interfere verbally |
| 口をそろえる | くちをそろえる | Align mouths | To say in unison, agree unanimously |
| 口に合う | くちにあう | Fits the mouth | To suit one's taste (food) |
| 口がうまい | くちがうまい | Mouth is skillful | Smooth-talking, silver-tongued |
| 口を滑らせる | くちをすべらせる | Mouth slips | To let slip, say by mistake |

### Example Sentences

- 彼女は**口が軽い**から、秘密を話さない方がいい。
  (She's loose-lipped, so you'd better not tell her secrets.)
- 人の問題に**口を出す**のはやめなさい。
  (Stop butting into other people's problems.)
- この料理は**口に合い**ますか。
  (Does this food suit your taste?)
- うっかり**口を滑らせて**、サプライズパーティーのことを言ってしまった。
  (I accidentally let slip about the surprise party.)

## 4. Hand Idioms (手)

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| 手を抜く | てをぬく | Pull out hands | To cut corners, do slipshod work |
| 手を貸す | てをかす | Lend a hand | To help, lend assistance |
| 手に入る | てにはいる | Enters the hand | To obtain, get hold of |
| 手がかかる | てがかかる | Hands are needed | To require effort / be troublesome |
| 手が出ない | てがでない | Hands don't come out | To be unable to afford / beyond reach |
| 手を打つ | てをうつ | Strike hands | To take measures / reach a deal |
| 手を焼く | てをやく | Burn hands | To have trouble dealing with |
| 手に負えない | てにおえない | Cannot carry in hands | Unmanageable, beyond control |
| 手も足も出ない | てもあしもでない | Neither hands nor feet come out | Completely helpless |
| 手をつける | てをつける | Put hands on | To start working on / to touch |

### Example Sentences

- この仕事は**手を抜かない**でください。
  (Please don't cut corners on this work.)
- 引っ越しの時、友達が**手を貸して**くれた。
  (My friend lent me a hand when I moved.)
- あのブランドのバッグは高くて**手が出ない**。
  (That brand's bags are so expensive I can't afford them.)
- この子は本当に**手がかかる**。
  (This child really requires a lot of effort.)

## 5. Foot/Leg Idioms (足)

| Idiom | Reading | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| 足を引っ張る | あしをひっぱる | Pull someone's leg | To hold someone back, drag down |
| 足が出る | あしがでる | Feet come out | To go over budget |
| 足を運ぶ | あしをはこぶ | Carry feet | To make the trip, go (respectful) |
| 足が棒になる | あしがぼうになる | Legs become sticks | To be dead tired from walking |
| 足元を見る | あしもとをみる | Look at someone's feet | To take advantage of someone's weakness |
| 二の足を踏む | にのあしをふむ | Step the second foot | To hesitate, have second thoughts |

### Example Sentences

- チームの**足を引っ張ら**ないように頑張る。
  (I'll work hard so I don't hold the team back.)
- 予算から**足が出て**しまった。
  (We went over budget.)
- ぜひ一度**足を運んで**みてください。
  (Please do make the trip to visit.)
- 一日中歩いて、**足が棒になった**。
  (I walked all day and my legs are dead tired.)

## 7. Onomatopoeia (オノマトペ)

Japanese has an extraordinarily rich onomatopoeia system. There are three types:

| Type | Japanese | Description | Example |
|------|----------|-------------|---------|
| 擬音語 (Giongo) | ぎおんご | Mimics actual sounds | ワンワン (dog bark), ザーザー (heavy rain) |
| 擬態語 (Gitaigo) | ぎたいご | Describes states/conditions | きらきら (sparkling), ぐちゃぐちゃ (messy) |
| 擬情語 (Gijougo) | ぎじょうご | Describes feelings | わくわく (excited), いらいら (irritated) |

### 7.1 Speaking and Communication

| Onomatopoeia | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| ぺらぺら | Fluently (speaking) | 日本語が**ぺらぺら**だ。 (Fluent in Japanese.) |
| ぶつぶつ | Mumbling, complaining | 何を**ぶつぶつ**言っているの？ (What are you mumbling?) |
| べらべら | Chattering non-stop | 秘密を**べらべら**話すな。 (Don't blabber secrets.) |
| ぼそぼそ | Speaking in a low mumble | **ぼそぼそ**話さないで。 (Don't mumble.) |
| はきはき | Speaking clearly | **はきはき**答えてください。 (Please answer clearly.) |

### 7.2 Emotions and Feelings

| Onomatopoeia | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| わくわく | Excited, thrilled | 旅行が**わくわく**する。 (I'm thrilled about the trip.) |
| どきどき | Heart pounding (nervous/excited) | 試験前に**どきどき**する。 (My heart pounds before exams.) |
| いらいら | Irritated, frustrated | 渋滞で**いらいら**する。 (Traffic jams make me irritated.) |
| うきうき | Cheerful, lighthearted | 春になると**うきうき**する。 (Spring makes me cheerful.) |
| びくびく | Fearful, nervous | 上司の前で**びくびく**している。 (Nervous in front of the boss.) |
| うんざり | Fed up, sick of | 同じ話に**うんざり**した。 (Fed up with the same story.) |
| しょんぼり | Dejected, downhearted | 試合に負けて**しょんぼり**している。 (Dejected after losing.) |
| にこにこ | Smiling happily | 子供が**にこにこ**笑っている。 (The child is smiling happily.) |

### 7.3 States and Conditions

| Onomatopoeia | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| ぐずぐず | Dawdling, procrastinating | **ぐずぐず**しないで！ (Stop dawdling!) |
| ぴかぴか | Shiny, sparkling clean | 床が**ぴかぴか**だ。 (The floor is sparkling clean.) |
| ぼろぼろ | Worn out, tattered | 靴が**ぼろぼろ**になった。 (Shoes became worn out.) |
| ふわふわ | Fluffy, soft | **ふわふわ**のタオル。 (A fluffy towel.) |
| ぎりぎり | Just barely, by the skin of one's teeth | **ぎりぎり**間に合った。 (Made it just barely in time.) |
| ばらばら | Scattered, in pieces | 意見が**ばらばら**だ。 (Opinions are all over the place.) |
| じめじめ | Damp, humid | 梅雨は**じめじめ**する。 (Rainy season is damp.) |

### 7.4 Actions and Movement

| Onomatopoeia | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| のろのろ | Slowly, sluggishly | **のろのろ**運転は危ない。 (Driving sluggishly is dangerous.) |
| すらすら | Smoothly, without hesitation | 日本語を**すらすら**読める。 (Can read Japanese smoothly.) |
| ごろごろ | Lazing around / rolling/rumbling | 休日は家で**ごろごろ**する。 (I laze around at home on holidays.) |
| うろうろ | Wandering aimlessly | 知らない町を**うろうろ**した。 (Wandered around an unfamiliar town.) |
| きちんと | Neatly, properly | 部屋を**きちんと**片付ける。 (Tidy the room properly.) |
| ぐっすり | Soundly (sleeping) | 昨夜は**ぐっすり**眠れた。 (I slept soundly last night.) |

### 7.5 Grammar of Onomatopoeia

| Usage | Pattern | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Adverb | Onomatopoeia + Verb | どきどき**する** (to feel nervous) |
| な-adjective | Onomatopoeia + な + Noun | ふわふわ**な**ケーキ (fluffy cake) |
| の-adjective | Onomatopoeia + の + Noun | ぴかぴか**の**車 (shiny car) |
| と-adverb | Onomatopoeia + と + Verb | にこにこ**と**笑う (smile happily) |

### Exercise 4: Situational Expression

Which idiom or expression would you use in each situation?

1. Your colleague always talks about confidential company information to outsiders.
2. You studied all day and your legs are exhausted from standing.
3. You want to tell someone you'll let go of past grudges.

### Exercise 5: Onomatopoeia Categories

Classify each onomatopoeia as 擬音語 (actual sound), 擬態語 (state/condition), or 擬情語 (feeling).

1. わくわく — excited
2. ワンワン — dog bark
3. ぴかぴか — sparkling
4. どきどき — heart pounding
5. ザーザー — heavy rain sound
6. ふわふわ — fluffy
7. いらいら — irritated
8. ごろごろ — thunder rumbling / lazing around

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **擬情語** (feeling) — describes the emotion of excitement
2. **擬音語** (sound) — mimics the sound a dog makes
3. **擬態語** (state) — describes the visual state of sparkling
4. **擬情語** (feeling) — describes the sensation of a pounding heart
5. **擬音語** (sound) — mimics the sound of heavy rain
6. **擬態語** (state) — describes the physical quality of fluffiness
7. **擬情語** (feeling) — describes the feeling of irritation
8. **擬音語** or **擬態語** — depends on context: rumbling sound (擬音語) or lazing around (擬態語)

</details>
