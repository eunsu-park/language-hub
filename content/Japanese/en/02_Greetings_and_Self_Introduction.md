## 1. Japanese Greetings: Overview

### 1.1 The Importance of Greetings in Japanese Culture

> **Greetings in Japanese Culture**
>
> Japanese greetings (挨拶 / あいさつ / aisatsu) are deeply
> embedded in daily life and social interactions.
>
> Key principles:
> - Greetings show respect and social awareness
> - The form of greeting depends on:
>   - Time of day
>   - Relationship (close friend vs boss vs stranger)
>   - Formality of the situation
>   - Whether meeting for the first time or regularly
> - Bowing (お辞儀 / おじぎ / ojigi) accompanies greetings
> - Japanese people greet more frequently than in Western culture
>   (e.g., greeting coworkers each morning is expected)

### 1.2 Bowing Basics

| Bow Type | Angle | When Used |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| 会釈 (eshaku) | ~15° | Casual greeting, passing someone |
| 敬礼 (keirei) | ~30° | Standard polite greeting |
| 最敬礼 (saikeirei) | ~45° | Deep respect, formal apology |

> **Note**: As a foreign learner, a slight nod or 15° bow is sufficient for most situations. The depth increases with formality.

## 2. Greetings by Time of Day

### 2.1 Morning, Afternoon, Evening

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning | Time | Level |
|----------|--------|---------|------|-------|
| おはようございます | ohayou gozaimasu | Good morning | Until ~10 AM | Polite |
| おはよう | ohayou | Morning! | Until ~10 AM | Casual |
| こんにちは | konnichiwa | Hello / Good afternoon | ~10 AM - ~6 PM | Neutral |
| こんばんは | konbanwa | Good evening | After ~6 PM | Neutral |

> **Timeline:**
> ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
> Morning          Afternoon         Evening/Night
> おはようございます  こんにちは         こんばんは
> (~until 10 AM)   (~10 AM - 6 PM)   (~after 6 PM)
> ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

> **Important spelling note**: こんにち**は** and こんばん**は** — the final character は is the particle は (read "wa"), NOT わ. This is a historical spelling convention.

### 2.2 Nighttime Expressions

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning | Usage |
|----------|--------|---------|-------|
| おやすみなさい | oyasuminasai | Good night | Polite, when someone goes to sleep |
| おやすみ | oyasumi | Night! | Casual, among friends/family |

## 3. Meeting and Parting Expressions

### 3.1 Farewell Expressions

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning | Level | Context |
|----------|--------|---------|-------|---------|
| さようなら | sayounara | Goodbye | Formal | Long separation, may not meet again soon |
| じゃあね | jaa ne | See ya | Casual | Friends, informal |
| じゃあ、また | jaa, mata | See you again | Casual | Friends |
| また明日（あした） | mata ashita | See you tomorrow | Neutral | Colleagues, classmates |
| お先に失礼します | osaki ni shitsurei shimasu | Excuse me for leaving first | Polite | Leaving work before others |
| お疲れ様でした | otsukaresama deshita | Thank you for your hard work | Polite | End of work/shared activity |
| 気をつけて | ki wo tsukete | Take care / Be careful | Neutral | Someone leaving on a trip |
| いってきます | ittekimasu | I'm leaving (and coming back) | Standard | Leaving home |
| いってらっしゃい | itterasshai | Go and come back safely | Standard | Response to いってきます |
| ただいま | tadaima | I'm home! | Standard | Arriving home |
| おかえりなさい | okaerinasai | Welcome back! | Standard | Response to ただいま |

### 3.2 Home Departure Ritual

This is a uniquely Japanese daily ritual:

```
Person leaving:    いってきます！ (Ittekimasu!)
                   "I'll go and come back!"

Person staying:    いってらっしゃい！ (Itterasshai!)
                   "Go and come back safely!"

  --- Later ---

Person returning:  ただいま！ (Tadaima!)
                   "I'm home!"

Person at home:    おかえりなさい！ (Okaerinasai!)
                   "Welcome back!"
```

### 3.3 First Meeting Expressions

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|----------|--------|---------|
| はじめまして | hajimemashite | Nice to meet you (for the first time) |
| どうぞよろしくおねがいします | douzo yoroshiku onegaishimasu | Please treat me favorably (full polite) |
| よろしくおねがいします | yoroshiku onegaishimasu | Nice to meet you / Please (polite) |
| よろしく | yoroshiku | Nice to meet you (casual) |

> **Cultural note**: はじめまして is ONLY used the first time you meet someone. Using it with someone you have already met is awkward.

## 4. Self-Introduction Pattern

### 4.1 The Basic Self-Introduction (自己紹介 / じこしょうかい / jikoshoukai)

A standard Japanese self-introduction follows a predictable pattern:

> **Basic Self-Introduction Template**
>
> はじめまして。
> (Hajimemashite.)
> "Nice to meet you."
>
> [名前]と申します / [名前]です。
> ([Name] to moushimasu / [Name] desu.)
> "My name is [Name]." / "I am [Name]."
>
> [国]から来ました。
> ([Country] kara kimashita.)
> "I came from [Country]."
>
> [職業/身分]です。
> ([Occupation/Status] desu.)
> "I am a [occupation]."
>
> [年齢]歳です。
> ([Age]-sai desu.)
> "I am [age] years old."
>
> どうぞよろしくおねがいします。
> (Douzo yoroshiku onegaishimasu.)
> "Please treat me well."

### 4.2 Key Patterns

#### Pattern 1: 私は___です。(Watashi wa ___ desu.)

"I am ___."

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 私はキムです。 | Watashi wa Kimu desu. | I am Kim. |
| 私は学生です。 | Watashi wa gakusei desu. | I am a student. |
| 私は韓国人です。 | Watashi wa kankokujin desu. | I am Korean. |
| 私は二十五歳です。 | Watashi wa nijuugo-sai desu. | I am 25 years old. |

#### Pattern 2: ___から来ました。(___ kara kimashita.)

"I came from ___."

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 韓国から来ました。 | Kankoku kara kimashita. | I came from Korea. |
| アメリカから来ました。 | Amerika kara kimashita. | I came from America. |
| ソウルから来ました。 | Souru kara kimashita. | I came from Seoul. |
| 東京から来ました。 | Toukyou kara kimashita. | I came from Tokyo. |

#### Pattern 3: ___歳です。(___-sai desu.)

"I am ___ years old."

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 二十歳です。 | hatachi desu. | I am 20 years old. (special reading!) |
| 二十五歳です。 | nijuugo-sai desu. | I am 25 years old. |
| 三十歳です。 | sanjussai desu. | I am 30 years old. |

| Country (Japanese) | Romaji | Country (English) | Nationality |
|--------------------|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| 日本 (にほん) | Nihon | Japan | 日本人 (nihonjin) |
| 韓国 (かんこく) | Kankoku | South Korea | 韓国人 (kankokujin) |
| アメリカ | Amerika | America/USA | アメリカ人 (amerikajin) |
| イギリス | Igirisu | England/UK | イギリス人 (igirisujin) |
| フランス | Furansu | France | フランス人 (furansujin) |
| ドイツ | Doitsu | Germany | ドイツ人 (doitsujin) |
| 中国 (ちゅうごく) | Chuugoku | China | 中国人 (chuugokujin) |

| カナダ | Kanada | Canada | カナダ人 (kanadajin) |

### 4.4 Common Occupations

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 学生 (がくせい) | gakusei | student |
| 会社員 (かいしゃいん) | kaishain | office worker |

```
I am an engineer.
I am 28 years old.
I am studying Japanese.
Please treat me well."
```

## 5. The です/ます Polite Form

### 5.1 What is です/ます Form?

> **です/ます — The Polite Form**
>
> - **です (desu)**: polite copula ("is/am/are")
> - **ます (masu)**: polite verb ending
>
> This is the STANDARD form for:
> - Speaking to strangers
> - Speaking to colleagues
> - Speaking to anyone you don't know well
> - Any situation where you want to be polite
>
> As a beginner, ALWAYS use です/ます form.
> It is never wrong to be polite in Japanese.

### 5.2 です (desu) — The Polite Copula

| Form | Japanese | Romaji | English |
|------|----------|--------|---------|
| Present affirmative | ___です | ___ desu | ___ is / I am ___ |
| Present negative | ___ではありません | ___ dewa arimasen | ___ is not |
| Past affirmative | ___でした | ___ deshita | ___ was |
| Past negative | ___ではありませんでした | ___ dewa arimasen deshita | ___ was not |

**Examples**:

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| 学生です。 | Gakusei desu. | I am a student. |
| 学生ではありません。 | Gakusei dewa arimasen. | I am not a student. |
| 学生でした。 | Gakusei deshita. | I was a student. |
| 学生ではありませんでした。 | Gakusei dewa arimasen deshita. | I was not a student. |

> **Shortcut**: ではありません is often shortened to じゃありません (ja arimasen) in spoken Japanese. Both are polite.

### 5.3 ます (masu) — Polite Verb Ending

| Form | Ending | Example (食べる → ) | Romaji |
|------|--------|---------------------|--------|
| Present affirmative | ます | 食べます | tabemasu (eat/will eat) |
| Present negative | ません | 食べません | tabemasen (don't eat) |
| Past affirmative | ました | 食べました | tabemashita (ate) |
| Past negative | ませんでした | 食べませんでした | tabemasen deshita (didn't eat) |

## 6. Politeness Levels: Casual vs Polite

### 6.1 Two Main Speech Levels for Beginners

| Level | Name | Used With | Verb Example | Copula |
|-------|------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Polite | 丁寧語 (teineigo) | Strangers, seniors, colleagues, formal situations | 食べます (tabemasu) | です (desu) |
| Casual/Plain | 普通体 (futsuu-tai) | Close friends, family, people younger than you | 食べる (taberu) | だ (da) |

### 6.2 Comparison Table

| English | Polite (です/ます) | Casual (plain) |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| I am a student. | 学生です。(Gakusei desu.) | 学生だ。(Gakusei da.) |
| I eat sushi. | すしを食べます。(Sushi wo tabemasu.) | すしを食べる。(Sushi wo taberu.) |
| It is delicious. | おいしいです。(Oishii desu.) | おいしい。(Oishii.) |
| I don't understand. | わかりません。(Wakarimasen.) | わからない。(Wakaranai.) |

### 6.3 When to Use Each Level

```
Use POLITE (です/ます) when speaking to:
  ✓ People you meet for the first time
  ✓ People older than you
  ✓ Teachers, bosses, customers
  ✓ Strangers
  ✓ In any situation you're unsure about

Use CASUAL (plain form) when speaking to:
  ✓ Close friends
  ✓ Family members (usually)
  ✓ People clearly younger than you (with care)
  ✓ Children

Golden rule: When in doubt, use polite form.
```

> **For this course**: We will primarily use the polite (です/ます) form. Plain form will be introduced gradually.

## 7. Essential Polite Expressions

### 7.1 Thank You

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning | Level |
|----------|--------|---------|-------|
| ありがとうございます | arigatou gozaimasu | Thank you (very much) | Polite |
| ありがとう | arigatou | Thanks | Casual |
| どうも | doumo | Thanks (quick/informal) | Very casual |
| どうもありがとうございます | doumo arigatou gozaimasu | Thank you very much indeed | Very polite |

### 7.2 Excuse Me / Sorry

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning | Usage |
|----------|--------|---------|-------|
| すみません | sumimasen | Excuse me / I'm sorry | Getting attention, light apology |
| ごめんなさい | gomen nasai | I'm sorry | Apology (polite) |
| ごめん | gomen | Sorry | Apology (casual) |
| しつれいします | shitsurei shimasu | Excuse me (for being rude) | Entering/leaving a room, hanging up phone |

### 7.3 Please / You're Welcome

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning |
|----------|--------|---------|
| おねがいします | onegaishimasu | Please (when requesting) |
| ください | kudasai | Please give me / Please do |
| どういたしまして | dou itashimashite | You're welcome |
| いいえ | iie | No / Not at all (in response to thanks) |

### 7.4 Eating Expressions

| Japanese | Romaji | Meaning | When |
|----------|--------|---------|------|
| いただきます | itadakimasu | I humbly receive (this food) | Before eating |
| ごちそうさまでした | gochisousama deshita | Thank you for the meal | After eating |

> **Cultural note**: いただきます and ごちそうさまでした are said every time, even when eating alone. They express gratitude for the food.

## 9. Cultural Notes on Greetings

### 9.1 Business Card Exchange (名刺交換 / めいしこうかん)

> **Business Card Exchange Etiquette**
>
> 1. Hold your card with both hands, text facing the recipient
> 2. Present at chest height with a slight bow
> 3. Receive the other person's card with both hands
> 4. Read it carefully — never write on it or put it in a pocket
> 5. Place it on the table during the meeting
> 6. Store it respectfully in a card case afterward
>
> Say: "頂戴いたします" (choudai itashimasu)
> = "I humbly receive this" when accepting

### 9.2 Seasonal Greetings

Japanese culture places great importance on acknowledging the seasons:

| Period | Greeting | Romaji | Meaning |
|--------|----------|--------|---------|
| New Year | あけましておめでとうございます | Akemashite omedetou gozaimasu | Happy New Year |
| After long absence | おひさしぶりです | Ohisashiburi desu | It's been a long time |
| Summer | 暑いですね | Atsui desu ne | It's hot, isn't it |
| Winter | 寒いですね | Samui desu ne | It's cold, isn't it |

### 9.3 The Role of あいづち (Aizuchi) — Active Listening

Japanese conversation relies heavily on **aizuchi** — verbal feedback showing you are listening:

| Aizuchi | Romaji | Meaning |
|---------|--------|---------|
| はい | hai | Yes / I see |
| ええ | ee | Yes (softer) |
| そうですか | sou desu ka | Is that so? |
| なるほど | naruhodo | I see / Indeed |
| へえ | hee | Oh really? (surprise) |
| うん | un | Yeah (casual) |

> **Important**: Not giving aizuchi makes Japanese speakers uncomfortable — they may think you are not listening or don't understand. Nod and respond frequently.

```
B: こちらこそ、よろしくおねがいします。
   (Kochira koso, yoroshiku onegaishimasu.)
   "Likewise, nice to meet you too."
```

Note: こちらこそ (kochira koso) means "likewise" or "it is I who should say that."

</details>
