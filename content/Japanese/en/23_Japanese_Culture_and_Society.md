## 1. Traditional Arts (伝統芸術)

### 1.1 Tea Ceremony (茶道 / さどう)

The tea ceremony, also called 茶の湯 (ちゃのゆ), embodies the aesthetic philosophy of
一期一会 (いちごいちえ — "one time, one meeting"), treating each encounter as a
once-in-a-lifetime event.

> **TEA CEREMONY OVERVIEW (茶道)**
>
> - **Philosophy**: 和敬清寂 (わけいせいじゃく)
> - 和 (wa) — Harmony
> - 敬 (kei) — Respect
> - 清 (sei) — Purity
> - 寂 (jaku) — Tranquility
>
> **Major Schools**:
> - 表千家 (おもてせんけ) — Omotesenke
> - 裏千家 (うらせんけ) — Urasenke (most common)
> - 武者小路千家 (むしゃこうじせんけ)
>
> **Key Equipment**:
> - 茶碗 (ちゃわん) — Tea bowl
> - 茶筅 (ちゃせん) — Bamboo whisk
> - 茶杓 (ちゃしゃく) — Tea scoop
> - 棗 (なつめ) — Tea caddy
> - 釜 (かま) — Iron kettle
>
> **Types of Tea**:
> - 薄茶 (うすちゃ) — Thin tea (everyday)
> - 濃茶 (こいちゃ) — Thick tea (formal)

**Basic tea ceremony etiquette for guests**:

| Step | Action | Japanese |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Bow when entering the room | お辞儀をして入室する |
| 2 | Admire the hanging scroll (掛軸) | 掛軸を拝見する |
| 3 | Take your seat in proper order | 席順に座る |
| 4 | When served sweets, say thank you | 「お先に」「頂戴いたします」 |
| 5 | Eat the sweet before the tea arrives | お菓子を先にいただく |
| 6 | When tea is placed before you | 「お点前、頂戴いたします」 |
| 7 | Turn the bowl clockwise twice | 茶碗を右に2回まわす |
| 8 | Drink in a few sips | 数口で飲む |
| 9 | Wipe the rim where you drank | 飲み口を拭く |
| 10 | Turn the bowl counterclockwise twice | 左に2回まわして戻す |

### 1.2 Flower Arrangement (華道 / かどう)

Also called 生け花 (いけばな), flower arrangement is an art of expressing nature's beauty
through carefully composed arrangements.

| School | Japanese | Characteristics |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Ikenobo | 池坊（いけのぼう） | Oldest school (550+ years), formal, classical |
| Ohara | 小原流（おはらりゅう） | Known for moribana (flat container) style |
| Sogetsu | 草月流（そうげつりゅう） | Modern, avant-garde, uses non-plant materials |

**Core principles**:
- 天・地・人 (てん・ち・じん) — Heaven, Earth, Human: the three main stems representing the
  cosmic order
- 間 (ま) — Negative space is as important as the flowers themselves
- Seasonal flowers should reflect the current season

## 4. Social Customs (社会慣習)

### 4.1 Bowing (お辞儀 / おじぎ)

> **TYPES OF BOWS**
>
> 会釈 (えしゃく) — Slight bow (15°)
> - Casual greeting, passing colleagues
> - Quick nod of acknowledgment
>
> 敬礼 (けいれい) — Standard bow (30°)
> - General greeting, meeting clients
> - Most common business bow
>
> 最敬礼 (さいけいれい) — Deep bow (45°)
> - Sincere apology, deep gratitude
> - Meeting someone very important
>
> **Rules**:
> - Back straight, bend from the waist
> - Hands at sides (men) or overlapping in front (women)
> - Eye contact before and after, not during
> - **• Duration**: 1-3 seconds depending on depth
> - The lower-ranking person bows deeper and longer

### 4.2 Gift-Giving (贈り物 / おくりもの)

Gift-giving in Japan is highly ritualized with specific seasonal obligations:

| Occasion | Japanese | When | What |
|----------|----------|------|------|
| Summer gift | お中元 (おちゅうげん) | July-August | Food, drinks, household items |
| Year-end gift | お歳暮 (おせいぼ) | December | Food, drinks, household items |
| Souvenir | お土産 (おみやげ) | After travel | Local specialties of the visited place |
| Hospital visit | お見舞い (おみまい) | When someone is ill | Flowers (not potted!), fruit |
| Housewarming | 新築祝い (しんちくいわい) | When moving | Practical items |
| Wedding gift | ご祝儀 (ごしゅうぎ) | At wedding | Money in special envelope |
| Funeral | 香典 (こうでん) | At funeral | Money in black-and-white envelope |

**Gift-giving rules**:

| Rule | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| Wrapping matters | Use 風呂敷 (furoshiki) or proper wrapping paper |
| Present with both hands | Show respect by using both hands |
| Decline once or twice | The receiver may initially refuse — offer again |
| Say 「つまらないものですが」 | "It's a trivial thing, but..." (humble deflection) |
| Avoid sets of 4 | 四 (し) = death (死). Give sets of 3 or 5 instead |
| Don't open immediately | Wait until the giver leaves (unlike Western culture) |
| Return the favor (お返し) | Return roughly half the value (半返し) |

### 4.3 Visiting Etiquette

| Step | Action | Japanese Phrase |
|------|--------|----------------|
| At the door | Ring the bell, step back | — |
| When greeted | Announce yourself | ○○です。お邪魔いたします。 |
| Entering | Remove shoes at the genkan | 「失礼します」 |
| Shoes | Point them toward the door | — |
| If offered slippers | Put them on | 「ありがとうございます」 |
| Entering tatami room | Remove slippers (socks only) | — |
| Seated | Wait to be told where to sit | — |
| Offered tea | Accept graciously | 「いただきます」 |
| Leaving | Thank the host | 「お邪魔しました。ありがとうございました。」 |

## 5. Seasons and Annual Events (年中行事)

### 5.1 The Four Seasons in Japanese Culture

Japan's cultural identity is deeply intertwined with the four seasons (四季 / しき).
Seasonal awareness (季節感 / きせつかん) permeates language, food, fashion, and daily life.

> **THE JAPANESE YEAR**
>
> 春 (はる) — Spring (March - May)
> - 花見 (はなみ) — Cherry blossom viewing
> - 入学式 (にゅうがくしき) — School entrance ceremony
> - ゴールデンウィーク — Golden Week holidays
>
> 夏 (なつ) — Summer (June - August)
> - 梅雨 (つゆ) — Rainy season
> - 花火大会 (はなびたいかい) — Fireworks festivals
> - お盆 (おぼん) — Festival of the Dead
> - 夏祭り (なつまつり) — Summer festivals
>
> 秋 (あき) — Autumn (September - November)
> - 紅葉 (こうよう) — Autumn leaf viewing
> - 七五三 (しちごさん) — Children's growth celebration
> - 運動会 (うんどうかい) — Sports day
>
> 冬 (ふゆ) — Winter (December - February)
> - 大掃除 (おおそうじ) — Year-end cleaning
> - 正月 (しょうがつ) — New Year
> - 節分 (せつぶん) — Bean-throwing day
> - バレンタインデー — Valentine's Day

### 5.2 Major Annual Events

| Date | Event | Japanese | Description |
|------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Jan 1-3 | New Year | 正月（しょうがつ） | Most important holiday. Family gatherings, shrine visits (初詣). |
| Jan (2nd Mon) | Coming of Age | 成人の日（せいじんのひ） | Celebration for those turning 20 (now 18). |
| Feb 3 | Bean Throwing | 節分（せつぶん） | Throw beans shouting 「鬼は外！福は内！」 |
| Feb 14 | Valentine's Day | バレンタインデー | Women give chocolate to men (義理チョコ or 本命チョコ). |
| Mar 3 | Doll Festival | ひな祭り（ひなまつり） | Girls' Day — display traditional dolls. |
| Mar 14 | White Day | ホワイトデー | Men return gifts to women (Japanese custom). |
| Late Mar-Apr | Cherry Blossoms | 花見（はなみ） | Picnic under cherry blossoms with food and drink. |
| May 5 | Children's Day | こどもの日 | Originally Boys' Day. Fly carp streamers (鯉のぼり). |
| Jul 7 | Star Festival | 七夕（たなばた） | Write wishes on paper strips, hang on bamboo. |
| Aug 13-16 | Bon Festival | お盆（おぼん） | Honor ancestors, many return to hometowns (帰省). |
| Nov 15 | 7-5-3 Festival | 七五三（しちごさん） | Shrine visit for children ages 7, 5, and 3. |
| Dec 25 | Christmas | クリスマス | Celebrated commercially (not religious). KFC tradition. |
| Dec 31 | New Year's Eve | 大晦日（おおみそか） | Eat year-crossing soba (年越しそば), watch 紅白歌合戦. |

## 7. Religion and Philosophy

### 7.1 Shinto and Buddhism Coexistence

Japan uniquely blends two major religious traditions:

> **SHINTO (神道) vs BUDDHISM (仏教)**
>
> **神道 (Shinto)**:
> - Indigenous Japanese religion
> - **Focus**: Nature spirits (神 / かみ)
> - **Place of worship**: 神社 (じんじゃ / shrine)
> - **Gate**: 鳥居 (とりい / torii)
> - **Rituals**: 祭り, お祓い, 初詣
> - **Life events**: Birth, marriage, festivals
>
> **仏教 (Buddhism)**:
> - Arrived from China/Korea (6th century)
> - **Focus**: Enlightenment, cycles of rebirth
> - **Place of worship**: 寺 (てら / temple)
> - **Gate**: 山門 (さんもん)
> - **Rituals**: Meditation, sutra chanting
> - **Life events**: Funerals, memorial services
>
> - **Common saying**: 「日本人は生まれたときは神道、
> 死んだときは仏教」
> (Japanese are Shinto at birth, Buddhist at death.)

### 7.2 Shrine vs Temple Etiquette

| Action | Shrine (神社) | Temple (寺) |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Entrance | Pass through 鳥居, bow | Enter through 山門 |
| Purification | 手水舎: Wash left hand, right hand, mouth | Same (if available) |
| Worship | 二拝二拍手一拝 (2 bows, 2 claps, 1 bow) | Bow, hands together silently (no clapping) |
| Offering | Toss coin, ring bell | Toss coin, ring bell |
| Fortune | おみくじ (fortune slip) | おみくじ |

## 8. Food Culture (食文化)

### 8.1 Dining Etiquette

| Rule | Japanese | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Before eating | 「いただきます」 | "I humbly receive" — said with palms together |
| After eating | 「ごちそうさまでした」 | "It was a feast" — expressing gratitude |
| Chopstick taboos | 箸のマナー | See below |
| Pouring drinks | お酌（おしゃく） | Pour for others; let them pour for you |
| Slurping noodles | ○ OK | Shows you enjoy the food (ramen, soba, udon) |
| Tipping | ✗ Never | Considered rude in Japan |

### 8.2 Chopstick Taboos (箸のマナー違反)

| Taboo | Japanese | Why |
|-------|----------|-----|
| Sticking chopsticks upright in rice | 立て箸（たてばし） | Resembles funeral incense offering |
| Passing food chopstick to chopstick | 箸渡し（はしわたし） | Resembles bone-picking funeral ritual |
| Pointing with chopsticks | 指し箸（さしばし） | Rude |
| Hovering over dishes indecisively | 迷い箸（まよいばし） | Shows indecision |
| Licking chopsticks | ねぶり箸 | Unsanitary, impolite |
| Pulling a dish closer with chopsticks | 寄せ箸（よせばし） | Use hands to move dishes |

### 8.3 Seasonal Foods

| Season | Foods | Japanese |

| Spring | Bamboo shoots, cherry blossom mochi | 筍（たけのこ）、桜餅 |
| Summer | Cold noodles, shaved ice, eel | そうめん、かき氷、鰻（うなぎ） |
| Autumn | Mushrooms, sweet potatoes, chestnuts | きのこ、さつまいも、栗（くり） |
| Winter | Hot pot, oden, mandarin oranges | 鍋（なべ）、おでん、みかん |

<summary>Answers</summary>

1. それ、すごく面白いじゃない。(That's really funny, isn't it.)
2. いくらするの？(How much is it?)
3. そんなのだめでしょう。(That's no good, right.)
4. 本当に違うよ。(It's really different / wrong.)

</details>

### Exercise 4: Gift-Giving Scenarios

What would be the appropriate action in each situation?

1. You are visiting a Japanese colleague's home for the first time. What do you bring and what do you say?
2. You received an お中元 worth about 5,000 yen. What should you do?
3. Your Japanese friend is in the hospital. What should you avoid bringing?
4. You are attending a Japanese wedding. What do you bring?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Bring a small, nicely wrapped gift (sweets, fruit, or something from your hometown). Say 「つまらないものですが、どうぞ」 or 「ほんの気持ちですが」. Present with both hands.

2. Send a thank-you note (お礼状) and consider sending a return gift (お返し) worth about half the value (半返し — approximately 2,500 yen).

3. Avoid potted plants (鉢植え — suggests "putting down roots" in the hospital, i.e., long stay). Also avoid flowers with strong scents, and sets of 4 (四 = 死 = death).

4. Bring ご祝儀 (monetary gift) in a special 祝儀袋 (celebration envelope) with red-and-gold decorations. The amount should be an odd number (奇数): 30,000 yen is standard for friends, 50,000 for close relatives. Never give 40,000 (四 = death) or 20,000 (even number = divisible = separation).

</details>

### Exercise 5: Cultural Essay

Write a 5-6 sentence paragraph in Japanese (常体 or 敬体) explaining one aspect of Japanese culture that interests you. Use at least 3 vocabulary items from this lesson.

<details>
<summary>Sample Answer</summary>

日本の食文化の中で、特に興味深いのは季節感との深い結びつきである。
日本では、春には桜餅や筍を楽しみ、夏にはそうめんやかき氷で涼を取る。
秋には栗やきのこが食卓に並び、冬には鍋料理で体を温める。
このように、食を通じて四季の移り変わりを感じることは、
日本文化の大きな特徴の一つである。
「いただきます」という食前の挨拶も、
自然の恵みへの感謝を表す日本独特の習慣だと言える。

</details>
