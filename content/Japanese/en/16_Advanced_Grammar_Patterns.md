## 1. ～ばかり — "Just Did" / "Only"

～ばかり has two distinct meanings depending on the form it attaches to.

### 1.1 Meaning A: "Just Did" (たばかり)

Expresses that an action was **recently completed**. The result or state is still fresh.

**Formation:**

```
Verb (た-form) + ばかり + だ / です
```

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 日本に来た**ばかり**です。 | I just came to Japan. |
| さっき食べた**ばかり**なのに、もうお腹がすいた。 | I just ate, but I'm already hungry. |
| 新しいパソコンを買った**ばかり**だ。 | I just bought a new computer. |
| 起きた**ばかり**で、まだ眠い。 | I just woke up, so I'm still sleepy. |

> **Note:** The time gap between the action and the present is subjective. Even hours
> or days ago can be expressed with たばかり if the speaker perceives it as recent.

### 1.2 Meaning B: "Only / Nothing But" (ばかり)

Emphasizes exclusivity or excess — doing **nothing but** one thing, or having **only** one thing.

**Formation:**

```
Verb (て-form) + ばかりいる         → keeps doing nothing but ~
Noun + ばかり                      → nothing but ~
Verb (dictionary form) + ばかり     → only does ~
```

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 彼はゲームをして**ばかり**いる。 | He does nothing but play games. |
| 甘いもの**ばかり**食べている。 | She eats nothing but sweets. |
| 文句**ばかり**言うな。 | Stop doing nothing but complaining. |
| 最近、悪いこと**ばかり**起きる。 | Lately, nothing but bad things happen. |

### 1.3 Key Distinction

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Vた + ばかり | "just did" | 始めたばかり (just started) |
| Vて + ばかりいる | "nothing but" | 遊んでばかりいる (does nothing but play) |
| Noun + ばかり | "only / nothing but" | 嘘ばかり (nothing but lies) |

## 2. ～っぽい — "Seems Like / -ish"

Attaches to nouns, verbs, and adjectives to express a tendency, resemblance, or impression.

**Formation:**

```
Noun + っぽい      → "-ish", "like a ~"
Verb stem + っぽい  → "tend to ~"
い-adj stem + っぽい → "somewhat ~"
```

> **Conjugation:** っぽい conjugates as an い-adjective:
> っぽくない (negative), っぽかった (past), っぽくて (て-form)

### 2.1 With Nouns — "Like ~, -ish"

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 子供**っぽい** | childish |
| 男**っぽい** | manly, masculine |
| 女**っぽい** | feminine, womanly |
| 水**っぽい** | watery (e.g., soup that's too thin) |
| 埃（ほこり）**っぽい** | dusty |
| 安**っぽい** | cheap-looking |

### 2.2 With Verb Stems — "Tend To ~"

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 忘れ**っぽい** | forgetful (tends to forget) |
| 怒り**っぽい** | short-tempered (tends to get angry) |
| 飽き**っぽい** | fickle (tends to get bored) |
| 惚れ**っぽい** | easily smitten |

### 2.3 With Adjective Stems

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 白**っぽい** | whitish |
| 黒**っぽい** | blackish |
| 赤**っぽい** | reddish |

### 2.4 っぽい vs. らしい vs. みたい

| Pattern | Nuance | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| っぽい | Has the quality of; subjective impression | 子供っぽい (childish — negative) |
| らしい | Befitting, proper to; or hearsay | 子供らしい (child-like — positive) |
| みたい | Looks like, appears to be | 子供みたい (like a child — neutral) |

> **子供っぽい** = acting immature (negative nuance).
> **子供らしい** = acting naturally like a child should (positive nuance).
> **子供みたい** = resembling a child (neutral observation).

## 3. ～がち — "Tend To"

Describes a **negative tendency** or something that happens **more often than desirable**.

**Formation:**

```
Verb (ます stem) + がち
Noun + がち
```

> **Conjugation:** がち behaves as a な-adjective:
> がちな + Noun, がちに + Verb, がちだ / がちです

### 3.1 Common Examples

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 休み**がち** | tends to be absent |
| 忘れ**がち** | tends to forget |
| 遅れ**がち** | tends to be late |
| 曇り**がち** | tends to be cloudy |
| 病気**がち** | tends to be sickly |
| 留守**がち** | tends to be away from home |

### 3.2 Example Sentences

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 最近、彼は学校を休み**がち**だ。 | Lately, he tends to be absent from school. |
| 冬は風邪をひき**がち**です。 | In winter, one tends to catch colds. |
| 忙しいと、食事を抜き**がち**になる。 | When busy, I tend to skip meals. |
| この地域は曇り**がち**な天気が続く。 | This region tends to have cloudy weather. |
| 一人暮らしは栄養が偏り**がち**だ。 | Living alone, nutrition tends to be unbalanced. |

### 3.3 がち vs. っぽい

| Pattern | Nuance |
|---------|--------|
| がち | Habitual negative tendency; objective observation |
| っぽい | Quality / impression; subjective feeling |

- 忘れ**がち** = tends to forget (it actually happens often)
- 忘れ**っぽい** = is forgetful (personality trait / impression)

## 4. ～気味 — "Slightly / A Touch Of"

Indicates a **slight degree** of something, often an undesirable condition.

**Formation:**

```
Verb (ます stem) + 気味（ぎみ）
Noun + 気味
```

> 気味 is read **ぎみ** (not きみ) when used as a suffix.

### 4.1 Common Examples

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 風邪**気味** | a touch of a cold |
| 太り**気味** | slightly overweight |
| 疲れ**気味** | slightly tired |
| 遅れ**気味** | running a bit late/behind |
| 下がり**気味** | trending slightly downward |

### 4.2 Example Sentences

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 今日は風邪**気味**なので、早く帰ります。 | I have a touch of a cold today, so I'll go home early. |
| 最近、太り**気味**だから、運動を始めた。 | I've been gaining a bit of weight, so I started exercising. |
| プロジェクトが遅れ**気味**です。 | The project is running a bit behind schedule. |
| 彼は最近、疲れ**気味**に見える。 | He looks a bit tired lately. |
| 株価は下がり**気味**だ。 | Stock prices are trending slightly downward. |

### 4.3 気味 vs. がち

| Pattern | Degree | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| 気味 | Slight, mild | 風邪気味 (a touch of a cold) |
| がち | Habitual tendency | 病気がち (tends to be sickly) |

## 5. ～ずに — "Without Doing"

Expresses doing something **without** performing another action. It is the literary equivalent of ～ないで.

**Formation:**

```
Verb (ない stem) + ずに
Exception: する → せずに
```

### 5.1 Conjugation

| Dictionary Form | ない-form | ～ずに Form |
|----------------|-----------|------------|
| 食べる (to eat) | 食べない | 食べ**ずに** |
| 行く (to go) | 行かない | 行か**ずに** |
| 読む (to read) | 読まない | 読ま**ずに** |
| する (to do) | しない | **せずに** |
| 見る (to see) | 見ない | 見**ずに** |
| 寝る (to sleep) | 寝ない | 寝**ずに** |

> **Important:** する becomes **せずに**, not しずに. This is the most common irregularity.

### 5.2 Example Sentences

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 朝ご飯を食べ**ずに**学校へ行った。 | I went to school without eating breakfast. |
| 辞書を使わ**ずに**読めた。 | I was able to read it without using a dictionary. |
| 何も言わ**ずに**帰った。 | He left without saying anything. |
| 予習**せずに**授業に出た。 | I attended class without preparing. |
| 諦め**ずに**頑張ろう。 | Let's keep trying without giving up. |

### 5.3 ～ずに vs. ～ないで

| Pattern | Register | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| ～ずに | Written / formal | 食べずに出かけた |
| ～ないで | Spoken / casual | 食べないで出かけた |

Both mean the same thing, but ～ずに is preferred in writing, newspapers, and formal speech.

## 6. ～にもかかわらず — "Despite / In Spite Of"

A formal expression meaning **"despite"** or **"in spite of."** It highlights a **contradiction** between two facts.

**Formation:**

```
Verb (plain form)     + にもかかわらず
い-adjective           + にもかかわらず
な-adjective + な/である + にもかかわらず
Noun + である/な        + にもかかわらず
```

### 6.1 Example Sentences

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 雨が降っている**にもかかわらず**、試合は続行された。 | Despite the rain, the match continued. |
| 何度も練習した**にもかかわらず**、うまくできなかった。 | Despite practicing many times, I couldn't do it well. |
| 彼は若い**にもかかわらず**、とても落ち着いている。 | Despite being young, he is very composed. |
| 高価である**にもかかわらず**、よく売れている。 | Despite being expensive, it sells well. |
| 休日**にもかかわらず**、出社しなければならない。 | Despite it being a holiday, I must go to work. |

### 6.2 Related Expressions

| Pattern | Formality | Nuance |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| にもかかわらず | Very formal / written | Strong contrast |
| のに | Neutral / spoken | Disappointment or surprise |
| にしても | Moderate | "Even considering ~" |
| くせに | Casual / critical | "Even though ~ (critical tone)" |

> **にもかかわらず** is primarily used in writing: newspapers, essays, reports.
> In conversation, **のに** is far more natural.

## 7. ～に関して — "Regarding / Concerning"

A formal way to say **"regarding," "concerning,"** or **"about."** Used to introduce a topic in formal contexts.

**Formation:**

```
Noun + に関して          → adverbial ("regarding ~")
Noun + に関する + Noun   → adjectival ("~ concerning ~")
```

### 7.1 Example Sentences

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| この問題**に関して**、ご質問はありますか。 | Do you have any questions regarding this issue? |
| 環境問題**に関する**研究が増えている。 | Research concerning environmental issues is increasing. |
| 日本文化**に関して**は、彼が詳しい。 | Regarding Japanese culture, he is knowledgeable. |
| 個人情報**に関する**法律が改正された。 | Laws concerning personal information were revised. |
| この件**に関して**は、後日連絡します。 | Regarding this matter, I will contact you at a later date. |

### 7.2 Related Topic-Marking Expressions

| Pattern | Formality | Usage |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| について | Standard | General topic introduction |
| に関して | Formal | Academic, business, official |
| に対して | Formal | "toward" — direction of action or attitude |
| をめぐって | Formal | "surrounding" — debated/controversial topics |

Comparison examples:

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 日本の教育**について**話しましょう。 | Let's talk about Japanese education. (neutral) |
| 日本の教育**に関して**調査した。 | I researched concerning Japanese education. (formal) |
| 日本の教育**に対して**批判がある。 | There is criticism toward Japanese education. (directed) |
| 日本の教育**をめぐって**議論が起きている。 | Debate has arisen surrounding Japanese education. (controversial) |

## 8. ～を通じて — "Through / Throughout"

Expresses **means** ("through / by means of") or **duration** ("throughout").

**Formation:**

```
Noun + を通じて / を通して
```

> を通じて and を通して are largely interchangeable, but を通じて is slightly more formal.

### 8.1 Meaning A: Means / Medium

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| インターネット**を通じて**情報を集めた。 | I gathered information through the internet. |
| 友人**を通じて**彼と知り合った。 | I got to know him through a friend. |
| 留学**を通じて**多くのことを学んだ。 | I learned many things through studying abroad. |
| この経験**を通じて**成長できた。 | I was able to grow through this experience. |

### 8.2 Meaning B: Duration / Throughout

| Japanese | English |
|----------|---------|
| 一年**を通じて**温暖な気候だ。 | The climate is warm throughout the year. |
| 生涯**を通じて**研究を続けた。 | He continued his research throughout his life. |
| 四季**を通じて**楽しめる公園です。 | It's a park you can enjoy throughout all four seasons. |

### 8.3 を通じて vs. によって

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| を通じて | through (medium/duration) | 友人を通じて知った (learned through a friend) |
| によって | by means of / depending on | 方法によって結果が変わる (results vary depending on the method) |

## 9. Comparison and Usage Map

```
+--------------------------------------------------------------+
|          ADVANCED GRAMMAR PATTERN USAGE MAP                   |
+--------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                              |
|  TENDENCIES / QUALITIES:                                     |
|  ・っぽい → impression, quality (-ish)                        |
|  ・がち  → negative habitual tendency                         |
|  ・気味  → slight/mild degree                                 |
|                                                              |
|  CONTRAST / CONCESSION:                                      |
|  ・にもかかわらず → despite (formal, written)                  |
|  ・のに          → despite (spoken, emotional)                |
|                                                              |
|  "WITHOUT":                                                  |
|  ・ずに   → without doing (written/formal)                    |
|  ・ないで → without doing (spoken/casual)                     |
|                                                              |
|  TOPIC MARKING:                                              |
|  ・に関して → regarding (formal)                              |
|  ・について → about (neutral)                                 |
|                                                              |
|  MEANS / DURATION:                                           |
|  ・を通じて → through / throughout (formal)                   |
|  ・で      → by means of (neutral)                           |
|                                                              |
|  RECENCY / EXCLUSIVITY:                                      |
|  ・たばかり         → just did                                |
|  ・てばかりいる     → nothing but                             |
+--------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Register Summary

| Pattern | Spoken | Written | Formal | Casual |
|---------|--------|---------|--------|--------|
| ～ばかり | Yes | Yes | -- | Yes |
| ～っぽい | Yes | -- | -- | Yes |
| ～がち | -- | Yes | Yes | -- |
| ～気味 | Yes | Yes | Yes | -- |
| ～ずに | -- | Yes | Yes | -- |
| ～にもかかわらず | -- | Yes | Yes | -- |
| ～に関して | -- | Yes | Yes | -- |
| ～を通じて | -- | Yes | Yes | -- |

## 10. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Choose the Correct Pattern

Fill in the blank with the appropriate grammar pattern.

1. 彼女はいつも遅刻して、怒り______人だ。 (she's the type who gets angry easily)
2. 最近、疲れ______なので、休みを取りたい。 (I've been a bit tired)
3. このスープは水______。 (this soup is watery)
4. 駅前の事故______、電車が遅れている。 (due to the accident in front of the station)
5. 朝ご飯を食べ______出かけた。 (I went out without eating breakfast)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **っぽい** — 怒りっぽい (short-tempered; personality trait)
2. **気味** — 疲れ気味 (slightly tired; mild degree)
3. **っぽい** — 水っぽい (watery; quality/impression)
4. **にもかかわらず** is not correct here; the sentence describes a cause, not contrast. Reinterpreting: 事故**に関して**情報はありますか (regarding the accident). However, with context "because of": this uses a different pattern. If the intended meaning is "despite the accident": 事故**にもかかわらず** (despite the accident, trains are delayed — the contrast being that one might expect them to run).
5. **ずに** — 食べずに (without eating; written register)

</details>

### Exercise 2: がち vs. 気味 vs. っぽい

Choose the most natural option for each sentence.

1. 冬は風邪をひき（がち / 気味 / っぽい）だ。 — habitual tendency in winter
2. 少し風邪（がち / 気味 / っぽい）です。 — slight cold right now
3. 彼は飽き（がち / 気味 / っぽい）性格だ。 — personality trait
4. 最近、仕事が遅れ（がち / 気味 / っぽい）です。 — happening somewhat
5. 彼は子供（がち / 気味 / っぽい）。 — acts childish

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **がち** — habitual tendency: 風邪をひきがち
2. **気味** — slight degree right now: 風邪気味
3. **っぽい** — personality impression: 飽きっぽい
4. **気味** — current slight condition: 遅れ気味
5. **っぽい** — impression/quality: 子供っぽい

</details>

### Exercise 3: Rewrite Using ～ずに

Convert the casual ～ないで sentences to the formal ～ずに form.

1. 何も食べないで寝た。
2. 辞書を使わないで翻訳した。
3. 準備しないで試験を受けた。
4. 誰にも相談しないで決めた。
5. 休まないで働き続けた。

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 何も食べ**ずに**寝た。 (I went to sleep without eating anything.)
2. 辞書を使わ**ずに**翻訳した。 (I translated without using a dictionary.)
3. 準備**せずに**試験を受けた。 (I took the exam without preparing.) — する → せずに
4. 誰にも相談**せずに**決めた。 (I decided without consulting anyone.) — 相談する → 相談せずに
5. 休ま**ずに**働き続けた。 (I kept working without resting.)

</details>

### Exercise 4: Translate to Japanese

Use the grammar patterns from this lesson.

1. Despite being expensive, this restaurant is always crowded.
2. I learned about Japanese history through books.
3. Regarding the schedule change, please contact the office.
4. He just moved to Tokyo. (recently)
5. She tends to forget things.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. 高い**にもかかわらず**、このレストランはいつも混んでいる。
2. 本**を通じて**日本の歴史について学んだ。
3. スケジュールの変更**に関して**は、事務所にお問い合わせください。
4. 彼は東京に引っ越した**ばかり**だ。
5. 彼女は忘れ**っぽい**。 / 彼女は忘れ**がち**だ。

</details>

### Exercise 5: Sentence Composition

Write a short paragraph (3-4 sentences) about a busy office worker, incorporating at least four patterns from this lesson. Try to use: ばかり, がち, 気味, ずに, にもかかわらず.

<details>
<summary>Sample Answer</summary>

田中さんは先月転職したばかりだ。新しい仕事が忙しくて、最近は疲れ気味に見える。昼ご飯を食べずに働くことが多く、食事を抜きがちだ。体調が悪いにもかかわらず、毎日遅くまで残業している。

(Tanaka just changed jobs last month. His new job is busy, and lately he looks a bit tired. He often works without eating lunch and tends to skip meals. Despite feeling unwell, he works overtime late every day.)

</details>
