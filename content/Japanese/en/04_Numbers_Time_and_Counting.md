## 1. Numbers 1-10

### 1.1 Two Number Systems

Japanese has two counting systems: a native Japanese system (和語 / wago) and a Chinese-derived system (漢語 / kango). For numbers 1-10, you need both:

| Number | Kanji | Kango (Chinese) | Wago (Japanese) |
|--------|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 一 | いち (ichi) | ひとつ (hitotsu) |
| 2 | 二 | に (ni) | ふたつ (futatsu) |
| 3 | 三 | さん (san) | みっつ (mittsu) |
| 4 | 四 | し / よん (shi / yon) | よっつ (yottsu) |
| 5 | 五 | ご (go) | いつつ (itsutsu) |
| 6 | 六 | ろく (roku) | むっつ (muttsu) |
| 7 | 七 | しち / なな (shichi / nana) | ななつ (nanatsu) |
| 8 | 八 | はち (hachi) | やっつ (yattsu) |
| 9 | 九 | きゅう / く (kyuu / ku) | ここのつ (kokonotsu) |
| 10 | 十 | じゅう (juu) | とお (too) |

> **When to Use Which System**
>
> **Kango (いち、に、さん...)**:
> - With specific counters (一本、二人、三台)
> - Phone numbers, addresses, math
> - Numbers above 10
>
> **Wago (ひとつ、ふたつ、みっつ...)**:
> - Counting things without a specific counter
> - Age of young children (informal)
> - Only goes up to 10
>
> **Note on 4, 7, 9**:
> - 4 = し (shi) sounds like 死 (death) → よん (yon) preferred
> - 7 = しち (shichi) → なな (nana) often preferred
> - 9 = く (ku) sounds like 苦 (suffering) → きゅう preferred

### 1.2 Zero

| Reading | Romaji | Usage |
|---------|--------|-------|
| ゼロ | zero | General, modern (from English) |
| れい | rei | Formal, telephone numbers |
| まる | maru | "Circle" — used when reading digits aloud |

### 3.3 Ten Thousands

| Number | Japanese | Romaji |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 10,000 | いちまん | ichi-man |

> **Japanese Large Number Units**
>
> English groups by 3 digits: thousand, million, billion
> Japanese groups by 4 digits: 万 (man), 億 (oku), 兆 (chou)
>
> - 1,0000 → いちまん (ichiman) = 10,000
> - 10,0000 → じゅうまん (juuman) = 100,000
> - 100,0000 → ひゃくまん (hyakuman) = 1,000,000
> - 1000,0000 → いっせんまん (issenman) = 10,000,000
> - 1,0000,0000 → いちおく (ichioku) = 100,000,000
>
> This 4-digit grouping is one of the trickiest parts of
> Japanese numbers for English speakers.

## 4. The Japanese Counter System

### 4.1 What are Counters?

> **The Japanese Counter System**
>
> In English: "three books", "two cats", "one cup"
> In Japanese: You MUST add a counter word between the number and the noun.
>
> - **English**: "3 books"
> - **Japanese**: 本を 三冊 ください。 (Hon o san-satsu kudasai.) — "Please give me 3 books." (三 = 3, 冊 = counter for books)
>
> There are 100+ counters in Japanese, but you only need about 10-15 for daily conversation.
>
> If you don't know the right counter, use the generic Japanese counter: ひとつ、ふたつ、みっつ...

### 4.2 Counter Word Order

Counters usually come right after the noun (or after the particle):

```
Pattern 1: [Noun]を [Number+Counter] [Verb]
  りんごを 三つ ください。
  Ringo o mittsu kudasai.
  "Please give me 3 apples."

Pattern 2: [Number+Counter]の [Noun]
  三冊の本
  San-satsu no hon
  "3 books"
```

## 7. Days of the Week

### 7.1 The Seven Days

Each day is named after a celestial body or element, followed by 曜日 (youbi):

| Day | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji | Element |
|-----|-------|----------|--------|---------|
| Monday | 月曜日 | げつようび | getsu-youbi | Moon (月) |
| Tuesday | 火曜日 | かようび | ka-youbi | Fire (火) |
| Wednesday | 水曜日 | すいようび | sui-youbi | Water (水) |
| Thursday | 木曜日 | もくようび | moku-youbi | Wood (木) |
| Friday | 金曜日 | きんようび | kin-youbi | Gold/Metal (金) |
| Saturday | 土曜日 | どようび | do-youbi | Earth (土) |
| Sunday | 日曜日 | にちようび | nichi-youbi | Sun (日) |
| What day? | 何曜日 | なんようび | nan-youbi | |

```
Mnemonic: The elements follow the same pattern as planets in many languages:
  月 Moon → Monday     (French: lundi)
  火 Mars → Tuesday    (French: mardi)
  水 Mercury → Wednesday (French: mercredi)
  木 Jupiter → Thursday (French: jeudi)
  金 Venus → Friday    (French: vendredi)
  土 Saturn → Saturday  (English: Saturday)
  日 Sun → Sunday      (English: Sunday)
```

"March 15, 2025"

| 2026 | にせんにじゅうろくねん | nisen-nijuuroku-nen |

### 10.2 Duration Counters

| Duration | Counter | Example | Romaji |
|----------|---------|---------|--------|
| ___ minutes | 〜分間 (funkan) | 三十分間 | sanjuppunkan (30 minutes) |
| ___ hours | 〜時間 (jikan) | 二時間 | ni-jikan (2 hours) |
| ___ days | 〜日間 (nichikan) | 三日間 | mikka-kan (3 days) |
| ___ weeks | 〜週間 (shuukan) | 一週間 | isshuukan (1 week) |
| ___ months | 〜か月 (kagetsu) | 六か月 | rokkagetsu (6 months) |
| ___ years | 〜年間 (nenkan) | 一年間 | ichinen-kan (1 year) |

### 10.3 Asking About Duration
