# 19. Idioms, Proverbs, and Colloquial German (Redewendungen, Sprichwörter und Umgangssprache)

## Learning Objectives

- Recognize and use common German idioms organized by theme (body, animal, color, food)
- Understand and apply popular German proverbs (Sprichwörter) with their English equivalents
- Identify features of colloquial spoken German: shortened forms, filler words, and slang
- Distinguish between formal, informal, and colloquial registers in German
- Recognize regional variations across Germany, Austria, and Switzerland
- Avoid common pitfalls with false friends (Falsche Freunde) between German and English

## 1. Body-Part Idioms

German is rich in idioms that reference parts of the body. These are very common in everyday speech.

| Idiom | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| jemandem **auf die Nerven gehen** | to go on someone's nerves | to annoy someone |
| **ein Auge zudrücken** | to press one eye shut | to turn a blind eye |
| **die Daumen drücken** | to press the thumbs | to keep one's fingers crossed |
| **die Nase voll haben** | to have the nose full | to be fed up |
| **auf großem Fuß leben** | to live on a big foot | to live in luxury |
| **Hals- und Beinbruch!** | break your neck and leg! | break a leg! (good luck!) |
| **den Kopf verlieren** | to lose one's head | to panic |
| jemandem **die Ohren voll jammern** | to whine someone's ears full | to complain endlessly to someone |
| **kalte Füße bekommen** | to get cold feet | to get cold feet / lose courage |
| **ein Brett vor dem Kopf haben** | to have a board in front of one's head | to be unable to think clearly |
| jemandem **unter die Arme greifen** | to grab under someone's arms | to help someone out |
| **ins Auge fallen** | to fall into the eye | to catch one's attention |
| **auf der Hand liegen** | to lie on the hand | to be obvious |

### Usage Examples

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Du **gehst** mir langsam **auf die Nerven**! | You're slowly getting on my nerves! |
| Ich **drücke** dir **die Daumen** für morgen! | I'm keeping my fingers crossed for you for tomorrow! |
| Ich habe **die Nase voll** von diesem Wetter. | I'm fed up with this weather. |
| Können Sie diesmal **ein Auge zudrücken**? | Could you turn a blind eye this time? |
| **Verlier** nicht gleich **den Kopf**! | Don't lose your head right away! |

## 6. Colloquial Spoken German

Spoken German differs significantly from written German. Here are common features of informal speech.

### 6.1 Shortened Forms

| Standard | Colloquial | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| habe | **hab'** | Ich **hab'** keine Zeit. |
| eine | **'ne** | Hast du **'ne** Idee? |
| einen | **'nen** | Ich brauche **'nen** Kaffee. |
| einem | **'nem** | In **'nem** Jahr. |
| einer | **'ner** | Aus **'ner** anderen Stadt. |
| etwas | **was** | Willst du **was** essen? |
| nicht | **nich** | Ich weiß **nich**. |
| nichts | **nix** | Da ist **nix** los. |
| einmal | **mal** | Komm **mal** her! |
| es | **'s** | **'s** regnet. |
| wir haben | **wir ham** | **Wir ham** kein Geld. |
| ist das | **is' das** | Was **is'** das? |

### 6.2 Dropping the -e

In spoken German, the first-person singular ending **-e** is often dropped:

| Standard | Colloquial |
|----------|-----------|
| Ich **habe** | Ich **hab** |
| Ich **gehe** | Ich **geh** |
| Ich **mache** | Ich **mach** |
| Ich **komme** | Ich **komm** |
| Ich **finde** | Ich **find** |
| Ich **glaube** | Ich **glaub** |

### 6.3 weil and obwohl with Main Clause Word Order

In spoken German, **weil** (because) and **obwohl** (although) are increasingly used with main clause word order (verb in second position) instead of subordinate clause order (verb at end):

| Written (correct) | Spoken (common) |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Ich bleibe, **weil** ich müde **bin**. | Ich bleibe, **weil** ich **bin** müde. |
| Er ging, **obwohl** er krank **war**. | Er ging, **obwohl** er **war** krank. |

> This is considered non-standard in writing but is extremely common in everyday speech.

## 7. Filler Words and Discourse Particles

German is famous for its **Modalpartikeln** (modal particles) — small words that add nuance, emphasis, or speaker attitude. They are essential for sounding natural.

| Particle | Function | Example | Effect |
|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| **halt** | resignation / "that's just how it is" | Das ist **halt** so. | That's just the way it is. |
| **eben** | emphasis / confirmation | Das ist **eben** das Problem. | That's exactly the problem. |
| **eigentlich** | "actually / come to think of it" | **Eigentlich** wollte ich bleiben. | Actually, I wanted to stay. |
| **also** | "so / well" (discourse starter) | **Also**, ich finde das gut. | So/well, I think that's good. |
| **sozusagen** | "so to speak" | Er ist **sozusagen** der Chef. | He's the boss, so to speak. |
| **quasi** | "basically / kind of" | Das ist **quasi** das Gleiche. | That's basically the same thing. |
| **doch** | contradiction / emphasis | Komm **doch** mit! | Come along! (encouragement) |
| **ja** | shared knowledge / surprise | Das ist **ja** toll! | That's great! (surprise/emphasis) |
| **mal** | softening / casualness | Zeig **mal**! | Show me! (casual) |
| **schon** | concession / reassurance | Das wird **schon**. | It'll be fine. |
| **denn** | curiosity / interest | Was machst du **denn** hier? | What are you doing here? (curious) |
| **wohl** | probability | Das ist **wohl** wahr. | That's probably true. |
| **bloß** | emphasis / warning | Mach das **bloß** nicht! | Don't you dare do that! |
| **ruhig** | permission / encouragement | Du kannst **ruhig** fragen. | Feel free to ask. |

### Usage Notes

These particles are difficult to translate directly because their meaning depends heavily on context and intonation. The best way to learn them is through exposure to spoken German.

| German | Literal Translation | Natural Translation |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Das ist **ja** interessant! | That is indeed interesting! | Oh, that's interesting! |
| Komm **doch mal** her! | Come indeed once here! | Come over here! (friendly) |
| Das habe ich **doch** gesagt! | I said that indeed! | But I told you that! |
| Hast du **denn** keine Lust? | Do you then have no desire? | Don't you feel like it? |

## 8. Youth Slang Basics

German youth slang evolves rapidly, but some terms have achieved wider currency.

| Slang | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **cool** | cool | Das ist echt **cool**. |
| **krass** | extreme / intense / crazy | Das war **krass**! (That was intense!) |
| **geil** | awesome / great (originally vulgar) | Der Film war **geil**! (The movie was awesome!) |
| **chillen** | to chill / relax | Wir **chillen** heute nur. (We're just chilling today.) |
| **Bock haben** | to feel like doing something | Ich hab **Bock** auf Pizza. (I feel like having pizza.) |
| **keinen Bock haben** | to not feel like it | Ich hab **keinen Bock** auf Arbeit. (I don't feel like working.) |
| **Alter!** | Dude! / Man! | **Alter**, das glaubst du nicht! (Dude, you won't believe this!) |
| **mega** | mega / super | Das war **mega** gut! |
| **voll** | totally | Das ist **voll** langweilig. (That's totally boring.) |
| **Digga/Digger** | dude / bro (especially Northern Germany) | Ey, **Digga**, was geht? (Hey dude, what's up?) |
| **läuft bei dir** | "things are going well for you" | Du hast bestanden? **Läuft bei dir!** |
| **cringe** | cringe / embarrassing | Das war so **cringe**. |

> Many slang terms are borrowed from English. Use them only in casual contexts with peers — never in professional or academic settings.

## 9. Regional Colloquialisms

German varies significantly across regions. Here are some notable differences.

### 9.1 Berlin (Berlinerisch)

| Berlinerisch | Standard German | English |
|-------------|----------------|---------|
| **ick** | ich | I |
| **det** | das | that |
| **wat** | was | what |
| **keen** | kein | no/none |
| **Schrippe** | Brötchen | bread roll |
| Dit is **jut**! | Das ist gut! | That's good! |

### 9.2 Bavaria (Bairisch)

| Bairisch | Standard German | English |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| **Grüß Gott!** | Guten Tag! | Hello! |
| **Servus!** | Hallo! / Tschüss! | Hi! / Bye! |
| **Brezn** | Brezel | pretzel |
| **Semmel** | Brötchen | bread roll |
| **Pfiat di!** | Auf Wiedersehen! | Goodbye! |
| **I mog di.** | Ich mag dich. | I like you. |
| **Oida!** | Alter! | Dude! (exclamation) |

### 9.3 Austria (Österreichisches Deutsch)

| Austrian German | Standard German | English |
|----------------|----------------|---------|
| **Erdäpfel** | Kartoffel | potato |
| **Paradeiser** | Tomate | tomato |
| **Topfen** | Quark | curd cheese |
| **Sackerl** | Tüte | bag |
| **Jänner** | Januar | January |
| **heuer** | dieses Jahr | this year |
| **Beisl** | Kneipe | pub |
| **Matura** | Abitur | high school leaving exam |

### 9.4 Switzerland (Schweizerdeutsch)

Swiss German dialects differ dramatically from Standard German. In writing, Swiss use Standard German but with notable differences:

| Swiss Usage | Standard German | English |
|------------|----------------|---------|
| **Grüezi!** | Guten Tag! | Hello! |
| **Velo** | Fahrrad | bicycle |
| **Natel** | Handy | mobile phone |
| **Rüebli** | Karotte | carrot |
| **Zmorge** | Frühstück | breakfast |
| **merci** / **merci vilmal** | danke / vielen Dank | thank you / thank you very much |

> Swiss German does not use the **ß** (Eszett). All instances are written as **ss**: *Straße → Strasse*, *groß → gross*.

## 10. Register: Formal, Informal, Colloquial

Choosing the right register is socially important in German-speaking cultures.

### 10.1 Comparison Table

| Situation | Formal (Sie) | Informal (du) | Colloquial |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| Greeting | Guten Tag, Herr Müller. | Hallo, Thomas! | Hey, was geht? |
| How are you? | Wie geht es Ihnen? | Wie geht's dir? | Na, und? / Alles klar? |
| Request | Könnten Sie mir bitte helfen? | Kannst du mir helfen? | Hilf mir mal! / Hilfst du mir? |
| Thank you | Ich danke Ihnen vielmals. | Danke dir! | Danke! / Merci! |
| Goodbye | Auf Wiedersehen. | Tschüss! / Bis dann! | Ciao! / Mach's gut! |
| Agreement | Da stimme ich Ihnen zu. | Ja, finde ich auch. | Ja, voll! / Total! |
| Disagreement | Das sehe ich anders. | Nee, glaub ich nicht. | Quatsch! / Ach was! |

### 10.2 When to Use Which Register

| Register | Context |
|----------|---------|
| **Formal (Sie)** | Business settings, official interactions, strangers (adults), professors, doctors, officials |
| **Informal (du)** | Friends, family, children, fellow students, colleagues (after agreement), social media |
| **Colloquial** | Very close friends, casual text messages, informal social situations |

> In German workplaces, the transition from **Sie** to **du** (called *Duzen*) is usually initiated by the more senior person and is considered a sign of trust and friendliness.

## 11. False Friends (Falsche Freunde)

False friends are words that look or sound similar in German and English but have different meanings. These are common sources of confusion.

| German Word | German Meaning | English Look-alike | English Meaning |
|------------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Gift** | poison | gift | present/Geschenk |
| **bekommen** | to receive | become | werden |
| **Handy** | mobile phone | handy | praktisch |
| **Chef** | boss | chef | Koch |
| **Rat** | advice / council | rat | Ratte |
| **sensibel** | sensitive | sensible | vernünftig |
| **aktuell** | current | actual | tatsächlich |
| **eventuell** | possibly / perhaps | eventually | schließlich |
| **Fabrik** | factory | fabric | Stoff |
| **Note** | grade / mark | note | Notiz |
| **Gymnasium** | college-prep high school | gymnasium | Turnhalle |
| **Kamera** | camera | camera | Kamera (same, but often confused with *Kamerad* = comrade) |
| **Mappe** | folder / briefcase | map | Karte / Landkarte |
| **Prospekt** | brochure / pamphlet | prospect | Aussicht |
| **Rente** | pension | rent | Miete |
| **sympathisch** | likeable / nice | sympathetic | mitfühlend |
| **brav** | well-behaved | brave | mutig / tapfer |
| **Billion** | trillion (10¹²) | billion | Milliarde (10⁹) |
| **Rezept** | recipe / prescription | receipt | Quittung |
| **Wand** | (interior) wall | wand | Zauberstab |
| **spenden** | to donate | to spend | ausgeben |

### Memory Tips

| Trap | Correct Translation |
|------|-------------------|
| "I became a book." ≠ Ich **bekam** ein Buch. | Ich **bekam** ein Buch. = I **received** a book. |
| "He is my chef." ≠ Er ist mein **Chef**. | Er ist mein **Chef**. = He is my **boss**. |
| "Where is my handy?" = Wo ist mein **Handy**? | Wo ist mein **Handy**? = Where is my **cell phone**? |
| "Gift" in German = **Gift** = **poison**! | Ein **Geschenk** = a **gift/present**. |

## 12. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Match the Idiom

Match each idiom with its meaning.

1. die Daumen drücken
2. einen Kater haben
3. ins Grüne fahren
4. seinen Senf dazugeben
5. schwarz sehen

a) to have a hangover
b) to be pessimistic
c) to keep one's fingers crossed
d) to put in one's two cents
e) to go to the countryside

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1 — **c** (die Daumen drücken = to keep one's fingers crossed)
2 — **a** (einen Kater haben = to have a hangover)
3 — **e** (ins Grüne fahren = to go to the countryside)
4 — **d** (seinen Senf dazugeben = to put in one's two cents)
5 — **b** (schwarz sehen = to be pessimistic)

</details>

### Exercise 2: Complete the Proverb

Fill in the missing word.

1. Übung macht den ______.
2. ______ Anfang ist schwer.
3. Reden ist Silber, ______ ist Gold.
4. Der Apfel fällt nicht weit vom ______.
5. Viele Köche verderben den ______.
6. Wer zuletzt ______, lacht am besten.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Meister** (Practice makes the master.)
2. **Aller** (Every beginning is hard.)
3. **Schweigen** (Speech is silver, silence is gold.)
4. **Stamm** (The apple doesn't fall far from the tree.)
5. **Brei** (Too many cooks spoil the broth.)
6. **lacht** (He who laughs last, laughs best.)

</details>

### Exercise 3: False Friends

Choose the correct German translation.

1. "I received a gift." = Ich habe ein ______ bekommen.
   a) Gift  b) Geschenk
2. "She became a teacher." = Sie ist Lehrerin ______.
   a) bekommen  b) geworden
3. "Where is my mobile phone?" = Wo ist mein ______?
   a) Handy  b) Mobile
4. "The boss is on vacation." = Der ______ ist im Urlaub.
   a) Koch  b) Chef
5. "I need a recipe." = Ich brauche ein ______.
   a) Rezept  b) Quittung

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **b) Geschenk** (Gift = poison!)
2. **b) geworden** (bekommen = to receive)
3. **a) Handy** (standard German for mobile phone)
4. **b) Chef** (Koch = cook/chef in the kitchen sense)
5. **a) Rezept** (Quittung = receipt)

</details>

### Exercise 4: Register Transformation

Rewrite the formal sentence in informal/colloquial German.

1. Könnten Sie mir bitte sagen, wo der Bahnhof ist?
2. Guten Tag, Frau Schmidt. Wie geht es Ihnen?
3. Ich möchte Sie bitten, mir das Dokument zu senden.
4. Es tut mir sehr leid, dass ich zu spät gekommen bin.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Kannst du mir sagen, wo der Bahnhof ist?** (or very casual: **Weißt du, wo der Bahnhof is'?**)
2. **Hallo! Wie geht's?** (or casual: **Hey, na, wie geht's?**)
3. **Kannst du mir das Dokument schicken?** (or: **Schick mir mal das Dokument!**)
4. **Sorry, dass ich zu spät bin!** (or: **Tschuldigung für die Verspätung!**)

</details>

### Exercise 5: Colloquial to Standard

Rewrite in standard written German.

1. Ich hab' keinen Bock auf Arbeit.
2. Hast du 'ne Ahnung, was da los is'?
3. Der Film war voll krass, Alter!
4. Wir ham nix gemacht.
5. Das is' halt so.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Ich **habe** keine **Lust zu arbeiten**.
2. **Hast du eine** Ahnung, **was da los ist**?
3. **Der Film war sehr beeindruckend!** (or: **Der Film war wirklich intensiv.**)
4. **Wir haben nichts gemacht.**
5. **Das ist eben so.** (or: **So ist es nun einmal.**)

</details>
