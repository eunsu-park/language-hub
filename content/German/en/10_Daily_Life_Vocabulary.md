# 10. Daily Life Vocabulary (Alltagswortschatz)

## Learning Objectives

- Learn essential food and drink vocabulary with correct grammatical gender (der/die/das)
- Order meals and interact confidently at a German restaurant
- Use shopping vocabulary for supermarkets, bakeries, and markets
- Navigate public transportation with key phrases and vocabulary
- Describe travel plans and interact at airports, train stations, and hotels
- Communicate basic health concerns at a doctor's office or pharmacy
- Describe housing, rooms, furniture, and household items in German
- Combine common verbs with nouns for daily activities and routines
- Understand cultural norms around food, shopping, tipping, and healthcare in Germany

## 1. Food and Drink (Essen und Trinken)

### 1.1 Meals of the Day

| German | English | Cultural Notes |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| das **Frühstück** | breakfast | Usually bread, rolls, cheese, cold cuts, eggs, and jam |
| das **Mittagessen** | lunch | Traditionally the main, warm meal of the day |
| das **Abendessen** / das **Abendbrot** | dinner / supper | Often cold: bread, cheese, cold cuts (*Abendbrot* = "evening bread") |
| der **Snack** / die **Zwischenmahlzeit** | snack | Eaten between main meals |
| die **Kaffeepause** | coffee break | Afternoon coffee and cake is a beloved tradition |

> **Cultural note**: The German meal structure traditionally centers the largest, hot meal at midday (Mittagessen). Dinner (Abendessen) is often lighter and cold, consisting of bread with toppings. However, this is changing in modern urban life, where many people now eat their main meal in the evening.

### 2.4 Sample Shopping Dialogue

> **An der Bäckerei (At the bakery)**
>
> Verkäuferin: Guten Morgen! Was darf es sein?

>
> Kunde: Guten Morgen! Ich hätte gern drei Brötchen und ein Schwarzbrot, bitte.
> *Customer: Good morning! I would like three bread rolls and one dark rye bread, please.*
>
> Verkäuferin: Gerne. Sonst noch etwas?
> *Saleswoman: Of course. Anything else?*
>
> Kunde: Ja, ein Stück Pflaumenkuchen, bitte.
> *Customer: Yes, one piece of plum cake, please.*
>
> Verkäuferin: Das macht zusammen fünf Euro achtzig.
> *Saleswoman: That comes to five euros eighty altogether.*
>
> Kunde: Bitte schön. Stimmt so.
> *Customer: Here you are. Keep the change.*

### 5.4 Paying

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Die Rechnung, bitte. | The check/bill, please. |
| Zahlen, bitte. | I'd like to pay, please. |
| Ich möchte bitte zahlen. | I would like to pay, please. |
| Zusammen oder getrennt? | Together or separate? |
| Zusammen, bitte. | Together, please. |
| Getrennt, bitte. | Separate, please. |
| Kann ich mit Karte zahlen? | Can I pay by card? |
| Stimmt so. | Keep the change. |
| Machen Sie 25 Euro. | Make it 25 euros. (including tip) |
| das Trinkgeld | the tip |

> **Cultural note**: Tipping in Germany differs from the US. Tips of **5-10%** are customary and appreciated but not as large as in the US. Instead of leaving cash on the table, you tell the server the total amount you want to pay (including tip). For example, if the bill is 23.50 EUR, you might say "Machen Sie 26 Euro" or simply "Stimmt so" if you are paying with an exact amount you are happy with. The server usually handles payment at the table rather than taking your card away.

> *Guest: Good, I would like the roast pork. And a wheat beer, please.*
>
> ... (nach dem Essen / after the meal) ...
>
> Gast: Die Rechnung, bitte.
> *Guest: The check, please.*
>
> Kellner: Das macht dreiundzwanzig Euro fünfzig.
> *Waiter: That comes to twenty-three euros fifty.*

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Ich brauche etwas gegen Kopfschmerzen. | I need something for a headache. |
| Haben Sie etwas gegen Erkältung? | Do you have something for a cold? |
| Was empfehlen Sie gegen Husten? | What do you recommend for a cough? |
| das Medikament | medication |
| die Tablette | tablet, pill |
| die Schmerztablette | pain pill, painkiller |
| der Hustensaft | cough syrup |

| die Tropfen | drops |

> **Cultural note**: In Germany, there is an important distinction between **die Apotheke** (pharmacy) and **die Drogerie** (drugstore). Apotheken sell actual pharmaceutical medications, including many that are available over-the-counter in other countries (like ibuprofen and aspirin). They are marked with a green cross and have a trained pharmacist on duty. A **Drogerie** (like dm, Rossmann, or Müller) sells toiletries, cosmetics, vitamins, and health products, but not pharmaceutical medicines.

### 6.5 Sample Doctor's Dialogue

> **Beim Arzt (At the doctor)**
>
> Ärztin: Guten Tag, was kann ich für Sie tun?
> *Doctor: Good day, what can I do for you?*
>
> Patient: Guten Tag. Ich fühle mich nicht gut. Ich habe seit zwei Tagen starke Kopfschmerzen und Fieber.
> *Patient: Good day. I don't feel well. I have had a severe headache and fever for two days.*
>
> Ärztin: Haben Sie auch Husten oder Halsschmerzen?
> *Doctor: Do you also have a cough or sore throat?*
>
> Patient: Ja, ich habe auch Halsschmerzen und ein bisschen Schnupfen.
> *Patient: Yes, I also have a sore throat and a bit of a runny nose.*
>
> Ärztin: Das klingt nach einer Erkältung. Ich verschreibe Ihnen ein Medikament. Nehmen Sie dreimal täglich eine Tablette und trinken Sie viel Wasser.
> *Doctor: That sounds like a cold. I will prescribe you a medication. Take one tablet three times daily and drink plenty of water.*
>
> Patient: Danke, Frau Doktor. Brauche ich eine Krankmeldung?
> *Patient: Thank you, Doctor. Do I need a sick note?*
>
> Ärztin: Ja, ich schreibe Sie für drei Tage krank.
> *Doctor: Yes, I will write you a sick note for three days.*

### 7.4 Describing a Home

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Die Wohnung hat drei Zimmer. | The apartment has three rooms. |
| Die Küche ist groß und hell. | The kitchen is big and bright. |

> **Cultural note**: When Germans count rooms (*Zimmer*) in an apartment, they typically count only the main living spaces (living room, bedrooms), NOT the kitchen, bathroom, or hallway.
