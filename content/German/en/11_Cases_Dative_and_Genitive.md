# 11. Cases: Dative and Genitive (Dativ und Genitiv)

## Learning Objectives

- Identify when the dative case is required and apply correct dative articles
- Use personal pronouns in the dative case fluently
- Memorize and apply the core dative prepositions (aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu)
- Distinguish between dative (location/wo?) and accusative (direction/wohin?) with two-way prepositions
- Form the genitive case with correct article and noun endings
- Use genitive prepositions (wegen, trotz, waehrend, statt) in sentences
- Recognize when colloquial German substitutes von + dative for the genitive
- Apply dative verbs (helfen, danken, gefallen, etc.) correctly in context

## 1. Introduction to the Dative Case

The dative case (der Dativ, also called *der 3. Fall*) marks the **indirect object** of a sentence — the person or thing **to whom** or **for whom** an action is done.

```
Nominative (1. Fall):  WER?    (Who?)       — Subject
Accusative (4. Fall):  WEN?    (Whom?)      — Direct Object
DATIVE (3. Fall):      WEM?    (To whom?)   — Indirect Object
Genitive (2. Fall):    WESSEN? (Whose?)     — Possession
```

**Core concept**: When you can ask **"to whom?"** or **"for whom?"** in English, the answer in German takes the dative case.

> Ich gebe **dem Mann** das Buch. (I give the book **to the man**.)
> Sie kauft **ihrer Mutter** Blumen. (She buys flowers **for her mother**.)

## 2. Dative Articles: Definite and Indefinite

### Definite Articles (the)

| Gender | Nominative | Accusative | **Dative** |
|--------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Masculine (der) | der | den | **dem** |
| Feminine (die) | die | die | **der** |
| Neuter (das) | das | das | **dem** |
| Plural (die) | die | die | **den** (+n) |

> **Plural rule**: In the dative, the plural article becomes **den** and the noun adds **-n** if it doesn't already end in -n or -s.
>
> - die Kinder → **den Kindern** (to the children)
> - die Frauen → **den Frauen** (already ends in -n, no change)
> - die Autos → **den Autos** (already ends in -s, no change)

### Indefinite Articles (a/an) and kein

| Gender | Nominative | Accusative | **Dative** |
|--------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Masculine | ein | einen | **einem** |
| Feminine | eine | eine | **einer** |
| Neuter | ein | ein | **einem** |
| Plural (kein) | keine | keine | **keinen** (+n) |

### Memory Tip

> Think **"MR MRS"**: Masculine = de**m**, feMinine = de**r**, neuteR = de**m**, plural = den + n (the **S** reminds you of the plural -n ending).

## 3. Personal Pronouns in the Dative

| Person | Nominative | Accusative | **Dative** | English |
|--------|-----------|------------|-----------|---------|
| 1st sg. | ich | mich | **mir** | to me |
| 2nd sg. informal | du | dich | **dir** | to you |
| 3rd sg. masc. | er | ihn | **ihm** | to him |
| 3rd sg. fem. | sie | sie | **ihr** | to her |
| 3rd sg. neut. | es | es | **ihm** | to it |
| 1st pl. | wir | uns | **uns** | to us |
| 2nd pl. informal | ihr | euch | **euch** | to you all |
| 3rd pl. | sie | sie | **ihnen** | to them |
| 2nd formal | Sie | Sie | **Ihnen** | to you (formal) |

**Examples**:

- Kannst du **mir** helfen? (Can you help **me**?)
- Ich schicke **dir** eine Nachricht. (I'm sending **you** a message.)
- Wir geben **ihm** das Geld. (We give **him** the money.)
- Das Kleid steht **ihr** gut. (The dress suits **her** well.)
- Ich zeige **Ihnen** den Weg. (I'll show **you** [formal] the way.)

## 4. Dative Prepositions

The following prepositions **always** take the dative case. A common mnemonic is:

> **"Aus-bei-mit, nach-seit, von-zu"** (sing it to a rhythm!)

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **aus** | out of, from (origin) | Er kommt **aus der** Schweiz. (He comes from Switzerland.) |
| **bei** | at, near, at the home of | Ich wohne **bei meinen** Eltern. (I live at my parents' place.) |
| **mit** | with | Sie fährt **mit dem** Bus. (She travels by/with the bus.) |
| **nach** | after, to (cities/countries) | **Nach dem** Essen gehen wir spazieren. (After the meal we go for a walk.) |
| **seit** | since, for (time) | Ich lerne Deutsch **seit einem** Jahr. (I've been learning German for a year.) |
| **von** | from, of, by | Das ist ein Geschenk **von meiner** Freundin. (That's a gift from my girlfriend.) |
| **zu** | to (a person/place) | Ich gehe **zum** Arzt. (I'm going to the doctor.) |
| **gegenüber** | opposite, across from | Die Bank ist **gegenüber dem** Bahnhof. (The bank is opposite the train station.) |

### Common Contractions

| Preposition + Article | Contraction |
|-----------------------|-------------|
| bei + dem | **beim** |
| von + dem | **vom** |
| zu + dem | **zum** |
| zu + der | **zur** |

**Examples**:

- Ich bin **beim** Arzt. (I'm at the doctor's.)
- Er kommt **vom** Büro. (He's coming from the office.)
- Wir fahren **zum** Flughafen. (We're driving to the airport.)
- Sie geht **zur** Schule. (She goes to school.)

## 5. Verbs That Take the Dative

Some German verbs take a **dative object** where English uses a direct object. You must memorize these.

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **helfen** | to help | Ich helfe **dir**. (I help you.) |
| **danken** | to thank | Ich danke **Ihnen**. (I thank you.) |
| **gehören** | to belong to | Das Buch gehört **mir**. (The book belongs to me.) |
| **gefallen** | to please, to like | Die Stadt gefällt **uns**. (We like the city.) |
| **schmecken** | to taste (good to) | Die Suppe schmeckt **ihm** gut. (The soup tastes good to him.) |
| **passen** | to fit, to suit | Die Schuhe passen **mir** nicht. (The shoes don't fit me.) |
| **folgen** | to follow | Der Hund folgt **seinem** Besitzer. (The dog follows its owner.) |
| **antworten** | to answer | Antworte **mir**! (Answer me!) |
| **gratulieren** | to congratulate | Wir gratulieren **dir** zum Geburtstag! (We congratulate you on your birthday!) |
| **glauben** | to believe | Niemand glaubt **ihm**. (Nobody believes him.) |
| **fehlen** | to be missing, to miss | Du fehlst **mir**. (I miss you. — lit. "You are missing to me.") |
| **zuhören** | to listen to | Hör **mir** zu! (Listen to me!) |
| **vertrauen** | to trust | Ich vertraue **dir**. (I trust you.) |
| **widersprechen** | to contradict | Er widerspricht **dem** Lehrer. (He contradicts the teacher.) |

> **Tip**: With **gefallen**, the thing you like is the subject and the person who likes it is in the dative. This is the opposite structure from English:
>
> - **Mir** gefällt das Bild. (I like the picture. — lit. "The picture is pleasing to me.")
> - **Ihm** gefallen die Blumen. (He likes the flowers.)

## 6. Two-Way Prepositions Revisited: Dative vs. Accusative

The nine **two-way prepositions** (Wechselpräpositionen) take either dative or accusative, depending on meaning:

| Rule | Case | Question | Meaning |
|------|------|----------|---------|
| **Location** (static) | **Dative** | **Wo?** (Where?) | being somewhere |
| **Direction** (motion toward) | **Accusative** | **Wohin?** (Where to?) | moving to somewhere |

### The Nine Two-Way Prepositions

**in** (in/into), **an** (at/on-vertical), **auf** (on/onto-horizontal), **über** (over/above), **unter** (under/below), **vor** (in front of/before), **hinter** (behind), **neben** (next to), **zwischen** (between)

### Dative (Location — Wo?) vs. Accusative (Direction — Wohin?)

| Dative (Wo? — Location) | Accusative (Wohin? — Direction) |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Ich bin **in der** Schule. (I am in school.) | Ich gehe **in die** Schule. (I go to school.) |
| Das Bild hängt **an der** Wand. (The picture hangs on the wall.) | Ich hänge das Bild **an die** Wand. (I hang the picture on the wall.) |
| Das Buch liegt **auf dem** Tisch. (The book is lying on the table.) | Ich lege das Buch **auf den** Tisch. (I put the book on the table.) |
| Die Katze sitzt **unter dem** Stuhl. (The cat is sitting under the chair.) | Die Katze kriecht **unter den** Stuhl. (The cat crawls under the chair.) |
| Er steht **vor dem** Haus. (He stands in front of the house.) | Er geht **vor das** Haus. (He goes to the front of the house.) |
| Die Lampe hängt **über dem** Tisch. (The lamp hangs above the table.) | Ich hänge die Lampe **über den** Tisch. (I hang the lamp above the table.) |

### Verb Pairs That Signal the Difference

| Dative (location — static) | Accusative (direction — motion) |
|----------------------------|-------------------------------|
| liegen (to lie/be lying) | legen (to lay/put down) |
| sitzen (to be sitting) | setzen (to set/sit down) |
| stehen (to be standing) | stellen (to place/stand up) |
| hängen (to be hanging) | hängen (to hang up) |
| stecken (to be stuck in) | stecken (to stick into) |

> **Memory tip**: Think of it as a **snapshot** (dative — still image, location) vs. a **video** (accusative — movement happening).

### Common Contractions with Two-Way Prepositions

| Preposition + Article | Contraction |
|-----------------------|-------------|
| in + dem | **im** |
| in + das | **ins** |
| an + dem | **am** |
| an + das | **ans** |
| auf + das | **aufs** |

## 7. Introduction to the Genitive Case

The genitive case (der Genitiv, also called *der 2. Fall*) expresses **possession** — it answers the question **Wessen?** (Whose?).

In English, we use **'s** or **of**: "the man**'s** book" / "the book **of** the man."

In German, the genitive works through article and noun endings:

> Das Auto **des Mannes** ist rot. (The man's car is red.)
> Die Tasche **der Frau** ist schwarz. (The woman's bag is black.)

---

## 8. Genitive Articles and Noun Endings

### Definite Articles

| Gender | Nominative | **Genitive** | Noun Change |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Masculine (der) | der Mann | **des** Mannes | add **-s** or **-es** |
| Feminine (die) | die Frau | **der** Frau | no change |
| Neuter (das) | das Kind | **des** Kindes | add **-s** or **-es** |
| Plural (die) | die Kinder | **der** Kinder | no change |

### Indefinite Articles

| Gender | Nominative | **Genitive** |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Masculine | ein Mann | **eines** Mannes |
| Feminine | eine Frau | **einer** Frau |
| Neuter | ein Kind | **eines** Kindes |

### Rules for Masculine and Neuter Noun Endings

1. **One-syllable nouns** usually add **-es**: des Mann**es**, des Kind**es**, des Haus**es**
2. **Multi-syllable nouns** usually add **-s**: des Autos, des Lehrers, des Computers
3. **Nouns ending in -s, -ss, -ß, -z, -tz, -x** always add **-es**: des Glases, des Flusses
4. **Feminine and plural nouns** do NOT change in the genitive

### N-Declension Nouns (Weak Nouns)

Some masculine nouns add **-n** or **-en** in all cases except nominative (including genitive):

- der Student → des Student**en**
- der Mensch → des Mensch**en**
- der Junge → des Junge**n**
- der Herr → des Herr**n**

### Examples

- Das Büro **des Chefs** ist groß. (The boss's office is large.)
- Die Farbe **des Himmels** ist blau. (The color of the sky is blue.)
- Die Meinung **der Lehrerin** ist wichtig. (The teacher's opinion is important.)
- Die Spielzeuge **der Kinder** liegen überall. (The children's toys are lying everywhere.)
- Der Name **eines Studenten** fehlt auf der Liste. (A student's name is missing from the list.)

### Genitive with Proper Names

Proper names add **-s** without an article (like English **'s**):

- **Annas** Buch (Anna's book)
- **Deutschlands** Hauptstadt (Germany's capital)
- **Goethes** Werke (Goethe's works)

> If the name ends in -s, -ss, -ß, -z, or -x, use an apostrophe:
> **Thomas'** Auto (Thomas's car) or **Max'** Hund (Max's dog)

## 9. Genitive Prepositions

The following prepositions take the **genitive** case:

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **wegen** | because of | **Wegen des** Regens bleiben wir zu Hause. (Because of the rain we stay home.) |
| **trotz** | despite, in spite of | **Trotz der** Kälte geht er joggen. (Despite the cold he goes jogging.) |
| **während** | during | **Während des** Unterrichts darf man nicht essen. (During class one may not eat.) |
| **statt / anstatt** | instead of | **Statt eines** Kuchens brachte er Blumen. (Instead of a cake he brought flowers.) |
| **außerhalb** | outside of | **Außerhalb der** Stadt gibt es Wälder. (Outside the city there are forests.) |
| **innerhalb** | within, inside of | **Innerhalb eines** Jahres hat er Deutsch gelernt. (Within a year he learned German.) |
| **oberhalb** | above | Das Dorf liegt **oberhalb des** Sees. (The village is above the lake.) |
| **unterhalb** | below | Die Straße verläuft **unterhalb des** Berges. (The road runs below the mountain.) |

## 10. Genitive vs. von + Dative

In **spoken** German, the genitive is increasingly replaced by **von + dative**, especially in casual conversation:

| Genitive (formal/written) | von + Dative (colloquial/spoken) |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Das Auto **des Mannes** | Das Auto **von dem Mann** / **vom Mann** |
| Die Farbe **des Himmels** | Die Farbe **vom Himmel** |
| Ein Freund **meines Bruders** | Ein Freund **von meinem Bruder** |

> **When to use which**:
> - **Written German, formal contexts, exams**: Use the genitive
> - **Spoken German, casual conversation**: von + dative is perfectly acceptable
> - **Genitive prepositions**: Even in speech, wegen, trotz, etc. still commonly appear (though *wegen dem Regen* is heard colloquially)

**Important**: The genitive is NOT disappearing from German. It remains standard in written language, media, and formal speech. Learn both forms.

### Exercise 1: Dative Articles

Fill in the correct dative article.

1. Ich gebe ________ (die Frau) das Geschenk.
2. Er hilft ________ (der Schüler) bei den Hausaufgaben.
3. Wir fahren mit ________ (das Auto) zur Arbeit.
4. Sie wohnt bei ________ (ihre Eltern).
5. Das Buch gehört ________ (ein Kind).

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **der Frau** (feminine dative)
2. **dem Schüler** (masculine dative)
3. **dem Auto** (neuter dative)
4. **ihren Eltern** (plural dative — the noun already ends in -n)
5. **einem Kind** (neuter dative, indefinite)

</details>

### Exercise 2: Dative Personal Pronouns

Replace the underlined noun with the correct dative pronoun.

1. Ich helfe **meiner Schwester**. → Ich helfe ________.
2. Er gibt **dem Lehrer** das Heft. → Er gibt ________ das Heft.
3. Wir danken **unseren Freunden**. → Wir danken ________.
4. Kannst du **dem Baby** die Flasche geben? → Kannst du ________ die Flasche geben?
5. Die Jacke passt **der Kundin** nicht. → Die Jacke passt ________ nicht.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **ihr** (her — feminine dative pronoun)
2. **ihm** (him — masculine dative pronoun)
3. **ihnen** (them — plural dative pronoun)
4. **ihm** (it — neuter dative pronoun)
5. **ihr** (her — feminine dative pronoun)

</details>

### Exercise 3: Dative or Accusative? (Two-Way Prepositions)

Choose the correct case based on the meaning (location = dative, direction = accusative).

1. Die Katze schläft auf ________ (das Sofa). [Wo?]
2. Ich stelle die Vase auf ________ (der Tisch). [Wohin?]
3. Er wartet vor ________ (die Tür). [Wo?]
4. Sie hängt das Bild an ________ (die Wand). [Wohin?]
5. Wir sitzen in ________ (das Restaurant). [Wo?]
6. Die Kinder rennen in ________ (der Garten). [Wohin?]

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **dem Sofa** (dative — location, the cat is sleeping ON the sofa)
2. **den Tisch** (accusative — direction, placing the vase ONTO the table)
3. **der Tür** (dative — location, he is standing in front of the door)
4. **die Wand** (accusative — direction, hanging the picture ONTO the wall)
5. **dem Restaurant** (dative — location, we are sitting IN the restaurant)
6. **den Garten** (accusative — direction, children are running INTO the garden)

</details>

### Exercise 4: Genitive Case

1. das Auto / der Lehrer → ________
2. die Meinung / die Professorin → ________
3. der Name / das Kind → ________
4. die Farbe / die Blumen (pl.) → ________
5. die Hauptstadt / Deutschland → ________

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

### Exercise 5: Genitive Prepositions

Complete the sentences with the correct genitive forms.

1. ________ (trotz / das schlechte Wetter) gehen wir spazieren.
2. ________ (während / die Pause) trinke ich Kaffee.
3. ________ (wegen / der Stau) kommen wir zu spät.
4. ________ (statt / ein Brief) schickte er eine E-Mail.
5. ________ (innerhalb / eine Woche) muss die Arbeit fertig sein.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

4. **Statt eines Briefes** (Instead of a letter)
5. **Innerhalb einer Woche** (Within a week)

</details>

### Exercise 6: Mixed Cases — Translate into German

1. I help my mother every day.
2. The book belongs to him.
3. She is sitting in the kitchen. (location)
4. We congratulate you (informal sg.) on your birthday.
5. The color of the house is white.
6. Despite the rain, the children play outside.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Ich helfe **meiner Mutter** jeden Tag. (dative — helfen takes dative)
2. Das Buch gehört **ihm**. (dative pronoun — gehören takes dative)
3. Sie sitzt **in der Küche**. (dative — location: Wo?)
4. Wir gratulieren **dir** zum Geburtstag! (dative — gratulieren takes dative)
5. Die Farbe **des Hauses** ist weiß. (genitive — possession)
6. **Trotz des Regens** spielen die Kinder draußen. (genitive — trotz takes genitive)

</details>
