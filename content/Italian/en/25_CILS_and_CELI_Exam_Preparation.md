## 1. Overview of Italian Proficiency Exams

> **ITALIAN LANGUAGE PROFICIENCY EXAMS**
>
> Three main internationally recognized exams:
>
> 1. **CILS** — Certificazione di Italiano come Lingua Straniera (Università per Stranieri di Siena)
> 2. **CELI** — Certificato di Conoscenza della Lingua Italiana (Università per Stranieri di Perugia)
> 3. **PLIDA** — Progetto Lingua Italiana Dante Alighieri (Società Dante Alighieri)
>
> All three are:
> - Recognized by the Italian Ministry of Education
> - Aligned with CEFR (A1-C2)
> - Valid for Italian citizenship applications (B1+)
> - Accepted by Italian universities (B2+)
> - Offered worldwide at authorized centers

### CEFR Level Descriptions

| CEFR Level | Description | Italian Exam Equivalent | What You Can Do |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **A1** | Breakthrough / Beginner | CILS A1 / CELI Impatto / PLIDA A1 | Basic greetings, simple questions, survival phrases |
| **A2** | Waystage / Elementary | CILS A2 / CELI 1 / PLIDA A2 | Daily routines, simple conversations, basic writing |
| **B1** | Threshold / Intermediate | CILS Uno-B1 / CELI 2 / PLIDA B1 | Travel, express opinions, describe experiences, citizenship requirement |
| **B2** | Vantage / Upper Intermediate | CILS Due-B2 / CELI 3 / PLIDA B2 | Complex discussion, professional contexts, university enrollment |
| **C1** | Effective Proficiency / Advanced | CILS Tre-C1 / CELI 4 / PLIDA C1 | Fluent expression, academic/professional discourse, nuanced understanding |
| **C2** | Mastery / Proficient | CILS Quattro-C2 / CELI 5 / PLIDA C2 | Near-native competence, complex texts, sophisticated expression |

## 2. CILS Exam (Università per Stranieri di Siena)

### 2.1 Overview

> **CILS — Certificazione di Italiano come Lingua Straniera**
>
> - **Issuing body**: Università per Stranieri di Siena
> - **Recognition**: Italian Ministry of Education (MIUR)
> - **Validity**: Lifetime (no expiration)
> - **Levels**: A1, A2, B1 (Uno), B2 (Due), C1 (Tre), C2 (Quattro)
> - **Special versions**: CILS A1/A2 Integrazione (for immigrants seeking Italian residency)
> - **Exam sessions**: June and December (some levels also in April and October)
>
> **SECTIONS (5 sections):**
> 1. Ascolto (Listening)
> 2. Comprensione della lettura (Reading)
> 3. Analisi delle strutture di comunicazione (Grammar)
> 4. Produzione scritta (Writing)
> 5. Produzione orale (Speaking)
>
> **PASSING**: Minimum score of 11/20 per section (55%). You can retake individual failed sections.

### 2.2 CILS Exam Duration by Level

| Level | Listening | Reading | Grammar | Writing | Speaking |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| A1 | 20 min | 30 min | 30 min | 30 min | 10 min |
| A2 | 20 min | 30 min | 30 min | 30 min | 10 min |
| B1 (Uno) | 30 min | 40 min | 40 min | 60 min | 10 min |
| B2 (Due) | 30 min | 50 min | 45 min | 90 min | 10 min |
| C1 (Tre) | 35 min | 60 min | 45 min | 90 min | 15 min |
| C2 (Quattro) | 35 min | 60 min | 45 min | 120 min | 15 min |

### 2.3 CILS Section Details

| Section | Task Types |
|---------|-----------|
| **Ascolto** | Multiple choice, true/false, gap-fill based on audio recordings (dialogues, monologues, announcements) |
| **Comprensione della lettura** | Multiple choice, matching, true/false based on written texts (articles, ads, emails, literature) |
| **Analisi delle strutture** | Cloze tests, sentence transformation, verb conjugation, word formation |
| **Produzione scritta** | B1: Short email/letter + description; B2: Essay/article + formal letter; C1-C2: Complex texts |
| **Produzione orale** | B1: Self-presentation, role-play, picture description; B2-C2: Discussion, argumentation, interaction |

## 3. CELI Exam (Università per Stranieri di Perugia)

### 3.1 Overview

> **CELI — Certificato di Conoscenza della Lingua Italiana**
>
> - **Issuing body**: Università per Stranieri di Perugia
> - **Recognition**: Italian Ministry of Education (MIUR)
> - **Validity**: Lifetime (no expiration)
> - **Levels**: CELI Impatto (A1), CELI 1 (A2), CELI 2 (B1), CELI 3 (B2), CELI 4 (C1), CELI 5 (C2)
> - **Exam sessions**: June and November (CELI Impatto and CELI 1 also in March)
>
> **SECTIONS (4 sections):**
> - Section A: Reading Comprehension + Grammar
> - Section B: Written Production
> - Section C: Listening Comprehension
> - Section D: Oral Production
>
> **PASSING**: Each section scored separately. Pass each section with minimum required score. Failed sections can be retaken individually.

### 3.2 CELI Exam Duration by Level

| Level | Reading + Grammar | Writing | Listening | Speaking |
|-------|------------------|---------|-----------|---------|
| CELI Impatto (A1) | 60 min | 30 min | 15 min | 10 min |
| CELI 1 (A2) | 90 min | 40 min | 15 min | 10 min |
| CELI 2 (B1) | 120 min | 60 min | 20 min | 15 min |
| CELI 3 (B2) | 135 min | 90 min | 25 min | 15 min |
| CELI 4 (C1) | 150 min | 120 min | 30 min | 15 min |
| CELI 5 (C2) | 180 min | 150 min | 30 min | 20 min |

### 3.3 Key Difference from CILS

| Feature | CILS | CELI |
|---------|------|------|
| Grammar section | Separate section (Analisi delle strutture) | Integrated with Reading (Section A) |
| Number of sections | 5 | 4 |
| Issuing body | Siena | Perugia |
| Special versions | CILS Integrazione (for immigrants) | CELI i (for immigrants) |

## 4. PLIDA Exam (Società Dante Alighieri)

### 4.1 Overview

> **PLIDA — Progetto Lingua Italiana Dante Alighieri**
>
> - **Issuing body**: Società Dante Alighieri
> - **Recognition**: Italian Ministry of Education (MIUR), Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs
> - **Validity**: Lifetime (no expiration)
> - **Levels**: A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2
> - **Exam sessions**: Multiple per year (varies by center) — typically May, June, November
> - **Special**: PLIDA Juniores (for ages 13-18)
>
> **SECTIONS (4 sections):**
> 1. Ascoltare (Listening)
> 2. Leggere (Reading)
> 3. Scrivere (Writing)
> 4. Parlare (Speaking)
>
> **PASSING**: Minimum 18/30 per section (60%). Failed sections can be retaken within 18 months.

## 6. Italian Citizenship Language Requirement

### 6.1 Requirements

> **LANGUAGE REQUIREMENT FOR ITALIAN CITIZENSHIP**
>
> Since December 2018 (Decreto Sicurezza):
>
> **Minimum level**: B1 (CEFR)
>
> **Accepted certificates:**
> - CILS B1 (Uno) or higher
> - CELI 2 (B1) or higher
> - PLIDA B1 or higher
> - Certificate from an Italian university (with B1+ level courses)
>
> **Exemptions:**
> - Holders of an Italian education qualification
> - Signatories of an "accordo di integrazione" who have obtained the relevant credits
> - Holders of an Italian long-term residence permit (permesso di soggiorno di lungo periodo)
>
> **Cost**: Varies by exam center (typically 50-150 EUR)

### 6.2 What B1 Level Means

At B1 level, you should be able to:

| Skill | B1 Descriptor |
|-------|--------------|
| **Listening** | Understand the main points of standard speech on familiar topics (work, school, leisure) |
| **Reading** | Understand texts on everyday topics and personal interests |
| **Writing** | Write simple connected text on familiar topics and personal experiences |
| **Speaking** | Deal with most situations while traveling, describe experiences, give reasons for opinions |
| **Grammar** | Use present, past, future tenses correctly; conditional; basic subjunctive; relative clauses |
| **Vocabulary** | ~3,000-4,000 words covering everyday topics |

## 7. Listening Section Strategies

### 7.1 General Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Read questions first** | Before the audio plays, read ALL questions to know what to listen for |
| **First listening: overall meaning** | Don't try to understand every word; focus on the general message |
| **Second listening: specific details** | Focus on the information needed to answer each question |
| **Key words** | Identify key words in the questions and listen for them or synonyms |
| **Distractors** | Be aware that incorrect options often contain words from the audio |
| **Don't panic** | Missing one answer should not affect your concentration on the next |

### 7.2 Common Audio Types by Level

| Level | Audio Types |
|-------|-----------|
| **A1-A2** | Short dialogues, announcements, phone messages, simple descriptions |
| **B1** | Radio programs, conversations, news summaries, interviews |
| **B2** | Lectures, debates, extended interviews, news reports |
| **C1-C2** | Academic lectures, complex debates, literary readings, specialized content |

### 7.3 Listening Practice Tips

| Tip | How to Practice |
|-----|----------------|
| RAI Radio/TV | Listen to RAI Radio 1 or watch RAI News for authentic Italian |
| Podcasts | "Coffee Break Italian", "News in Slow Italian" for learners |
| Speed practice | Start with slow/clear speech, gradually increase to natural speed |
| Subtitles | Watch Italian films with Italian subtitles (not English!) |
| Dictation | Write down what you hear; compare with transcript |

## 8. Reading Section Strategies

### 8.1 General Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Skim first** | Read the whole text quickly to understand the general idea |
| **Read questions** | Know what information you need before detailed reading |
| **Key words** | Underline or circle key words in questions and text |
| **Process of elimination** | For multiple choice, eliminate clearly wrong answers first |
| **Don't translate** | Think in Italian; avoid translating word-by-word to your language |
| **Context clues** | Use surrounding words to guess unknown vocabulary |
| **Time management** | Don't spend too long on one question; move on and come back |

### 8.2 Common Text Types

| Level | Text Types |
|-------|-----------|
| **A1-A2** | Signs, menus, simple emails, short ads, form filling |
| **B1** | Newspaper articles, informal letters, brochures, blog posts |
| **B2** | Complex articles, formal letters, literature excerpts, essays |
| **C1-C2** | Academic papers, literary texts, legal documents, specialized articles |

### 8.3 Grammar Focus Areas for Reading

| Level | Grammar Focus |
|-------|-------------|
| B1 | Present, passato prossimo, imperfetto, future, basic conditional, relative pronouns |
| B2 | Subjunctive (present + past), conditional perfect, passive voice, indirect speech |
| C1 | All subjunctive forms, gerund constructions, complex subordination, literary tenses |
| C2 | All above + passato remoto (literary), implicit clauses, register variation |

## 9. Writing Section Strategies

### 9.1 General Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Read the prompt carefully** | Understand exactly what is being asked (format, topic, length) |
| **Plan before writing** | Spend 5 minutes organizing your ideas |
| **Follow the structure** | Introduction → body → conclusion (for essays/letters) |
| **Use connectors** | Show logical connections between ideas |
| **Vary vocabulary** | Avoid repeating the same words; use synonyms |
| **Check grammar** | Leave 5 minutes to review verb forms, agreements, accents |
| **Word count** | Stay within the required word count (usually +/- 10%) |
| **Accent marks** | Ensure all accents are correctly placed (perché, è, città, più) |

### 9.2 Writing Task Types by Level

| Level | Task Types | Word Count |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **A1-A2** | Fill in a form, write a short email or postcard, simple description | 50-80 words |
| **B1** | Personal email/letter, describe an experience, express an opinion | 120-180 words |
| **B2** | Formal letter, article, essay, review, report | 200-250 words |
| **C1** | Complex essay, formal report, argumentative text, summary | 250-300 words |
| **C2** | Academic essay, critical review, synthesis of multiple texts | 300-400 words |

### 9.3 Key Phrases for Different Writing Tasks

| Task | Useful Phrases |
|------|---------------|
| **Email (informal)** | Ciao [Nome]! Ti scrivo per... / Un abbraccio / A presto! |
| **Email (formal)** | Gentile Dott./Sig., Le scrivo per... / Cordiali saluti |
| **Opinion essay** | A mio avviso... / Credo che... / D'altra parte... / In conclusione... |
| **Description** | Si tratta di... / È caratterizzato da... / Si trova... |
| **Narration** | Un giorno... / Improvvisamente... / Alla fine... |

## 10. Speaking Section Strategies

### 10.1 General Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Don't panic** | Nervousness is normal; take a deep breath before starting |
| **Listen to the examiner** | Make sure you understand the question before answering |
| **Ask for clarification** | "Potrebbe ripetere, per favore?" is perfectly acceptable |
| **Don't memorize scripts** | Examiners can tell; be natural and spontaneous |
| **Self-correct** | If you make a mistake, correct yourself (this shows awareness) |
| **Use fillers naturally** | Allora... / Dunque... / Ecco... (to give yourself thinking time) |
| **Develop your answers** | Don't just say "Sì" or "No" -- explain, give examples, add details |
| **Show range** | Use a variety of tenses, structures, and vocabulary |

### 10.2 Speaking Task Types by Level

| Level | Task Types |
|-------|-----------|
| **A1-A2** | Self-introduction, simple Q&A about daily life, ordering food |
| **B1** | Describe a photo/situation, role-play (at a shop, hotel, etc.), discuss a familiar topic |
| **B2** | Express and defend an opinion, discuss advantages/disadvantages, react to a text or image |
| **C1** | Present an argument, debate a topic, summarize and comment on a text |
| **C2** | Complex discussion, negotiate, analyze multiple viewpoints, present a nuanced position |

### 10.3 Common Speaking Topics

| Level | Topics |
|-------|--------|
| **B1** | Family, hobbies, travel, work, education, food, daily routine, weather, city vs. countryside |
| **B2** | Environment, technology, media, health, globalization, immigration, education system, work-life balance |
| **C1-C2** | Politics, economy, ethics, science, culture, social issues, philosophy, comparative analysis |

## 11. Sample Exercises (B1-B2)

### 11.1 Sample Reading Exercise (B1)

Read the text and answer the questions.

> **Vivere in Italia: il fascino delle piccole città**
>
> Sempre più stranieri scelgono di trasferirsi nelle piccole città italiane, attratti dalla qualità della vita, dal cibo genuino e dal ritmo più lento rispetto alle grandi metropoli. Secondo un recente sondaggio, il 45% degli expatriati in Italia preferisce vivere in centri con meno di 50.000 abitanti.
>
> "Mi sono trasferita a Lucca tre anni fa," racconta Sarah, 35 anni, americana. "A New York lavoravo 12 ore al giorno e non avevo tempo per niente. Qui la vita è completamente diversa: faccio la spesa al mercato, conosco i miei vicini, e ogni sera faccio una passeggiata sulle mura della città."
>
> Tuttavia, non tutto è rose e fiori. Molti stranieri lamentano la burocrazia complicata, le difficoltà con la lingua e, in alcuni casi, la mancanza di opportunità lavorative. "Trovare lavoro non è stato facile," ammette Thomas, 40 anni, tedesco, che vive a Orvieto. "Ho dovuto reinventarmi completamente e ho aperto un bed and breakfast."
>
> Nonostante le sfide, la maggior parte degli intervistati si dichiara soddisfatta della propria scelta. "Non tornerei mai indietro," conclude Sarah. "L'Italia mi ha insegnato a vivere, non solo a lavorare."

**Questions:**

1. Why are foreigners moving to small Italian cities?
2. What percentage of expatriates prefer towns with fewer than 50,000 inhabitants?
3. How has Sarah's life changed since moving to Lucca?
4. What challenges do foreigners face?
5. How did Thomas solve the employment problem?
6. What does Sarah say about returning to her previous life?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. They are attracted by the **quality of life, genuine food, and slower pace** compared to big cities.
2. **45%** of expatriates prefer smaller towns.
3. She went from working **12 hours a day** in New York with no free time to shopping at the market, knowing her neighbors, and **taking evening walks** on the city walls.
4. **Complicated bureaucracy, language difficulties, and lack of job opportunities** in some cases.
5. He **reinvented himself and opened a bed and breakfast** (*ha aperto un bed and breakfast*).
6. She says she would **never go back** (*Non tornerei mai indietro*) -- Italy taught her to live, not just work.

</details>

### 11.2 Sample Grammar Exercise (B1-B2)

Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb in parentheses.

1. Penso che Maria ________ (essere) molto intelligente.
2. Se io ________ (avere) più tempo, viaggerei di più.
3. Quando ero piccolo, ________ (andare) al mare ogni estate.
4. Mi hanno detto che il treno ________ (arrivare) in ritardo domani.
5. Vorrei che tu ________ (venire) alla festa.
6. Nonostante ________ (piovere), siamo usciti lo stesso.
7. Se ieri non ________ (piovere), saremmo andati al parco.
8. È il film più bello che io ________ (vedere) mai.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **sia** (congiuntivo presente -- after *penso che*)
2. **avessi** (congiuntivo imperfetto -- *se* + imperfect subjunctive in hypothetical)
3. **andavo** (imperfetto -- habitual past action)
4. **arriverà** (futuro semplice -- future event)
5. **venissi** (congiuntivo imperfetto -- wish/desire about present/future)
6. **piovesse** (congiuntivo imperfetto -- concessive clause with *nonostante*)
7. **avesse piovuto** (congiuntivo trapassato -- unreal past condition) OR **fosse piovuto**
8. **abbia visto** (congiuntivo passato -- superlative + relative clause)

</details>

4. **d (B2)** -- Abstract topics + defending opinions = Upper Intermediate
5. **c (B1)** -- Travel situations + expressing opinions = Intermediate
6. **e (C1)** -- Professional and academic fluency = Advanced

</details>

### Exercise 3: Grammar for Exam (B1-B2)

Choose the correct answer.

1. Se io ________ italiano, viaggerei in Italia ogni anno.
   - a) parlo  b) parlassi  c) parlerò  d) parlerei

2. Credo che Marco ________ a Roma l'anno scorso.
   - a) è andato  b) sia andato  c) andava  d) andrà

3. Sebbene ________ molto, non ha superato l'esame.
   - a) ha studiato  b) studia  c) abbia studiato  d) studierà

4. Mi hanno detto che la riunione ________ rinviata.
   - a) è  b) è stata  c) sia  d) fosse stata

5. Non conosco nessuno che ________ il cinese e l'arabo.
   - a) parla  b) parli  c) parlando  d) parlare

6. Prima di ________, controlla i documenti.
   - a) parti  b) partire  c) partendo  d) parta

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **b) parlassi** -- *Se* + imperfect subjunctive for hypothetical conditions (Type 2 conditional)
2. **b) sia andato** -- *Credo che* triggers subjunctive; past event → *congiuntivo passato*
3. **c) abbia studiato** -- *Sebbene* always triggers subjunctive; completed action → past subjunctive
4. **b) è stata** -- Reported speech with *hanno detto che* + indicative (factual report)
5. **b) parli** -- *Nessuno che* + subjunctive (expressing uncertainty/non-existence)
6. **b) partire** -- *Prima di* + infinitive (before doing something)

</details>

### Exercise 5: Strategy Application

For each scenario, identify the best exam strategy.

1. During the listening section, you miss the answer to question 3.
   - a) Rewind the audio  b) Keep focusing on the next questions  c) Leave the exam

2. In the reading section, you encounter a word you don't know.
   - a) Look it up in a dictionary  b) Skip the entire text  c) Use context clues to guess the meaning

3. In the writing section, you finish 10 minutes early.
   - a) Leave immediately  b) Review for grammar, spelling, and accent errors  c) Add more text to fill the page

4. In the speaking section, you don't understand the examiner's question.
   - a) Guess and answer something random  b) Stay silent  c) Ask: "Potrebbe ripetere, per favore?"

5. You have 3 months before the exam and are currently at A2.
   - a) Sign up for C2  b) Sign up for B1 and study intensively  c) Sign up for A1

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **b) Keep focusing on the next questions** -- Don't let one missed answer ruin your concentration for the rest.
2. **c) Use context clues to guess the meaning** -- Dictionaries are not allowed; context clues are your best tool.
3. **b) Review for grammar, spelling, and accent errors** -- Always use extra time to proofread!
4. **c) Ask: "Potrebbe ripetere, per favore?"** -- Asking for clarification is perfectly acceptable and shows communication skills.
5. **b) Sign up for B1 and study intensively** -- A realistic target one level above current ability is best.

</details>
