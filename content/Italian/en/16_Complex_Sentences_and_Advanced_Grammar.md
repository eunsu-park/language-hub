## 1. Relative Pronouns: CHE

**Che** is the most common relative pronoun. It can refer to people or things and serves as either **subject** or **direct object** of the relative clause.

### CHE as Subject

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| L'uomo **che** parla è mio padre. | The man who is speaking is my father. |
| Il libro **che** è sul tavolo è mio. | The book that is on the table is mine. |
| La ragazza **che** studia medicina è brava. | The girl who studies medicine is talented. |
| I treni **che** partono da qui vanno a Roma. | The trains that leave from here go to Rome. |

### CHE as Direct Object

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Il libro **che** leggo è interessante. | The book (that) I'm reading is interesting. |
| La persona **che** ho incontrato è simpatica. | The person (that) I met is nice. |
| Le cose **che** dici sono vere. | The things (that) you say are true. |
| Il film **che** abbiamo visto era bellissimo. | The film (that) we saw was beautiful. |

> **Key point**: Unlike English, Italian **cannot** omit the relative pronoun. You must always include *che*:
> - The book I read → Il libro **che** ho letto (NOT *Il libro ho letto*)

## 2. Relative Pronouns: CUI

**Cui** is used with **prepositions** (as indirect object or after prepositions). It is invariable (does not change for gender or number).

### Preposition + CUI

| Preposition + cui | English | Example |
|-------------------|---------|---------|
| **a cui** | to whom / to which | La persona **a cui** ho scritto. (The person to whom I wrote.) |
| **di cui** | of whom / about which | Il libro **di cui** ti ho parlato. (The book I told you about.) |
| **con cui** | with whom | L'amico **con cui** viaggio. (The friend with whom I travel.) |
| **per cui** | for whom / for which | Il motivo **per cui** sono qui. (The reason I am here.) |
| **in cui** | in which / when | Il giorno **in cui** ci siamo incontrati. (The day we met.) |
| **da cui** | from whom / from which | La città **da cui** vengo. (The city I come from.) |
| **su cui** | on which / about which | Il tavolo **su cui** ho messo il libro. (The table on which I put the book.) |
| **tra/fra cui** | among which/whom | Gli studenti **tra cui** c'ero anch'io. (The students, among whom I was.) |

### CUI Without a Preposition = "To Whom"

When *cui* appears between the verb and an indirect object without an explicit preposition, it implies **a cui**:

- La persona **cui** ho scritto. = La persona **a cui** ho scritto.
  (The person to whom I wrote.) — This form is literary/formal.

### Article + CUI = "Whose"

The construction **article + cui** translates as "whose." The article agrees with the **possessed noun** (not the possessor).

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| L'uomo **il cui** figlio è medico. | The man whose son is a doctor. |
| La donna **la cui** borsa è rossa. | The woman whose bag is red. |
| I ragazzi **i cui** genitori sono qui. | The boys whose parents are here. |
| Le case **le cui** porte sono chiuse. | The houses whose doors are closed. |

## 3. Relative Pronouns: IL QUALE / LA QUALE

**Il quale / la quale / i quali / le quali** are more formal alternatives to *che* and *cui*. They agree in gender and number with the antecedent and are particularly useful when the antecedent might be ambiguous.

### Forms

| Gender/Number | Form |
|---------------|------|
| Masculine singular | **il quale** |
| Feminine singular | **la quale** |
| Masculine plural | **i quali** |
| Feminine plural | **le quali** |

### With Prepositions (Contractions Apply)

| Preposition + article | Example |
|----------------------|---------|
| **del quale** / **della quale** | La ragazza della quale ti ho parlato. (The girl I told you about.) |
| **al quale** / **alla quale** | L'uomo al quale ho dato il libro. (The man to whom I gave the book.) |
| **nel quale** / **nella quale** | La stanza nella quale lavoro. (The room in which I work.) |
| **con il quale** / **con la quale** | L'amico con il quale viaggio. (The friend with whom I travel.) |
| **per il quale** / **per la quale** | Il motivo per il quale sono qui. (The reason I am here.) |

### When to Use IL QUALE vs. CHE/CUI

| Context | Preferred form |
|---------|---------------|
| Everyday speech | che / cui |
| Formal writing | il quale / la quale |
| Ambiguous antecedent | il quale / la quale (for clarity) |

**Ambiguity example**:
- La madre di Marco, **che** vive a Roma... — Who lives in Rome? Marco or his mother?
- La madre di Marco, **la quale** vive a Roma... — Clearly **the mother** (feminine).
- La madre di Marco, **il quale** vive a Roma... — Clearly **Marco** (masculine).

## 4. Relative Pronoun: CHI

**Chi** means "he who / she who / whoever / anyone who." It combines the antecedent and relative pronoun into one word, always taking a **singular** verb.

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| **Chi** dorme non piglia pesci. | He who sleeps doesn't catch fish. (Proverb) |
| **Chi** cerca, trova. | He who seeks, finds. |
| **Chi** vuole venire, venga. | Whoever wants to come, let them come. |
| Non capisco **chi** parla troppo veloce. | I don't understand people who speak too fast. |
| Dillo a **chi** vuoi. | Tell it to whoever you want. |
| **C'è chi** dice di sì e **chi** dice di no. | There are those who say yes and those who say no. |

## 5. Restrictive vs. Non-Restrictive Clauses

### Restrictive (No Comma) — Essential Information

The relative clause is necessary to identify the noun. Without it, the meaning changes.

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Gli studenti **che studiano** superano l'esame. | The students **who study** pass the exam. (Not all students — only those who study.) |
| I libri **che ho comprato** sono sul tavolo. | The books **that I bought** are on the table. (Only those specific books.) |

### Non-Restrictive (With Comma) — Additional Information

The relative clause adds extra information. Removing it doesn't change the fundamental meaning.

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Marco**, che è il mio migliore amico,** vive a Roma. | Marco, who is my best friend, lives in Rome. |
| Roma**, che è la capitale d'Italia,** è bellissima. | Rome, which is the capital of Italy, is beautiful. |
| I miei genitori**, che vivono a Milano,** vengono a trovarmi. | My parents, who live in Milan, are coming to visit me. |

> **Note**: In non-restrictive clauses, *il quale / la quale* can replace *che* and is often preferred in formal writing.

## 6. Passive Voice: Complete Review

### Four Passive Constructions

| Construction | Tenses | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| **essere + pp** | All tenses | La casa **è stata costruita** nel 1900. |
| **venire + pp** | Simple tenses only | La porta **viene chiusa** alle otto. |
| **andare + pp** | Obligation/necessity | Questo lavoro **va fatto** subito. |
| **SI passivante** | Simple tenses mainly | Qui **si parla** italiano. |

### Essere + PP in All Tenses

| Tense | Example | English |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Presente | La pizza **è preparata** dal cuoco. | The pizza is prepared by the chef. |
| Passato prossimo | La lettera **è stata scritta** da Marco. | The letter was written by Marco. |
| Imperfetto | La cena **era servita** alle otto. | Dinner was served at eight. |
| Futuro | Il progetto **sarà completato** domani. | The project will be completed tomorrow. |
| Condizionale | La casa **sarebbe venduta** rapidamente. | The house would be sold quickly. |
| Congiuntivo | Penso che **sia stato fatto** bene. | I think it was done well. |

### Agent: DA

The agent (who performs the action) is introduced by **da**:

- Il libro è stato scritto **da** un autore famoso. (The book was written by a famous author.)
- La torta è stata fatta **dalla** nonna. (The cake was made by grandma.)

## 7. Advanced CI Uses

**CI** has multiple functions beyond its basic meaning of "here/there."

### CI = There (Location)

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| **Ci** vado domani. | I'm going there tomorrow. |
| **Ci** sono molte persone. | There are many people. |
| Non **ci** sono mai stato. | I've never been there. |

### CI in Idiomatic Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **volerci** | to be necessary / to take (time) | **Ci vuole** un'ora. (It takes an hour.) / **Ci vogliono** due ore. (It takes two hours.) |
| **metterci** | to take (personal effort/time) | **Ci metto** dieci minuti. (It takes me ten minutes.) |
| **pensarci** | to think about it | **Ci penso** io. (I'll take care of it.) |
| **farcela** | to manage / succeed | Non **ce la faccio** più! (I can't take it anymore!) |
| **entrarci** | to have to do with | Questo non **c'entra** niente. (This has nothing to do with it.) |
| **crederci** | to believe in it | Non **ci credo**! (I don't believe it!) |
| **contarci** | to count on it | **Ci conto**! (I'm counting on it!) |
| **tenerci** | to care about it | **Ci tengo** molto. (I care about it a lot.) |
| **vederci** | to be able to see | Non **ci vedo** bene. (I can't see well.) |
| **sentirci** | to be able to hear | Non **ci sento** bene. (I can't hear well.) |

### Volerci vs. Metterci

| Volerci (impersonal) | Metterci (personal) |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Ci vuole** pazienza. (Patience is needed.) | **Ci metto** un'ora. (It takes me an hour.) |
| **Ci vogliono** tre ore in treno. (It takes three hours by train.) | Quanto **ci metti** ad arrivare? (How long does it take you to arrive?) |
| **Ci vuole** molto tempo. (It takes a lot of time.) | **Ci ho messo** tutto il giorno. (It took me all day.) |

## 8. Advanced NE Uses

**NE** replaces partitive or prepositional phrases with **di**.

### NE = Of It/Them (Partitive)

| Question | Answer with NE |
|----------|---------------|
| Vuoi della pasta? | Sì, **ne** voglio un po'. (Yes, I want some.) |
| Quanti libri hai? | **Ne** ho dieci. (I have ten [of them].) |
| Hai comprato delle mele? | Sì, **ne** ho comprate tre. (Yes, I bought three.) |
| Bevi caffè? | Sì, **ne** bevo molto. (Yes, I drink a lot.) |

### NE in Idiomatic Expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **andarsene** | to go away/leave | Me **ne** vado! (I'm leaving!) |
| **averne abbastanza** | to have had enough | **Ne** ho abbastanza! (I've had enough!) |
| **non poterne più** | to not be able to take it anymore | Non **ne** posso più! (I can't take it anymore!) |
| **valerne la pena** | to be worth it | **Ne** vale la pena. (It's worth it.) |
| **parlarne** | to talk about it | **Ne** parliamo domani. (Let's talk about it tomorrow.) |
| **farne a meno** | to do without it | Non posso far**ne** a meno. (I can't do without it.) |
| **combinarne** | to get up to mischief | **Ne** ha combinata un'altra! (He's done it again!) |
| **intendersene** | to be knowledgeable about | **Se ne** intende di vini. (He knows about wines.) |

## 9. Combined Pronouns in Depth

When two object pronouns are used together, the **indirect object** comes first, then the **direct object** (or NE).

### Combined Pronoun Chart

| | lo | la | li | le | ne |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **mi** | me lo | me la | me li | me le | me ne |
| **ti** | te lo | te la | te li | te le | te ne |
| **gli/le/Le** | glielo | gliela | glieli | gliele | gliene |
| **ci** | ce lo | ce la | ce li | ce le | ce ne |
| **vi** | ve lo | ve la | ve li | ve le | ve ne |
| **gli (loro)** | glielo | gliela | glieli | gliele | gliene |

> **Key changes**:
> - **mi → me**, **ti → te**, **ci → ce**, **vi → ve** before another pronoun
> - **gli/le/Le** all become **glie-** and merge with the next pronoun into one word

### Examples

| Separate | Combined | English |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Mi dai il libro? | **Me lo** dai? | Will you give it to me? |
| Ti ho portato le chiavi. | **Te le** ho portate. | I brought them to you. |
| Gli ho detto la verità. | **Gliel'**ho detta. | I told it to him. |
| Ci hanno mandato i documenti. | **Ce li** hanno mandati. | They sent them to us. |
| Le ho comprato un regalo. | **Gliel'**ho comprato. | I bought it for her. |
| Quanti ne vuoi? — Due. | **Me ne** dai due? | Will you give me two of them? |

### Position of Combined Pronouns

| Context | Position | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Before conjugated verb | Before | **Me lo** dai? |
| With infinitive | Attached to end | Puoi dar**melo**? |
| With gerundio | Attached to end | Sta spiegando**glielo**. |
| With affirmative imperative (tu/noi/voi) | Attached to end | Da**mmelo**! |
| With negative imperative (tu) | Either | Non dar**melo**! / Non **me lo** dare! |
| With formal imperative (Lei) | Before | **Me lo** dia! |

## 10. Fare + Infinitive (Causative)

**Fare + infinitive** means "to make/have someone do something" or "to have something done." This is the causative construction.

### Basic Structure

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| **Faccio riparare** la macchina. | I'm having the car repaired. |
| **Ha fatto costruire** una casa. | He had a house built. |
| **Mi fa ridere**. | He makes me laugh. |
| **Fammi vedere**! | Let me see! / Show me! |
| **Ci ha fatto aspettare** un'ora. | He made us wait an hour. |

### With One Object (Direct Object)

When there is only one object (the person or thing acted upon), it is a direct object:

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Faccio **entrare** gli studenti. | I let the students in. / I have the students come in. |
| La notizia **ha fatto piangere** Maria. | The news made Maria cry. |
| Il professore **fa parlare** gli studenti. | The teacher has the students speak. |

### With Two Objects (Person = Indirect, Thing = Direct)

When both a doer and a thing are present, the person becomes an **indirect object** (with **a**):

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Faccio leggere il libro **a Marco**. | I have Marco read the book. |
| Ho fatto scrivere la lettera **a Maria**. | I had Maria write the letter. |
| Faccio lavare i piatti **ai bambini**. | I have the children wash the dishes. |

### With DA (to Avoid Ambiguity)

Sometimes **da** is used instead of **a** for the person performing the action, to avoid confusion:

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Ho fatto riparare la macchina **dal meccanico**. | I had the car repaired by the mechanic. |
| Faccio tradurre il testo **da un professionista**. | I'm having the text translated by a professional. |

### With Pronouns

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| La faccio riparare. (la = la macchina) | I'm having it repaired. |
| Lo faccio entrare. (lo = Marco) | I let him in. |
| Glielo faccio fare. | I'll have him/her do it. |
| Fammelo sapere! | Let me know (about it)! |

### Farsi + Infinitive (Reflexive Causative)

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Mi **faccio tagliare** i capelli. | I'm getting my hair cut. |
| Si è **fatta operare**. | She had surgery. (lit. She had herself operated on.) |
| Si è **fatto costruire** una villa. | He had a villa built for himself. |

## 11. Lasciare + Infinitive

**Lasciare + infinitive** means "to let/allow someone to do something."

### Basic Structure

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| **Lascia stare**! | Leave it alone! / Let it be! |
| **Lasciami parlare**! | Let me speak! |
| Non **lo lasciano uscire**. | They don't let him go out. |
| **Lascia perdere**! | Forget about it! / Never mind! |
| I genitori **lasciano giocare** i bambini. | The parents let the children play. |
| **Lasciala entrare**! | Let her in! |

### Fare vs. Lasciare

| fare + infinitive | lasciare + infinitive |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Implies **causing** or **having** someone do something | Implies **allowing** or **permitting** |
| Faccio lavorare Marco. (I make Marco work.) | Lascio lavorare Marco. (I let Marco work.) |
| Mi ha fatto aspettare. (He made me wait.) | Mi ha lasciato aspettare. (He let me wait.) |

### Exercise 1: Relative Pronouns

Fill in the blank with **che**, **cui** (+ preposition), **il/la quale**, or **chi**.

1. La ragazza ________ ho incontrato ieri è simpatica.
2. Il ristorante ________ ti ho parlato è in centro.
3. L'amico ________ viaggio è brasiliano.
4. ________ cerca trova.
5. La casa ________ porte sono rosse è la mia.
6. Il motivo ________ sono venuto è importante.
7. Maria, ________ vive a Roma, verrà a trovarci.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **che** (direct object)
2. **di cui** (parlare di)
3. **con cui** (viaggiare con)
4. **Chi** (whoever)
5. **le cui** (whose — le agrees with porte, f. pl.)
6. **per cui** / **per il quale** (the reason for which)
7. **che** / **la quale** (non-restrictive, subject)
