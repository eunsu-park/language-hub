## 1. Italian Verb System Overview

```
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|           ITALIAN VERB CLASSIFICATION                     |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                          |
|  By ending:   -ARE (most common: ~60% of verbs)          |
|               -ERE (second largest group)                 |
|               -IRE (two sub-groups: regular + -isc-)      |
|                                                          |
|  By regularity:  Regular                                 |
|                   Irregular (unique stems/patterns)        |
|                   -ISC- verbs (capire, finire, preferire) |
|                   Reflexive (-si ending: lavarsi)          |
|                                                          |
|  By mood:  Indicativo (facts, certainty)                  |
|            Congiuntivo (subjectivity, doubt)              |
|            Condizionale (hypothetical, polite)            |
|            Imperativo (commands)                          |
|                                                          |
|  Non-finite forms:  Infinito (parlare)                    |
|                     Gerundio (parlando)                   |
|                     Participio (parlato, parlante)        |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Indicative Tenses at a Glance

| Tense | Italian Name | Use |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Present | Presente | Current actions, habits, general truths |
| Imperfect | Imperfetto | Past habits, descriptions, ongoing states |
| Passato Prossimo | Passato prossimo | Completed past actions (spoken) |
| Passato Remoto | Passato remoto | Completed past actions (literary/historical) |
| Pluperfect | Trapassato prossimo | Action before another past action |
| Simple Future | Futuro semplice | Future actions, probability |
| Future Perfect | Futuro anteriore | Completed future, past probability |

## 5. Passato Remoto: Regular Formation

The passato remoto (remote past / preterite) is used for **completed actions in the distant past**, especially in literary writing and in southern Italian speech. In northern Italy and everyday conversation, the passato prossimo is preferred.

### Regular Endings

| Person | -ARE (parlare) | -ERE (credere) | -IRE (dormire) |
|--------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| io | parl**ai** | cred**ei** (cred**etti**) | dorm**ii** |
| tu | parl**asti** | cred**esti** | dorm**isti** |
| lui/lei | parl**ò** | cred**é** (cred**ette**) | dorm**ì** |
| noi | parl**ammo** | cred**emmo** | dorm**immo** |
| voi | parl**aste** | cred**este** | dorm**iste** |
| loro | parl**arono** | cred**erono** (cred**ettero**) | dorm**irono** |

> **Note**: -ERE verbs have two possible sets of endings for io, lui/lei, and loro. Both are correct, but the -etti/-ette/-ettero forms are more common with regular -ERE verbs.

## 7. Gerundio and Progressive (Stare + Gerundio)

### Gerundio Formation

| Infinitive ending | Gerundio ending | Example |
|-------------------|----------------|---------|
| **-ARE** | **-ando** | parlare → parl**ando** |
| **-ERE** | **-endo** | scrivere → scriv**endo** |
| **-IRE** | **-endo** | dormire → dorm**endo** |

### Irregular Gerundi

| Infinitive | Gerundio |
|------------|---------|
| fare | **facendo** |
| dire | **dicendo** |
| bere | **bevendo** |
| tradurre | **traducendo** |
| porre | **ponendo** |
| trarre | **traendo** |

### Stare + Gerundio (Progressive)

Italian uses **stare + gerundio** for actions in progress (like English "be + -ing").

| Tense | Formation | Example | English |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Present progressive** | sto + gerundio | Sto **parlando**. | I am speaking. |
| **Past progressive** | stavo + gerundio | Stavo **dormendo**. | I was sleeping. |
| **Future progressive** | starò + gerundio | Starò **lavorando**. | I will be working. |

### Examples

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Cosa **stai facendo**? | What are you doing? |
| **Sto leggendo** un libro. | I'm reading a book. |
| **Stava piovendo** quando sono uscito. | It was raining when I went out. |
| I bambini **stanno giocando** in giardino. | The children are playing in the garden. |
| Non mi disturbare, **sto studiando**! | Don't bother me, I'm studying! |

### Gerundio as an Adverbial Clause

The gerundio can also function like English "while/by/when + -ing":

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| **Camminando** per strada, ho incontrato Marco. | While walking down the street, I met Marco. |
| **Studiando** molto, ha superato l'esame. | By studying a lot, he passed the exam. |
| **Non avendo** soldi, non posso comprarlo. | Not having money, I can't buy it. |

## 8. Participio Passato: Agreement Rules

### Rule 1: With ESSERE — Always Agrees with Subject

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Maria **è andata**. (f. sing.) | Maria went. |
| I ragazzi **sono partiti**. (m. pl.) | The boys left. |
| Le ragazze **sono arrivate**. (f. pl.) | The girls arrived. |

### Rule 2: With AVERE — Agrees with Preceding Direct Object Pronoun

| Without pronoun (no agreement) | With pronoun (agreement) |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------|
| Ho mangiato **la pizza**. | **L'**ho mangiata. (la pizza = f. sing.) |
| Ho visto **i film**. | **Li** ho visti. (i film = m. pl.) |
| Ho letto **le lettere**. | **Le** ho lette. (le lettere = f. pl.) |
| Ho comprato **il libro**. | **L'**ho comprato. (il libro = m. sing.) |

### Rule 3: With AVERE — No Agreement with Indirect Object Pronouns

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Le ho **parlato**. (NOT *parlata*) | I spoke to her. (*le* = indirect object) |
| Gli ho **scritto**. (NOT *scritti*) | I wrote to him. (*gli* = indirect object) |

### Rule 4: With NE — Agreement

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Quante mele hai comprato? — **Ne** ho comprat**e** tre. | How many apples did you buy? I bought three (of them). |
| Quanti libri hai letto? — **Ne** ho lett**i** due. | How many books did you read? I read two (of them). |

## 9. Infinitive Constructions

### Preposition + Infinitive

| Construction | Example | English |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **di** + infinitive | Ho deciso **di partire**. | I decided to leave. |
| **a** + infinitive | Comincio **a capire**. | I'm beginning to understand. |
| **per** + infinitive | Studio **per imparare**. | I study to learn. |
| **senza** + infinitive | È uscito **senza parlare**. | He left without speaking. |
| **prima di** + infinitive | **Prima di uscire**, chiudi la porta. | Before leaving, close the door. |
| **dopo** + past infinitive | **Dopo aver mangiato**, siamo usciti. | After eating, we went out. |
| **invece di** + infinitive | **Invece di studiare**, guarda la TV. | Instead of studying, he watches TV. |

## 10. Passive Voice

### Formation with ESSERE + Participio Passato

The most common passive construction. The past participle agrees with the subject.

| Active | Passive |
|--------|---------|
| Marco mangia la pizza. | La pizza **è mangiata** da Marco. |
| Marco ha mangiato la pizza. | La pizza **è stata mangiata** da Marco. |
| Marco mangerà la pizza. | La pizza **sarà mangiata** da Marco. |

### Formation with VENIRE + Participio Passato

Used only with **simple tenses** (not compound). Emphasizes the action happening.

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| La porta **viene chiusa** alle otto. | The door is closed at eight. (habitual) |
| Questa legge **verrà approvata** domani. | This law will be approved tomorrow. |

> *Venire* passive cannot be used in compound tenses: NOT *La porta è venuta chiusa*.

### Formation with ANDARE + Participio Passato

Expresses obligation or necessity ("must be" / "should be").

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| Questo lavoro **va fatto** subito. | This work must be done immediately. |
| Le regole **vanno rispettate**. | The rules must be respected. |
| Il latte **va conservato** in frigo. | Milk must be kept in the fridge. |

## 11. SI Impersonale and SI Passivante

### SI Impersonale (Impersonal SI)

SI impersonale is equivalent to English "one," "you" (general), "people," or "they" (general). The verb is always in the **third person singular**.

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| In Italia **si mangia** bene. | In Italy, one eats well. / People eat well in Italy. |
| **Si dice** che sia ricco. | They say he is rich. |
| Come **si dice** "cat" in italiano? | How do you say "cat" in Italian? |
| Qui non **si può** fumare. | You can't smoke here. |
| **Si deve** studiare per imparare. | One must study to learn. |

> **With adjectives**: SI impersonale takes a **plural** adjective/noun, even though the verb is singular:
> - Se si è **stanchi**, bisogna riposare. (If one is tired, one must rest.)
> - Quando si è **giovani**, si ha più energia. (When you're young, you have more energy.)

### SI Passivante (Passive SI)

SI passivante is a passive construction where the subject comes after the verb. The verb agrees with the subject in number.

| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| **Si vendono** appartamenti. | Apartments are sold. / Apartments for sale. |
| Qui **si parla** italiano. | Italian is spoken here. |
| **Si affittano** camere. | Rooms for rent. |
| In questo negozio **si vendono** scarpe. | Shoes are sold in this shop. |
| Queste cose non **si fanno**! | These things are not done! |

### SI Impersonale vs. SI Passivante

| SI Impersonale | SI Passivante |
|----------------|---------------|
| Verb always **singular** | Verb agrees with subject (can be plural) |
| No true grammatical subject | Has a grammatical subject |
| Si mangia bene. (One eats well.) | Si mangiano gli spaghetti. (Spaghetti are eaten.) |
| Si deve lavorare. (One must work.) | Si devono pagare le tasse. (Taxes must be paid.) |
