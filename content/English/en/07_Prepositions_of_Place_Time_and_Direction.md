## 1. Prepositions vs Korean Postpositions

### 1.1 The Fundamental Difference

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   Korean: POSTPOSITIONS (조사) — come AFTER the noun            │
│   학교에    학교에서    학교로    학교까지                          │
│   school+에  school+에서  school+으로  school+까지                │
│                                                                 │
│   English: PREPOSITIONS — come BEFORE the noun                  │
│   at school   in school   to school   from school               │
│   AT+school   IN+school   TO+school   FROM+school               │
│                                                                 │
│   Korean: noun + particle (학교 + 에)                            │
│   English: preposition + noun (at + school)                     │
│   → The order is REVERSED!                                     │
│                                                                 │
│   ※ Korean particles map to multiple English prepositions:     │
│     에 → at, in, on, to (depends on context!)                  │
│     에서 → at, in, from (depends on context!)                   │
│     으로 → to, by, with, as (depends on context!)               │
│     This is WHY prepositions are so hard for Korean speakers.  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 2. in/on/at for Place

### 2.1 The in/on/at Place Hierarchy

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   at → specific POINT (점)                                      │
│   "at the bus stop"  "at the door"  "at school"                 │
│                                                                 │
│   on → SURFACE (면)                                              │
│   "on the table"  "on the wall"  "on the floor"                 │
│                                                                 │
│   in → ENCLOSED SPACE (안)                                       │
│   "in the room"  "in the box"  "in the car"                     │
│                                                                 │
│   Think of it as:                                               │
│   at = a dot •           (가장 좁은 개념)                        │
│   on = a surface ▬       (면 위에)                               │
│   in = a container □     (안에)                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 2.2 at — Specific Points

```
at + specific location/address:
  at the bus stop            버스 정류장에서
  at the door                문 앞에서
  at the traffic light       신호등에서
  at the corner              모퉁이에서
  at 123 Main Street         메인 스트리트 123번지에
  at the top / at the bottom 꼭대기에 / 바닥에

at + institutions (as a function/purpose):
  at school (studying)       학교에서 (공부 중)
  at work (working)          직장에서 (근무 중)
  at home                    집에서
  at the hospital            병원에서
  at the airport             공항에서

at + events:
  at a party                 파티에서
  at a meeting               회의에서
  at a concert               콘서트에서
```

### 2.3 on — Surfaces

```
on + horizontal surfaces:
  on the table               탁자 위에
  on the floor               바닥 위에
  on the ground              땅 위에
  on the shelf               선반 위에
  on the desk                책상 위에

on + vertical surfaces:
  on the wall                벽에
  on the ceiling             천장에
  on the screen              화면에

on + "lines" and routes:
  on the road                도로에서
  on the river               강에서
  on the border              국경에

on + transportation (that you "step onto"):
  on the bus                 버스에서
  on the train               기차에서
  on the plane               비행기에서
  on the ship                배에서
  ※ BUT: in the car, in the taxi (enclosed, personal vehicles)

on + floors:
  on the first floor         1층에
  on the second floor        2층에

on + media/technology:
  on TV / on the radio       TV/라디오에서
  on the internet            인터넷에서
  on the phone               전화 중
  on page 5                  5페이지에
```

### 2.4 in — Enclosed Spaces

```
in + containers and enclosed spaces:
  in the room                방 안에
  in the box                 상자 안에
  in the bag                 가방 안에
  in the drawer              서랍 안에
  in the cupboard            찬장 안에

in + buildings (inside):
  in the house               집 안에
  in the office              사무실 안에
  in the library             도서관 안에

in + geographic areas:
  in Korea                   한국에
  in Seoul                   서울에
  in Asia                    아시아에
  in the world               세계에서

in + personal vehicles:
  in the car                 차 안에
  in a taxi                  택시 안에

in + water/liquid:
  in the water               물 안에
  in the pool                수영장에서
  in the sea                 바다에서

in + other:
  in a line / in a queue     줄에
  in a book                  책에서 (written content)
  in the newspaper           신문에서
  in a photo                 사진에서
  in the mirror              거울에서
  in the sky                 하늘에
```

## 3. in/on/at for Time

### 3.1 The Time Hierarchy

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   at → specific TIME POINT (시점)                                │
│   "at 3 o'clock"  "at noon"  "at midnight"  "at night"          │
│                                                                 │
│   on → specific DAY or DATE (날)                                 │
│   "on Monday"  "on March 5th"  "on Christmas Day"               │
│                                                                 │
│   in → LONGER PERIODS (기간)                                     │
│   "in March"  "in 2024"  "in summer"  "in the morning"          │
│                                                                 │
│   at = point of time  •                                         │
│   on = day           ●                                          │
│   in = period        ━━━━━                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 3.2 at — Time Points

```
at + clock time:
  at 3 o'clock               3시에
  at 7:30                    7시 30분에
  at noon                    정오에
  at midnight                자정에

at + meal times:
  at breakfast               아침 식사 시간에
  at lunchtime               점심시간에
  at dinnertime              저녁 식사 시간에

at + special times:
  at night                   밤에    ※ NOT "in the night"!
  at the weekend (BrE)       주말에   (AmE: on the weekend)
  at Christmas / at Easter   크리스마스에 / 부활절에
  at the moment              지금, 현재
  at the same time           동시에
```

### 3.3 on — Days and Dates

```
on + days of the week:
  on Monday                  월요일에
  on Friday afternoon        금요일 오후에
  on Sunday morning          일요일 아침에

on + specific dates:
  on March 5th               3월 5일에
  on January 1st, 2024       2024년 1월 1일에

on + special days:
  on my birthday             내 생일에
  on Christmas Day           크리스마스 날에
  on New Year's Day          새해 첫날에
  on Independence Day        독립기념일에

on + day with adjective:
  on a cold day              추운 날에
  on a sunny morning         화창한 아침에
```

### 3.4 in — Periods

```
in + months:
  in January / in March      1월에 / 3월에

in + years:
  in 2024 / in 1998          2024년에 / 1998년에

in + seasons:
  in spring / in summer      봄에 / 여름에
  in fall (autumn) / in winter  가을에 / 겨울에

in + parts of day:
  in the morning              아침에
  in the afternoon            오후에
  in the evening              저녁에
  ※ BUT: at night (NOT "in the night")!

in + centuries/decades:
  in the 21st century        21세기에
  in the 1990s               1990년대에

in + time-until-something:
  "I'll be there in 5 minutes."     5분 후에 도착할게
  "She'll finish in an hour."       1시간 후에 끝날 거야
  ※ "in 5 minutes" = 5분 뒤에 (future)
     "5 minutes ago" = 5분 전에 (past)
```

### 3.5 No Preposition: this/last/next/every

```
NO preposition with this/last/next/every:

  this morning       (NOT "in this morning")     오늘 아침
  last week          (NOT "in last week")         지난주
  next year          (NOT "in next year")         내년
  every day          (NOT "in every day")         매일
  yesterday          (NOT "on yesterday")         어제
  tomorrow           (NOT "on tomorrow")          내일
  today              (NOT "on today")             오늘

  ※ This is a VERY common Korean speaker error:
    "I went there in last week." ✗
    "I went there last week." ✓
```

## 4. Direction and Movement

### 4.1 Basic Direction Prepositions

```
to      → destination/direction     ~로, ~에       I go TO school.
from    → origin/starting point     ~에서, ~로부터  I come FROM Korea.
into    → entering (outside → inside) ~안으로       She walked INTO the room.
out of  → exiting (inside → outside)  ~에서 밖으로  He ran OUT OF the house.
toward(s) → direction (not arrival)  ~쪽으로       She walked TOWARD the park.
through → from one side to other    ~을 통해       We drove THROUGH the tunnel.
across  → from one side to other (surface) ~를 건너 Walk ACROSS the bridge.
along   → following a line          ~을 따라       Walk ALONG the river.
past    → going by                  ~을 지나서     Drive PAST the hospital.
up      → higher                    위로           Walk UP the stairs.
down    → lower                     아래로         Go DOWN the hill.
around  → circular movement         ~주위를        Walk AROUND the park.
```

### 4.2 to vs toward

```
to = arrival at destination:
  "I went to school."      (I arrived at school)

toward(s) = direction, no guarantee of arrival:
  "I walked toward the station." (I moved in that direction)
```

### 4.3 Korean Postposition Mapping

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   Korean 에:                                                     │
│   학교에 간다    → go TO school     (direction/목적지)            │
│   학교에 있다    → be AT/IN school  (location/위치)               │
│                                                                 │
│   Korean 에서:                                                   │
│   학교에서 공부한다 → study AT/IN school  (location of action)    │
│   서울에서 왔다    → come FROM Seoul     (origin/출발점)          │
│                                                                 │
│   Korean 으로/로:                                                │
│   학교로 간다     → go TO school      (direction)                │
│   버스로 간다     → go BY bus         (means)                     │
│   한국어로 말한다  → speak IN Korean   (language)                 │
│                                                                 │
│   Korean 까지:                                                   │
│   학교까지 걸었다  → walked TO school  (endpoint)                 │
│   3시까지        → BY 3 o'clock / UNTIL 3 o'clock               │
│                                                                 │
│   ※ One Korean particle maps to MANY English prepositions!    │
│     You must learn which English preposition fits each context. │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 5. Other Essential Prepositions

### 5.1 by / with / for / about / of

```
by — method, agent, deadline:
  by bus / by car / by train      버스/차/기차로 (교통수단)
  by email / by phone             이메일/전화로 (수단)
  by 5 o'clock                    5시까지 (마감)
  a book by Tolkien               톨킨에 의한 책 (행위자)
  by the window                   창가에 (위치 — near)

with — accompaniment, instrument:
  with my friend                  내 친구와 (함께)
  with a knife                    칼로 (도구)
  with sugar                      설탕을 넣은 (포함)
  a man with a hat                모자를 쓴 남자 (소유/특징)

for — purpose, beneficiary, duration:
  for you                         너를 위해 (수혜자)
  for breakfast                   아침으로 (용도)
  for two hours                   2시간 동안 (기간)
  for example                     예를 들어

about — topic:
  about Korea                     한국에 대해
  about 30 minutes                약 30분 (approximately)
  think about                     ~에 대해 생각하다
  talk about                      ~에 대해 이야기하다

of — possession, part, material:
  the capital of Korea            한국의 수도
  a cup of coffee                 커피 한 잔
  made of wood                    나무로 만든
  most of the students            학생들의 대부분
```

### 5.2 between / among / during / until

```
between — two things:
  between Seoul and Busan         서울과 부산 사이에
  between Monday and Friday       월요일과 금요일 사이에

among — three or more:
  among friends                   친구들 사이에서
  among the students              학생들 중에서

during — within a time period:
  during the summer               여름 동안
  during the meeting              회의 중에
  during lunch                    점심시간 동안

until (till) — up to a point in time:
  until 5 o'clock                 5시까지
  until Monday                    월요일까지
  until she comes                 그녀가 올 때까지
```

## 6. Preposition + Noun Collocations

### 6.1 Fixed Expressions

```
at:  at home, at work, at school, at war, at peace, at risk,
     at first, at last, at least, at once, at present

on:  on time (정시에), on fire (불타는), on sale (할인 중),
     on purpose (고의로), on vacation (휴가 중), on TV,
     on foot (걸어서), on the phone, on average

in:  in time (제시간에), in fact (사실은), in general (일반적으로),
     in love (사랑에 빠진), in trouble (곤경에 빠진),
     in English (영어로), in a hurry (급히), in common (공통으로)

by:  by accident (우연히), by chance (우연히),
     by mistake (실수로), by heart (암기하여)

for: for fun (재미로), for free (무료로), for sure (확실히),
     for sale (판매 중), for a while (잠시 동안)

out of: out of order (고장난), out of date (기한이 지난),
        out of control (통제 불능의)
```

### 6.2 on time vs in time

```
on time = exactly at the scheduled time (정시에)
  "The train arrived on time." (기차가 정시에 도착했다.)

in time = before the deadline, not late (제시간에)
  "We arrived in time for the movie." (영화에 맞춰 도착했다.)

  ※ "on time" = 정확히 그 시간에
    "in time" = 늦지 않게, 충분한 시간을 가지고
```

## 9. Common Mistakes by Korean Speakers

### 9.1 Error Chart

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   Error                          Correct                        │
│   ─────                          ───────                        │
│   "I go to home."                "I go home."                   │
│   (home doesn't need "to")       ※ home = adverb here          │
│                                                                 │
│   "I arrive to Seoul."           "I arrive in Seoul."           │
│   (arrive uses in/at, not to)                                   │
│                                                                 │
│   "I arrived at Seoul."          "I arrived in Seoul."          │
│   (city = in, point/building = at)                              │
│                                                                 │
│   "in last Monday"               "last Monday"                  │
│   (no preposition with last/next/this)                          │
│                                                                 │
│   "I'm interested about..."      "I'm interested in..."        │
│   (interested + in, not about)                                  │
│                                                                 │
│   "She married with him."        "She married him."             │
│   (marry is transitive — no preposition)                        │
│                                                                 │
│   "I listen the music."          "I listen to the music."       │
│   (listen requires "to")                                        │
│                                                                 │
│   "Discuss about the problem."   "Discuss the problem."         │
│   (discuss is transitive — no "about")                          │
│                                                                 │
│   "I go to there."               "I go there."                  │
│   (there/here are adverbs — no "to")                            │
│                                                                 │
│   "She is good in cooking."      "She is good at cooking."      │
│   (good at, not in)                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```


### Exercise 5: Error Correction

```
1. "I go to home every day."
2. "She arrived to the airport."
3. "I was born at 1995."
4. "He is good in math."
5. "We discussed about the plan."
6. "I listen music every day."
7. "She married with him in 2020."
8. "I will go in next Monday."
```

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

```
1. "I go home every day."              (home = adverb, no "to")
2. "She arrived at the airport."       (arrive at/in, not to)
3. "I was born in 1995."              (year = in)
4. "He is good at math."              (good at, not in)
5. "We discussed the plan."           (discuss = transitive, no "about")
6. "I listen to music every day."     (listen to)
7. "She married him in 2020."         (marry = transitive, no "with")
8. "I will go next Monday."           (no preposition with next)
```

</details>

```
│                                                                 │
│   Place: at (point) / on (surface) / in (enclosed)             │
```
