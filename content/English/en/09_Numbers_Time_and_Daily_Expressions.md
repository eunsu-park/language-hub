## 1. Cardinal Numbers

### 1.1 Numbers 1-100

```
1 one          11 eleven       21 twenty-one
2 two          12 twelve       30 thirty
3 three        13 thirteen     40 forty (NOT "fourty")
4 four         14 fourteen     50 fifty
5 five         15 fifteen      60 sixty
6 six          16 sixteen      70 seventy
7 seven        17 seventeen    80 eighty
8 eight        18 eighteen     90 ninety
9 nine         19 nineteen     100 one hundred
10 ten         20 twenty

※ Teens (13-19) stress: thirTEEN, fourTEEN, fifTEEN
   Tens (30-90) stress: THIRty, FORty, FIFty
   Korean speakers often confuse: thirteen (13) vs thirty (30)
   Listen for stress: thirTEEN /θɜːrˈtiːn/ vs THIRty /ˈθɜːrti/
```

### 1.2 Large Numbers

> **100         one hundred / a hundred**
>
> 1,000       one thousand / a thousand
> 10,000      ten thousand
> 100,000     one hundred thousand / a hundred thousand
> 1,000,000   one million / a million
> 1,000,000,000  one billion / a billion
> ※ Korean uses 만 (10,000) as a base unit:
> 1만 = 10,000    10만 = 100,000    100만 = 1,000,000
> English uses thousand as a base unit:
> 10,000 = "ten thousand" (NOT "one man/만")
> 50,000 = "fifty thousand"
> 100,000 = "one hundred thousand"
> Commas separate every 3 digits: 1,000  10,000  1,000,000
> Korean separates every 4 digits: 1만  10만  100만
> This difference causes MANY conversion errors!
> Examples:
> 5,432 = five thousand four hundred (and) thirty-two
> 23,100 = twenty-three thousand one hundred
> 1,500,000 = one million five hundred thousand
> / one and a half million

### 1.3 Number Pronunciation Tips

```
"Hundred", "thousand", etc. — NO plural -s when used with a number:
  "five hundred" ✓     "five hundreds" ✗
  "two thousand" ✓     "two thousands" ✗
  "three million" ✓    "three millions" ✗

  BUT as vague quantity: "Hundreds of people came." ✓
                         "Thousands of books." ✓

"and" in numbers:
  AmE: often omitted     BrE: usually included
  205 = "two hundred (and) five"
  1,015 = "one thousand (and) fifteen"
```

## 2. Ordinal Numbers

### 2.1 Formation

```
1st  first        11th eleventh     21st twenty-first
2nd  second       12th twelfth      22nd twenty-second
3rd  third        13th thirteenth   23rd twenty-third
4th  fourth       14th fourteenth   30th thirtieth
5th  fifth        15th fifteenth    40th fortieth
6th  sixth        16th sixteenth    50th fiftieth
7th  seventh      17th seventeenth  60th sixtieth
8th  eighth       18th eighteenth   100th (one) hundredth
9th  ninth        19th nineteenth   1,000th (one) thousandth
10th tenth        20th twentieth    1,000,000th millionth

※ Irregulars to memorize: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 5th, 8th, 9th, 12th
  Most: add -th (four→fourth, six→sixth)
  Ending in -y: change to -ieth (twenty→twentieth)
```

### 2.2 Uses of Ordinal Numbers

```
Dates:
  March 5th (the fifth of March)    3월 5일

Floors:
  the first floor / the second floor  1층 / 2층

Rankings:
  She finished first / in first place. 그녀는 1등으로 끝냈다.

Fractions:
  1/3 = one third        2/3 = two thirds
  1/4 = one quarter      3/4 = three quarters
  1/5 = one fifth        4/5 = four fifths
  ※ 1/2 = one half (NOT "one second")
```

## 3. Telling Time

### 3.1 Three Formats

> Format 1: Digital (hours + minutes) — MOST COMMON in AmE
> 3:00  "three o'clock" / "three"
> 3:15  "three fifteen"
> 3:30  "three thirty"
> 3:45  "three forty-five"
> 3:05  "three oh five"  (oh = zero)
> Format 2: Traditional (past/to) — COMMON in BrE
> 3:00  "three o'clock"
> 3:15  "quarter past three"  (a quarter after three)
> 3:30  "half past three"
> 3:45  "quarter to four"     (15 minutes before 4)
> 3:10  "ten past three"      (10 minutes after 3)
> 3:50  "ten to four"         (10 minutes before 4)
> Format 3: 24-hour (military/formal)
> 15:00  "fifteen hundred" / "fifteen hundred hours"
> 08:30  "oh eight thirty"
> 23:45  "twenty-three forty-five"
> ※ Korean: 3시 15분 (시 + 분 — very straightforward)
> English: multiple formats — "three fifteen" is safest

### 3.2 AM and PM

```
AM = ante meridiem (before noon): midnight to 11:59 AM
PM = post meridiem (after noon):  12:00 PM to 11:59 PM

  12:00 AM = midnight (자정)
  12:00 PM = noon (정오)

  ※ Confusing: 12 AM = midnight, 12 PM = noon (counter-intuitive!)

Special times:
  noon       = 12:00 PM    정오
  midnight   = 12:00 AM    자정
  morning    = AM          아침
  afternoon  = PM (12-5)   오후
  evening    = PM (5-9)    저녁
  night      = PM (9-12)   밤
```

### 3.3 Asking and Telling Time

```
Asking:
  "What time is it?"              몇 시예요?
  "What's the time?"              몇 시예요? (informal)
  "Do you have the time?"         지금 몇 시예요? (polite)
  "Could you tell me the time?"   시간 좀 알려주시겠어요? (very polite)

  ※ "Do you have the time?" = asking for clock time
    "Do you have time?" = asking if someone is free (시간 있어요?)

Answering:
  "It's three o'clock."           3시예요.
  "It's three fifteen." / "It's quarter past three."  3시 15분이에요.
  "It's about / around three."    대략 3시쯤이에요.
```

## 4. Days of the Week

### 4.1 The Seven Days

```
Monday     /ˈmʌndeɪ/    월요일    (Mon.)
Tuesday    /ˈtuːzdeɪ/   화요일    (Tue./Tues.)
Wednesday  /ˈwenzdeɪ/   수요일    (Wed.) ※ silent 'd'!
Thursday   /ˈθɜːrzdeɪ/  목요일    (Thu./Thur./Thurs.)
Friday     /ˈfraɪdeɪ/   금요일    (Fri.)
Saturday   /ˈsætərdeɪ/  토요일    (Sat.)
Sunday     /ˈsʌndeɪ/    일요일    (Sun.)

※ Always CAPITALIZED in English! (Korean 월요일 is not capitalized)
※ Week starts on Sunday (AmE calendar) or Monday (BrE/international)

Preposition: ON + day
  "I have a meeting on Monday."
  "She plays tennis on Fridays." (every Friday — plural)
```

### 4.2 Day Expressions

```
today               오늘
tomorrow             내일
yesterday            어제
the day before yesterday  그저께
the day after tomorrow    모레

this Monday          이번 주 월요일
last Monday          지난 주 월요일
next Monday          다음 주 월요일
every Monday         매주 월요일

on weekdays          평일에 (Monday-Friday)
on weekends          주말에 (Saturday-Sunday)
on the weekend (AmE) / at the weekend (BrE)
```

## 5. Months and Dates

### 5.1 The Twelve Months

```
January    /ˈdʒænjueri/   1월    (Jan.)
February   /ˈfebjueri/    2월    (Feb.) ※ first 'r' often silent
March      /mɑːrtʃ/       3월    (Mar.)
April      /ˈeɪprəl/     4월    (Apr.)
May        /meɪ/          5월    (May)
June       /dʒuːn/        6월    (Jun.)
July       /dʒuˈlaɪ/     7월    (Jul.)
August     /ˈɔːɡʌst/     8월    (Aug.)
September  /sepˈtembər/   9월    (Sep./Sept.)
October    /ɒkˈtoʊbər/   10월   (Oct.)
November   /noʊˈvembər/  11월   (Nov.)
December   /dɪˈsembər/   12월   (Dec.)

※ Always CAPITALIZED!
※ Preposition: IN + month → "in January", "in March"
```

### 5.2 Writing Dates

> American format: Month Day, Year
> Written:  March 5, 2024       OR   3/5/2024
> Spoken:   "March fifth, twenty twenty-four"
> British format: Day Month Year
> Written:  5 March 2024        OR   5/3/2024
> Spoken:   "the fifth of March, twenty twenty-four"
> ※ DANGER: 3/5/2024
> American: March 5, 2024
> British: 5 March 2024 (= May 3, 2024 in American!)
> Always write out the month name to avoid confusion!
> Korean format: Year.Month.Day
> 2024년 3월 5일  →  March 5, 2024 (American)
> 5 March 2024 (British)

### 5.3 Years

```
Reading years:
  1990  "nineteen ninety"
  1776  "seventeen seventy-six"
  2000  "two thousand" / "the year two thousand"
  2001  "two thousand (and) one"
  2010  "two thousand (and) ten" / "twenty ten"
  2024  "twenty twenty-four" / "two thousand twenty-four"

  1900  "nineteen hundred"
  1800  "eighteen hundred"

Decades:
  the 1990s  "the nineteen nineties" / "the nineties"
  the 2000s  "the two thousands" / "the aughts"
  the 2010s  "the twenty tens" / "the teens"
```

## 6. Seasons

### 6.1 The Four Seasons

```
spring   /sprɪŋ/    봄    (March - May)
summer   /ˈsʌmər/   여름   (June - August)
fall/autumn          가을   (September - November)
winter   /ˈwɪntər/  겨울   (December - February)

※ "fall" = American English, "autumn" = British English
※ NOT capitalized (unlike days and months)
※ Preposition: in + season → "in spring", "in winter"

  "I love spring."              나는 봄을 좋아한다.
  "It snows in winter."         겨울에 눈이 온다.
  "Last summer was hot."        지난여름은 더웠다.
```

## 7. Common Time Expressions

### 7.1 Present Time

```
now               지금        right now            지금 바로
at the moment     현재        at present           현재
today             오늘        these days           요즘
currently         현재        nowadays             요즘에는
```

### 7.2 Past Time

```
yesterday                 어제
the day before yesterday  그저께
last night/week/month/year  지난밤/주/달/해
... ago                    ~전에 (two days ago, a year ago)
in the past               과거에
the other day              며칠 전에
once                       한 번, 한때
just now                   방금
recently                   최근에
```

### 7.3 Future Time

```
tomorrow                  내일
the day after tomorrow    모레
tonight                   오늘 밤
next week/month/year      다음 주/달/해
in ... (in 3 days)        ~후에 (3일 후에)
soon                      곧
later                     나중에
someday / one day          언젠가
in the future             미래에
```

### 7.4 Duration

```
for + period:     "for two hours"        2시간 동안
during + event:   "during the meeting"   회의 동안
from ... to ...:  "from 9 to 5"          9시부터 5시까지
until / till:     "until Monday"         월요일까지
since:            "since 2020"           2020년부터 (계속)
all day/week:     "all day"              하루 종일

※ for vs during:
  "for" + time period: "for two hours" (2시간 동안)
  "during" + event/noun: "during the class" (수업 동안)
  "for the class" ✗ (when meaning 동안)
  "during two hours" ✗ (use "for" with time periods)
```

## 8. Frequency Adverbs and Expressions

### 8.1 Single-Word Frequency Adverbs

```
always          항상     100%
usually         보통      90%
often           자주      70%
sometimes       가끔      50%
occasionally    때때로     30%
rarely/seldom   거의 안    10%
never           절대 안     0%

Position: BEFORE main verb, AFTER be:
  "I always eat breakfast."
  "She is usually on time."
```

### 8.2 Frequency Expressions

```
every day / every week / every month      매일 / 매주 / 매달
every other day                           이틀에 한 번
once a week / twice a month               일주일에 한 번 / 한 달에 두 번
three times a year                        일 년에 세 번
once in a while                           가끔, 때때로
from time to time                         때때로
all the time                              항상, 늘
most of the time                          대부분의 시간

Position: usually at END of sentence:
  "I exercise three times a week."
  "She visits her parents once a month."
  "We go there from time to time."
```

## 9. Numbers in Daily Life

### 9.1 Phone Numbers

```
Say each digit individually:
  555-1234 → "five five five, one two three four"
  010-1234-5678 → "oh one oh, one two three four, five six seven eight"

  ※ 0 = "oh" (not "zero") in phone numbers
  ※ double digit: 555 → "five five five" or "triple five"
```

### 9.2 Prices

```
$5.99   "five ninety-nine" / "five dollars and ninety-nine cents"
$10     "ten dollars"
$1.50   "a dollar fifty" / "one fifty"
$0.50   "fifty cents"

₩50,000  "fifty thousand won"
€25.00   "twenty-five euros"
£100     "one hundred pounds" / "a hundred pounds"

  ※ Currency symbol comes BEFORE the number: $5 (not 5$)
    Korean: 5,000원 (number comes first)
```

### 9.3 Measurements

```
Temperature:
  32°F = "thirty-two degrees Fahrenheit"
  0°C  = "zero degrees Celsius"
  ※ USA uses Fahrenheit; most other countries use Celsius

Distance:
  5 km     = "five kilometers"
  10 miles  = "ten miles"
  ※ USA uses miles; most other countries use kilometers

Weight:
  5 kg     = "five kilograms" / "five kilos"
  10 pounds = "ten pounds" (= ~4.5 kg)
  ※ USA uses pounds (lbs); most other countries use kilograms
```

### 9.4 Math

```
+ plus / and           2 + 3 = "two plus three"
- minus                5 - 2 = "five minus two"
× times / multiplied by  4 × 3 = "four times three"
÷ divided by           10 ÷ 2 = "ten divided by two"
= equals / is          2 + 3 = 5 "two plus three equals five"
% percent              50% = "fifty percent"
. point                3.14 = "three point one four"
```

## Quick Reference

> **Numbers & Time Quick Guide**
>
> 13 vs 30: thirTEEN (stress 2nd) vs THIRty (stress 1st)
> Korean 만 → English thousand (different base units!)
> Time: "three fifteen" (safest), "quarter past three" (BrE)
> AM/PM: 12 AM = midnight, 12 PM = noon
> Days: Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu/Fri/Sat/Sun — ALWAYS capitalized
> Months: Jan-Dec — ALWAYS capitalized
> Seasons: spring/summer/fall/winter — NOT capitalized
> Date format: March 5, 2024 (AmE) / 5 March 2024 (BrE)
> Prepositions: AT + time, ON + day/date, IN + month/year
> Frequency: always/usually/often/sometimes/rarely/never
> Position: before main verb, after BE
> for + duration (for 2 hours)
> during + event (during the meeting)
> since + start point (since 2020)
> ago + after time (2 days ago)
