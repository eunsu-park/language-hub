## 2. TOEIC: Format and Structure

### 2.1 Listening Section (45 minutes, 100 questions)

```
Part 1: Photographs (6 questions)
  → Listen to 4 statements about a photo, choose the best one
  → Tips: Look at the photo before audio plays;
    focus on people, actions, objects, locations

Part 2: Question-Response (25 questions)
  → Listen to a question + 3 possible responses
  → Tips: Focus on the FIRST WORD of the question
    (Who/What/Where/When/Why/How — determines answer type)
  → Watch out for similar-sounding distractors

Part 3: Conversations (39 questions = 13 conversations x 3 Qs)
  → Listen to a conversation between 2-3 people
  → Answer 3 questions per conversation
  → Tips: Read questions BEFORE the audio plays;
    predict what to listen for

Part 4: Talks (30 questions = 10 talks x 3 Qs)
  → Listen to a short monologue (announcement, voicemail, etc.)
  → Answer 3 questions per talk
  → Tips: Same as Part 3 — pre-read questions
```

### 2.2 Reading Section (75 minutes, 100 questions)

```
Part 5: Incomplete Sentences (30 questions)
  → Choose the correct word to complete a sentence
  → Tests: grammar, vocabulary, word forms
  → Tips: Identify what's being tested (grammar vs vocab);
    look at words BEFORE and AFTER the blank

Part 6: Text Completion (16 questions = 4 texts x 4 Qs)
  → Choose words or sentences to complete a passage
  → Tests: grammar, vocabulary, context
  → Tips: Read the FULL passage for context, not just
    the sentence with the blank

Part 7: Reading Comprehension (54 questions)
  → Single passages (29 questions): emails, ads, articles
  → Multiple passages (25 questions): 2-3 related texts
  → Tips: Read questions first, then scan for answers;
    manage time carefully (this section is the longest)
```

## 3. TOEIC: Listening Strategies

### 3.1 Part 1 (Photos)

```
STRATEGY:
  1. Look at the photo for 5 seconds before audio starts
  2. Identify: WHO (people), WHAT (actions), WHERE (location)
  3. Listen for the statement that accurately describes the photo
  4. Beware of:
     - Words that sound similar but have different meanings
     - Statements that describe only part of the photo
     - Statements that make assumptions not shown in the photo

Example traps:
  Photo: A woman typing on a laptop
  ✗ "The woman is talking on the phone." (wrong action)
  ✗ "The woman is working in a garden." (wrong location)
  ✗ "The laptop is being repaired." (not shown)
  ✓ "The woman is using a computer."
```

### 3.2 Part 2 (Question-Response)

```
KEY STRATEGY: Focus on the FIRST WORD

  Who  → expect a person/name in the answer
  What → expect a thing/action
  Where → expect a place
  When → expect a time
  Why  → expect a reason (Because...)
  How  → expect a method or degree

  TRAPS:
  - Answers with similar sounds (e.g., "meet" vs "meat")
  - Answers that repeat words from the question (often wrong!)
  - Yes/No questions: the answer might not start with yes/no
    Q: "Didn't you attend the meeting?"
    A: "I had another appointment." (valid answer without yes/no)
```

### 3.3 Parts 3-4 (Conversations and Talks)

```
1. PRE-READ: Read the 3 questions BEFORE the audio plays
2. PREDICT: Guess what the conversation/talk will be about
3. LISTEN ACTIVELY: Focus on answering each question as you hear
   the relevant information
4. DON'T WAIT: Mark your answer as soon as you hear it;
   don't wait until the audio ends
5. MOVE ON: If you miss an answer, don't dwell; focus on
   the next question

Common question types:
  - What is the main topic/purpose?
  - Where does the conversation take place?
  - What does the speaker suggest/recommend?
  - What will the listener probably do next?
  - Look at the graphic — which [option] matches?
```

## 4. TOEIC: Reading Strategies

### 4.1 Part 5 (Incomplete Sentences) — Target: 10 minutes

```
Grammar questions — look for clues:
  1. WORD FORM: What part of speech is needed?
     "The presentation was very ___."
     → adjective needed (impressive, not impress/impression)

  2. VERB TENSE: Check time markers
     "She ___ the report yesterday."
     → past tense needed (submitted)

  3. PREPOSITIONS: Learn common collocations
     "interested ___" → in
     "depend ___" → on
     "responsible ___" → for

  4. CONNECTORS: Check logical relationship
     "___ the rain, they continued." → Despite/In spite of

Vocabulary questions — use context:
  "The company will ___ the event next month."
  → host/hold (not have a dinner party)
```

### 4.2 Part 6 (Text Completion) — Target: 8 minutes

```
1. Read the ENTIRE passage first for context
2. For vocabulary blanks: use surrounding context
3. For sentence insertion: check logical flow
   - Does the sentence connect to what comes before AND after?
   - Does it match the tone and topic?
4. For grammar blanks: same strategies as Part 5
```

### 4.3 Part 7 (Reading Comprehension) — Target: 57 minutes

```
TIME MANAGEMENT IS CRITICAL!

Single passages: ~1 minute per question
Multiple passages: ~1.5 minutes per question

STRATEGY:
1. Read questions FIRST (know what to look for)
2. SKIM the passage for main idea
3. SCAN for specific answers
4. For "NOT" questions, find the 3 that ARE mentioned
5. For inference questions, look for clues in the text
6. For vocabulary-in-context, re-read the sentence carefully

Common text types:
  emails, letters, advertisements, announcements,
  articles, forms, schedules, reviews, text message chains
```

## 5. TOEFL iBT: Format and Structure

### 5.1 Four Sections

> READING (35 minutes)
> • 2 passages (~700 words each)
> • 10 questions per passage = 20 questions total
> • Academic topics: science, history, social science, arts
> ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
> LISTENING (36 minutes)
> • 3 lectures (6 questions each) = 18 questions
> • 2 conversations (5 questions each) = 10 questions
> • Total: 28 questions
> • Note-taking allowed
> ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
> SPEAKING (16 minutes)
> • 4 tasks
> • Task 1: Independent (give opinion) — 45 seconds
> • Tasks 2-4: Integrated (read/listen + speak) — 60 sec each
> • Scored 0-30
> ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
> WRITING (29 minutes)
> • Task 1: Integrated (read + listen + write) — 20 minutes
> • Task 2: Academic Discussion — 10 minutes
> • Scored 0-30

```

Total score: 0-120 (30 per section)
Total time: ~2 hours
```

## 6. TOEFL: Section Strategies

### 6.1 Reading

```
QUESTION TYPES:
  1. Factual information (What does the passage say about X?)
  2. Negative factual (Which is NOT mentioned?)
  3. Inference (What can be inferred?)
  4. Vocabulary in context (The word X means...)
  5. Reference (What does "they" refer to?)
  6. Sentence insertion (Where should this sentence go?)
  7. Summary (Select 3 sentences that summarize the passage)

STRATEGIES:
  • Skim the passage first (2 minutes)
  • Read the topic sentence of each paragraph
  • For vocabulary questions, use context clues
  • For summary questions, eliminate minor details
  • Don't spend more than 1.5 minutes per question
```

### 6.2 Listening

```
STRATEGIES:
  • TAKE NOTES — this is essential!
  • Note: main idea, key details, examples, speaker's attitude
  • Listen for signal words:
    "The important thing is..." (key point coming)
    "For example..." (supporting detail)
    "However..." (contrast/change of direction)
    "In other words..." (rephrasing main idea)
  • Don't try to write everything — capture key words
  • Pay attention to the speaker's tone and emphasis
```

### 6.3 Speaking

```
TASK 1 (Independent — 15 sec prep, 45 sec speak):
  Template:
    "I believe that [opinion]. First, [reason 1 + example].
     Second, [reason 2 + example]. That's why I think [restate]."

TASKS 2-4 (Integrated — 30 sec prep, 60 sec speak):
  Template for reading + listening:
    "The reading passage discusses [topic]. The lecture
     [agrees/disagrees] with this by explaining [points].
     Specifically, the professor mentions [detail 1] and
     [detail 2]."

GENERAL TIPS:
  • Speak clearly and at a natural pace
  • Don't worry about accent — clarity matters more
  • Use transition words (first, second, however, therefore)
  • Practice with a timer — 45 and 60 seconds go fast!
  • It's OK to pause briefly to think (1-2 seconds)
```

### 6.4 Writing

```
TASK 1 (Integrated — 20 minutes):
  • Read a passage (3 minutes), listen to a lecture
  • Write about how the lecture relates to the reading
  • Aim for 250-300 words
  • Template:
    "The reading argues that [X]. However, the lecturer
     challenges this by arguing that [Y]. First, while the
     reading states [point 1], the lecturer counters that
     [response 1]. Second,..."

TASK 2 (Academic Discussion — 10 minutes):
  • Read a professor's question and two student responses
  • Write your own contribution to the discussion
  • Aim for 100+ words
  • Express your opinion, support it, and engage with
    at least one student's point
```

## 7. IELTS: Format and Structure

### 7.1 Two Types

```
IELTS ACADEMIC:  For university admission
IELTS GENERAL:   For immigration and work

Both have the same Listening and Speaking sections.
Reading and Writing sections differ.
```

### 7.2 Four Sections

> LISTENING (30 minutes + 10 min transfer time)
> • 4 sections, 40 questions total
> • Section 1: Social conversation (2 speakers)
> • Section 2: Social monologue (1 speaker)
> • Section 3: Academic conversation (2-4 speakers)
> • Section 4: Academic lecture (1 speaker)
> • Each recording played ONCE only
> ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
> READING (60 minutes)
> • 3 passages, 40 questions total
> • Academic: journals, magazines, books
> • General: everyday texts, work-related, general interest
> ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
> WRITING (60 minutes)
> • Task 1 (20 min): Academic = describe chart/graph/diagram
> General = write a letter
> • Task 2 (40 min): Both = essay (250+ words)
> ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
> SPEAKING (11-14 minutes, face-to-face with examiner)
> • Part 1: Introduction + general questions (4-5 min)
> • Part 2: Long turn — speak for 2 minutes on a topic (3-4 min)
> • Part 3: Discussion — abstract/complex questions (4-5 min)

```

Band score: 0-9 (each section + overall band)
```

## 8. IELTS: Section Strategies

### 8.1 Listening

```
STRATEGIES:
  • Read questions BEFORE each section plays
  • Predict answer types (number? name? adjective?)
  • Listen for synonyms — answers rarely use exact words
    from the question
  • Write answers as you hear them
  • Check word limits ("No more than THREE words")
  • Spelling counts! Practice commonly misspelled words

QUESTION TYPES:
  Multiple choice, matching, map/plan labeling,
  form completion, note completion, sentence completion,
  summary completion, table completion
```

### 8.2 Reading

```
STRATEGIES:
  • DON'T read the passage word by word first
  • Skim headings, first sentences, and bold text
  • Read questions, then scan for answers
  • For True/False/Not Given:
    TRUE = information matches the passage
    FALSE = information contradicts the passage
    NOT GIVEN = information is not in the passage
  • For matching headings: read each paragraph's
    first and last sentences
  • Time: ~20 minutes per passage

COMMON TRAP: "NOT GIVEN" is not the same as "FALSE"
  FALSE = the passage says the opposite
  NOT GIVEN = the passage doesn't mention it at all
```

### 8.3 Writing

```
TASK 1 — Academic (20 minutes, 150+ words):
  Describe a chart, graph, table, diagram, or process
  • Start with a paraphrase of what the visual shows
  • Identify key trends, comparisons, and notable features
  • Use data to support observations
  • Do NOT give your opinion (just describe)
  • Key phrases:
    "The graph shows that..."
    "It is noticeable that..."
    "The most significant change was..."
    "Between 2010 and 2020, the figure rose from X to Y."

TASK 2 — Essay (40 minutes, 250+ words):
  Write an essay responding to a prompt
  Structure:
    Introduction: paraphrase the question + state your position
    Body 1: first main point + evidence
    Body 2: second main point + evidence
    (Optional Body 3 or counterargument)
    Conclusion: restate position + final thought

  Band 7+ tips:
  • Use a range of vocabulary (don't repeat the same words)
  • Use complex sentence structures
  • Develop each point fully with examples
  • Stay on topic — answer the question directly
  • Leave 3-5 minutes to proofread
```

### 8.4 Speaking

```
PART 1 (General questions — 4-5 minutes):
  • Answer in 2-3 sentences, not just "yes" or "no"
  • Extend your answers with reasons and examples
  Q: "Do you enjoy reading?"
  Poor: "Yes, I do."
  Better: "Yes, I really enjoy reading, especially novels.
   I try to read for at least 30 minutes before bed.
   Recently, I've been reading mystery novels by Korean
   authors."

PART 2 (Long turn — speak for 2 minutes):
  • You get 1 minute to prepare + make notes
  • Use your notes as a guide, not a script
  • Structure: introduction, details, personal reflection
  • Keep talking! Silence is worse than a small mistake
  • Practice with a timer

PART 3 (Discussion — 4-5 minutes):
  • Give detailed, thoughtful answers
  • Show you can discuss abstract topics
  • Use phrases like:
    "I think..." / "In my opinion..."
    "On the one hand... on the other hand..."
    "It depends on..." / "Generally speaking..."
    "That's an interesting question. I think..."

SCORING CRITERIA:
  1. Fluency and coherence
  2. Lexical resource (vocabulary range)
  3. Grammatical range and accuracy
  4. Pronunciation
```

## 10. Study Plan and Tips

### 10.1 General Study Plan

> **12-WEEK STUDY PLAN**
>
> Weeks 1-2:  Diagnostic test + identify weaknesses
> Weeks 3-4:  Focus on weakest section (intensive practice)
> Weeks 5-6:  Vocabulary building + grammar review
> Weeks 7-8:  Full practice tests + timed sections
> Weeks 9-10: Focus on test-taking strategies
> Weeks 11-12: Full mock exams under test conditions
> Daily routine (1.5-2 hours):
> • 30 min: Vocabulary study (flashcards, apps)
> • 30 min: Section practice (rotate daily)
> • 30 min: Review mistakes and learn from them
> • Weekend: Full-length practice test (every 2 weeks)

### 10.2 Universal Tips

```
1. Take a DIAGNOSTIC TEST first — know where you stand
2. Focus on your WEAKEST section — that's where you'll
   gain the most points
3. PRACTICE UNDER TIMED CONDITIONS — time pressure is real
4. REVIEW YOUR MISTAKES — understand WHY you got it wrong
5. BUILD VOCABULARY daily — even 10 new words per day
6. IMMERSE yourself — English podcasts, news, movies
7. DON'T leave blanks — there's no penalty for guessing
   (TOEIC, TOEFL)
8. GET FAMILIAR with the test format — no surprises on test day
9. Sleep well the night before — fatigue hurts performance
10. Read the instructions carefully — every time
```

### Exercise 3: IELTS Writing Task 2 — Plan

Write an outline (not a full essay) for this IELTS prompt:

"Some people believe that university education should be free for everyone. Others argue that students should pay for their education. Discuss both views and give your own opinion."

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal sample outline</summary>

```
Introduction:
  - Paraphrase the question
  - State: both views have merit, but I believe [your position]

Body Paragraph 1 — Free education view:
  - Everyone deserves equal access to education
  - Reduces inequality and social mobility barriers
  - Benefits the economy (more educated workforce)
  - Example: Countries with free education (Germany, Norway)

Body Paragraph 2 — Students should pay view:
  - Universities need funding for quality education
  - Students value education more when they invest in it
  - Taxpayers shouldn't fund others' personal advancement
  - Student loans and scholarships provide alternatives

Body Paragraph 3 — My opinion:
  - A balanced approach: subsidized education with income-based
    repayment after graduation
  - Government covers basic costs; students contribute fairly
  - This ensures access while maintaining quality

Conclusion:
  - Restate position
  - Both access and quality matter
  - A balanced system serves society best
```
