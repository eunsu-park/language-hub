## 1. Overview of English Tenses

### 1.1 The 12-Tense Grid

English has **12 tenses** organized by time (past/present/future) and aspect (simple/continuous/perfect/perfect continuous).

> **│    PAST       │   PRESENT     │    FUTURE**
>
> ├──────────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤
> | SIMPLE | worked | work(s) | will work |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | ate | eat(s) | will eat |  |
> | CONTINUOUS | was working | am/is/are | will be |
> | were eating | working | working |  |
> | am/is/are | will be |  |  |
> | eating | eating |  |  |
> | PERFECT | had worked | have/has | will have |
> | had eaten | worked | worked |  |
> | have/has | will have |  |  |
> | eaten | eaten |  |  |
> | PERFECT | had been | have/has been | will have been |
> | CONTINUOUS | working | working | working |
> | had been | have/has been | will have been |  |
> | eating | eating | eating |  |
>
> ├──────────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤
> ├──────────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤
> ├──────────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼──────────────────┤

### 1.2 Understanding Aspect

```
SIMPLE:             Action as a fact or habit
                    "I eat breakfast."

CONTINUOUS:         Action in progress (ongoing, temporary)
                    "I am eating breakfast."

PERFECT:            Action completed before a reference point,
                    with relevance to that point
                    "I have eaten breakfast." (so I'm not hungry now)

PERFECT CONTINUOUS: Action started before a reference point,
                    continued up to (or near) that point,
                    emphasizing duration
                    "I have been eating for 30 minutes."
```

## 2. Present Tenses

### 2.1 Simple Present

> Formation: base verb (+s/es for he/she/it)
> I work          We work
> You work        You work
> He/She works    They work
> Negative:  do not (don't) + base verb
> does not (doesn't) + base verb (he/she/it)
> Question:  Do/Does + subject + base verb?

```

Uses:
  1. Habits and routines:     "I drink coffee every morning."
  2. Facts and general truths: "Water boils at 100°C."
  3. Scheduled events:        "The train leaves at 9:00 AM."
  4. States and conditions:   "She lives in Seoul."
  5. Narration (sports, etc): "He shoots! He scores!"

Signal words: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never,
              every day/week/month, on Mondays
```

### 2.2 Present Continuous

> Formation: am/is/are + verb-ing
> I am working         We are working
> You are working      You are working
> He/She is working    They are working
> Negative:  am/is/are + not + verb-ing
> Question:  Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?

```

Uses:
  1. Action happening right now:   "I am reading a book."
  2. Temporary situations:         "She is staying with friends."
  3. Trends and changes:           "Prices are rising."
  4. Future arrangements:          "We are meeting tomorrow at 3."
  5. Annoying habits (with always): "He is always complaining!"

Signal words: now, right now, at the moment, currently, today,
              this week, these days, Look!, Listen!

Non-continuous (stative) verbs — do NOT use in continuous:
  ✗ I am knowing the answer.     ✓ I know the answer.
  ✗ She is believing in ghosts.  ✓ She believes in ghosts.
  ✗ I am wanting coffee.         ✓ I want coffee.

  Stative verbs: know, believe, want, need, prefer, like, love,
  hate, understand, remember, forget, belong, own, possess,
  seem, appear, mean, consist, contain, deserve
```

### 2.3 Present Perfect

> Formation: have/has + past participle
> I have worked         We have worked
> You have worked       You have worked
> He/She has worked     They have worked
> Negative:  have/has + not + past participle
> Question:  Have/Has + subject + past participle?

```

Uses:
  1. Life experience (ever/never):
     "Have you ever been to Paris?"

  2. Actions completed at an unspecified time:
     "I have read that book." (when doesn't matter)

  3. Recently completed actions:
     "She has just finished her homework."

  4. Situations from past to present:
     "I have lived here for 10 years." (still living here)

  5. Results visible now:
     "Someone has broken the window!" (it's broken now)

Signal words: already, yet, just, ever, never, recently, so far,
              since, for, up to now, still (negative), lately
```

### 2.4 Present Perfect Continuous

> Formation: have/has + been + verb-ing
> I have been working         We have been working
> You have been working       You have been working
> He/She has been working     They have been working
> Negative:  have/has + not + been + verb-ing
> Question:  Have/Has + subject + been + verb-ing?

```

Uses:
  1. Duration of an ongoing activity (emphasis on how long):
     "I have been studying English for 3 years."

  2. Recent activity with visible results:
     "You're sweating. Have you been running?"

  3. Repeated actions in a period up to now:
     "She has been calling you all morning."

Signal words: for, since, all day/morning/week, how long,
              recently, lately

Present Perfect vs Present Perfect Continuous:
  "I have written 3 emails."     (focus on RESULT: 3 completed)
  "I have been writing emails."  (focus on ACTIVITY: still doing it)
```

## 3. Past Tenses

### 3.1 Simple Past

> Formation: verb + -ed (regular) or irregular form
> I worked / ate        We worked / ate
> You worked / ate      You worked / ate
> He/She worked / ate   They worked / ate
> Negative:  did not (didn't) + base verb
> Question:  Did + subject + base verb?

```

Uses:
  1. Completed actions at a specific time:
     "I visited Jeju Island last summer."

  2. A series of completed actions:
     "I woke up, brushed my teeth, and ate breakfast."

  3. Past habits (used to):
     "When I was young, I played soccer every day."

  4. Past states:
     "She was a teacher for 20 years."

Signal words: yesterday, last week/month/year, ago, in 2020,
              when I was young, at that time, then
```

### 3.2 Past Continuous

> Formation: was/were + verb-ing
> I was working         We were working
> You were working      You were working
> He/She was working    They were working
> Negative:  was/were + not + verb-ing
> Question:  Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?

```

Uses:
  1. Action in progress at a specific past time:
     "At 8 PM, I was watching TV."

  2. Interrupted action (background):
     "I was sleeping when the phone rang."

  3. Two simultaneous past actions:
     "While she was cooking, he was cleaning."

  4. Setting the scene in a story:
     "The sun was shining and birds were singing."

Signal words: while, when, as, at that time, at 3 o'clock,
              all day yesterday
```

### 3.3 Past Perfect

> Formation: had + past participle
> I had worked         We had worked
> You had worked       You had worked
> He/She had worked    They had worked
> Negative:  had not (hadn't) + past participle
> Question:  Had + subject + past participle?

```

Uses:
  1. Action completed BEFORE another past action:
     "When I arrived, the movie had already started."

  2. Experience before a past time:
     "I had never seen snow before I went to Canada."

  3. Third conditional (unreal past):
     "If I had known, I would have helped."

  4. Reported speech (backshifted past):
     "He said he had finished the project."

Signal words: before, after, by the time, already, just,
              never (before), until, when
```

### 3.4 Past Perfect Continuous

> Formation: had + been + verb-ing
> I had been working         We had been working
> You had been working       You had been working
> He/She had been working    They had been working
> Negative:  had not (hadn't) + been + verb-ing
> Question:  Had + subject + been + verb-ing?

```

Uses:
  1. Duration of an activity before another past action:
     "I had been waiting for 2 hours when the bus finally came."

  2. Cause of a past state (visible evidence):
     "She was tired because she had been working all night."

  3. Repeated actions before a past point:
     "He had been calling her for days before she answered."

Signal words: for, since, all day, before, when, by the time,
              how long
```

## 4. Future Tenses

### 4.1 Simple Future

> Formation:
> (a) will + base verb (predictions, decisions, promises)
> (b) am/is/are going to + base verb (plans, predictions
> with evidence)
> will:
> I will work         We will work
> Negative: will not (won't) + base verb
> Question: Will + subject + base verb?
> going to:
> I am going to work  We are going to work
> Negative: am/is/are + not + going to + base verb
> Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + base verb?

```

will vs going to:
  Spontaneous decision:    "I'll have the pasta." (decided now)
  Planned intention:       "I'm going to visit my parents." (planned earlier)
  Prediction (opinion):    "I think it will rain tomorrow."
  Prediction (evidence):   "Look at those clouds. It's going to rain."
  Promise:                 "I will always love you."
  Offer:                   "I'll carry that for you."

Signal words: tomorrow, next week/month/year, in the future,
              soon, later, tonight
```

### 4.2 Future Continuous

> Formation: will be + verb-ing
> I will be working         We will be working
> You will be working       You will be working
> He/She will be working    They will be working
> Negative:  will not (won't) be + verb-ing
> Question:  Will + subject + be + verb-ing?

```

Uses:
  1. Action in progress at a future time:
     "At 10 PM tonight, I will be studying."

  2. Future actions as a matter of course:
     "I'll be seeing John tomorrow. (It's already arranged.)"

  3. Polite inquiries:
     "Will you be using the car tonight?"

Signal words: at this time tomorrow, at 8 PM, all day tomorrow,
              this time next week
```

### 4.3 Future Perfect

> Formation: will have + past participle
> I will have worked         We will have worked
> You will have worked       You will have worked
> He/She will have worked    They will have worked
> Negative:  will not (won't) have + past participle
> Question:  Will + subject + have + past participle?

```

Uses:
  1. Action completed before a future time:
     "By next June, I will have graduated."

  2. Duration up to a future point:
     "By December, I will have lived here for 5 years."

Signal words: by (+ future time), by the time, before,
              by next week/month/year
```

### 4.4 Future Perfect Continuous

> Formation: will have been + verb-ing
> I will have been working        We will have been working
> You will have been working      You will have been working
> He/She will have been working   They will have been working
> Negative:  will not have been + verb-ing
> Question:  Will + subject + have been + verb-ing?

```

Uses:
  1. Duration of an action up to a future point (emphasis on how long):
     "By 2027, I will have been working here for 10 years."

  2. Cause of a future state:
     "She will be tired because she will have been traveling
      for 20 hours."

Signal words: by, by the time, for, since (with future reference)
```

## 6. Tense Selection Guide

### 6.1 Decision Flowchart

> ├─ PRESENT ──────────────────────────────────────────┤
> ├─ Habit/fact/routine?      → Simple Present
> ├─ Happening right now?     → Present Continuous
> ├─ Past action connected    → Present Perfect
> │  to now? (result matters)
> └─ Duration up to now?      → Pres. Perf. Cont.
> (how long?)
> ├─ PAST ─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
> ├─ Completed at specific    → Simple Past
> │  past time?
> ├─ In progress at a past    → Past Continuous
> │  time?
> ├─ Before another past      → Past Perfect
> │  event?
> └─ Duration before another  → Past Perf. Cont.
> past event?
> ├─ FUTURE ───────────────────────────────────────────┤
> ├─ Prediction/plan/promise? → Simple Future
> ├─ In progress at future    → Future Continuous
> │  time?
> ├─ Completed before future  → Future Perfect
> │  deadline?
> └─ Duration up to future    → Fut. Perf. Cont.
> point?

### 6.2 Quick Reference Questions

| Ask Yourself... | Use This Tense |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Is it a regular habit or fact? | Simple Present |
| Is it happening right now? | Present Continuous |
| Did it happen (I don't know/care when)? | Present Perfect |
| How long has it been going on? | Present Perfect Continuous |
| Did it happen at a specific past time? | Simple Past |
| Was it happening when something else occurred? | Past Continuous |
| Did it happen before another past event? | Past Perfect |
| How long had it been going on before...? | Past Perfect Continuous |
| Will it happen? | Simple Future |
| Will it be in progress at a future time? | Future Continuous |
| Will it be done before a future deadline? | Future Perfect |
| How long will it have been going on by...? | Future Perfect Continuous |

## 7. Tense Contrasts

### 7.1 Simple Present vs Present Continuous

```
Simple Present (permanent/habitual):
  "I live in Seoul."        (permanent home)
  "She works at a bank."    (regular job)
  "I drink coffee."         (general habit)

Present Continuous (temporary/now):
  "I'm living in Seoul."    (temporary, might move)
  "She's working at a bank." (temporarily, this summer)
  "I'm drinking coffee."    (right now, this moment)
```

### 7.2 Simple Past vs Present Perfect

```
Simple Past (specific time, finished):
  "I visited Paris in 2019."          (specific time: 2019)
  "I ate lunch."                       (finished, earlier today)
  "She lived in London for 3 years."   (she doesn't live there now)

Present Perfect (unspecified time or continuing):
  "I have visited Paris."              (at some point in my life)
  "I have eaten lunch."                (result: I'm not hungry)
  "She has lived in London for 3 years." (she still lives there)
```

### 7.3 Present Perfect vs Present Perfect Continuous

```
Present Perfect (focus on RESULT/COMPLETION):
  "I have written 5 emails."    (5 completed emails — result)
  "She has read the book."      (she finished it)

Present Perfect Continuous (focus on ACTIVITY/DURATION):
  "I have been writing emails." (activity ongoing, maybe not done)
  "She has been reading."       (activity, emphasizing duration)
```

### 7.4 Will vs Going To

```
Will (spontaneous, predictions, promises):
  "I'll answer the phone."     (spontaneous decision)
  "It will probably rain."     (prediction based on opinion)
  "I'll help you."             (promise/offer)

Going to (planned, predictions with evidence):
  "I'm going to study abroad." (planned decision)
  "Look! It's going to rain."  (prediction with evidence)
  "We're going to move."       (intention, already decided)
```

### 7.5 Past Simple vs Past Continuous

```
Past Simple (completed action, main event):
  "I ate dinner at 7 PM."          (completed)
  "The phone rang."                (single event)

Past Continuous (background, interrupted action):
  "I was eating dinner at 7 PM."   (in progress at that time)
  "I was cooking when the phone rang." (background action)
```

## 9. Common Errors for Korean Speakers

### 9.1 Korean Has No Tense Marking Like English

> Korean tense system is much simpler:
> Korean has 3 main tenses:
> • Present: -ㄴ다/-는다  (간다, 먹는다)
> • Past: -았/-었        (갔다, 먹었다)
> • Future: -겠-/-ㄹ 것이다 (가겠다, 먹을 것이다)
> English has 12 tenses!
> Korean does NOT have:
> • Continuous aspect (am/is/are + -ing)
> • Perfect aspect (have/has + past participle)
> • Perfect continuous
> This is why Korean speakers often default to Simple tenses
> in English, ignoring continuous and perfect forms.

### 9.2 Top 10 Errors

| # | Error | Correct | Explanation |
|---|-------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | ✗ I live here since 2020. | ✓ I **have lived** here since 2020. | "since" requires present perfect |
| 2 | ✗ I am knowing the answer. | ✓ I **know** the answer. | "know" is a stative verb |
| 3 | ✗ I have been to Paris last year. | ✓ I **went** to Paris last year. | Specific time = simple past |
| 4 | ✗ When I will arrive, I call you. | ✓ When I **arrive**, I **will call** you. | No "will" in time clauses |
| 5 | ✗ I am work now. | ✓ I **am working** now. | Continuous needs -ing |
| 6 | ✗ Did you ever eat sushi? | ✓ **Have** you **ever eaten** sushi? | Life experience = pres. perfect |
| 7 | ✗ I will going to study. | ✓ I **am going to** study. / I **will** study. | Don't mix will + going to |
| 8 | ✗ Before he came, I finish. | ✓ Before he came, I **had finished**. | Earlier past = past perfect |
| 9 | ✗ She is study English for 3 years. | ✓ She **has been studying** English for 3 years. | Duration to present = pres. perf. cont. |
| 10 | ✗ I eat breakfast already. | ✓ I **have already eaten** breakfast. | "already" signals pres. perfect |

### 9.3 The "Since/For" Problem

```
Korean speakers often use Simple Present with "since" and "for":

  ✗ I study English for 5 years.
  ✓ I have studied English for 5 years.
  ✓ I have been studying English for 5 years.

  ✗ She lives here since 2018.
  ✓ She has lived here since 2018.

Rule:
  for + DURATION    (for 3 hours, for 2 years, for a long time)
  since + POINT     (since Monday, since 2020, since I was born)
  Both require Present Perfect or Present Perfect Continuous
  (when the situation continues to the present)
```

me. I told (simple past) her that I would help (future in the past)
her after work. By 5 PM, I had finished (past perfect — completed
before a past time) all my tasks, so I went (simple past) to help
her. We found (simple past) the wallet in her car. She had not
checked (past perfect — she hadn't done this before) there before!

## Quick Reference

> **The 12 English Tenses**
>
> | PRESENT | PAST | FUTURE |
> |---|---|---|
> | work(s) | worked | will work |
> | am/is/are | was/were | will be |
> | working | working | working |
> | have/has | had | will have |
> | worked | worked | worked |
> | have/has been | had been | will have been |
> | working | working | working |
>
> ─────────────────┼───────────────────┼─────────────────────
> Key Korean Speaker Tips:
> 1. Use Present Perfect with since/for (not Simple Present)
> 2. Don't use continuous with stative verbs
> 3. Use Simple Past with specific past times
> 4. No "will" in time clauses (when, before, after, until)
> 5. Use Past Perfect for the earlier of two past events
