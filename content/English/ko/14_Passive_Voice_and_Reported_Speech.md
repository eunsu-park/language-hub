## 1. 태(Voice) 개요

### 1.1 태(Voice)란 무엇인가?

> **능동태 vs 수동태 (Active vs Passive Voice)**
>
> 능동태 (Active Voice): 주어가 행동을 수행
> → "The chef cooked the meal."
> 주어(chef) → 행동(cooked) → 목적어(meal)
> 수동태 (Passive Voice): 주어가 행동을 받음
> → "The meal was cooked by the chef."
> 주어(meal) ← 행동(was cooked) ← 행위자(chef)
> 한국어 비교:
> 능동: 요리사가 음식을 요리했다.
> 수동: 음식이 (요리사에 의해) 요리되었다.

### 1.2 수동태를 사용하는 이유

수동태는 다음과 같은 경우에 사용됩니다:
- **행동** 자체가 행위자보다 더 중요할 때
- **행위자**를 모르거나, 뻔하거나, 중요하지 않을 때
- **격식적이거나 객관적인** 톤이 필요할 때 (학술 논문, 뉴스 기사)
- 누군가에게 직접 **책임을 돌리지 않으려** 할 때

```
행위자 불명:    "My car was stolen last night."
               (누가 훔쳤는지 모름.)

행위자 자명:    "The suspect was arrested."
               (당연히 경찰에 의해.)

격식/객관적:    "The experiment was conducted in 2024."
               (과학적 글쓰기 스타일.)

책임 회피:      "Mistakes were made."
               (누가 실수했는지? 명시하지 않음.)
```

## 2. 수동태 형성

### 2.1 기본 공식

> **수동태 = BE + 과거분사 (Past Participle, PP)**
>
> 주어 + BE (시제에 따라 활용) + 과거분사 (+ by 행위자)
> "The letter   was          written       by Maria."
> └─주어──┘    └─BE─┘       └──PP──┘      └─행위자─┘
> BE 동사의 형태는 시제에 따라 변합니다:
> • 현재:  am/is/are + PP
> • 과거:  was/were + PP
> • 미래:  will be + PP
> • 완료:  have/has been + PP
> • 조동사: can/must/should be + PP

### 2.2 규칙 및 불규칙 과거분사

과거분사는 동사의 세 번째 형태입니다.

| 유형 | 원형 | 과거형 | 과거분사 |
|------|------|--------|----------|
| 규칙 | cook | cooked | **cooked** |
| 규칙 | clean | cleaned | **cleaned** |
| 규칙 | play | played | **played** |
| 불규칙 | write | wrote | **written** |
| 불규칙 | break | broke | **broken** |
| 불규칙 | take | took | **taken** |
| 불규칙 | see | saw | **seen** |
| 불규칙 | give | gave | **given** |
| 불규칙 | make | made | **made** |
| 불규칙 | eat | ate | **eaten** |

> **암기할 불규칙 과거분사**: done, gone, known, shown, spoken, stolen, chosen, driven, forgotten, hidden, risen, worn, torn, sworn, thrown, blown, grown, drawn, flown, frozen

## 3. 능동태에서 수동태로 전환

### 3.1 단계별 과정

```
1단계: 능동문에서 주어, 동사, 목적어를 확인
2단계: 목적어를 주어 자리로 이동
3단계: 동사를 "be + 과거분사"로 변환
4단계: 원래 주어를 "by + 행위자"로 이동 (선택적)

예시:
  능동: [Shakespeare] [wrote] [Hamlet].
         주어          동사     목적어

  1단계: 주어 = Shakespeare, 동사 = wrote, 목적어 = Hamlet
  2단계: Hamlet → 주어 자리로
  3단계: wrote → was written (과거형 "be" + PP)
  4단계: Shakespeare → by Shakespeare

  수동: Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.
```

### 3.2 추가 예시

| 능동태 | 수동태 |
|--------|--------|
| The dog chased the cat. | The cat **was chased** by the dog. |
| Someone has stolen my phone. | My phone **has been stolen**. |
| They will announce the results. | The results **will be announced**. |
| People speak English worldwide. | English **is spoken** worldwide. |
| The company is building a new office. | A new office **is being built** by the company. |

### 3.3 수동태로 만들 수 없는 문장

**타동사**(직접 목적어가 있는 동사)만 수동태로 만들 수 있습니다.

```
수동태 불가 (자동사):
  ✗ "The baby cried."       → 목적어 없음, 수동태 불가
  ✗ "She arrived early."    → 목적어 없음, 수동태 불가
  ✗ "He slept for hours."   → 목적어 없음, 수동태 불가
  ✗ "The sun rose."         → 목적어 없음, 수동태 불가

수동태 가능 (타동사):
  ✓ "She wrote a letter."   → "A letter was written by her."
  ✓ "They ate the cake."    → "The cake was eaten by them."
```

## 4. "By"를 사용한 행위자 표현

### 4.1 행위자를 포함하는 경우

> "by + 행위자"를 포함하는 경우:
> 1. 행위자가 새롭거나 중요한 정보를 제공할 때
> "The Mona Lisa was painted by Leonardo da Vinci."
> 2. 행위자의 정체가 놀라운 경우
> "The window was broken by the teacher!"
> 3. 누가 책임인지 명시해야 할 때
> "This report was written by the marketing team."

### 4.2 행위자를 생략하는 경우

> "by + 행위자"를 생략하는 경우:
> 1. 행위자를 모르는 경우
> "My wallet was stolen." (누군지 모르는 사람에 의해)
> 2. 행위자가 자명한 경우
> "He was arrested." (당연히 경찰에 의해)
> 3. 행위자가 중요하지 않거나 일반적인 경우
> "English is spoken in many countries."
> (사람들에 의해 — 너무 일반적이라 불필요)
> 4. 행위자가 "people," "someone," "they" (일반적) 인 경우
> "The building was constructed in 1990."
> (건설 노동자들에 의해 — 자명함)

## 5. 시제별 수동태

### 5.1 시제표 전체

| 시제 | 능동태 | 수동태 |
|------|--------|--------|
| 현재 단순 | They **make** cars here. | Cars **are made** here. |
| 현재 진행 | They **are building** a bridge. | A bridge **is being built**. |
| 현재 완료 | They **have finished** the project. | The project **has been finished**. |
| 과거 단순 | She **wrote** the report. | The report **was written** by her. |
| 과거 진행 | They **were cleaning** the office. | The office **was being cleaned**. |
| 과거 완료 | He **had completed** the task. | The task **had been completed**. |
| 미래 단순 | They **will deliver** the package. | The package **will be delivered**. |
| 미래 완료 | They **will have built** the house. | The house **will have been built**. |
| 조동사 (can) | You **can see** the mountains. | The mountains **can be seen**. |
| 조동사 (must) | You **must submit** the form. | The form **must be submitted**. |
| 조동사 (should) | They **should fix** the problem. | The problem **should be fixed**. |

### 5.2 조동사와 수동태

```
조동사 + be + 과거분사

  can be done       "This can be done quickly."
  could be seen     "The stars could be seen clearly."
  must be finished  "The work must be finished by Friday."
  should be sent    "The email should be sent today."
  may be delayed    "The flight may be delayed."
  might be changed  "The plan might be changed."
  has to be paid    "The bill has to be paid."
  ought to be told  "She ought to be told the truth."
```

## 6. 특수 수동 구문

### 6.1 Get + 과거분사 (비격식 수동태)

비격식 영어에서는 **get**이 **be**를 대체할 수 있습니다.

```
격식 (be):     "He was fired from his job."
비격식 (get):  "He got fired from his job."

일반적인 "get" 수동태:
  get married     "They got married last June."
  get divorced    "She got divorced in 2020."
  get hurt        "Nobody got hurt in the accident."
  get lost        "We got lost in the city."
  get arrested    "The thief got arrested."
  get paid        "I get paid every two weeks."
  get stuck       "The car got stuck in the mud."
  get killed      "Three people got killed in the crash."
```

> **참고**: "Get" 수동태는 종종 부정적이거나 예상치 못한 일을 암시합니다.

### 6.2 Have/Get Something Done (사역 수동태)

**다른 사람에게 어떤 일을 하도록 시키는** 경우에 사용됩니다.

> **have + 목적어 + 과거분사**
>
> get  + 목적어 + 과거분사
> "I had my hair cut."
> = 누군가에게 머리를 잘라달라고 부탁했다.
> ≠ 내가 직접 머리를 잘랐다.
> "She got her car repaired."
> = 누군가에게 차를 수리해달라고 부탁했다.
> 추가 예시:
> "I need to have my eyes tested."
> "We had the house painted last summer."
> "You should get your teeth checked."
> "He's getting his suit dry-cleaned."

### 6.3 It is said that... / He is said to...

```
사람들이 말하거나 믿는 것을 보도하는 표현 (격식체):

  패턴 1: It + 수동 전달 동사 + that절
    "It is said that he is very rich."
    "It is believed that the earth is 4.5 billion years old."
    "It is reported that the company will close."
    "It was thought that the disease was incurable."

  패턴 2: 주어 + 수동 전달 동사 + to부정사
    "He is said to be very rich."
    "The earth is believed to be 4.5 billion years old."
    "The company is reported to be closing."
    "The disease was thought to be incurable."

  이 패턴에 사용되는 전달 동사:
    say, believe, think, know, report, consider, expect, understand,
    claim, allege, assume, estimate, feel, suppose
```

## 7. 간접화법 소개

### 7.1 직접화법 vs 간접화법

> 직접화법 (Direct Speech): 인용부호 안에 정확한 말 그대로
> → She said, "I am tired."
> 간접화법 (Reported Speech): 인용부호 없이 다른 사람의 말을 전달
> → She said (that) she was tired.
> 주요 변화:
> 1. 인용부호가 제거됨
> 2. 대명사가 변함 (I → she)
> 3. 시제가 한 단계 과거로 후퇴 (am → was) — "시제 후퇴"
> 4. 시간/장소 표현이 바뀔 수 있음 (today → that day)
> 5. "That"은 선택 사항이지만 일반적으로 사용

### 7.2 간접화법이 중요한 이유

간접화법은 다음과 같은 경우에 필수적입니다:
- 대화를 다시 전달할 때
- 뉴스 기사를 작성할 때
- 학술 논문 (출처 인용)
- 일상적인 스토리텔링 ("He told me that...")

## 8. 시제 후퇴 규칙

### 8.1 시제 변화

전달 동사가 **과거 시제**(said, told, asked)인 경우, 간접절의 시제가 한 단계 과거로 후퇴합니다:

> **직접화법                 │   간접화법**
>
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> 현재 단순                  │ 과거 단순
> "I like coffee."           │ She said she liked coffee.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> 현재 진행                  │ 과거 진행
> "I am studying."           │ He said he was studying.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> 현재 완료                  │ 과거 완료
> "I have finished."         │ She said she had finished.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> 과거 단순                  │ 과거 완료
> "I went to Seoul."         │ He said he had gone to Seoul.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> 과거 진행                  │ 과거 완료 진행
> "I was reading."           │ She said she had been reading.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> will                       │ would
> "I will call you."         │ He said he would call me.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> can                        │ could
> "I can help."              │ She said she could help.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> may                        │ might
> "I may come."              │ He said he might come.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> must                       │ had to
> "I must leave."            │ She said she had to leave.
> ├───────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
> shall                      │ should / would
> "Shall I open it?"         │ He asked if he should open it.

### 8.2 시제 후퇴 예외

시제 후퇴가 **선택적**이거나 **불필요**한 경우:

```
1. 정보가 여전히 사실인 경우:
   직접: "The earth revolves around the sun."
   간접: He said (that) the earth revolves around the sun.
         (여전히 사실 — 후퇴 불필요)

2. 전달 동사가 현재 시제인 경우:
   직접: "I'm coming to the party."
   간접: She says (that) she's coming to the party.
         (후퇴 불필요)

3. 변하지 않는 조동사: would, could, should, might, ought to
   직접: "You should study harder."
   간접: He said I should study harder.
         (변화 없음)

4. 과거 완료는 과거 완료 그대로:
   직접: "I had already eaten."
   간접: She said she had already eaten.
         (이미 "가장 먼 과거" 시제)
```

### 8.3 시간과 장소 표현 변화

| 직접화법 | 간접화법 |
|----------|----------|
| today | that day |
| tonight | that night |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| last week | the week before / the previous week |
| next month | the following month |
| ago | before / earlier |
| now | then / at that time |
| here | there |
| this | that |
| these | those |
| come | go |

```
직접: "I will see you here tomorrow."
간접: He said he would see me there the following day.

직접: "I finished this project yesterday."
간접: She said she had finished that project the day before.
```

## 9. 의문문의 간접 전환

### 9.1 예/아니오 의문문

```
공식: asked + if / whether + 주어 + 동사 (서술문 어순)

직접: "Do you like Korean food?"
간접: She asked (me) if/whether I liked Korean food.

직접: "Can you swim?"
간접: He asked if I could swim.

직접: "Have you been to London?"
간접: She asked whether I had been to London.

직접: "Will you come to the party?"
간접: He asked if I would come to the party.

중요: 간접 의문문에서는 의문문 어순을 사용하지 않습니다!
  ✗ She asked if did I like Korean food.
  ✓ She asked if I liked Korean food.

  ✗ He asked could I swim.
  ✓ He asked if I could swim.
```

### 9.2 Wh-의문문

```
공식: asked + 의문사 + 주어 + 동사 (서술문 어순)

직접: "Where do you live?"
간접: She asked (me) where I lived.

직접: "What time does the train leave?"
간접: He asked what time the train left.

직접: "Why are you crying?"
간접: She asked why I was crying.

직접: "How long have you been waiting?"
간접: He asked how long I had been waiting.

중요: 서술문 어순을 사용합니다! 의문문 어순이 아닙니다!
  ✗ She asked where did I live.
  ✓ She asked where I lived.

  ✗ He asked what was my name.
  ✓ He asked what my name was.
```

### 9.3 명령문과 요청문의 간접 전환

```
공식: told/asked/ordered + 사람 + (not) + to부정사

명령:
  직접: "Close the door."
  간접: He told me to close the door.

  직접: "Don't touch that!"
  간접: She told us not to touch that.

요청:
  직접: "Could you please help me?"
  간접: She asked me to help her.

  직접: "Please don't be late."
  간접: He asked us not to be late.

조언:
  직접: "You should see a doctor."
  간접: She advised me to see a doctor.
```

## 10. say vs tell

### 10.1 주요 차이점

> **SAY                          │   TELL**
>
> • 간접 목적어 불필요           │   • 간접 목적어 필요
> • say (that)...              │   • tell + 사람 + (that)...
> • say + 직접화법 가능          │   • 직접화법 인용 불가
> ✓ He said (that) he was      │   ✓ He told me (that) he was
> tired.                     │     tired.
> ✓ He said, "I'm tired."     │   ✗ He told, "I'm tired."
> ✗ He said me that...        │   ✓ He told me that...
> ✓ He said to me that...     │
> (가능하지만 덜 일반적)       │

### 10.2 요약 규칙

```
SAY:  said / said that / said to someone
      He said he was happy.
      He said that he was happy.
      He said to me that he was happy.

TELL: told + 사람 / told + 사람 + that
      He told me he was happy.
      He told me that he was happy.
      ✗ He told that he was happy. (오류 — 사람이 필요!)
```

### 10.3 tell의 관용 표현

```
tell the truth    "Always tell the truth."       진실을 말하다
tell a lie        "She told a lie."              거짓말하다
tell a story      "Grandma told us a story."     이야기하다
tell a joke       "He told a funny joke."        농담하다
tell the time     "Can you tell the time?"       시간을 말하다
tell the difference "Can you tell the difference?" 차이를 구별하다
tell a secret     "Don't tell anyone the secret." 비밀을 말하다
```

## 12. 한국어 화자를 위한 참고사항

### 12.1 수동태 비교

> 한국어는 다양한 수동 구문을 사용합니다:
> 1. -되다 한자어 명사에 붙음:
> 건설되다 (to be constructed), 발견되다 (to be discovered)
> 2. -받다 "받다" 수동:
> 사랑받다 (to be loved), 존경받다 (to be respected)
> 3. 동사 접미사 수동 (-이/-히/-리/-기):
> 잡다 → 잡히다 (catch → be caught)
> 열다 → 열리다 (open → be opened)
> 주요 차이점:
> • 한국어 수동태는 영어보다 덜 자주 사용됨
> • 한국어 화자는 영어에서 능동태를 과도하게 사용하는 경향
> • 영어 수동태는 더 체계적 (be + PP)이며
> 거의 모든 타동사에 적용 가능
> • 한국어 어순 (SOV) vs 영어 수동태 (SVO)
> 전환이 까다로움
> 흔한 오류:
> ✗ "The meeting was held" → "The meeting held"로 쓰는 경우
> ✓ 영어 수동태에서는 반드시 BE 동사를 포함해야 합니다!

### 12.2 간접화법 비교

```
한국어 간접화법은 다음을 사용합니다:
  -고 하다 / -라고 하다 / -다고 하다 / -냐고 하다

  "나는 행복하다"고 그가 말했다.
  → He said (that) he was happy.

주요 차이점:
  • 한국어는 간접화법에서 시제를 후퇴시키지 않음
  • 한국어는 서술(-다고), 의문(-냐고), 명령(-라고),
    제안(-자고) 등 다른 인용 조사를 사용
  • 영어는 모든 서술문 보도에 동일한 "that"을 사용
  • 한국어 학습자들은 시제 후퇴를 자주 잊음!

한국어 화자의 흔한 오류:
  ✗ He said he is happy. (시제 후퇴 누락)
  ✓ He said he was happy.

  ✗ She asked where is the station. (의문문 어순 유지)
  ✓ She asked where the station was.

  ✗ He told that he was tired. ("tell" 뒤에 사람 누락)
  ✓ He told me that he was tired.
```

## 13. 연습 문제

### 연습 1: 능동태 → 수동태

다음 문장을 수동태로 바꾸세요.

1. The teacher explains the lesson every day.
2. Someone stole my bicycle yesterday.
3. They are building a new hospital.
4. The company has hired 50 new employees.
5. People will remember this event for years.
6. You must complete the form before Friday.
7. They had already solved the problem.
8. The mechanic is repairing my car.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. The lesson is explained by the teacher every day.
2. My bicycle was stolen yesterday.
   (행위자 "someone" 생략 — 불명)
3. A new hospital is being built.
   (행위자 "they" 생략 — 불필요)
4. 50 (Fifty) new employees have been hired by the company.
5. This event will be remembered for years.
6. The form must be completed before Friday.
7. The problem had already been solved.
8. My car is being repaired by the mechanic.
```

</details>

### 연습 2: 수동태 → 능동태

다음 문장을 능동태로 바꾸세요.

1. The window was broken by the children.
2. A cure for the disease has been found.
3. The email will be sent tomorrow.
4. The suspects are being questioned by detectives.
5. This book was written by Haruki Murakami.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. The children broke the window.
2. (Scientists/Researchers) have found a cure for the disease.
   (능동태 전환 시 행위자를 제공해야 함)
3. (Someone/They) will send the email tomorrow.
4. Detectives are questioning the suspects.
5. Haruki Murakami wrote this book.
```

</details>

### 연습 3: 간접화법 — 시제 후퇴

다음 직접화법을 간접화법으로 바꾸세요.

1. "I love this city," she said.
2. "We are moving to Busan next month," they told us.
3. "I have never been to Japan," he said.
4. "I saw the accident yesterday," she told the police.
5. "I will call you tomorrow," he said to me.
6. "You can borrow my car," she said.
7. "I must finish this report today," he said.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. She said (that) she loved that city.
2. They told us (that) they were moving to Busan the following month.
3. He said (that) he had never been to Japan.
4. She told the police (that) she had seen the accident the day before.
5. He told me (that) he would call me the next day / the following day.
6. She said (that) I could borrow her car.
7. He said (that) he had to finish that report that day.
```

</details>

### 연습 4: 의문문의 간접 전환

다음 직접 의문문을 간접 의문문으로 바꾸세요.

1. "Do you speak Korean?" she asked me.
2. "Where did you buy this?" he asked.
3. "What time does the movie start?" she asked.
4. "Have you ever been to Europe?" he asked me.
5. "Why are you late?" the teacher asked the student.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. She asked me if/whether I spoke Korean.
2. He asked where I had bought that.
3. She asked what time the movie started.
4. He asked me if/whether I had ever been to Europe.
5. The teacher asked the student why he/she was late.
```

</details>

### 연습 5: say인가 tell인가?

빈칸에 "said"나 "told"를 넣으세요.

1. He ___ that he was tired.
2. She ___ me a funny story.
3. They ___ they would come later.
4. The teacher ___ the students to be quiet.
5. He ___ to me that he disagreed.
6. She ___ a lie about her age.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. He said that he was tired.
2. She told me a funny story.
```

### 연습 6: 종합 연습

올바른 형태를 선택하여 간접화법으로 바꾸세요.

1. "Please close the window," he said to me.
2. "Don't eat too much sugar," the doctor said to the patient.
3. "Let's go to the beach," she said.
4. "I can't come because I'm sick," he told me.
5. "Where were you last night?" she asked him.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. He asked me to close the window.
   (또는: He told me to close the window.)
2. The doctor advised the patient not to eat too much sugar.
   (또는: The doctor told the patient not to eat too much sugar.)
3. She suggested going to the beach.
   (또는: She suggested that they go to the beach.)
4. He told me (that) he couldn't come because he was sick.
5. She asked him where he had been the night before / the previous night.
```

</details>

## 빠른 참조

> **수동태 & 간접화법 빠른 가이드**
>
> 수동태:
> 공식: 주어 + BE (활용) + 과거분사
> 현재: is/are + PP    과거: was/were + PP
> 완료: has/have been + PP   미래: will be + PP
> 조동사: can/must/should be + PP
> 행위자가 불명, 자명, 불필요한 경우 "by" 생략
> 간접화법:
> 서술문: said (that) / told + 사람 + (that)
> 의문문: asked + if/whether (예/아니오)
> asked + 의문사 (wh-의문문)
> 명령문: told/asked + 사람 + (not) to + 원형
> 시제 후퇴: present→past, past→past perfect,
> will→would, can→could, may→might, must→had to
> SAY vs TELL:
> say — 사람 불필요: "He said he was happy."
> tell — 사람 필요: "He told me he was happy."
