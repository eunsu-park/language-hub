## 1. 비즈니스 영어 소개

### 1.1 비즈니스 영어가 중요한 이유

> 비즈니스 영어가 사용되는 분야:
> • 국제 상거래와 무역
> • 다국적 기업
> • 글로벌 컨퍼런스와 정상회의
> • 국제 취업 시장
> • 과학 및 학술 협력
> 핵심 원칙:
> 1. 명확성 — 직접적이고 구체적으로
> 2. 공손함 — 적절한 어조 사용
> 3. 전문성 — 속어와 캐주얼한 표현 피하기
> 4. 간결함 — 상대방의 시간 존중
> 5. 문화적 인식 — 독자에 맞게 조정

## 2. 전문 이메일

### 2.1 비즈니스 이메일 유형

```
요청 (REQUEST):      무언가를 부탁하는 이메일
후속 (FOLLOW-UP):    회의나 통화 후 이메일
불만 (COMPLAINT):    문제를 보고하는 이메일
사과 (APOLOGY):      실수를 인정하는 이메일
확인 (CONFIRMATION): 계획이나 정보를 확인하는 이메일
소개 (INTRODUCTION): 자신이나 타인을 소개하는 이메일
감사 (THANK YOU):    감사를 표현하는 이메일
거절 (DECLINE):      정중하게 거절하는 이메일
```

### 2.2 기능별 핵심 표현

```
서두:
  "I am writing to inform you that..."
  "I am writing regarding..."
  "Further to our conversation on [날짜],..."
  "I would like to draw your attention to..."
  "Thank you for your email dated [날짜]."

요청하기:
  "Could you please send me...?"
  "I would be grateful if you could..."
  "Would it be possible to...?"
  "I would appreciate it if you could..."
  "At your earliest convenience, could you...?"

나쁜 소식 전하기:
  "I regret to inform you that..."
  "Unfortunately, we are unable to..."
  "I'm afraid that we will have to..."
  "We regret any inconvenience this may cause."

문서 첨부:
  "Please find attached the [문서명]."
  "I have enclosed the relevant documents."
  "Attached is the report for your review."

마무리:
  "Please do not hesitate to contact me."
  "I look forward to your reply."
  "Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter."
  "Should you require any further information, please let me know."
```

### 2.3 이메일 작성 지침

```
해야 할 것:
  ✓ 명확하고 구체적인 제목 작성
  ✓ 인사말로 시작하고 맺음말로 끝내기
  ✓ 단락을 짧게 유지 (3-5줄)
  ✓ 보내기 전 교정
  ✓ 여러 항목은 글머리 기호 사용
  ✓ 24-48시간 내에 답장

하지 말아야 할 것:
  ✗ 전체 대문자 사용 (소리 지르는 것으로 읽힘)
  ✗ 격식 이메일에 이모지 사용
  ✗ 지나치게 긴 이메일 작성 (핵심을 말할 것)
  ✗ 불필요하게 "전체 답장" 사용
  ✗ 격식 이메일에서 "ur," "pls," "thx" 같은 약어 사용
  ✗ "please find attached"라고 쓰고 첨부 파일 빠뜨리기
```

## 3. 회의 어휘

### 3.1 회의 전

```
agenda           회의 안건      "Let me share the agenda."
reschedule       일정 변경하다  "Can we reschedule to Thursday?"
chair (v)        회의 진행하다  "Who will chair the meeting?"
attendees        참석자        "Please confirm the attendees."
minutes          회의록        "I'll take the minutes."
conference call  전화 회의      "Let's set up a conference call."
dial in          (전화) 접속하다 "The dial-in number is..."
```

### 3.2 회의 중

```
시작:
  "Let's get started."
  "Thank you all for being here today."
  "The purpose of today's meeting is to..."
  "Shall we begin?"

토론 관리:
  "Let's move on to the next item."
  "Going back to what [이름] said..."
  "Can we stay on topic, please?"
  "Let's table that discussion for now."
  "I'd like to open the floor for discussion."

의견 구하기:
  "What are your thoughts on this?"
  "[이름], what's your take on this?"
  "Does anyone have any comments?"
  "How do you feel about this proposal?"

의견 제시:
  "In my opinion, we should..."
  "I believe that..."
  "From my perspective,..."
  "I'd like to suggest that..."

동의하기:
  "I completely agree."
  "That's a good point."
  "I'm in favor of that."
  "I couldn't agree more."

정중하게 반대하기:
  "I see your point, but..."
  "I'm not sure I agree with that."
  "I understand your perspective, however..."
  "With all due respect, I think..."
  "That's an interesting point, but I'd like to offer
   a different perspective."

정중하게 끼어들기:
  "Sorry to interrupt, but..."
  "Could I just add something?"
  "If I may..."
  "Before we move on, I'd like to mention..."

마무리:
  "To sum up today's discussion..."
  "Let me summarize the key action items."
  "The next meeting will be on [날짜]."
  "Thank you all for your time today."
```

## 4. 프레젠테이션

### 4.1 구조

> 1. 시작 (도입부 + 자기소개 + 개요)
> 2. 본론 (주요 요점과 증거)
> 3. 요약 (핵심 내용)
> 4. Q&A (질의응답)
> 5. 마무리 (청중에게 감사)

### 4.2 유용한 표현

```
자기소개:
  "Good morning, everyone. My name is [이름], and I'm the
   [직책] at [회사]."
  "Thank you for the opportunity to present today."

주제 소개:
  "Today, I'd like to talk about..."
  "The topic of my presentation is..."
  "I'm here today to discuss..."

구조 안내:
  "I've divided my presentation into three parts."
  "First, I'll cover... Then, I'll move on to... Finally,..."
  "My presentation will take approximately 20 minutes."

전환:
  "Moving on to the next point..."
  "Now let's turn our attention to..."
  "This brings me to my next point."
  "As you can see from this slide..."

시각 자료 참조:
  "As this graph shows..."
  "If you look at this chart, you'll notice..."
  "This diagram illustrates..."
  "The figures on the right indicate that..."

강조:
  "I'd like to emphasize that..."
  "The key takeaway here is..."
  "It's important to note that..."
  "This is particularly significant because..."

Q&A 대응:
  "I'd be happy to answer any questions."
  "That's an excellent question."
  "I'm glad you raised that point."
  "I'll get back to you on that."
  "Unfortunately, that's outside the scope of today's
   presentation, but I'd be happy to discuss it afterward."

마무리:
  "To conclude, I'd like to highlight three key points..."
  "Thank you for your attention."
  "I appreciate your time and questions."
```

## 5. 협상

### 5.1 협상 단계

```
1. 준비:    목표, 최저선, 대안을 파악
2. 시작:    라포 형성, 목표 진술
3. 교섭:    제안, 역제안, 타협점 찾기
4. 마무리:  합의 도달, 조건 확인
```

### 5.2 유용한 표현

```
제안하기:
  "We'd like to propose..."
  "Our initial offer is..."
  "We're prepared to offer..."

제안에 대응하기:
  "That's an interesting proposal, but..."
  "We appreciate the offer. However,..."
  "That's slightly outside our budget."
  "We were hoping for something closer to..."

역제안하기:
  "How about we meet in the middle at...?"
  "Would you consider...?"
  "What if we adjusted the terms to...?"
  "We could agree to X if you could offer Y."

타협하기:
  "I think we can work something out."
  "Let's find a middle ground."
  "We're willing to make a concession on..."
  "If you can agree to X, we'll accept Y."

거절하기:
  "I'm afraid that doesn't work for us."
  "We can't go any lower than..."
  "Unfortunately, that's not something we can agree to."
  "I appreciate the offer, but we'll have to decline."

합의 마무리:
  "I think we have a deal."
  "Let's put this in writing."
  "We'll have the contract drawn up."
  "I'm pleased we could reach an agreement."
```

## 6. 취업 면접

### 6.1 일반적인 면접 질문과 전략

```
"Tell me about yourself."
  전략: 간단한 직업 요약 (최대 2-3분)
  구조: 현재 → 과거 → 미래
  "I'm currently a software developer at [회사]. Before that,
   I worked at [회사] for 3 years, where I led a team of 5.
   I'm now looking for a role where I can take on more leadership
   responsibilities."

"What are your strengths?"
  전략: 관련된 강점 2-3개를 예시와 함께 선택
  "One of my key strengths is problem-solving. For example,
   at my previous company, I identified a bottleneck in our
   production process and implemented a solution that reduced
   delays by 30%."

"What is your greatest weakness?"
  전략: 솔직하되 개선 과정을 보여주기
  "I used to struggle with public speaking. To address this,
   I joined a speaking club and have been practicing regularly.
   I recently gave a presentation to 50 people and received
   positive feedback."

"Why do you want to work here?"
  전략: 회사에 대한 조사를 보여주기
  "I admire [회사]'s commitment to innovation, particularly
   your recent work in [분야]. I believe my skills in [기술]
   would be a valuable addition to your team."

"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
  전략: 회사와 맞는 포부 보여주기
  "In five years, I hope to have grown into a senior role
   where I can contribute to strategic decision-making while
   mentoring junior team members."

"Do you have any questions for us?"
  반드시 YES라고 답하세요. 다음에 대해 물어보세요:
  • "What does a typical day look like in this role?"
  • "What are the biggest challenges facing the team right now?"
  • "How do you measure success in this position?"
  • "What opportunities for professional development does
     the company offer?"
```

### 6.2 행동 면접을 위한 STAR 기법

> S — Situation (상황):  배경 설명
> T — Task (과업):       자신의 책임 설명
> A — Action (행동):     자신이 한 일 설명
> R — Result (결과):     결과 공유 (가능하면 숫자와 함께)
> 예시:
> Q: "Tell me about a time you solved a difficult problem."
> S: "At my previous company, we had a client complaint
> about delayed deliveries."
> T: "As project manager, I was responsible for identifying
> the cause and fixing it."
> A: "I analyzed our supply chain and found that a particular
> supplier was consistently late. I negotiated new terms
> and set up a backup supplier."
> R: "As a result, on-time delivery improved from 78% to 96%,
> and client satisfaction increased by 25%."

## 7. 전화와 화상 통화

### 7.1 전화 통화 표현

```
전화 받기:
  "Good morning, [회사명]. [이름] speaking."
  "Hello, this is [이름] from [회사]."
  "[이름] speaking. How can I help you?"

전화 걸기:
  "Hello, this is [이름] from [회사]."
  "I'm calling about..."
  "Could I speak to [이름], please?"

연결 / 대기:
  "One moment, please. I'll transfer you."
  "Could you hold for a moment?"
  "I'll put you through to [부서/이름]."

메시지 남기기:
  "Could I leave a message?"
  "Could you ask [이름] to call me back?"
  "My number is..."

문제 대응:
  "I'm sorry, could you repeat that?"
  "Could you speak a little more slowly, please?"
  "I didn't quite catch that. Could you say it again?"
  "I'm afraid the line is bad. Could you call back?"

마무리:
  "Thank you for calling."
  "I'll follow up with an email."
  "It was nice speaking with you."
```

### 7.2 화상 통화 에티켓

```
시작:
  "Can everyone hear me?" / "Can you see my screen?"
  "I think we're all here. Let's get started."

기술적 문제:
  "You're on mute." / "I think you're muted."
  "Your screen seems to be frozen."
  "I'm having some connection issues."
  "Could you turn on your camera?"
  "Let me share my screen."

토론 관리:
  "[이름], would you like to go first?"
  "Let's go around the room."
  "Please type your questions in the chat."
  "I'll address the questions in the chat at the end."

마무리:
  "I think that covers everything."
  "Thanks everyone. Have a great day."
  "I'll send the meeting notes by end of day."
```

## 8. 네트워킹과 스몰토크

### 8.1 대화 시작하기

```
컨퍼런스에서:
  "Hi, I'm [이름]. I work at [회사]."
  "What brings you to this conference?"
  "Are you enjoying the event?"
  "I really enjoyed that presentation. What did you think?"

비즈니스 만찬에서:
  "Have you been here before? The food is excellent."
  "How do you know [주최자 이름]?"
  "What line of work are you in?"

안전한 스몰토크 주제:
  ✓ 날씨, 여행, 음식, 지역 명소
  ✓ 업계 동향, 최근 뉴스 (비정치적)
  ✓ 스포츠, 취미, 주말 계획
  ✓ 공통 경험 (행사, 장소)

피해야 할 주제:
  ✗ 정치, 종교
  ✗ 개인 급여나 수입
  ✗ 나이, 체중, 외모
  ✗ 논쟁적인 사회 이슈
  ✗ 동료에 대한 험담
```

### 8.2 대화 이어가기

```
관심 표현:
  "That sounds interesting. Tell me more."
  "Really? How did that work out?"
  "That's fascinating."

주제 전환:
  "By the way, have you heard about...?"
  "Speaking of [주제], I was wondering..."
  "That reminds me..."

자연스럽게 빠져나오기:
  "It was great talking to you."
  "I should mingle a bit more. Let's stay in touch."
  "Let me give you my card."
  "I'll connect with you on LinkedIn."
```

### 9.2 서양 비즈니스 환경에서의 한국어 화자 팁

```
1. 초대받으면 이름(first name)을 사용하세요 (계속 "Mr./Ms."라고 하지 마세요)
2. 회의에서 적극적으로 발언하세요 — 침묵은 무관심으로 해석될 수 있습니다
3. "아니오"나 반대 의견을 더 직접적으로 표현하세요
4. 특별히 요청받지 않아도 의견을 말하세요
5. 윗사람이 먼저 말할 때까지 기다리지 마세요
6. 깊은 인사 대신 악수 (단단하고 짧게)
7. 대화 중 눈 맞춤 유지
8. 업무 전 스몰토크가 기대되지만 짧게
```

### 연습 2: 회의 표현

각 상황에 가장 적절한 표현을 선택하세요.

1. 동료에게 정중하게 반대하고 싶을 때:
   a) "You're wrong about that."
   b) "I see your point, but I'd like to offer a different perspective."
   c) "That's a terrible idea."

2. 누군가의 의견을 물어보고 싶을 때:
   a) "Tell me what you think right now."
   b) "Do you even have an opinion?"
   c) "[Name], what are your thoughts on this?"

3. 회의를 마무리하고 싶을 때:
   a) "OK, we're done. Bye."
   b) "To sum up, let me review the key action items."
   c) "Finally! This meeting is over."

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. b) "I see your point, but I'd like to offer a different
      perspective." (정중한 반대)
2. c) "[Name], what are your thoughts on this?"
      (전문적이고 포용적)
3. b) "To sum up, let me review the key action items."
      (체계적이고 전문적인 마무리)
```

</details>

### 연습 3: STAR 기법 연습

STAR 기법을 사용하여 답변하세요: "Tell me about a time you worked under pressure."

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
Situation: "Last year, our team was given a project with a
 deadline that was moved up by two weeks due to a client
 request."

Task: "As the project lead, I needed to reorganize the
 workflow and ensure we met the new deadline without
 sacrificing quality."

Action: "I immediately held a team meeting to reprioritize
 tasks. I created a new timeline, delegated responsibilities
 based on each team member's strengths, and set up daily
 check-ins to monitor progress. I also worked extra hours
 on the most critical components."

Result: "We delivered the project two days before the new
 deadline. The client was impressed with the quality, and
 we received a follow-up contract worth $50,000. My manager
 recognized the team's effort with an internal award."
```

### 연습 4: 역할극 시나리오

다음 전화 통화 상황을 연습하세요.

1. Mr. Park이 부재중이어서 메시지를 남겨야 합니다.
2. 연결 상태가 나빠서 상대방 말을 잘 들을 수 없습니다.
3. 발신자를 회계 부서로 연결해야 합니다.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. "Could I leave a message for Mr. Park, please? Could you
    let him know that [이름] from [회사] called
    regarding the quarterly report? My number is 010-1234-5678.
    I'd appreciate it if he could call me back at his earliest
    convenience. Thank you."

2. "I'm sorry, I'm having trouble hearing you. The line seems
    to be bad. Could you speak a little louder, please? ...
    I'm afraid the connection is still poor. Would you mind
    calling back, or shall I call you back?"

3. "Certainly. Let me transfer you to our accounting department.
    One moment, please. ... I'll put you through now. If we
    get disconnected, you can reach them directly at extension
    302."
```

</details>

## 빠른 참조

> **비즈니스 영어 빠른 가이드**
>
> 이메일: 제목 → 인사 → 목적 → 세부사항 → 마무리
> 요청: "Could you please..." / "I would appreciate if..."
> 나쁜 소식: "I regret to inform you..." / "Unfortunately..."
> 회의: "Let's get started" → "What are your thoughts?"
> 반대: "I see your point, but..." / "With respect,..."
> 마무리: "To sum up..." / "Key action items are..."
> 면접: STAR 기법 사용 (Situation-Task-Action-Result)
> "Tell me about yourself" → 현재 → 과거 → 미래
> 전화: "This is [이름] from [회사]."
> 대기: "Could you hold for a moment?"
> 문제: "Could you repeat that?" / "The line is bad."
> 한국어 화자: 더 직접적으로, 초대받으면 이름 사용,
> 회의에서 적극 발언, 눈 맞춤 유지
