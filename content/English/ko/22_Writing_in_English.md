## 1. 영어 글쓰기 소개

### 1.1 한국어 vs 영어 글쓰기 스타일

> 한국어 글쓰기 패턴 (기-승-전-결):
> 서론 → 전개 → 전환 → 결론
> 간접적 접근: 배경을 먼저, 핵심은 마지막에
> 영어 글쓰기 패턴:
> 핵심 먼저 → 뒷받침 → 세부사항 → 결론
> 직접적 접근: 논지(thesis statement)를 앞에 제시
> 핵심 차이:
> 한국어 독자는 핵심이 나중에 나오길 기대
> 영어 독자는 핵심이 먼저 나오길 기대
> 한국어: "날씨가 나빴고, 교통이 끔찍했고,
> 많은 사람들이 영향을 받아서, 저는 늦었습니다."
> 영어: "I was late because of bad weather and traffic."
> (핵심을 먼저, 이유는 나중에)

## 2. 이메일 작성

### 2.1 이메일 형식

> To:      recipient@email.com
> Cc:      copied.person@email.com
> Subject: 명확하고 구체적인 제목
> 인사말,
> 첫 문장 (이메일의 목적).
> 본문 단락 (세부사항, 요청, 정보).
> 마무리 문장 (실행 항목, 감사, 다음 단계).
> 맺음말,
> 이름

### 2.2 격식체 이메일 예시

```
Subject: Request for Meeting — Q1 Budget Review

Dear Mr. Johnson,

I hope this email finds you well.

I am writing to request a meeting to discuss the Q1 budget
review. Could we schedule a 30-minute meeting sometime
next week? I am available on Tuesday and Thursday afternoon.

Please find the preliminary budget report attached for
your review.

I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
Minjun Kim
Financial Analyst, ABC Corporation
```

### 2.3 비격식체 이메일 예시

```
Subject: Dinner this Saturday?

Hey Sarah,

How's it going?

A few of us are planning to grab dinner this Saturday
at that new Italian place downtown. Want to join?
We're thinking around 7 PM.

Let me know if you can make it!

Cheers,
Jihye
```

### 2.4 인사말과 맺음말

```
격식체 인사말:
  Dear Mr./Ms./Dr. [성],
  Dear Sir/Madam, (이름 모를 때)
  To Whom It May Concern, (매우 격식적, 이름 모를 때)
  Dear Hiring Manager, (입사 지원 시)

준격식체:
  Dear [이름],
  Hello [이름],

비격식체:
  Hi [이름],
  Hey [이름],

격식체 맺음말:
  Sincerely, / Yours sincerely,
  Best regards, / Kind regards,
  Respectfully,

준격식체:
  Best, / All the best,
  Thank you, / Thanks,
  Regards,

비격식체:
  Cheers, / Talk soon,
  Take care, / See you,
```

### 2.5 유용한 이메일 표현

```
서두:
  "I am writing to inquire about..."
  "I am writing in reference to..."
  "Thank you for your email regarding..."
  "Following up on our conversation..."
  "I hope this email finds you well."

요청:
  "Could you please...?"
  "I would appreciate it if you could..."
  "Would it be possible to...?"
  "I was wondering if you could..."

파일 첨부:
  "Please find attached..."
  "I have attached the document for your reference."
  "The requested file is attached."

사과:
  "I apologize for the delay in responding."
  "I'm sorry for any inconvenience caused."
  "Please accept my apologies for..."

마무리:
  "I look forward to hearing from you."
  "Please don't hesitate to contact me if you have questions."
  "Thank you for your time and consideration."
  "I would be happy to discuss this further."
```

## 3. 에세이 구조

### 3.1 다섯 문단 에세이

> 1문단: 서론 (INTRODUCTION)
> • 도입부 (Hook: 관심을 끄는 첫 문장)
> • 배경 정보
> • 논지문 (Thesis statement: 주요 주장 — 마지막 문장)
> 2문단: 본론 1 (BODY PARAGRAPH 1)
> • 주제문 (Topic sentence: 첫 번째 뒷받침 요점)
> • 증거 / 예시
> • 분석 / 설명
> 3문단: 본론 2 (BODY PARAGRAPH 2)
> • 주제문 (두 번째 뒷받침 요점)
> • 증거 / 예시
> • 분석 / 설명
> 4문단: 본론 3 (BODY PARAGRAPH 3)
> • 주제문 (세 번째 뒷받침 요점)
> • 증거 / 예시
> • 분석 / 설명
> 5문단: 결론 (CONCLUSION)
> • 논지 재진술 (다른 표현으로)
> • 핵심 요점 요약
> • 최종 생각 / 행동 촉구 / 더 넓은 함의

### 3.2 강력한 논지문 (Thesis Statement) 작성법

```
논지문이란:
  • 에세이의 주요 주장을 진술하는 문장
  • 서론의 마지막에 위치
  • 구체적(vague하지 않게)
  • 논쟁 가능(단순한 사실이 아닌)
  • 에세이의 구조를 예고

약한 논지문:
  "Pollution is bad."
  (너무 막연함. 누구나 동의함. 논쟁 불가.)

강한 논지문:
  "Urban air pollution can be significantly reduced through
   three key measures: expanding public transportation,
   implementing stricter emissions standards, and increasing
   green spaces in cities."
  (구체적, 논쟁 가능, 세 개의 본론 단락 예고.)
```

### 3.3 도입부 (Hook) 유형

```
질문:        "Have you ever wondered why some languages
              are easier to learn than others?"

통계:        "According to a 2024 study, 1.5 billion people
              are currently learning English worldwide."

인용:        "As Nelson Mandela once said, 'If you talk to a
              man in a language he understands, that goes to
              his head. If you talk to him in his own language,
              that goes to his heart.'"

일화:        "When I first arrived in New York, I couldn't
              order a cup of coffee without pointing."

대담한 진술:  "English is the most important skill a Korean
              student can develop today."
```

## 4. 단락 구성

### 4.1 PEEL 방법

```
P — Point (요점):       주제문으로 핵심 아이디어 제시
E — Evidence (증거):     사실, 데이터, 예시 제공
E — Explanation (설명):  증거를 분석하고 중요성 설명
L — Link (연결):         논지로 다시 연결하거나 전환

예시:
  [P] Regular exercise has been shown to improve mental health.
  [E] A study by Harvard Medical School found that 30 minutes
      of moderate exercise three times per week reduced symptoms
      of depression by up to 47%.
  [E] This suggests that physical activity triggers the release
      of endorphins and serotonin, chemicals that directly
      affect mood and well-being.
  [L] Therefore, incorporating exercise into daily routines
      should be a priority for anyone seeking to improve their
      mental health.
```

### 4.2 단락 간 전환어

```
추가:       Furthermore, Moreover, In addition, Similarly
대조:       However, On the other hand, In contrast, Nevertheless
인과:       Therefore, Consequently, As a result, Thus
예시:       For example, For instance, Specifically
시간:       First, Next, Then, Finally, Meanwhile
결론:       In conclusion, To sum up, Overall, In summary
```

## 5. 구두점 규칙

### 5.1 마침표 (Period: .)

```
• 평서문 끝에: "I live in Seoul."
• 약어에: Dr., Mr., Mrs., Jr. 등
• 참고: 현대식 표기에서는 약어의 마침표를 생략하기도 함:
  US, UK, PhD, CEO, FBI
```

### 5.2 쉼표 (Comma: ,)

```
1. 나열:
   "I bought apples, oranges, and bananas."
   (옥스퍼드 쉼표: "and" 앞의 쉼표는 권장하지만 선택사항)

2. 등위접속사로 독립절 연결 시:
   "I was tired, but I kept working."

3. 도입 요소 뒤:
   "However, the results were surprising."
   "In 2020, the world changed dramatically."
   "After finishing dinner, we went for a walk."

4. 비제한적 정보:
   "My sister, who lives in London, is visiting."
   "Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is vibrant."

5. 직접 호칭:
   "John, can you help me?"
   "Thank you, everyone, for coming."

6. 날짜:
   "March 7, 2026"  단, "7 March 2026" (영국식은 쉼표 없음)

흔한 오류:
  ✗ 쉼표 연결(Comma splice): "I was tired, I went to bed."
  ✓ "I was tired, so I went to bed."
  ✓ "I was tired. I went to bed."
  ✓ "I was tired; I went to bed."
```

### 5.3 세미콜론 (Semicolon: ;)

```
1. 접속사 없이 관련된 독립절 두 개 연결:
   "She studied hard; she passed the exam."

2. however, therefore, moreover 같은 부사 앞에:
   "I was tired; however, I kept working."

3. 복잡한 나열에서:
   "We visited Seoul, South Korea; Tokyo, Japan; and
    Beijing, China."
```

### 5.4 콜론 (Colon: :)

```
1. 목록 앞에:
   "You need three things: pen, paper, and patience."

2. 설명 앞에:
   "There was one problem: we had no money."

3. 인용 앞에 (격식체):
   "The teacher said: 'Study hard.'"

4. 시간:
   "The meeting starts at 3:00 PM."
```

### 5.5 아포스트로피 (Apostrophe: ')

```
1. 축약형:
   don't, can't, I'm, she's, they're, it's (it is)

2. 소유격:
   "John's book" / "the students' projects" (복수 소유격)
   "the children's toys" (불규칙 복수)

흔한 오류:
  its = 소유격 ("The cat licked its paw.")
  it's = it is ("It's raining.")
  ✗ "The dog wagged it's tail." (틀림)
  ✓ "The dog wagged its tail."
```

### 5.6 따옴표 (Quotation Marks: " ")

```
1. 직접 화법:
   "She said, 'I'm coming tomorrow.'"

2. 기사, 장, 노래 제목:
   "I read an article called 'The Future of AI.'"

3. 특이한 용법 강조:
   "The so-called 'expert' had no qualifications."

미국식 vs 영국식:
  미국식: "큰따옴표" 기본, '작은따옴표'는 내부에
  영국식: '작은따옴표' 기본, "큰따옴표"는 내부에
```

## 6. 대문자 규칙

### 6.1 대문자를 써야 할 때

```
1. 문장의 첫 단어:
   "The meeting starts at 9."

2. 고유명사 (특정 인물, 장소, 조직의 이름):
   "Kim Minjun lives in Seoul, South Korea."
   "She works at Samsung Electronics."

3. 이름 앞 직함:
   "President Yoon" / "Dr. Park" / "Professor Lee"

4. 요일, 월, 공휴일 (계절은 아님):
   "Monday, January, Christmas"
   "summer, winter, spring, fall" (소문자)

5. 국적, 언어, 종교:
   "Korean, English, Buddhism, Christianity"

6. 작품 제목 (주요 단어 대문자):
   "The Lord of the Rings"
   "To Kill a Mockingbird"

7. 대명사 "I":
   "My friend and I went to the park."

대문자로 쓰지 않는 경우 (한국어 관습과 다름):
  ✗ "I study English every Day."
  ✓ "I study English every day."
  ✗ "She is a Doctor."
  ✓ "She is a doctor." (일반 직업은 대문자 아님)
```

## 7. 한국어 화자의 흔한 영작 실수

### 7.1 영작 오류 Top 10

```
1. 관사 오류 (a/an/the/무관사)
   ✗ "I want to be doctor."
   ✓ "I want to be a doctor."
   ✗ "I went to the school yesterday."
   ✓ "I went to school yesterday."
   (한국어에는 관사가 없음 — 1위 오류)

2. 주어-동사 수 일치
   ✗ "The students is studying."
   ✓ "The students are studying."
   ✗ "Everyone have a book."
   ✓ "Everyone has a book."

3. 어순 (한국어 SOV → 영어 SVO)
   ✗ "I yesterday the movie watched."
   ✓ "I watched the movie yesterday."
   ✗ "Very much I like English."
   ✓ "I like English very much."

4. 복수형 (한국어는 복수 표시 방식이 다름)
   ✗ "I have three book."
   ✓ "I have three books."
   ✗ "Many student came."
   ✓ "Many students came."

5. 대명사 혼동
   ✗ "My sister, he is a teacher."
   ✓ "My sister, she is a teacher."
   (한국어의 그/그녀 구분이 덜 엄격함)

6. 시제 일관성
   ✗ "Yesterday I go to the store and buy some food."
   ✓ "Yesterday I went to the store and bought some food."

7. 전치사 오류
   ✗ "I'm interested for music."
   ✓ "I'm interested in music."
   ✗ "She arrived to the airport."
   ✓ "She arrived at the airport."

8. 쉼표 연결 (Comma Splices)
   ✗ "I was tired, I went to bed."
   ✓ "I was tired, so I went to bed."

9. 간접적 글쓰기 스타일
   ✗ 에세이에서 핵심 전에 긴 배경 설명으로 시작
   ✓ 서론에서 핵심(논지)을 명확히 진술

10. 만연체 (Run-on Sentences)
    ✗ "I went to Seoul and I met my friend and we had lunch
       and then we went shopping and after that we went home."
    ✓ "I went to Seoul and met my friend. We had lunch and
       went shopping. After that, we went home."
```

## 8. 학술 글쓰기 요령

### 8.1 해야 할 것과 하지 말아야 할 것

```
해야 할 것:
  ✓ 격식적 언어 사용 (축약형, 속어 피하기)
  ✓ 3인칭으로 작성 (격식 에세이에서 "I think" 피하기)
  ✓ 완화 표현 사용 ("may," "suggests," "appears to")
  ✓ 출처를 올바르게 인용
  ✓ 모든 단락에 주제문 사용
  ✓ 꼼꼼히 교정

하지 말아야 할 것:
  ✗ 축약형 사용 (don't → do not)
  ✗ "etc." 또는 "and so on" 사용 (구체적으로 쓸 것)
  ✗ "And," "But," "So"로 문장 시작 (격식 글에서)
  ✗ 감정적 언어나 느낌표 사용
  ✗ "a lot of" 사용 ("numerous," "many," "significant" 사용)
  ✗ 과제가 허용하지 않으면 1인칭 사용
```

### 8.2 유용한 학술 표현

```
서두:
  "This essay examines..."
  "The purpose of this paper is to..."
  "This study investigates the relationship between..."

증거 제시:
  "According to Smith (2023),..."
  "Research conducted by Kim (2022) demonstrates that..."
  "The data suggests that..."

분석:
  "This finding indicates that..."
  "It can be inferred that..."
  "A possible explanation for this is..."

결론:
  "In conclusion, this study has shown that..."
  "The evidence presented suggests that..."
  "Further research is needed to..."
```

## 9. 편집과 교정

### 9.1 자기 편집 체크리스트

```
내용:
  □ 서론에 명확한 논지문이 있는가?
  □ 각 본론 단락에 주제문이 있는가?
  □ 각 요점을 뒷받침하는 충분한 증거가 있는가?
  □ 결론이 요약하고 마무리를 제공하는가?

구성:
  □ 단락이 논리적으로 흐르는가?
  □ 단락 사이에 전환어가 사용되었는가?
  □ 핵심이 초반에 진술되었는가 (끝에 묻혀 있지 않은가)?

문법:
  □ 주어-동사 수 일치?
  □ 올바른 시제 사용과 일관성?
  □ 올바른 관사 사용 (a/an/the)?
  □ 올바른 전치사?

형식:
  □ 올바른 철자?
  □ 올바른 구두점 (쉼표, 마침표, 아포스트로피)?
  □ 올바른 대문자?
  □ 쉼표 연결이나 만연체가 없는가?

문체:
  □ 적절한 격식 수준 (격식/비격식)?
  □ 불필요한 반복이 없는가?
  □ 문장 구조가 다양한가?
  □ 명확하고 간결한 언어?
```

### 9.2 교정 요령

```
1. 글을 소리 내어 읽기 — 귀가 눈이 놓치는 오류를 잡아냄
2. 거꾸로 읽기 (문장 단위) — 철자 오류를 발견
3. 한 번에 한 가지 유형의 오류에 집중 (먼저 시제, 다음 관사 등)
4. 교정 전 휴식 취하기 (새로운 눈으로 더 많은 오류 발견)
5. 체크리스트 사용 (위와 같은)
6. 다른 사람에게 읽어달라고 하기
```

## 10. 연습 문제

### 연습 1: 이메일 작성

상사인 Mr. Williams에게 1주일 휴가를 요청하는 격식체 이메일을 작성하세요. 날짜(3월 15-22일), 이유(가족 행사), 그리고 출발 전 모든 미결 업무를 완료하겠다는 내용을 포함하세요.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
Subject: Vacation Request — March 15-22

Dear Mr. Williams,

I hope this email finds you well.

I am writing to request one week of annual leave from March 15
to March 22. I need to travel to Busan for an important family
event.

I will ensure that all pending tasks and projects are completed
before my departure. I will also brief my colleague, Ms. Park,
on any ongoing matters so that there is no disruption to the
team's work.

I would be grateful if you could approve this request. Please
let me know if you need any additional information.

Thank you for your consideration.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

</details>

### 연습 3: 구두점 교정

다음 문장에 구두점을 추가하거나 수정하세요.

1. I bought apples oranges and bananas at the store
2. The meeting which was scheduled for Monday has been cancelled
3. Its important to bring you're own laptop
4. She asked where is the library
5. Dear Mr Kim I am writing to inform you that

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. I bought apples, oranges, and bananas at the store.
2. The meeting, which was scheduled for Monday, has been cancelled.
   (비제한적 절에는 쉼표 필요)
3. It's important to bring your own laptop.
   (It's = it is; your = 소유격, you're 아님)
4. She asked where the library is. / She asked, "Where is the library?"
5. Dear Mr. Kim, I am writing to inform you that...
   (Mr. 뒤에 마침표; 인사말 뒤에 쉼표)
```

</details>

### 연습 4: 한국어 화자 오류 교정

다음 문장의 오류를 교정하세요.

1. I want to be teacher in the future.
2. She is very beautiful woman.
3. I yesterday to the store went.
4. Many information is available online.
5. I am interested for learning English.

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
1. I want to be a teacher in the future.
   (단수 가산명사 앞에 관사 "a" 필요)
2. She is a very beautiful woman.
   (관사 "a" 필요)
3. I went to the store yesterday.
   (영어 어순: SVO + 시간)
4. A lot of / Much information is available online.
   ("information"은 불가산명사 — "many" 사용 불가)
5. I am interested in learning English.
   (전치사: interested IN, FOR 아님)
```

</details>

### 연습 5: 단락 다시 쓰기

다음 단락을 배경 우선(한국식) 대신 핵심 우선(영어식)으로 다시 쓰세요.

원문: "In Korea, students study very hard. They go to school early in the morning and attend academies at night. They take many exams. Education is very competitive. Because of all this pressure, Korean students often experience stress and burnout."

<details>
<summary>정답 보기</summary>

```
Korean students often experience stress and burnout due to
the intense pressure of the education system. They attend
school from early morning and continue studying at private
academies late into the night. With numerous exams and a
highly competitive environment, students face enormous
academic demands that frequently take a toll on their
mental and physical health.
```

</details>

## 빠른 참조

> **영작 빠른 가이드**
>
> 에세이: 서론 (도입부 + 논지) → 본론 (증거) →
> 결론 (재진술 + 더 넓은 함의)
> 이메일: 인사 → 목적 → 세부사항 → 마무리 → 맺음말
> 단락: 주제문 → 증거 → 설명 → 연결
> 주요 구두점:
> 쉼표: 나열, 도입구, 비제한적 정보
> 세미콜론: 관련 절 연결, however/therefore 앞
> 아포스트로피: 축약형 (it's), 소유격 (John's)
> its = 소유격 / it's = it is
> 한국어 화자 핵심 교정사항:
> 1. 관사 추가 (a/an/the)
> 2. SVO 어순 사용
> 3. 핵심을 먼저 제시, 마지막이 아닌
> 4. 주어-동사 수 일치 확인
> 5. 올바른 전치사 사용
