# 13. Participles, Infinitives, and Gerunds (Participia, Infinitivi, et Gerundia)

## Learning Objectives
- Form and decline the present active participle for all conjugations
- Recognize and use the perfect passive participle (4th principal part)
- Form the future active participle and understand its predictive function
- Identify all six Latin infinitive forms (present/perfect/future x active/passive)
- Understand the gerund as a verbal noun and decline it correctly
- Distinguish the gerundive from the gerund and recognize obligation usage
- Form and translate both active and passive periphrastic conjugations

## 1. Overview of Verbal Nouns and Adjectives

Latin has a rich system of verbal forms that function as nouns or adjectives while retaining verbal qualities (taking objects, being modified by adverbs).

> **Verbal Adjectives** (agree with nouns in gender/number/case):
>
> - **Present Active Participle**: amāns — "loving"
> - **Perfect Passive Participle**: amātus — "having been loved"
> - **Future Active Participle**: amātūrus — "about to love"
> - **Gerundive**: amandus — "needing to be loved"
>
> **Verbal Nouns:**
>
> - **Infinitive**: amāre — "to love"
> - **Gerund**: amandum — "the act of loving"
> - **Supine**: amātum — "(in order) to love"

> Note: Latin has **no present passive participle** and **no perfect active participle**. This is a gap that deponent verbs partially fill (their PPP is active in meaning).

## 2. Present Active Participle

### 2.1 Formation

**Present stem + -ns (nom. sg.) / -ntis (gen. sg.)**

The present active participle is a **3rd declension adjective** (one-termination type).

| Conjugation | Nom. sg. | Gen. sg. | Example |
|-------------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1st (amō) | amā**ns** | amā**ntis** | loving |
| 2nd (moneō) | monē**ns** | monē**ntis** | warning |
| 3rd (dūcō) | dūcē**ns** | dūcē**ntis** | leading |
| 3rd-iō (capiō) | capiē**ns** | capiē**ntis** | taking |
| 4th (audiō) | audiē**ns** | audiē**ntis** | hearing |

### 2.2 Declension

The participle declines like a 3rd declension i-stem adjective. Using *amāns, amantis*:

| Case | M./F. sg. | N. sg. | M./F. pl. | N. pl. |
|------|----------|--------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | amāns | amāns | amantēs | amantia |
| Gen. | amantis | amantis | amantium | amantium |
| Dat. | amantī | amantī | amantibus | amantibus |
| Acc. | amantem | amāns | amantēs | amantia |
| Abl. | amantī / amante | amantī / amante | amantibus | amantibus |

> **Ablative note**: The ablative singular is **-ī** when used as a pure adjective, **-e** when used as a noun or in an ablative absolute construction.

### 2.3 Usage and Translation

The present active participle expresses an action **simultaneous** with the main verb:

| Latin | Literal | Natural English |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| *Puella cantāns ambulat.* | The girl singing walks. | The girl walks while singing. |
| *Mīlitēs pugnantēs cecidērunt.* | The soldiers fighting fell. | The soldiers fell while fighting. |
| *Virum currentem vīdī.* | I saw the man running. | I saw the man as he was running. |

## 3. Perfect Passive Participle

### 3.1 Formation

The PPP is the **4th principal part** of the verb. It declines as a regular 1st/2nd declension adjective (-us, -a, -um).

| Verb | PPP (m./f./n.) | Meaning |
|------|----------------|---------|
| amō | amātus, -a, -um | (having been) loved |
| moneō | monitus, -a, -um | (having been) warned |
| dūcō | ductus, -a, -um | (having been) led |
| capiō | captus, -a, -um | (having been) taken |
| audiō | audītus, -a, -um | (having been) heard |
| mittō | missus, -a, -um | (having been) sent |
| scrībō | scrīptus, -a, -um | (having been) written |
| faciō | factus, -a, -um | (having been) made |

### 3.2 Usage

The PPP expresses an action **completed before** the main verb:

| Latin | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| *Urbs capta dēlēta est.* | The captured city was destroyed. (= The city, having been captured, was destroyed.) |
| *Litterae missae pervēnērunt.* | The sent letters arrived. |
| *Verba dicta nōn revocāntur.* | Spoken words are not recalled. |

> **Deponent PPPs are active**: *profectus* = "having departed," *secūtus* = "having followed," *locūtus* = "having spoken."

---

## 4. Future Active Participle

### 4.1 Formation

**4th principal part stem + -ūrus, -ūra, -ūrum**

| Verb | PPP Stem | Future Active Participle | Meaning |
|------|----------|-------------------------|---------|
| amō | amāt- | amāt**ūrus**, -a, -um | about to love |
| moneō | monit- | monit**ūrus**, -a, -um | about to warn |
| dūcō | duct- | duct**ūrus**, -a, -um | about to lead |
| capiō | capt- | capt**ūrus**, -a, -um | about to take |
| audiō | audīt- | audīt**ūrus**, -a, -um | about to hear |
| esse | — | fut**ūrus**, -a, -um | about to be |

### 4.2 Usage

Expresses **intention, imminence, or destiny**:

| Latin | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| *Mīlitēs pugnatūrī arma cēpērunt.* | The soldiers, about to fight, took up arms. |
| *Caesar profectūrus erat.* | Caesar was about to depart. |
| *Morītūrī tē salūtant.* | Those about to die salute you. |

### 4.3 Summary of All Three Participles

> **Participle Overview**
>
> |  | Active | Passive |
> |---|---|---|
> | **Present** | amāns (loving) | ——— |
> | **Perfect** | ——— | amātus (having been loved) |
> | **Future** | amātūrus (about to love) | ——— (gerundive fills gap) |

## 5. The Six Infinitive Forms

Latin has six infinitive forms — three tenses, each in active and passive voice.

### 5.1 Complete Infinitive Table

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Present** | amāre (to love) | amārī (to be loved) |
| **Perfect** | amāvisse (to have loved) | amātus, -a, -um esse (to have been loved) |
| **Future** | amātūrus, -a, -um esse (to be about to love) | amātum īrī (to be about to be loved) |

### 5.2 Formation Details

**Present infinitives** (from Lesson 4):

| Conjugation | Active | Passive |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| 1st | amāre | amārī |
| 2nd | monēre | monērī |
| 3rd | dūcere | dūcī |
| 3rd-iō | capere | capī |
| 4th | audīre | audīrī |

**Perfect infinitives**:

| Form | Construction | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Active | Perfect stem + **-isse** | amāvisse, monu**isse**, dūx**isse** |
| Passive | PPP + **esse** | amātum esse, monitum esse, ductum esse |

> Note: The perfect active infinitive ending **-isse** sometimes contracts: *amāvisse* → *amāsse*, *dīxisse* → *dīxe* (in poetry).

**Future infinitives**:

| Form | Construction | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Active | Future active participle + **esse** | amātūrum esse |
| Passive | Supine (acc.) + **īrī** | amātum īrī |

> The future passive infinitive (*amātum īrī*) is rare and often avoided in favor of a *fore ut* + subjunctive construction.

### 5.3 Infinitives of *esse*

| Tense | Form |
|-------|------|
| Present | esse (to be) |
| Perfect | fuisse (to have been) |
| Future | futūrum, -am, -um esse / fore (to be about to be) |

## 6. The Gerund

The gerund is a **verbal noun** — it lets you use a verb in noun positions (as object of a preposition, in the genitive, etc.).

### 6.1 Formation

**Present stem + -ndum** (2nd declension neuter singular only)

| Conjugation | Gerund (gen. sg.) | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| 1st | ama**ndī** | of loving |
| 2nd | monē**ndī** | of warning |
| 3rd | dūcē**ndī** | of leading |
| 3rd-iō | capiē**ndī** | of taking |
| 4th | audiē**ndī** | of hearing |

### 6.2 Declension

The gerund exists only in the **singular** and only in **oblique cases** (no nominative — the infinitive fills that role):

| Case | Form | Use | Example |
|------|------|-----|---------|
| Nom. | — (use infinitive) | Subject | *Errāre hūmānum est.* (To err is human.) |
| Gen. | amandī | "of loving" | *ars amandī* (the art of loving) |
| Dat. | amandō | "for loving" | (rare) |
| Acc. | amandum | after *ad* | *ad amandum* (for the purpose of loving) |
| Abl. | amandō | "by loving" | *amandō discimus* (we learn by loving) |

### 6.3 Common Patterns

| Pattern | Latin | Translation |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| *causā/grātiā* + gen. | *discendī causā* | for the sake of learning |
| *ad* + acc. | *ad discendum* | for the purpose of learning |
| Ablative of means | *legēndō* | by reading |
| Genitive with noun | *cupīditās discendī* | desire of learning |

## 7. The Gerundive

The gerundive (also called the future passive participle) is a **verbal adjective** that expresses **necessity or obligation**.

### 7.1 Formation

**Present stem + -ndus, -nda, -ndum**

The gerundive declines as a 1st/2nd declension adjective and **agrees with its noun**.

| Conjugation | M. | F. | N. |
|-------------|----|----|-----|
| 1st | amandus | amanda | amandum |
| 2nd | monendus | monenda | monendum |
| 3rd | dūcendus | dūcenda | dūcendum |
| 4th | audiendus | audienda | audiendum |

### 7.2 Gerundive of Obligation (Passive Periphrastic)

The gerundive + a form of *esse* expresses what **must** or **ought to** be done:

| Latin | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| *Carthāgō dēlenda est.* | Carthage must be destroyed. |
| *Liber legendus est.* | The book must be read. |
| *Pāx servanda erat.* | Peace had to be preserved. |
| *Hoc faciendum erit.* | This will have to be done. |

### 7.3 Dative of Agent with the Gerundive

With the gerundive of obligation, the person who **must** do the action is expressed in the **dative** (not *ā/ab* + ablative):

| Latin | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| *Liber **mihi** legendus est.* | The book must be read **by me**. (= I must read the book.) |
| *Hoc **nōbīs** faciendum est.* | This must be done **by us**. (= We must do this.) |
| *Hostēs **vōbīs** vincendī sunt.* | The enemies must be conquered **by you**. |

### 7.4 Gerund vs. Gerundive

When the gerund would take a **direct object**, Latin prefers replacing it with the gerundive construction:

| Gerund (less preferred) | Gerundive (preferred) | Translation |
|------------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| *ad legendum librōs* | *ad librōs legendōs* | for reading books |
| *legendī librōs causā* | *librōrum legendōrum causā* | for the sake of reading books |

> The gerundive **agrees** with the noun (here *librōs* → *legendōs*; *librōrum* → *legendōrum*).

## 8. Periphrastic Conjugations

### 8.1 Active Periphrastic (Future Active Participle + esse)

Expresses **intention, imminence, or likelihood**:

| Latin | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| *Profectūrus sum.* | I am about to depart. |
| *Ventūrī erant.* | They were about to come. |
| *Dictūrus erō.* | I shall be about to say. (= I am going to say.) |

### 8.2 Passive Periphrastic (Gerundive + esse)

Expresses **obligation or necessity** (see Section 7.2 above):

| Latin | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| *Faciendum est.* | It must be done. |
| *Nōbīs eundum est.* | We must go. (lit. "It must be gone by us.") |
| *Tibi respondendum erat.* | You had to answer. |

### 8.3 Complete Paradigm: Active Periphrastic (amō)

| Tense | Form | Translation |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Present | amātūrus, -a sum | I am about to love |
| Imperfect | amātūrus, -a eram | I was about to love |
| Future | amātūrus, -a erō | I shall be about to love |
| Perfect | amātūrus, -a fuī | I was about to love |
| Pluperfect | amātūrus, -a fueram | I had been about to love |

1. amāre
2. amāvisse

1. The art of speaking (loquor — use gerund: *loquendī*)
2. For the sake of defending the city (dēfendō; urbs — use gerundive)
3. By reading books (legō; liber — use gerundive)
4. The desire of learning (discō — use gerund)

## Quick Reference Card

> **PARTICIPLES**
> - **Present Active**: stem + -ns, -ntis (3rd decl.)
> - **Perfect Passive**: 4th PP (-us, -a, -um) (1st/2nd decl.)
> - **Future Active**: PPP stem + -ūrus, -a, -um (1st/2nd decl.)
>
> **INFINITIVES**
> - **Pres. Act.**: -āre / -ēre / -ere / -īre
> - **Pres. Pass.**: -ārī / -ērī / -ī / -īrī
> - **Perf. Act.**: perfect stem + -isse
> - **Perf. Pass.**: PPP + esse
> - **Fut. Act.**: fut. act. participle + esse
> - **Fut. Pass.**: supine + īrī (rare)
>
> - **GERUND**: present stem + -ndī/-ndō/-ndum (neut. sg. noun)
> - **GERUNDIVE**: present stem + -ndus/-nda/-ndum (adjective)
>
> **PERIPHRASTIC**
> - **Active**: fut. act. participle + esse — "about to ___"
> - **Passive**: gerundive + esse — "must be ___ed"
