## 1. Fourth Declension Overview

The fourth declension is characterized by the stem vowel **-u-**. Most nouns are **masculine**; a few are **feminine** (notably *manus* and *domus*); a small number are **neuter**.

### Key Characteristics

- **Genitive singular** ends in **-ūs** (this identifies the declension)
- Most nouns are **masculine**
- Notable feminine exceptions: **manus** (hand), **domus** (house), **porticus** (colonnade), **tribus** (tribe)
- Only a handful of **neuter** nouns exist

> **FOURTH DECLENSION IDENTIFICATION**
>
> - Nom. sg.: -us (m./f.) or -ū (n.)
> - Gen. sg.: -ūs
>
> Compare with 2nd declension:
> - 2nd: -us, gen. -ī
> - 4th: -us, gen. -ūs
>
> Always check the genitive!

## 2. Fourth Declension: -us Stems (Masculine/Feminine)

### Endings

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | -us | -ūs |
| Genitive | -ūs | -uum |
| Dative | -uī | -ibus |
| Accusative | -um | -ūs |
| Ablative | -ū | -ibus |

### Model Noun: manus, manūs (f.) — *hand*

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | man-us | man-ūs |
| Gen. | man-ūs | man-uum |
| Dat. | man-uī | man-ibus |
| Acc. | man-um | man-ūs |
| Abl. | man-ū | man-ibus |

> **Note**: Three forms look identical: nom. sg. (-us), nom. pl. (-ūs), and acc. pl. (-ūs). The difference is vowel length — *manus* (short) vs. *manūs* (long) — but context usually clarifies.

### Model Noun: exercitus, exercitūs (m.) — *army*

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | exercit-us | exercit-ūs |
| Gen. | exercit-ūs | exercit-uum |
| Dat. | exercit-uī | exercit-ibus |
| Acc. | exercit-um | exercit-ūs |
| Abl. | exercit-ū | exercit-ibus |

### Common Fourth Declension Nouns

| Noun | Gender | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| adventus, -ūs | m. | arrival |
| cursus, -ūs | m. | course, running |
| exercitus, -ūs | m. | army |
| frūctus, -ūs | m. | fruit, enjoyment |
| gradus, -ūs | m. | step, degree |
| impetus, -ūs | m. | attack |
| manus, -ūs | f. | hand; band (of men) |
| metus, -ūs | m. | fear |
| passus, -ūs | m. | pace, step |
| portus, -ūs | m. | port, harbor |
| senātus, -ūs | m. | senate |
| spīritus, -ūs | m. | spirit, breath |
| versus, -ūs | m. | verse, line |
| vultus, -ūs | m. | face, expression |

### Example Sentences

- **Exercitus Rōmānus ad portum vēnit.** — *The Roman army came to the harbor.*
- **Manūs mīlitum fortēs erant.** — *The hands of the soldiers were strong.*
- **Metus mortis magnus est.** — *The fear of death is great.*
- **Senātus populusque Rōmānus** (SPQR) — *The Senate and People of Rome*
- **Impetum in hostēs fēcērunt.** — *They made an attack against the enemies.*

## 3. Fourth Declension: -ū Stems (Neuter)

Neuter fourth declension nouns are rare but important. They follow the **neuter rule**: nominative = accusative.

### Endings

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | -ū | -ua |
| Genitive | -ūs | -uum |
| Dative | -ū | -ibus |
| Accusative | -ū | -ua |
| Ablative | -ū | -ibus |

### Model Noun: cornū, cornūs (n.) — *horn, wing (of an army)*

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | corn-ū | corn-ua |
| Gen. | corn-ūs | corn-uum |
| Dat. | corn-ū | corn-ibus |
| Acc. | corn-ū | corn-ua |
| Abl. | corn-ū | corn-ibus |

### Model Noun: genū, genūs (n.) — *knee*

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | gen-ū | gen-ua |
| Gen. | gen-ūs | gen-uum |
| Dat. | gen-ū | gen-ibus |
| Acc. | gen-ū | gen-ua |
| Abl. | gen-ū | gen-ibus |

> **한국어 화자 참고**: The neuter 4th declension has very few nouns. The three most important are **cornū** (horn), **genū** (knee), and **verū** (spit/skewer). Notice that the singular forms nom., dat., acc., and abl. are all identical (-ū).

### Example Sentences

- **In dextrō cornū equitēs pugnābant.** — *On the right wing the cavalry were fighting.*
- **Mīlitēs in genua cecidērunt.** — *The soldiers fell to their knees.*

## 4. Fifth Declension

The fifth declension is the **smallest** declension. It is characterized by the stem vowel **-ē-**. Almost all nouns are **feminine**; the only common masculine exception is **diēs** (day).

### Endings

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | -ēs | -ēs |
| Genitive | -eī / -ēī | -ērum |
| Dative | -eī / -ēī | -ēbus |
| Accusative | -em | -ēs |
| Ablative | -ē | -ēbus |

> **Note on genitive/dative singular**: After a consonant, the ending is **-eī** (one syllable less); after a vowel, **-ēī** (two syllables). So: *r-eī* but *di-ēī*.

### Model Noun: rēs, reī (f.) — *thing, matter, affair*

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | r-ēs | r-ēs |
| Gen. | r-eī | r-ērum |
| Dat. | r-eī | r-ēbus |
| Acc. | r-em | r-ēs |
| Abl. | r-ē | r-ēbus |

> **rēs** is one of the most common Latin nouns. Key phrases:
> - **rēs pūblica** — *the republic, public affair*
> - **rēs mīlitāris** — *military matters*
> - **rēs novae** — *revolution* (lit. "new things")
> - **rē vērā** — *in truth, actually*

### Model Noun: diēs, diēī (m./f.) — *day*

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | di-ēs | di-ēs |
| Gen. | di-ēī | di-ērum |
| Dat. | di-ēī | di-ēbus |
| Acc. | di-em | di-ēs |
| Abl. | di-ē | di-ēbus |

> **diēs** is usually **masculine** but can be **feminine** when referring to an appointed day or date.

### Common Fifth Declension Nouns

| Noun | Gender | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| aciēs, -ēī | f. | battle line, sharp edge |
| diēs, -ēī | m. (f.) | day |
| effigiēs, -ēī | f. | image, likeness |
| faciēs, -ēī | f. | face, appearance |
| fidēs, -ēī | f. | faith, trust |
| glaciēs, -ēī | f. | ice |
| rēs, reī | f. | thing, matter |
| spēs, speī | f. | hope |
| speciēs, -ēī | f. | appearance, kind |

> **Note**: Most fifth declension nouns lack a complete plural. Only **rēs** and **diēs** have full plural forms.

### Relationship to First Declension

Several fifth declension nouns have **first declension doublets**:

| 5th Declension | 1st Declension | Meaning |
|---------------|----------------|---------|
| māteriēs, -ēī | māteria, -ae | material |
| lūxuriēs, -ēī | lūxuria, -ae | luxury |

This shows the historical connection between the -ē- stem and the -ā- stem declensions.

## 5. All Five Declensions Compared

### Side-by-Side Singular Endings

| Case | 1st (f.) | 2nd (m.) | 2nd (n.) | 3rd (cons.) | 4th (m.) | 4th (n.) | 5th (f.) |
|------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|----------|----------|----------|
| Nom. | -a | -us | -um | (varies) | -us | -ū | -ēs |
| Gen. | -ae | -ī | -ī | -is | -ūs | -ūs | -eī |
| Dat. | -ae | -ō | -ō | -ī | -uī | -ū | -eī |
| Acc. | -am | -um | -um | -em | -um | -ū | -em |
| Abl. | -ā | -ō | -ō | -e | -ū | -ū | -ē |

### Side-by-Side Plural Endings

| Case | 1st (f.) | 2nd (m.) | 2nd (n.) | 3rd (cons.) | 4th (m.) | 4th (n.) | 5th (f.) |
|------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|----------|----------|----------|
| Nom. | -ae | -ī | -a | -ēs | -ūs | -ua | -ēs |
| Gen. | -ārum | -ōrum | -ōrum | -um | -uum | -uum | -ērum |
| Dat. | -īs | -īs | -īs | -ibus | -ibus | -ibus | -ēbus |
| Acc. | -ās | -ōs | -a | -ēs | -ūs | -ua | -ēs |
| Abl. | -īs | -īs | -īs | -ibus | -ibus | -ibus | -ēbus |

> **HOW TO IDENTIFY THE DECLENSION**
>
> Look at the GENITIVE SINGULAR:
> - -ae   → 1st declension
> - -ī    → 2nd declension
> - -is   → 3rd declension
> - -ūs   → 4th declension
> - -eī   → 5th declension
>
> The genitive NEVER lies!

## 6. The Special Case of Domus

**domus, domūs** (f.) — *house, home*

This is one of the most frequently encountered nouns in Latin, and it mixes forms from the **second** and **fourth** declensions.

### Full Declension

| Case | Singular | Plural | Notes |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| Nom. | domus | domūs | 4th decl. |
| Gen. | domūs / domī | domuum / domōrum | 4th / 2nd |
| Dat. | domuī / domō | domibus | 4th / 2nd |
| Acc. | domum | domūs / domōs | 4th / 2nd |
| Abl. | domū / domō | domibus | 4th / 2nd |
| Locative | **domī** | — | special form |

### The Locative Case

**domus** is one of a small group of nouns that retains the **locative case**, expressing "at/in" without a preposition:

- **domī** — *at home*
- **domum** — *homeward, (to) home* (accusative of direction)
- **domō** — *from home* (ablative of separation)

> Compare with English: "I'm going **home**" (no preposition needed, just like Latin **domum**).

### Example Sentences

- **Domī manēbō.** — *I will stay at home.*
- **Domum redeō.** — *I am returning home.*
- **Domō discēdō.** — *I am leaving from home.*
- **Domus senātōris magna est.** — *The house of the senator is large.*

## 7. Reading Practice

### Passage: Dē Rē Pūblicā

> Rēs pūblica Rōmāna ā senātū et populō regēbātur. Senātus in **domō** pūblicā conveniēbat. **Exercitūs** magnī in aciē stābant. **Manūs** mīlitum gladiōs tenēbant. Post multōs **diēs** bellī, **spēs** pācis vēnit. **Cornua** exercitūs hostēs superāvērunt. Haec **rēs** glōriam populō Rōmānō dedit.

Translation:

> The Roman republic was governed by the senate and the people. The senate used to meet in the public **house** (senate house). Great **armies** stood in the battle line. The **hands** of the soldiers held swords. After many **days** of war, the **hope** of peace came. The **wings** of the army overcame the enemies. This **affair** gave glory to the Roman people.
