## 1. Perfect Subjunctive

### 1.1 Active Formation

**Perfect stem + -eri- + personal endings**

| Person | Ending | amō (perf. stem: amāv-) |
|--------|--------|------------------------|
| 1sg | -erim | amāv**erim** |
| 2sg | -erīs | amāv**erīs** |
| 3sg | -erit | amāv**erit** |
| 1pl | -erīmus | amāv**erīmus** |
| 2pl | -erītis | amāv**erītis** |
| 3pl | -erint | amāv**erint** |

> **Caution**: The perfect subjunctive (*amāverim*) looks similar to the future perfect indicative (*amāverō*). Compare carefully:

| Tense/Mood | 1sg | 3sg | 3pl |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Future Perfect Indicative | amāv**erō** | amāv**erit** | amāv**erint** |
| Perfect Subjunctive | amāv**erim** | amāv**erit** | amāv**erint** |

The 1sg form is the clearest distinguisher: **-erō** (indicative) vs. **-erim** (subjunctive).

### 1.2 Perfect Subjunctive of *esse*

| Person | Form |
|--------|------|
| 1sg | fuerim |
| 2sg | fuerīs |
| 3sg | fuerit |
| 1pl | fuerīmus |
| 2pl | fuerītis |
| 3pl | fuerint |

### 1.3 Passive Formation

**PPP + present subjunctive of *esse***

| Person | Masculine sg. | Feminine sg. |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| 1sg | amātus sim | amāta sim |
| 2sg | amātus sīs | amāta sīs |
| 3sg | amātus sit | amāta sit |
| 1pl | amātī sīmus | amātae sīmus |
| 2pl | amātī sītis | amātae sītis |
| 3pl | amātī sint | amātae sint |

## 2. Pluperfect Subjunctive

### 2.1 Active Formation

**Perfect active infinitive + personal endings**

This is the easiest subjunctive to form — just add endings to the perfect infinitive (*amāvisse*).

| Person | Ending | amō (perf. inf.: amāvisse) |
|--------|--------|---------------------------|
| 1sg | -m | amāvisse**m** |
| 2sg | -s | amāvissē**s** |
| 3sg | -t | amāvisse**t** |
| 1pl | -mus | amāvissē**mus** |
| 2pl | -tis | amāvissē**tis** |
| 3pl | -nt | amāvisse**nt** |

### 2.2 Pluperfect Subjunctive of *esse*

| Person | Form |
|--------|------|
| 1sg | fuissem |
| 2sg | fuissēs |
| 3sg | fuisset |
| 1pl | fuissēmus |
| 2pl | fuissētis |
| 3pl | fuissent |

### 2.3 Passive Formation

**PPP + imperfect subjunctive of *esse***

| Person | Masculine sg. | Plural (m.) |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| 1sg | amātus essem | amātī essēmus |
| 2sg | amātus essēs | amātī essētis |
| 3sg | amātus esset | amātī essent |

### 2.4 Complete Subjunctive System Overview

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present | stem vowel change + endings | stem vowel change + passive endings |
| Imperfect | infinitive + endings | infinitive + passive endings |
| Perfect | perf. stem + -eri- + endings | PPP + sim/sīs/sit... |
| Pluperfect | perf. stem + -isse- + endings | PPP + essem/essēs/esset... |

## 3. Deponent Verbs: Full Treatment

Deponent verbs have **passive forms** but **active meanings**. They appear in all tenses and moods.

### 3.1 Principal Parts

Deponent verbs have only **three** principal parts (all passive-looking):

| Verb | 1st PP | 2nd PP | 3rd PP | Meaning |
|------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| cōnor (1st) | cōnor | cōnārī | cōnātus sum | try |
| vereor (2nd) | vereor | verērī | veritus sum | fear |
| sequor (3rd) | sequor | sequī | secūtus sum | follow |
| loquor (3rd) | loquor | loquī | locūtus sum | speak |
| morior (3rd-iō) | morior | morī | mortuus sum | die |
| patior (3rd-iō) | patior | patī | passus sum | suffer, allow |
| ūtor (3rd) | ūtor | ūtī | ūsus sum | use (+ abl.) |
| proficīscor (3rd) | proficīscor | proficīscī | profectus sum | set out |
| ingredior (3rd-iō) | ingredior | ingredī | ingressus sum | enter |
| ēgredior (3rd-iō) | ēgredior | ēgredī | ēgressus sum | go out |
| orior (4th) | orior | orīrī | ortus sum | arise |

### 3.2 Complete Conjugation: sequor, sequī, secūtus sum

**Indicative:**

| Tense | 1sg | 2sg | 3sg | 1pl | 2pl | 3pl |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Present | sequor | sequeris | sequitur | sequimur | sequiminī | sequuntur |
| Imperfect | sequēbar | sequēbāris | sequēbātur | sequēbāmur | sequēbāminī | sequēbantur |
| Future | sequar | sequēris | sequētur | sequēmur | sequēminī | sequentur |
| Perfect | secūtus sum | secūtus es | secūtus est | secūtī sumus | secūtī estis | secūtī sunt |
| Pluperfect | secūtus eram | secūtus erās | secūtus erat | secūtī erāmus | secūtī erātis | secūtī erant |
| Fut. Perf. | secūtus erō | secūtus eris | secūtus erit | secūtī erimus | secūtī eritis | secūtī erunt |

**Subjunctive:**

| Tense | 1sg | 3sg | 3pl |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|
| Present | sequar | sequātur | sequantur |
| Imperfect | sequerer | sequerētur | sequerentur |
| Perfect | secūtus sim | secūtus sit | secūtī sint |
| Pluperfect | secūtus essem | secūtus esset | secūtī essent |

### 3.3 Deponent Participles and Infinitives

Deponent verbs have **active-meaning** participles and infinitives despite passive forms:

| Form | Regular (passive meaning) | Deponent (active meaning!) |
|------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| Pres. Act. Participle | — | **sequēns** (following) |
| Perf. Pass. Participle | amātus (having been loved) | **secūtus** (having followed) |
| Fut. Act. Participle | — | **secūtūrus** (about to follow) |
| Pres. Infinitive | amārī (to be loved) | **sequī** (to follow) |
| Perf. Infinitive | amātum esse (to have been loved) | **secūtum esse** (to have followed) |
| Fut. Infinitive | — | **secūtūrum esse** (to be about to follow) |

> **Key point**: The PPP of a deponent verb is **active in meaning**: *secūtus* = "having followed" (not "having been followed"). This is the one exception to the rule that PPPs are passive.

### 3.4 Special Constructions with Deponent Verbs

| Verb | Special Feature | Example |
|------|----------------|---------|
| ūtor (+ abl.) | Takes ablative, not accusative | *Gladiō ūtitur.* (He uses a sword.) |
| fruor (+ abl.) | Takes ablative | *Vītā fruor.* (I enjoy life.) |
| fungor (+ abl.) | Takes ablative | *Mūnere fungor.* (I perform the duty.) |
| potior (+ abl./gen.) | Takes ablative or genitive | *Urbe potītus est.* (He gained control of the city.) |
| vēscor (+ abl.) | Takes ablative | *Carne vēscuntur.* (They feed on meat.) |

## 4. Semi-Deponent Verbs

Semi-deponent verbs are **active in the present system** but **deponent (passive form, active meaning) in the perfect system**.

### 4.1 Common Semi-Deponent Verbs

| Verb | Present (active) | Perfect (deponent) | Meaning |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| audeō, audēre | audeō, audēs, audet... | ausus sum | dare |
| gaudeō, gaudēre | gaudeō, gaudēs, gaudet... | gāvīsus sum | rejoice |
| soleō, solēre | soleō, solēs, solet... | solitus sum | be accustomed |
| fīdō, fīdere | fīdō, fīdis, fīdit... | fīsus sum | trust (+ dat.) |

### 4.2 Conjugation Example: audeō

| Tense | Form | Translation |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Present ind. | audeō | I dare |
| Imperfect ind. | audēbam | I was daring |
| Future ind. | audēbō | I will dare |
| Perfect ind. | ausus sum | I dared |
| Pluperfect ind. | ausus eram | I had dared |
| Future perfect ind. | ausus erō | I will have dared |

> The present system looks like a normal 2nd conjugation verb. The perfect system uses the PPP (*ausus*) + *esse*, just like a deponent.

## 5. Imperative Mood

### 5.1 Present Imperative

The present imperative is the most common form for direct commands.

| | Singular | Plural |
|--|----------|--------|
| **Formation** | Pure stem (2nd PP minus -re) | Stem + **-te** |
| 1st (amō) | **amā** | **amāte** |
| 2nd (moneō) | **monē** | **monēte** |
| 3rd (dūcō) | **dūce** (or **dūc**) | **dūcite** |
| 3rd-iō (capiō) | **cape** | **capite** |
| 4th (audiō) | **audī** | **audīte** |
| esse | **es** (be!) | **este** |

> **Irregular 3rd conjugation imperatives**: *dīc* (say!), *dūc* (lead!), *fac* (do!), *fer* (carry!) — these drop the final -e in the singular.

### 5.2 Negative Commands

Latin does **not** simply add *nōn* to the imperative. Instead:

| Construction | Example | Translation |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **Nōlī** (sg.) / **Nōlīte** (pl.) + infinitive | *Nōlī timēre.* | Don't be afraid. |
| | *Nōlīte abīre.* | Don't go away. |
| **Nē** + perfect subjunctive (literary) | *Nē timuerīs.* | Don't be afraid. |

### 5.3 Future Imperative

The future imperative is rare, found mainly in legal language, proverbs, and religious texts.

| | Singular | Plural |
|--|----------|--------|
| **2nd person** | stem + **-tō** | stem + **-tōte** |
| **3rd person** | stem + **-tō** | stem + **-ntō** |

| Form | Example | Translation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 2sg | *amātō* | You shall love (at a future time) |
| 3sg | *amātō* | He/she shall love |
| 2pl | *amātōte* | You (pl.) shall love |
| 3pl | *amantō* | They shall love |

Examples in legal/proverbial language:
- *Mementō morī.* — Remember that you will die. (*mementō* = future imperative of *meminī*)
- *Iūs dīcitō.* — He shall declare the law.

## 7. Master Reference Chart

### 7.1 Tense Sign / Mood Sign Quick Finder

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   INDICATIVE                                                     │
│   Present:     stem + personal endings                           │
│   Imperfect:   stem + -bā- (1st/2nd) / -ēbā- (3rd/4th)         │
│   Future:      stem + -bi- (1st/2nd) / -a-/-ē- (3rd/4th)        │
│   Perfect:     perf. stem + -ī/-istī/-it/-imus/-istis/-ērunt     │
│   Pluperfect:  perf. stem + -era- + endings                     │
│   Fut. Perf.:  perf. stem + -eri- + endings                     │
│                                                                  │
│   SUBJUNCTIVE                                                    │
│   Present:     vowel change (ē/eā/ā/iā) + endings               │
│   Imperfect:   infinitive + endings                              │
│   Perfect:     perf. stem + -erī- + endings                      │
│   Pluperfect:  perf. stem + -issē- + endings                    │
│                                                                  │
│   IMPERATIVE                                                     │
│   Present:     sg. = stem; pl. = stem + -te                      │
│   Future:      stem + -tō (sg.) / -tōte, -ntō (pl.)             │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 7.2 All Active Personal Endings

| Tense System | 1sg | 2sg | 3sg | 1pl | 2pl | 3pl |
|-------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Present system | -ō/-m | -s | -t | -mus | -tis | -nt |
| Perfect indicative | -ī | -istī | -it | -imus | -istis | -ērunt |
| Subjunctive | -m | -s | -t | -mus | -tis | -nt |

### 7.3 All Passive Personal Endings

| | 1sg | 2sg | 3sg | 1pl | 2pl | 3pl |
|--|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Present system | -or/-r | -ris | -tur | -mur | -minī | -ntur |
| Perfect system | PPP + sum | PPP + es | PPP + est | PPP + sumus | PPP + estis | PPP + sunt |

### 7.4 The Five Verb Properties

Every finite Latin verb form encodes five properties:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   1. PERSON:       1st, 2nd, 3rd                             │
│   2. NUMBER:       singular, plural                          │
│   3. TENSE:        present, imperfect, future,               │
│                    perfect, pluperfect, future perfect        │
│   4. VOICE:        active, passive                           │
│   5. MOOD:         indicative, subjunctive, imperative       │
│                                                              │
│   Total possible forms per verb:                             │
│   3 × 2 × 6 × 2 × 2 = 144 finite forms                     │
│   + imperative (4) + infinitives (6) + participles (3-4)     │
│   + gerund (4 cases) + gerundive + supine (2)                │
│   ≈ 165+ distinct forms per verb                             │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

1. Cum mīlitēs profectī essent, urbs ab hostibus capta est.

1. After the soldiers had set out, the enemy attacked. (Use *cum* + pluperfect subjunctive; *proficīscor*; *oppugnō*)

4. Lead the troops, general! (singular imperative; *dūcō*; *cōpiae, -ārum*; *imperātor*)
5. Those who had followed Caesar received rewards. (*sequor*; *praemium, -iī*)
