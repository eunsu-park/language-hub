## 1. Introduction to Principal Parts

Every Latin verb has **four principal parts** from which all tenses are formed. You must memorize them for every new verb.

> **Principal Parts Overview**
>
> | 1st PP | 2nd PP | 3rd PP | 4th PP |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | 1st sg. Present Active | Present Infinitive | 1st sg. Perfect Active | Perfect Passive Participle (PPP) |
> | amō "I love" | amāre "to love" | amāvī "I loved" | amātum "having been loved" |
>
> PRESENT STEM (amā-) from 2nd PP; PERFECT STEM (amāv-) from 3rd PP

### 1.1 How Stems Are Derived

| Principal Part | Stem | How to Find | Used For |
|----------------|------|-------------|----------|
| 1st (amō) | Present stem | Drop -ō | Present system active/passive |
| 2nd (amāre) | Present stem | Drop -re | Confirms conjugation class |
| 3rd (amāvī) | Perfect stem | Drop -ī | Perfect system **active** |
| 4th (amātum) | Participial stem | Drop -um | Perfect system **passive** |

### 1.2 Regular Principal Parts by Conjugation

| Conj. | 1st PP | 2nd PP | 3rd PP | 4th PP | Pattern |
|-------|--------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| 1st | amō | amāre | amāv**ī** | amāt**um** | -āvī, -ātum |
| 2nd | moneō | monēre | monu**ī** | monit**um** | -uī, -itum |
| 3rd | dūcō | dūcere | dūx**ī** | duct**um** | varies |
| 3rd-iō | capiō | capere | cēp**ī** | capt**um** | varies |
| 4th | audiō | audīre | audīv**ī** | audīt**um** | -īvī, -ītum |

> **Key insight**: The 1st and 4th conjugations form their perfects regularly (-āvī, -īvī). The 2nd and 3rd conjugations are far less predictable — you must memorize their principal parts.

## 2. Perfect Tense Active

The perfect tense is formed from the **3rd principal part** (perfect stem) plus personal endings unique to the perfect system.

### 2.1 Perfect Active Endings

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **-ī** | **-imus** |
| 2nd | **-istī** | **-istis** |
| 3rd | **-it** | **-ērunt** (-ēre) |

> Note: The 3rd person plural has an alternate form **-ēre** (e.g., *amāvēre*) found in poetry and formal prose.

### 2.2 Full Conjugation: amō, amāre, amāvī, amātum

| Person | Singular | Translation | Plural | Translation |
|--------|----------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| 1st | amāv**ī** | I loved / I have loved | amāv**imus** | we loved |
| 2nd | amāv**istī** | you loved | amāv**istis** | you (pl.) loved |
| 3rd | amāv**it** | he/she/it loved | amāv**ērunt** | they loved |

### 2.3 Perfect Tense Across All Conjugations

| Person | 1st: amō | 2nd: moneō | 3rd: dūcō | 4th: audiō |
|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 1sg | amāvī | monuī | dūxī | audīvī |
| 2sg | amāvistī | monuistī | dūxistī | audīvistī |
| 3sg | amāvit | monuit | dūxit | audīvit |
| 1pl | amāvimus | monuimus | dūximus | audīvimus |
| 2pl | amāvistis | monuistis | dūxistis | audīvistis |
| 3pl | amāvērunt | monuērunt | dūxērunt | audīvērunt |

## 3. Common Irregular Perfect Stems

Many high-frequency verbs form their perfects irregularly. These must be memorized.

### 3.1 Perfect Stem Formation Types

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **-v-/-u- suffix** | Most regular | amāvī, monuī, audīvī |
| **-s- suffix** | Sigmatic perfect | scrībō → scrīpsī, dīcō → dīxī |
| **Reduplication** | Doubled initial consonant | dō → dedī, currō → cucurrī |
| **Lengthened vowel** | Stem vowel lengthens | legō → lēgī, agō → ēgī |
| **Unchanged** | Stem = perfect | dēfendō → dēfendī, vertō → vertī |

### 3.2 Essential Irregular Perfects

| Present (1st PP) | Meaning | Perfect (3rd PP) | Perfect Stem | Type |
|-------------------|---------|-------------------|-------------|------|
| faciō | I make/do | fēcī | fēc- | lengthened vowel |
| dīcō | I say | dīxī | dīx- | sigmatic |
| dūcō | I lead | dūxī | dūx- | sigmatic |
| scrībō | I write | scrīpsī | scrīps- | sigmatic |
| vincō | I conquer | vīcī | vīc- | lengthened vowel |
| capiō | I take | cēpī | cēp- | lengthened vowel |
| mittō | I send | mīsī | mīs- | sigmatic |
| ferō | I carry | tulī | tul- | suppletive |
| veniō | I come | vēnī | vēn- | lengthened vowel |
| videō | I see | vīdī | vīd- | lengthened vowel |
| agō | I do/drive | ēgī | ēg- | lengthened vowel |
| legō | I read | lēgī | lēg- | lengthened vowel |
| pōnō | I place | posuī | posu- | -u- suffix |
| dō | I give | dedī | ded- | reduplication |
| stō | I stand | stetī | stet- | reduplication |
| currō | I run | cucurrī | cucurr- | reduplication |
| cadō | I fall | cecidī | cecid- | reduplication |
| pellō | I drive | pepulī | pepul- | reduplication |
| tangō | I touch | tetigī | tetig- | reduplication |

### 3.3 Mnemonic for Sigmatic Perfects

> **Consonant Changes Before -sī**
>
> - b + s → ps:  scrībō  → scrīp-sī
> - g + s → x:   dīcō    → dīx-ī  (= dīc-s-ī)
> - c + s → x:   dūcō    → dūx-ī  (= dūc-s-ī)
> - t + s → ss:  mittō   → mīs-ī  (= mitt-s-ī, simplified)

## 4. Pluperfect Tense Active

The pluperfect ("more than perfect") expresses an action completed **before** another past action. English: "I had loved."

### 4.1 Formation

**Perfect stem + era- + personal endings**

The pluperfect looks like the perfect stem + the imperfect of *esse* (eram, erās, erat...).

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | perf. stem + **-eram** | perf. stem + **-erāmus** |
| 2nd | perf. stem + **-erās** | perf. stem + **-erātis** |
| 3rd | perf. stem + **-erat** | perf. stem + **-erant** |

### 4.2 Full Conjugation: amō

| Person | Singular | Translation | Plural | Translation |
|--------|----------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| 1st | amāv**eram** | I had loved | amāv**erāmus** | we had loved |
| 2nd | amāv**erās** | you had loved | amāv**erātis** | you (pl.) had loved |
| 3rd | amāv**erat** | he/she had loved | amāv**erant** | they had loved |

### 4.3 Examples with Irregular Stems

- *Caesar cōpiās dūx**erat** ante proelium.* — Caesar had led the troops before the battle.
- *Mīlitēs urbem cēp**erant** priusquam nūntius vēnit.* — The soldiers had taken the city before the messenger came.
- *Litterās scrīps**erāmus** sed nōn mīs**erāmus**.* — We had written the letters but had not sent them.

## 5. Future Perfect Tense Active

The future perfect expresses an action that **will have been completed** before another future action.

### 5.1 Formation

**Perfect stem + eri- + personal endings**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | perf. stem + **-erō** | perf. stem + **-erimus** |
| 2nd | perf. stem + **-eris** | perf. stem + **-eritis** |
| 3rd | perf. stem + **-erit** | perf. stem + **-erint** |

> Note: The future perfect looks like the future of *esse* (erō, eris, erit...) except for the 3rd plural: **-erint** (not -erunt, which is perfect tense).

### 5.2 Full Conjugation: amō

| Person | Singular | Translation | Plural | Translation |
|--------|----------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| 1st | amāv**erō** | I will have loved | amāv**erimus** | we will have loved |
| 2nd | amāv**eris** | you will have loved | amāv**eritis** | you (pl.) will have loved |
| 3rd | amāv**erit** | he/she will have loved | amāv**erint** | they will have loved |

### 5.3 Distinguishing Perfect, Pluperfect, and Future Perfect

| Tense | 3sg ending | Example | Translation |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Perfect | -it | amāv**it** | he loved / has loved |
| Pluperfect | -erat | amāv**erat** | he had loved |
| Future Perfect | -erit | amāv**erit** | he will have loved |

> **Caution**: The 3sg perfect (*amāvit*) and 3sg future perfect (*amāverit*) look similar. Context and vowel length distinguish them: *amāvit* (short -i-) vs. *amāverit* (has -er- infix).

## 6. Uses of the Perfect System Tenses

### 6.1 Perfect Tense — Two Meanings

The Latin perfect conflates two English tenses:

| Use | Latin | English |
|-----|-------|---------|
| **Simple past** (aoristic) | *Vēnī, vīdī, vīcī.* | I came, I saw, I conquered. |
| **Present perfect** (resultative) | *Patriam amāvī.* | I have loved my country. |

### 6.2 Pluperfect — Past Before Past

- *Postquam Caesar pervēnerat, proelium commīsit.*
- After Caesar had arrived, he joined battle.

### 6.3 Future Perfect — Future Before Future

Common in temporal and conditional clauses:

- *Sī hoc fēceris, laetus erō.* — If you will have done (= do) this, I will be happy.
- *Ubi pervēnerō, litterās mittam.* — When I have arrived, I will send a letter.

> **Note**: English often uses a simple present or simple future where Latin uses the future perfect. This is one of the most common translation adjustments.

### 6.4 Summary of Temporal Relations

> **Tense Timeline**
>
> Past ←──────────────────────────── → Future
>
> Pluperfect → Perfect / Imperfect → Future Perfect → Future
> (had done)    (did / was doing)     (will have)      (will)
>
> amāveram → amāvī / amābam → amāverō → amābō

## 7. Reading Passage: Caesar's Gallic War

The following is adapted from Caesar, *Bellum Gallicum* I.1:

> **Gallia est omnis dīvīsa in partēs trēs, quārum ūnam incolunt Belgae, aliam Aquītānī, tertiam quī ipsōrum linguā Celtae, nostrā Gallī appellantur.**

### 7.1 Vocabulary

| Latin | Form | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| Gallia, -ae (f.) | nom. sg. | Gaul |
| omnis, -e | nom. sg. f. | all, as a whole |
| dīvīsa | PPP of dīvidō | having been divided |
| pars, partis (f.) | acc. pl. | part |
| trēs | acc. pl. | three |
| incolō, -ere, -uī | 3rd PP: incoluī | inhabit |
| appellō (1) | passive: appellantur | call, name |

### 7.2 Translation

"Gaul as a whole is divided into three parts, of which the Belgae inhabit one, the Aquitani another, and those who in their own language are called Celts, in ours Gauls, [inhabit] the third."

### 7.3 Grammar Notes

- *dīvīsa est*: perfect passive (PPP + est) — covered in Lesson 12
- *incolunt*: present tense — "they inhabit" (the Belgae still live there as Caesar writes)
- *appellantur*: present passive — "they are called"
- *quārum*: genitive plural of the relative pronoun *quī, quae, quod*
