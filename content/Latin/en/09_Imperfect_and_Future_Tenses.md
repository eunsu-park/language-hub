# 9. Imperfect and Future Tenses (Imperfectum et Futurum)

## Learning Objectives

- Form the imperfect tense for all four conjugations and irregular verbs
- Understand the three main uses of the imperfect (ongoing, habitual, attempted action)
- Form the future tense for 1st/2nd conjugations (-bō system) and 3rd/4th conjugations (-am system)
- Conjugate **esse** in the future tense
- Recognize and translate temporal clauses with dum, cum, postquam, and antequam
- Read and translate narrative passages using imperfect and future tenses
- Distinguish when to use imperfect vs. perfect tense in translation

## 1. The Imperfect Tense: Formation

The imperfect tense describes **ongoing, repeated, or habitual** actions in the past. It is formed by adding the tense sign **-bā-** to the present stem, followed by personal endings.

### Formation Formula

```
IMPERFECT TENSE FORMULA
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Present Stem + -bā- + Personal Endings  │
│                                          │
│ Personal Endings (active):               │
│   -m, -s, -t, -mus, -tis, -nt           │
│                                          │
│ Combined with -bā-:                      │
│   -bam, -bās, -bat, -bāmus, -bātis,     │
│   -bant                                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 1st Conjugation: amō, amāre (to love) — stem: amā-

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | amā-**bam** | amā-**bāmus** |
| 2nd | amā-**bās** | amā-**bātis** |
| 3rd | amā-**bat** | amā-**bant** |

### 2nd Conjugation: moneō, monēre (to warn) — stem: monē-

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | monē-**bam** | monē-**bāmus** |
| 2nd | monē-**bās** | monē-**bātis** |
| 3rd | monē-**bat** | monē-**bant** |

### 3rd Conjugation: dūcō, dūcere (to lead) — stem: dūc- (+ connecting vowel -ē-)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | dūcē-**bam** | dūcē-**bāmus** |
| 2nd | dūcē-**bās** | dūcē-**bātis** |
| 3rd | dūcē-**bat** | dūcē-**bant** |

> **Note**: Third conjugation inserts **-ē-** before -bam (dūc-ē-bam), not the short -e- of the present tense.

### 3rd Conjugation -iō: capiō, capere (to take) — stem: capi- (+ connecting vowel -ē-)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | capiē-**bam** | capiē-**bāmus** |
| 2nd | capiē-**bās** | capiē-**bātis** |
| 3rd | capiē-**bat** | capiē-**bant** |

### 4th Conjugation: audiō, audīre (to hear) — stem: audī- (+ connecting vowel -ē-)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | audiē-**bam** | audiē-**bāmus** |
| 2nd | audiē-**bās** | audiē-**bātis** |
| 3rd | audiē-**bat** | audiē-**bant** |

### Imperfect of esse (to be)

The imperfect of **sum** is completely regular, built on the stem **er-**:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **eram** | **erāmus** |
| 2nd | **erās** | **erātis** |
| 3rd | **erat** | **erant** |

### Summary: Recognizing the Imperfect

```
IMPERFECT RECOGNITION
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Look for -BA- in the verb:           │
│                                      │
│ amā-BA-m    = I was loving           │
│ monē-BA-t   = he/she was warning     │
│ dūcē-BA-mus = we were leading        │
│ audiē-BA-nt  = they were hearing     │
│                                      │
│ ESSE: er- + personal endings         │
│ eram, erās, erat, erāmus...          │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 2. Uses of the Imperfect

The imperfect tense has three main uses in Latin. Understanding these helps you choose the right English translation.

### 2.1 Ongoing / Continuous Past Action

Describes an action **in progress** at a point in the past.

- **Rēx in aulā sedēbat.** — *The king was sitting in the hall.*
- **Mīlitēs per silvam ambulābant.** — *The soldiers were walking through the forest.*
- **Puerī in viā lūdēbant.** — *The boys were playing in the street.*

### 2.2 Habitual / Repeated Past Action

Describes what **used to happen** regularly.

- **Cotīdiē ad forum ambulābam.** — *I used to walk to the forum every day.*
- **Magister puerōs docēbat.** — *The teacher used to teach the boys.*
- **Rōmānī deōs suōs colēbant.** — *The Romans used to worship their gods.*

### 2.3 Attempted / Conative Action

Describes an action that was **tried but not completed** (less common).

- **Hostēs mūrōs superābant.** — *The enemies were trying to climb the walls.*
- **Eum persuādēbam.** — *I was trying to persuade him.*

### Imperfect vs. Perfect: A Comparison

| Imperfect | Perfect | Difference |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Scrībēbam epistulam. | Scrīpsī epistulam. | I was writing... / I wrote... |
| In urbe habitābat. | In urbem vēnit. | He was living... / He came... |
| Bellum gerēbant. | Bellum gessērunt. | They were waging... / They waged... |

> **한국어 화자 참고**: The imperfect is like -고 있었다 (ongoing) or -(으)곤 했다 (habitual), while the perfect is like the simple past -았/었다. For example: 쓰고 있었다 (imperfect: was writing) vs. 썼다 (perfect: wrote).

## 3. The Future Tense: Formation

Latin has **two different future tense formations**, depending on the conjugation.

### 3.1 First and Second Conjugations: -bō System

The future of 1st and 2nd conjugation verbs uses **-bō, -bis, -bit, -bimus, -bitis, -bunt**.

**amō, amāre** (1st conj.) — *to love*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | amā-**bō** | amā-**bimus** |
| 2nd | amā-**bis** | amā-**bitis** |
| 3rd | amā-**bit** | amā-**bunt** |

**moneō, monēre** (2nd conj.) — *to warn*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | monē-**bō** | monē-**bimus** |
| 2nd | monē-**bis** | monē-**bitis** |
| 3rd | monē-**bit** | monē-**bunt** |

> **Tip**: The 1st/2nd conjugation future looks similar to the imperfect (both use -b-), but the vowels differ: future -bi- (except 1st sg. -bō and 3rd pl. -bunt) vs. imperfect -bā-.

### 3.2 Third and Fourth Conjugations: -am System

The future of 3rd and 4th conjugation verbs uses **-am, -ēs, -et, -ēmus, -ētis, -ent**.

**dūcō, dūcere** (3rd conj.) — *to lead*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | dūc-**am** | dūc-**ēmus** |
| 2nd | dūc-**ēs** | dūc-**ētis** |
| 3rd | dūc-**et** | dūc-**ent** |

**capiō, capere** (3rd -iō conj.) — *to take*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | capi-**am** | capi-**ēmus** |
| 2nd | capi-**ēs** | capi-**ētis** |
| 3rd | capi-**et** | capi-**ent** |

**audiō, audīre** (4th conj.) — *to hear*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | audi-**am** | audi-**ēmus** |
| 2nd | audi-**ēs** | audi-**ētis** |
| 3rd | audi-**et** | audi-**ent** |

### Comparison: Two Future Systems

```
FUTURE TENSE SYSTEMS
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1st/2nd CONJUGATION        3rd/4th CONJUGATION│
│ (the -bō system)          (the -am system)   │
│                                              │
│ -bō    -bimus             -am    -ēmus       │
│ -bis   -bitis             -ēs    -ētis       │
│ -bit   -bunt              -et    -ent         │
│                                              │
│ amābō = I will love       dūcam = I will lead│
│ monēbit = he will warn    audiet = he'll hear│
│                                              │
│ ⚠ 3rd/4th future 1st sg. -am looks like      │
│   present subjunctive — context resolves it!  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 4. Future of Esse

The future of **sum, esse** is irregular but essential:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | **erō** | **erimus** |
| 2nd | **eris** | **eritis** |
| 3rd | **erit** | **erunt** |

### Example Sentences with Future of Esse

- **Crās in urbe erō.** — *Tomorrow I will be in the city.*
- **Discipulī bonī eritis.** — *You will be good students.*
- **Hoc bellum longum erit.** — *This war will be long.*
- **Nōn semper miserī erimus.** — *We will not always be miserable.*

### Compound Verbs of Esse

The future pattern applies to compounds of esse as well:

| Verb | Meaning | Future 3rd sg. |
|------|---------|----------------|
| adsum | I am present | **aderit** |
| absum | I am absent | **aberit** |
| possum | I am able | **poterit** |
| prōsum | I am useful | **prōderit** |

> **possum** future: **poterō, poteris, poterit, poterimus, poteritis, poterunt**

## 5. Temporal Clauses

Temporal (time) clauses tell **when** something happens. They use specific conjunctions and follow particular tense rules.

### 5.1 dum — *while, as long as*

With the **present indicative** (even when the main verb is past):

- **Dum haec geruntur, Caesar advēnit.** — *While these things were happening, Caesar arrived.*
- **Dum vīvimus, spērāmus.** — *While/as long as we live, we hope.*

> **Key rule**: **dum** meaning "while" takes the **present tense** in Latin, even in past narrative. This is called the "historical present with dum."

### 5.2 cum — *when, since, although*

With the **imperfect** or **pluperfect** indicative/subjunctive:

- **Cum Rōmae essem, multa vīdī.** — *When I was in Rome, I saw many things.* (circumstantial)
- **Cum haec dīxisset, discessit.** — *When he had said these things, he departed.* (temporal)

> **Note**: **cum** with the indicative is purely temporal ("when"); with the subjunctive it can also mean "since" or "although" — this will be covered in later lessons.

### 5.3 postquam — *after*

Usually with the **perfect indicative**:

- **Postquam rēx discessit, cīvēs laetī erant.** — *After the king departed, the citizens were happy.*
- **Postquam urbem cēpit, mīlitēs laudāvit.** — *After he captured the city, he praised the soldiers.*

### 5.4 antequam / priusquam — *before*

With the **indicative** (factual) or **subjunctive** (anticipated):

- **Antequam sōl oritur, surgō.** — *Before the sun rises, I get up.* (factual/habitual)
- **Antequam hostēs venīrent, mūrōs mūnīvit.** — *Before the enemies could come, he fortified the walls.* (anticipatory)

### Temporal Clause Summary

```
TEMPORAL CONJUNCTIONS
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ dum + present     = while (even in past) │
│ cum + impf./plpf. = when/since/although  │
│ postquam + perfect = after               │
│ antequam + indic.  = before (factual)    │
│ antequam + subj.   = before (anticipated)│
│                                          │
│ ubi + perfect      = when (single event) │
│ simul atque + perf. = as soon as         │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 6. Reading Narrative Passages

### Passage 1: Imperfect Narrative — Daily Life in Rome

> Ōlim Rōmae multī cīvēs **habitābant**. Agricolae agrōs **colēbant** et frūmentum **parābant**. Mercātōrēs per forum **ambulābant** et mercem **vēndēbant**. Puerī in lūdō **discēbant**; magister eōs **docēbat**. Fēminae cibum **parābant** et līberōs **cūrābant**. Servī dominīs **serviēbant**. Haec vīta cotīdiāna **erat**.

Translation:

> Once upon a time many citizens **used to live** in Rome. Farmers **used to cultivate** fields and **would prepare** grain. Merchants **used to walk** through the forum and **would sell** goods. Boys **used to learn** in school; a teacher **would teach** them. Women **used to prepare** food and **would care for** children. Slaves **used to serve** their masters. This **was** daily life.

### Passage 2: Future Narrative — A General's Promise

> Crās exercitum **dūcam** et hostēs **superābimus**. Mīlitēs fortēs **erunt** et magnā cum virtūte **pugnābunt**. Post victōriam ad urbem **redībimus**. Senātus nōs **laudābit** et populus nōbīs grātiās **aget**. Haec bellī glōria semper in memoriā nostrā **erit**.

Translation:

> Tomorrow I **will lead** the army and we **will overcome** the enemies. The soldiers **will be** brave and **will fight** with great courage. After the victory we **will return** to the city. The senate **will praise** us and the people **will give** us thanks. This glory of war **will** always **be** in our memory.

### Passage 3: Mixed Tenses

> Dum mīlitēs **pugnant**, imperātor in colle **stābat** et aciem **spectābat**. Postquam hostēs **fūgērunt**, imperātor "Vīcimus!" **clāmāvit**. "Crās," inquit, "domum **redībimus** et pācem **habēbimus**."

Translation:

> While the soldiers **were fighting**, the commander **was standing** on a hill and **was watching** the battle line. After the enemies **fled**, the commander **shouted**, "We have won!" "Tomorrow," he said, "we **will return** home and **will have** peace."
