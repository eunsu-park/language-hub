# 17. Indirect Discourse (Ōrātiō Oblīqua)

## Learning Objectives
- Construct indirect statements using the **accusative + infinitive** pattern
- Select the correct tense of the infinitive (present, perfect, future) to convey time relative to the main verb
- Identify verbs of saying, thinking, knowing, and perceiving that introduce indirect statement
- Form indirect questions using the **subjunctive** with interrogative words
- Express indirect commands with **ut/ne** + subjunctive after verbs of commanding and persuading
- Apply reflexive pronouns (**sē, suus**) correctly within indirect discourse
- Translate freely between direct and indirect speech in Latin

## 1. Indirect Statement

### 1.1 The Accusative + Infinitive Construction

In English, indirect statements use "that" clauses:
- Direct: "The soldiers **are brave**."
- Indirect: He says **that the soldiers are brave**.

In Latin, indirect statements use an **accusative subject** + **infinitive verb** (no conjunction like "that"):
- Direct: Mīlitēs **fortēs sunt**.
- Indirect: Dīcit mīlitēs **fortēs esse**.

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **Dīcit** | Main verb (verb of saying/thinking) |
| **mīlitēs** | Subject of indirect statement (accusative) |
| **fortēs** | Predicate adjective (accusative, agrees with subject) |
| **esse** | Infinitive (main verb of indirect statement) |

### 1.2 Tense of the Infinitive

The infinitive's tense indicates time **relative to the main verb**, not absolute time:

| Infinitive Tense | Time Relative to Main Verb | Form (active, 1st conj.) |
|-------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Present** | Same time | amāre |
| **Perfect** | Before | amāvisse |
| **Future** | After | amātūrum (-am, -um) esse |

| Infinitive Tense | Form (passive, 1st conj.) |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Present passive** | amārī |
| **Perfect passive** | amātum (-am, -um) esse |
| **Future passive** | amātum īrī |

### 1.3 Tense Examples

**Main verb in primary tense (dīcit — "he says"):**

| Latin | Translation | Time |
|-------|------------|------|
| Dīcit mīlitēs **pugnāre**. | He says the soldiers **are fighting**. | Same time |
| Dīcit mīlitēs **pugnāvisse**. | He says the soldiers **fought / have fought**. | Before |
| Dīcit mīlitēs **pugnātūrōs esse**. | He says the soldiers **will fight**. | After |

**Main verb in secondary tense (dīxit — "he said"):**

| Latin | Translation | Time |
|-------|------------|------|
| Dīxit mīlitēs **pugnāre**. | He said the soldiers **were fighting**. | Same time |
| Dīxit mīlitēs **pugnāvisse**. | He said the soldiers **had fought**. | Before |
| Dīxit mīlitēs **pugnātūrōs esse**. | He said the soldiers **would fight**. | After |

> **Key insight:** The Latin infinitive tense does not change based on the main verb tense. Only the English translation shifts.

### 1.4 Negation in Indirect Statement

The infinitive clause is negated by **nōn** (placed before the infinitive or accusative subject):

| Latin | English |
|-------|---------|
| Dīcit mīlitēs **nōn** pugnāre. | He says the soldiers **are not** fighting. |
| Putō hoc **nōn** vērum esse. | I think this **is not** true. |

## 2. Verbs that Introduce Indirect Statement

### 2.1 Verbs of Saying

| Latin | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **dīcō, dīcere, dīxī, dictum** | say, tell | Dīcit sē venīre. |
| **negō, negāre** | say...not, deny | Negat sē venīre. (= Dīcit sē nōn venīre.) |
| **nārrō, nārrāre** | narrate, tell | Nārrat rem gestam esse. |
| **referō, referre** | report | Refert urbem captam esse. |
| **respondeō, respondēre** | reply | Respondet sē parātum esse. |
| **trādō, trādere** | hand down, relate | Trādit Rōmulum urbem condidisse. |

### 2.2 Verbs of Thinking and Believing

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **putō, putāre** | think, consider |
| **crēdō, crēdere** | believe |
| **exīstimō, exīstimāre** | judge, consider |
| **arbitror, arbitrārī** | think, suppose |
| **cēnseō, cēnsēre** | think, be of the opinion |
| **spērō, spērāre** | hope (takes future infinitive) |

### 2.3 Verbs of Knowing and Perceiving

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **sciō, scīre** | know |
| **cognōscō, cognōscere** | learn, recognize |
| **sentiō, sentīre** | feel, perceive |
| **videō, vidēre** | see |
| **audiō, audīre** | hear |
| **intellegō, intellegere** | understand |
| **meminī** (defective) | remember |

### 2.4 Special Notes

- **spērō** ("hope") and **prōmittō** ("promise") typically take the **future** infinitive.
- **negō** means "say...not" and is preferred to *dīcō...nōn*:
  - **Negat sē venīre.** = He says he is not coming. (more natural than *Dīcit sē nōn venīre.*)
- **iubeō** ("order") takes acc. + infinitive but is classified as a command, not indirect statement.

## 3. Indirect Questions

### 3.1 Formation

Indirect questions use the **subjunctive** mood. The tense follows the **sequence of tenses** (see Lesson 16).

**Direct question:** Quid facis? — "What are you doing?"
**Indirect question:** Rogat **quid faciās**. — "He asks **what you are doing**."

### 3.2 Introductory Words

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **quis, quid** | who, what | Rogat **quis** vēnerit. |
| **quī, quae, quod** | which, what | Rogat **quī** librī sint bonī. |
| **ubi** | where | Nescit **ubi** sit. |
| **unde** | from where | Rogat **unde** veniat. |
| **quō** | to where | Quaerit **quō** eat. |
| **quandō** | when | Rogat **quandō** ventūrus sit. |
| **cūr** | why | Nescit **cūr** abierit. |
| **quōmodo** | how | Rogat **quōmodo** id factum sit. |
| **num** | whether (expecting "no") | Rogat **num** vērum sit. |
| **nōnne** | whether (expecting "yes") | Rogat **nōnne** hoc vērum sit. |
| **-ne** | whether (neutral) | Rogat veniat**ne**. |
| **utrum...an** | whether...or | Rogat **utrum** maneat **an** abeat. |

### 3.3 Sequence of Tenses in Indirect Questions

| Main Verb | Subordinate Time | Subjunctive | Example |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| Primary | Same time | Present subj. | Rogat quid **faciās**. |
| Primary | Prior | Perfect subj. | Rogat quid **fēceris**. |
| Secondary | Same time | Imperfect subj. | Rogāvit quid **facerēs**. |
| Secondary | Prior | Pluperfect subj. | Rogāvit quid **fēcissēs**. |

### 3.4 Verbs Introducing Indirect Questions

Common verbs: **rogō** (ask), **quaerō** (ask, seek), **nesciō** (not know), **sciō** (know), **dīcō** (tell), **intellegō** (understand), **videō** (see to it), **dubitō** (doubt, hesitate).

## 4. Indirect Commands

### 4.1 Formation

Indirect commands use **ut** (positive) or **ne** (negative) + **subjunctive**:

**Direct command:** Venī! — "Come!"
**Indirect command:** Imperat **ut veniat**. — "He orders **that he come** / **him to come**."

| Latin | English |
|-------|---------|
| Imperāvit **ut mīlitēs prōgrederentur**. | He ordered **that the soldiers advance**. |
| Monuit **nē hoc faceret**. | He warned **him not to do this**. |
| Ōrāvit **ut sibī ignōsceret**. | He begged **him to forgive him**. |
| Persuāsit eī **ut manēret**. | He persuaded him **to stay**. |

### 4.2 Verbs of Commanding and Persuading

| Latin | Meaning | Construction |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **imperō** (+ dat.) | command | ut/ne + subj. |
| **moneō** | warn, advise | ut/ne + subj. |
| **hortor** | encourage, urge | ut/ne + subj. |
| **ōrō / rogō** | beg, ask | ut/ne + subj. |
| **persuādeō** (+ dat.) | persuade | ut/ne + subj. |
| **petō** | seek, ask | ut/ne + subj. |
| **cōgō** | compel | (usually acc. + inf.) |
| **iubeō** | order | (acc. + inf., NOT ut) |
| **vetō** | forbid | (acc. + inf., NOT ne) |

> **Important:** **iubeō** and **vetō** do NOT use *ut/ne* — they take the accusative + infinitive construction instead:
> - **Iubet eum venīre.** — He orders him to come.
> - **Vetat eum venīre.** — He forbids him to come.

## 5. Reflexive Pronouns in Indirect Discourse

### 5.1 The Rule

Within indirect discourse, the reflexive pronoun **sē** (and possessive **suus, -a, -um**) refers to the **subject of the main verb** (the original speaker/thinker):

| Latin | English |
|-------|---------|
| Caesar dīxit **sē** ventūrum esse. | Caesar said **he (himself)** would come. |
| Caesar dīxit **eum** ventūrum esse. | Caesar said **he (someone else)** would come. |

### 5.2 Direct vs Indirect Comparison

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|---------------|----------------|
| "Veniam." (I will come.) | Dīxit **sē** ventūrum esse. (He said he [himself] would come.) |
| "Meum librum legō." (I am reading my book.) | Dīxit **sē suum** librum legere. (He said he was reading his [own] book.) |
| "Tū mē adiuvās." (You are helping me.) | Dīxit eum **sē** adiuvāre. (He said that he was helping him[self].) |

### 5.3 Clarification Table

| Pronoun | In Indirect Discourse | Refers to... |
|---------|----------------------|-------------|
| **sē** (acc./abl.) | Reflexive | Subject of main verb |
| **sibi** (dat.) | Reflexive | Subject of main verb |
| **suus, -a, -um** | Reflexive possessive | Subject of main verb |
| **eum / eam / id** | Non-reflexive | Someone other than the main subject |
| **eius** | Non-reflexive possessive | Someone other than the main subject |

## 7. Practice Exercises

### Exercise A: Identify the Construction

State whether each sentence contains an **indirect statement**, **indirect question**, or **indirect command**:

1. Putō eum bonum virum esse.
2. Rogāvit ubi Caesar esset.
3. Imperāvit ut portās clauderent.
4. Audiō tē Rōmam profectūrum esse.
5. Nescit num amīcī ventūrī sint.
6. Hortātus est nē timērent.
7. Crēdimus exercitum victum esse.
8. Dīcit sē hoc numquam factūrum esse.

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

1. **Indirect statement** — putō + acc. (eum) + inf. (esse). "I think he is a good man."
2. **Indirect question** — rogāvit + ubi + subjunctive (esset). "He asked where Caesar was."
3. **Indirect command** — imperāvit + ut + subjunctive (clauderent). "He ordered them to close the gates."
4. **Indirect statement** — audiō + acc. (tē) + inf. (profectūrum esse). "I hear you are going to set out for Rome."
5. **Indirect question** — nescit + num + subjunctive (sint). "He does not know whether friends will come."
6. **Indirect command** — hortātus est + nē + subjunctive (timērent). "He urged them not to be afraid."
7. **Indirect statement** — crēdimus + acc. (exercitum) + inf. (victum esse). "We believe the army was defeated."
8. **Indirect statement** — dīcit + acc. (sē) + inf. (factūrum esse). "He says he will never do this."

</details>

### Exercise B: Choose the Correct Infinitive

Select the correct infinitive tense (present, perfect, or future) for the blank:

1. Dīcit mīlitēs fortiter ________. (pugn- : same time)
2. Scīmus Rōmam ā Rōmulō ________. (cond- : before)
3. Spērat exercitum ________. (vinc- : after)
4. Audīvī tē aegrōtum ________. (sum : same time as audīvī)
5. Putāmus eōs iam ________. (proficīsc- : before)

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

1. **pugnāre** — present infinitive (same time as main verb). "He says the soldiers are fighting bravely."
2. **conditam esse** — perfect passive infinitive (prior). "We know Rome was founded by Romulus."
3. **victūrum esse** — future active infinitive (after). "He hopes the army will conquer."
4. **esse** — present infinitive (same time as secondary main verb). "I heard that you were sick."
5. **profectōs esse** — perfect (deponent) infinitive (prior). "We think they have already set out."

</details>

### Exercise C: Translate into Latin

1. He says that he is coming.
2. We believed that the city had been captured.
3. She asked why the boy was crying.
4. The consul ordered the soldiers to defend the walls.
5. He promised that he would return.

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

1. **Dīcit sē venīre.** (Indirect statement; sē = reflexive referring to subject; present infinitive = same time)
2. **Crēdēbāmus urbem captam esse.** (Indirect statement; perfect passive infinitive = prior to main verb)
3. **Rogāvit cūr puer flēret.** (Indirect question; secondary sequence → imperfect subjunctive)
4. **Cōnsul imperāvit ut mīlitēs mūrōs dēfenderent.** (Indirect command; ut + imperfect subjunctive)
5. **Prōmīsit sē reditūrum esse.** (Indirect statement; future active infinitive = after main verb)

</details>

### Exercise D: Reflexive or Non-Reflexive?

Choose **sē/suus** or **eum/eius** for the blank, and explain:

1. Marcus dīxit ________ venīre. (Marcus himself is coming)
2. Marcus dīxit ________ venīre. (Lucius is coming)
3. Fēmina crēdit ________ librum bonum esse. (her own book)
4. Fēmina crēdit ________ librum bonum esse. (that other woman's book)

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

1. **sē** — reflexive; refers to Marcus, the subject of the main verb. "Marcus said he (himself) was coming."
2. **eum** — non-reflexive; refers to someone other than Marcus. "Marcus said he (Lucius) was coming."
3. **suum** — reflexive possessive; refers to the woman (main subject). "The woman believes her (own) book is good."
4. **eius** — non-reflexive possessive; refers to a different woman. "The woman believes her (that other woman's) book is good."

</details>

## Quick Reference Card

| Type | Construction | Tense/Mood | Negation |
|------|-------------|------------|----------|
| **Indirect Statement** | acc. + infinitive | Inf. tense = relative time | nōn (or negō) |
| **Indirect Question** | interrogative word + subjunctive | Follows sequence of tenses | nōn / num...nōn |
| **Indirect Command** | ut/nē + subjunctive | Follows sequence of tenses | nē |

**Infinitive Tenses:**

| Infinitive | Active | Passive | Relative Time |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------------|
| Present | -āre, -ēre, -ere, -īre | -ārī, -ērī, -ī, -īrī | Same time |
| Perfect | -āvisse, -uisse, -isse | PPP + esse | Before |
| Future | -ūrum esse | -um īrī | After |
