# 12. Passive Voice (Vox Passiva)

## Learning Objectives
- Conjugate verbs in the present passive indicative across all four conjugations
- Form the imperfect and future passive indicative
- Construct the perfect passive system using the PPP + forms of *esse*
- Express the agent with *ā/ab* + ablative
- Understand the stylistic and grammatical uses of the passive voice
- Recognize and use common deponent verbs
- Translate passive constructions accurately into natural English

## 1. Present Passive Indicative

### 1.1 Passive Personal Endings

The passive voice uses a distinct set of personal endings in the present system:

| Person | Active Ending | Passive Ending |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| 1st sg. | -ō / -m | **-or** / **-r** |
| 2nd sg. | -s | **-ris** (-re) |
| 3rd sg. | -t | **-tur** |
| 1st pl. | -mus | **-mur** |
| 2nd pl. | -tis | **-minī** |
| 3rd pl. | -nt | **-ntur** |

> Note: The 2nd sg. ending **-ris** has an alternate form **-re** (e.g., *amāris* or *amāre*). Be careful not to confuse the passive *amāre* (you are loved) with the infinitive *amāre* (to love) — context distinguishes them.

### 1.2 Present Passive Across All Conjugations

| Person | 1st: amor | 2nd: moneor | 3rd: dūcor | 3rd-iō: capior | 4th: audior |
|--------|-----------|------------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| 1sg | amor | moneor | dūcor | capior | audior |
| 2sg | amāris | monēris | dūceris | caperis | audīris |
| 3sg | amātur | monētur | dūcitur | capitur | audītur |
| 1pl | amāmur | monēmur | dūcimur | capimur | audīmur |
| 2pl | amāminī | monēminī | dūciminī | capiminī | audīminī |
| 3pl | amantur | monentur | dūcuntur | capiuntur | audiuntur |

### 1.3 Example Sentences

| Latin | Translation |
|-------|-------------|
| *Liber ā discipulō legitur.* | The book is read by the student. |
| *Mīlitēs ab imperātōre dūcuntur.* | The soldiers are led by the commander. |
| *Urbs mūrīs dēfenditur.* | The city is defended by walls. |

## 2. Imperfect Passive Indicative

### 2.1 Formation

Same as imperfect active, but with **passive endings** instead of active.

**Present stem + -bā- (1st/2nd) or -ēbā- (3rd/4th) + passive endings**

| Person | 1st: amor | 2nd: moneor | 3rd: dūcor | 4th: audior |
|--------|-----------|------------|-----------|------------|
| 1sg | amābar | monēbar | dūcēbar | audiēbar |
| 2sg | amābāris | monēbāris | dūcēbāris | audiēbāris |
| 3sg | amābātur | monēbātur | dūcēbātur | audiēbātur |
| 1pl | amābāmur | monēbāmur | dūcēbāmur | audiēbāmur |
| 2pl | amābāminī | monēbāminī | dūcēbāminī | audiēbāminī |
| 3pl | amābantur | monēbantur | dūcēbantur | audiēbantur |

### 2.2 Examples

- *Fābulae ā mātre narrābantur.* — Stories were being told by the mother.
- *Oppidum ā Rōmānīs oppugnābātur.* — The town was being attacked by the Romans.

## 3. Future Passive Indicative

### 3.1 Formation

Same tense markers as future active, with passive endings.

| Person | 1st: amor | 2nd: moneor | 3rd: dūcor | 4th: audior |
|--------|-----------|------------|-----------|------------|
| 1sg | amābor | monēbor | dūcar | audiar |
| 2sg | amāberis | monēberis | dūcēris | audiēris |
| 3sg | amābitur | monēbitur | dūcētur | audiētur |
| 1pl | amābimur | monēbimur | dūcēmur | audiēmur |
| 2pl | amābiminī | monēbiminī | dūcēminī | audiēminī |
| 3pl | amābuntur | monēbuntur | dūcentur | audientur |

> **Recall**: 1st/2nd conjugation future uses -bi-/-bō-; 3rd/4th uses -a-/-ē-.

### 3.2 Examples

- *Epistula crās mittētur.* — The letter will be sent tomorrow.
- *Ab omnibus laudāberis.* — You will be praised by everyone.

## 4. Perfect Passive System

The perfect passive system works **completely differently** from the present passive system. Instead of personal endings, it uses a **compound construction**.

### 4.1 Formation

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   PERFECT PASSIVE = PPP + present tense of ESSE               │
│   PLUPERFECT PASSIVE = PPP + imperfect tense of ESSE          │
│   FUTURE PERFECT PASSIVE = PPP + future tense of ESSE         │
│                                                                │
│   The PPP is the 4th principal part and behaves as a           │
│   1st/2nd declension adjective: it AGREES with the subject    │
│   in gender, number, and case.                                 │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 4.2 Perfect Passive Indicative: amō (PPP: amātus, -a, -um)

| Person | Masculine sg. | Feminine sg. | Neuter sg. |
|--------|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| 1st | amātus sum | amāta sum | — |
| 2nd | amātus es | amāta es | — |
| 3rd | amātus est | amāta est | amātum est |

| Person | Masculine pl. | Feminine pl. | Neuter pl. |
|--------|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| 1st | amātī sumus | amātae sumus | — |
| 2nd | amātī estis | amātae estis | — |
| 3rd | amātī sunt | amātae sunt | amāta sunt |

### 4.3 Pluperfect Passive Indicative

Replace *sum/es/est* with *eram/erās/erat*:

| Person | Singular (m.) | Plural (m.) |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| 1st | amātus eram | amātī erāmus |
| 2nd | amātus erās | amātī erātis |
| 3rd | amātus erat | amātī erant |

### 4.4 Future Perfect Passive Indicative

Replace with *erō/eris/erit*:

| Person | Singular (m.) | Plural (m.) |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| 1st | amātus erō | amātī erimus |
| 2nd | amātus eris | amātī eritis |
| 3rd | amātus erit | amātī erunt |

### 4.5 Common PPPs

| Verb | PPP | Meaning |
|------|-----|---------|
| amō | amātus | loved |
| moneō | monitus | warned |
| dūcō | ductus | led |
| capiō | captus | taken, captured |
| audiō | audītus | heard |
| mittō | missus | sent |
| scrībō | scrīptus | written |
| faciō | factus | made, done |
| dīcō | dictus | said |
| videō | vīsus | seen |
| vincō | victus | conquered |
| ferō | lātus | carried, borne |

## 5. Agent Construction

### 5.1 Agent vs. Instrument

Latin distinguishes between the **agent** (a person performing the action) and the **instrument/means** (a thing used to accomplish the action):

| Construction | Used For | Formation | Example |
|-------------|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Agent** | Person | *ā/ab* + **ablative** | *ā mīlite* (by the soldier) |
| **Means/Instrument** | Thing | **ablative alone** (no preposition) | *gladiō* (by/with a sword) |

### 5.2 Examples

| Latin | Translation | Type |
|-------|-------------|------|
| *Urbs **ā Rōmānīs** capta est.* | The city was captured **by the Romans**. | Agent |
| *Urbs **flammīs** dēlēta est.* | The city was destroyed **by flames**. | Means |
| *Liber **ab auctōre** scrīptus est.* | The book was written **by the author**. | Agent |
| *Liber **atrāmentō** scrīptus est.* | The book was written **with ink**. | Means |

> **Rule**: Use *ā* before consonants, *ab* before vowels and *h*: *ā mīlite* but *ab imperātōre*.

## 6. Uses of the Passive Voice

### 6.1 When the Agent is Unknown or Unimportant

- *Templum aedificātum est.* — The temple was built. (We don't know or care by whom.)

### 6.2 Focus on the Receiver of the Action

- *Caesar ab hostibus circumventus est.* — Caesar was surrounded by the enemy. (Focus on Caesar.)

### 6.3 Impersonal Passive

Latin can use passive constructions with intransitive verbs impersonally:

- *Pugnātum est ācriter.* — It was fought fiercely. (= There was fierce fighting.)
- *Itur ad castra.* — It is gone to the camp. (= People go to the camp.)

### 6.4 Active ↔ Passive Transformation

```
ACTIVE:   Rōmānī    urbem      cēpērunt.
          (nom.)    (acc.)     (active verb)
          The Romans captured the city.

PASSIVE:  Urbs    ā Rōmānīs   capta est.
          (nom.)  (ā + abl.)  (passive verb)
          The city was captured by the Romans.
```

## 7. Introduction to Deponent Verbs

Deponent verbs have **passive forms** but **active meanings**. They have "set aside" (*dēpōnere*) their active forms.

### 7.1 How to Recognize Deponent Verbs

Their principal parts look passive:

| Regular Verb | Deponent Verb |
|-------------|---------------|
| amō, amāre, amāvī, amātum | cōnor, cōnārī, cōnātus sum |
| (4 active-looking PPs) | (passive-looking PPs) |

### 7.2 Common Deponent Verbs

| Verb | Conjugation | Meaning |
|------|-------------|---------|
| cōnor, cōnārī, cōnātus sum | 1st | try, attempt |
| vereor, verērī, veritus sum | 2nd | fear, respect |
| sequor, sequī, secūtus sum | 3rd | follow |
| loquor, loquī, locūtus sum | 3rd | speak |
| morior, morī, mortuus sum | 3rd-iō | die |
| patior, patī, passus sum | 3rd-iō | suffer, endure |
| ūtor, ūtī, ūsus sum | 3rd | use (+ abl.) |
| proficīscor, proficīscī, profectus sum | 3rd | set out, depart |
| hortor, hortārī, hortātus sum | 1st | encourage, urge |
| mīror, mīrārī, mīrātus sum | 1st | wonder at, admire |

### 7.3 Conjugation Example: sequor (I follow)

| Tense | Singular (1st/2nd/3rd) | Plural (1st/2nd/3rd) |
|-------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Present | sequor, sequeris, sequitur | sequimur, sequiminī, sequuntur |
| Imperfect | sequēbar, sequēbāris, sequēbātur | sequēbāmur, sequēbāminī, sequēbantur |
| Future | sequar, sequēris, sequētur | sequēmur, sequēminī, sequentur |
| Perfect | secūtus sum, es, est | secūtī sumus, estis, sunt |

> **Important**: Deponent verbs DO have present and future active participles (*sequēns* = following) and a future active infinitive. Only the finite verb forms are passive in appearance.

## 8. Practice Exercises

### Exercise A: Convert Active to Passive
Transform these active sentences into passive, keeping the same tense.

1. Magister discipulōs docet.
2. Mīlitēs pontem fēcērunt.
3. Rēx cīvēs monēbit.
4. Servī cēnam parāverant.
5. Imperātor cōpiās dūcēbat.

### Exercise B: Identify the Form
For each verb form, state: tense, voice, person, number.

1. capitur
2. ductī erant
3. audīēminī
4. missae sunt
5. monēbāmur
6. amātus erō

### Exercise C: Translate into English

1. Litterae ā poētā scrīptae sunt.
2. Multae urbēs ā Rōmānīs captae erant.
3. Hostēs ā nostrīs mīlitibus victī sunt.
4. Haec verba ab omnibus audiēntur.
5. Fābula ā mātre narrābātur.
6. Pugnātum est usque ad noctem.

### Exercise D: Translate Using Deponent Verbs

1. The soldiers set out at dawn. (proficīscor; use *prīmā lūce* for "at dawn")
2. He follows the leader. (sequor)
3. We tried to conquer the city. (cōnor + infinitive)
4. She spoke with great wisdom. (loquor; *cum magnā sapientiā*)
5. They died bravely. (morior; *fortiter*)

### Exercise E: Agent vs. Means
Fill in the blank with the correct construction (with or without *ā/ab*):

1. Urbs ______ (the soldiers) dēfēnsa est.
2. Urbs ______ (walls) dēfēnsa est.
3. Liber ______ (the poet) scrīptus est.
4. Liber ______ (a pen / calamus) scrīptus est.
