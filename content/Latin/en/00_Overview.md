# Latin (Lingua Latina) — Overview

## About This Topic

This topic provides a comprehensive, progressive curriculum for learning Latin from scratch to an advanced level. The 25 lessons are organized into 5 stages that build upon each other, moving from foundational concepts to reading classical texts.

Latin was the language of ancient Rome and the Roman Empire, spoken from the 8th century BC. Though no longer a native spoken language, Latin remains profoundly influential: it is the ancestor of all Romance languages (Spanish, French, Italian, Portuguese, Romanian), and has contributed extensively to English vocabulary (approximately 60% of English words have Latin roots). Latin is still used in the Vatican, in scientific nomenclature, legal terminology, medical vocabulary, and academic mottos worldwide. It belongs to the Indo-European family, specifically the Italic branch.

## Prerequisites

- No prior Latin knowledge required (the course begins from zero)
- Basic understanding of grammar concepts in your native language is helpful
- Familiarity with English or any Romance language is beneficial but not required

## Difficulty

- **Tier**: Beginner (starts from scratch, progresses to advanced)
- **Estimated scope**: A1 through C1 CEFR levels

## Learning Path

```
Stage 1: Foundations (01-05)
  Alphabet, pronunciation, 1st-2nd declension, essential verbs, present tense
  ↓
Stage 2: Elementary (06-10)
  All declensions, pronouns, prepositions, imperfect/future, numbers
  ↓
Stage 3: Intermediate Grammar (11-15)
  Perfect system, passive voice, participles, subjunctive, deponent verbs
  ↓
Stage 4: Advanced (16-20)
  Subordinate clauses, indirect discourse, conditions, ablative absolute
  ↓
Stage 5: Classical Mastery (21-25)
  Reading prose and poetry, Roman civilization, philosophical/legal Latin
```

## How to Use This Course

1. **Follow the order**: Lessons build on each other. Complete each stage before moving to the next.
2. **Do the exercises**: Every lesson includes practice exercises with answers. Work through them.
3. **Review regularly**: Language learning requires repetition. Review previous lessons periodically.
4. **Practice actively**: Don't just read — write out declensions and conjugations, say sentences aloud, use new vocabulary.
5. **Reference lessons**: Lessons 15 (conjugation), 20 (vocabulary), and 23 (civilization) serve as useful references to return to.
